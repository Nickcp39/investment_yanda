# Tech Investor Watchlist

Date: 2026-05-05

This file lists investors worth studying for technology, internet, software, AI,
and platform-company investing over roughly the past 20 years.

It is not a pure performance ranking. Many funds have private returns, changing
mandates, drawdowns, and incomplete public data. The purpose is to identify
useful lenses that can be tested and converted into agent cards later.

## Public Market / Crossover Investors

| Investor | Vehicle | Why Study | Main Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| James Anderson / Tom Slater | Baillie Gifford / Scottish Mortgage | Long-duration growth, Tesla, Amazon, Tencent-style exceptional growth investing. Strong at thinking about exponential change. | Can overpay for duration; painful in rate shocks and growth drawdowns. |
| Brad Gerstner | Altimeter | Tech/platform concentration, cloud/AI, Meta cost-discipline letter, Nvidia/AI supercycle thinking. | High volatility; may lean into narrative and concentration. |
| Philippe Laffont | Coatue | Technology-focused public/private crossover, data-driven growth investing. | Crossover marks and crowding risk; source transparency varies. |
| Chase Coleman | Tiger Global | Internet/platform and global tech exposure; strong Tiger-style growth heritage. | Major drawdown history; late-stage private tech risk. |
| Ron Baron | Baron Capital | Long-term founder-led bets, especially Tesla and SpaceX. | Extreme key-person/founder concentration and valuation tolerance. |
| Bill Miller | Miller Value / Legg Mason history | Value investor who understood Amazon early; useful for "tech can be value" lens. | Style is broad, not pure tech; drawdowns and idiosyncratic bets. |
| David Gardner | Motley Fool Rule Breakers | Early disruptive-company framework: visionary leaders, category creation, long holding periods. | Newsletter/recommendation context differs from fund management; needs careful validation. |
| Nick Sleep / Qais Zakaria | Nomad Partnership | Internet-scale compounding, customer obsession, scale economies shared; Amazon/Costco lens. | Not tech-only; source pack depends heavily on letters. |
| Cathie Wood | ARK | Useful as an innovation-bubble and thematic-research case study. | Not approved as a default lens because of severe drawdowns and valuation discipline concerns. |

## Venture / Private Tech Investors

These are not public-stock templates, but they are useful for technology worldview,
market-creation, and founder-quality questions.

## Who To Convert Into Agent Cards First

Priority 1:

- Brad Gerstner: modern public-market tech/platform/AI lens.
- James Anderson / Tom Slater: long-duration exceptional growth lens.
- Bill Gurley: marketplace economics and venture valuation discipline.
- Peter Thiel: monopoly and category-creation lens.

Priority 2:

- Ron Baron: founder-led long-duration concentration lens.
- David Gardner: rule-breaker early growth lens.
- Nick Sleep: internet-scale compounding lens.
- Philippe Laffont: crossover tech lens.

Priority 3 / caution:

- Chase Coleman: useful, but must include drawdown and crowding tests.
- Cathie Wood: useful mainly as a thematic-investing stress test, not as a
  default approved agent.

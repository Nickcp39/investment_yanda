# Exceptional Bottleneck / Position Engineering

Last updated: 2026-06-16

Purpose: patch the research pipeline so it does not automatically miss rare companies like NVIDIA in 2024, where classic margin-of-safety valuation looks too expensive but the company controls a real industry bottleneck with extraordinary evidence.

This is not permission to speculate. It is a stricter action layer for exceptional bottleneck companies.

---

## Trigger

Use this module only if all are true:

1. Direction Selection identifies a real bottleneck, not a vague theme.
2. Revenue growth is extraordinary and filed, not narrative.
3. Gross margin / operating margin confirms pricing power.
4. Customers have strong ROI and are increasing spend.
5. The moat is technical, ecosystem, switching-cost, and supply-chain based.
6. Operator underwriting is high.
7. Inversion risks are explicit and monitored.
8. Classic MOS fails at the current spot price.

If any item fails, use the normal valuation / MOS process.

---

## Approved Actions

| Action | When allowed | Risk control |
|---|---|---|
| `WATCH` | Quality is high but price/risks are not acceptable. | No action. |
| `STARTER common` | Bottleneck proof is overwhelming and position size is small. | Size must reflect valuation risk. |
| `SELL PUT` | Strike is a price where you would happily own shares. | Cash-secured only; no leverage. |
| `BUY-WRITE / covered call` | Effective basis falls into underwriting range and upside cap is acceptable. | Must be comfortable losing upside above strike. |
| `DO NOTHING` | Premium or price does not compensate risk. | Always allowed. |

Forbidden:

- Buying calls for speculation.
- Selling options on companies not deeply understood.
- Treating premium as free money.
- Using options to hide a bad valuation.
- Oversizing because the company is exciting.

---

## Required Output

Every use of this module must answer:

1. What is the bottleneck?
2. Why is it durable?
3. What price is too expensive for ordinary common-stock ownership?
4. What basis would be acceptable?
5. What structure gets to that basis, if any?
6. What is the worst case if assigned or called away?
7. What monitor would force exit or stop selling premium?

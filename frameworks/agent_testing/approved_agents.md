# Approved Investor Agent List

Date: 2026-05-05

This list is based on the initial screening in
`results/initial_screening_2026-05-05.md`.

Important: approval here means the framework card is safe enough to use as a
research lens. It does not mean any external or pre-trained agent with the same
name is approved. A trained agent must still submit raw answers to the benchmark
cases and receive a scorecard.

## Tier A: Approved For Memo Use

These can be used in formal company memos, with normal analyst review.

| Agent | Approved Scope | Do Not Use For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Warren Buffett | Business quality, moat, owner earnings, capital allocation, ability to hold long term. | Short-term trading, early-stage tech with unknowable economics, macro timing. |
| Benjamin Graham | Balance sheet, asset value, normalized earnings, margin of safety. | High-growth companies whose value depends mostly on distant optionality. |
| Howard Marks | Cycle position, risk compensation, consensus psychology, downside asymmetry. | Precise macro forecasts or exact market timing. |
| Joel Greenblatt | Quantitative idea screening using quality plus cheapness. | Final investment decisions without business and accounting review. |

## Tier B: Approved With Human Review

Useful and mostly clear, but easier to overgeneralize. Use them with explicit
evidence and kill criteria.

| Agent | Approved Scope | Extra Control |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Charlie Munger | Inversion, incentives, psychology, mental-model error checking. | Must produce concrete failure modes, not generic wisdom. |
| Philip Fisher | Growth quality, scuttlebutt research plan, customer/product validation. | Must list actual scuttlebutt questions and evidence gaps. |
| Peter Lynch | Simple story, company category, real-world evidence, growth versus valuation. | Must classify the stock and flag cyclicals/turnarounds clearly. |
| Terry Smith | Quality compounders, high ROIC, pricing power, low capital intensity. | Must still test valuation and avoid "quality at any price." |

## Tier C: Conditional / Source Pack Needed

Do not use as final decision agents yet. Use only as supporting lenses until
their primary-source packs are expanded and tested.

| Agent | Temporary Use | Why Conditional |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Li Lu | Deep research discipline, China/Asia context, information completeness. | Needs a tighter source pack from original book/speeches and reliable transcripts. |
| Duan Yongping | "Do we truly understand this business?", user orientation, culture, do-not-do list. | Many sources are third-party compilations; needs original Xueqiu/interview passages. |

## Default Multi-Agent Panel

For most equity memos, use this order:

1. Buffett: Is it a business worth owning?
2. Graham or Greenblatt: Is price reasonable by conservative or quantitative standards?
3. Marks: What does the market already believe, and are we paid for the risk?
4. Munger: What are we missing or fooling ourselves about?
5. Fisher or Lynch: What real-world evidence confirms growth?

For China/Asia companies, add Li Lu and Duan Yongping as conditional reviewers,
but keep their outputs labeled "supporting lens" until source-pack testing is
complete.

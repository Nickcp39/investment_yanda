## S001 - PayPal Q2 2021 Earnings Release (8-K Exhibit 99.1, Jul 28 2021)

Headline metrics (quarter ended June 30, 2021):

- Total Payment Volume (TPV): $311 billion, growing 40% (36% on an FX-neutral basis). Exact figure in tables: $310,992 million.
- Net revenue: $6.24 billion ($6,238 million), growing 19% (17% FXN).
- GAAP EPS: $1.00, down 23% vs $1.29 in Q2'20.
- Non-GAAP EPS: $1.15, up 8% vs $1.07 in Q2'20.
- Net New Active Accounts (NNAs): 11.4 million added (vs 21.3 million in Q2'20, down 47%).
- Total active accounts: 403 million, up 16%.
- Payment transactions: 4.7 billion, up 27%.
- Transactions per active account (TTM): 43.5, growing 11%.
- GAAP operating margin: 18.1% (flat YoY). Non-GAAP operating margin: 26.5%, declining 171 bps.
- GAAP operating income: $1.13B; non-GAAP operating income: $1.65B.
- Annualized TPV run rate: ~$1.25 trillion.

EPS composition flagged by the company:

- GAAP and non-GAAP EPS include ~$0.11 benefit from release of credit reserves (vs ~$0.07 negative credit-provision impact in Q2'20).
- GAAP EPS also includes a net unrealized gain of $0.20 on PayPal's strategic investment portfolio (vs $0.58 net unrealized gain in Q2'20).

Mix / channel:

- Merchant Services volume grew 48% spot / 43% FXN, representing 96% of TPV.
- eBay Marketplaces volume declined (37%) spot / (41%) FXN, representing 4% of TPV, compared to 9% in the prior-year period.
- Venmo processed ~$58 billion in TPV, growing 58%.

Cash flow / balance sheet:

- Operating cash flow: $1.31 billion, declining 26%. Free cash flow: $1.06 billion, declining 33%.
- Cash, cash equivalents, and investments: $19.4 billion as of June 30, 2021. Debt: $8.9 billion.
- Repurchased ~765,000 shares in Q2'21, returning ~$200 million to stockholders.

FY'21 guidance:

- TPV growth ~33%-35% (spot and FXN).
- Net revenue to grow ~20% spot / ~18.5% FXN, to ~$25.75 billion.
- GAAP EPS ~$3.49 (vs $3.54 in FY'20); non-GAAP EPS to grow ~21% to ~$4.70.
- NNAs of 52-55 million expected in FY'21.

Q3'21 guidance:

- Net revenue ~$6.15B to ~$6.25B, growth ~13%-14% at current spot rates.
- GAAP EPS ~$0.68 (vs $0.86 prior-year period); non-GAAP EPS ~$1.07, in line with prior-year period.

CEO Dan Schulman quote (in release): "On the heels of a record year, we continued to drive strong results in the second quarter, reflecting some of the best performance in our history. Our platform now supports 403 million active accounts, with an annualized TPV run rate of approximately $1.25 trillion. Clearly PayPal has evolved into an essential service in the emerging digital economy."

Strategic updates noted: launched PayPal Zettle in the U.S.; expanded "Buy Now, Pay Later" (Pay in 4) internationally (Australia, Germany); Venmo crypto fully ramped following April launch; acquired Chargehound and Happy Returns; ~$1 billion in PPP loans to ~43,000 small businesses YTD.

Use: core financial, growth, mix, guidance, and management-framing evidence.

## S003 - Q2 2021 Earnings Call Transcript (Jul 28 2021)

eBay managed-payments migration (quantified by management):

- eBay exited the quarter at under 4% of PayPal's volume; expected to approach ~2.5% of total volumes by year-end.
- eBay expected to be essentially 100% complete with its managed-payments migration by the end of Q3 2021.
- CFO John Rainey: full-year ~7-point negative impact to revenue growth from eBay, corresponding to ~$0.85 negative impact to non-GAAP EPS.
- For Q3: eBay drag expected to be greater than previously thought - about 8.5 points of drag, or ~$465 million negative revenue impact - attributed in part to accelerated pace of international merchant migration and some additional core pressure.

Ex-eBay core performance (management framing):

- Excluding eBay, revenues grew ~32%.
- Excluding eBay, volumes grew ~48% on a spot basis.

Venmo:

- Venmo TPV ~$58 billion, up 58% YoY; Venmo revenue growth accelerated to almost 70% in Q2.

Buy Now, Pay Later:

- ~$3.5 billion in cumulative BNPL TPV, with more than $1.5 billion in Q2 alone; ~650,000 merchants; ~39% cart-size uplift cited.

Use: load-bearing evidence on the size/timing of the eBay headwind and on underlying ex-eBay growth and Venmo/BNPL momentum. Management's optimistic framing is recorded as framing, not verified fact.

## S006 - Crypto on Venmo (Apr 20 2021)

- Venmo launched the ability to buy, hold, and sell cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum, Litecoin, Bitcoin Cash) directly in the app, with as little as $1, for its more than 70 million customers.
- Enabled via PayPal's partnership with Paxos Trust Company and under a conditional BitLicense from the New York State Department of Financial Services (NYDFS).

Use: evidence of Venmo monetization / product expansion ahead of the as-of date.

## S007 - PayPal Investor Day Medium-Term Targets (Feb 11 2021)

At its Feb 11, 2021 virtual investor day, PayPal laid out 2020-2025 medium-term targets:

- Active accounts: from 377 million (2020) toward ~750 million by 2025.
- Annual revenue: more than double to over $50 billion.
- Total payment volume: nearly triple to ~$2.8 trillion.
- Annual free cash flow: from ~$5 billion toward more than $10 billion.
- Strategy emphasis on a digital-wallet "super app," cryptocurrency, and increasing average revenue per user.

Use: establishes the growth narrative and the bar management set; these are company targets/aspirations, not realized results.

## Downside if wrong / upside if right

- **Downside (if WATCH is wrong and it keeps compounding):** an opportunity cost — PayPal continues ~20%+ growth, Venmo/BNPL monetize, and the multiple holds; we miss upside by waiting for a better price. This is a missed-return risk, **survivable and acceptable** under a price-discipline framework.

## Kill criteria (reject / do not add / trim toward cash)

1. **Ex-eBay / organic revenue growth decelerates through the high-teens toward low-double-digits or below** after the eBay migration completes (Q3–Q4'21+) → the durable-compounder thesis at this price breaks → reject / cut.
2. **Take-rate (net revenue / TPV) compresses materially** as competition (Square/Cash App, Apple Pay, BNPL, merchant wallets) pressures pricing → profit-pool quality erodes → don't initiate / trim.
3. **Non-GAAP operating margin keeps contracting** (S&M + tech/dev outgrowing revenue) and **FCF conversion stays depressed** → growth is being bought, not earned → reject / trim.
4. **Management leans further into premium-priced large M&A** (another Honey-style ~30–40x deal) at a near-ATH valuation rather than disciplined organic reinvestment / buybacks → capital-allocation red flag realized → don't add.
5. **NNAs / engagement deteriorate further** (NNAs keep falling YoY, TTM txns/account stall) → the user-growth and 2025-target narrative is outrunning reality → avoid.
6. **Price runs further above ~$283** without any improvement in normalized growth/margins/cash conversion → multiple gets even more stretched → strict no-chase / trim for holders.

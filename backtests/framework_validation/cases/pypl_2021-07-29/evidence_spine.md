## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation

### Evidence (as-of, verifiable)

- Headline growth was strong (TPV +40%, revenue +19%, 403M accounts) but several growth lines decelerated: NNAs nearly halved YoY (11.4M vs 21.3M) and Q3 guidance implied a step-down to ~13%-14% revenue growth.
- The eBay managed-payments migration is a known, company-quantified drag (~7 pts of FY revenue growth; ~8.5 pts in Q3) that is expected to be essentially complete by end of Q3'21.
- Non-GAAP EPS quality was aided by a one-time credit-reserve release (~$0.11) and GAAP by a $0.20 portfolio gain.
- Balance sheet is strong (net cash positive; $19.4B cash+investments vs $8.9B debt).
- Management set very large multi-year targets in Feb 2021 and is layering monetization (Venmo crypto, BNPL, Zettle, Honey-style acquisitions).
- The stock sits near its all-time high; the as-of close (~$283) is a ~$333B market cap, ~60x FY'21 non-GAAP EPS guidance of ~$4.70 (high-growth multiple).

### Interpretation (for a future runner; not concluded here)

- The pivotal, un-resolvable-at-as-of question is whether the ~32% ex-eBay growth management cites is durable as the pandemic e-commerce surge normalizes, or whether decel signals (NNA drop, Q3 guide) point to a faster slowdown.
- Whether the valuation prices in continued ~20%+ growth and a credible path toward the 2025 targets.

### Sentiment / Narrative Only

- Management framing ("essential service," "best performance in our history," 2025 super-app targets) is recorded as framing/aspiration, not as verified outcomes. Peer Square data and BNPL-landscape items are context, not proof of PayPal's own trajectory.

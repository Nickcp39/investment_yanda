**Larry Page** *(co-founder / controller)*

*Formation (unchooseable → value formation; highest weight):*
- **Family & home:** b. **March 26, 1973, East Lansing, Michigan**. **Father Carl Victor Page was a computer-science professor at Michigan State** (an AI/CS pioneer); **mother Gloria taught computer programming**. The house was full of computers, science magazines, and Tesla/Edison lore — a **CS-academic household** where building and tinkering were the family language; reportedly the first kid in his elementary school to turn in an assignment from a word processor. (S016)
- **Place & era:** a Midwestern university-town childhood in the 1970s–80s; comfortable, intellectual, intact family — **not** hardship or immigrant displacement, but **engineering-and-academia as the water he swam in**. (S016)
- **Schooling primary → high:** attended a **Montessori** school (self-directed, question-authority pedagogy he has repeatedly credited); Okemos Montessori then East Lansing High School. (S016)
- **University & education:** **University of Michigan, BS in computer engineering** (where he absorbed Michigan's "leaders and best" / build-things ethos, did a co-op, and led the solar-car / engineering-honor-society world), then **Stanford CS PhD program — left unfinished** to build Google. His advisor's world (Terry Winograd) and the Stanford Digital Library project are where PageRank was born. (S016)
- **First job / first venture:** essentially **Google itself** — the BackRub/PageRank research with Brin (1996–98) was his first real "venture," built in a dorm/lab before any audience. He behaved, pre-investor, as a **systems builder obsessed with the structure of information** and with "10x" moonshots. (S016)
- → **What this likely formed** (cautious pattern, not determinism): an **engineering-first, first-principles, moonshot-scaled** worldview (Montessori "ignore the rules" + CS-academic home + Michigan/Stanford); a **builder who wants control of the long-term agenda** (the super-voting structure is consistent with a founder who values insulation from short-term pressure to pursue big bets). High internal consistency between the formation and the adult choice to lock in control.

*Adult track (compressed):* co-founded Google 1998 with Brin; CEO 1998–2001 and again **2011–2015**; became **CEO of Alphabet at its 2015 creation**, then **stepped down as Alphabet CEO in December 2019**, handing operations to Pichai while **retaining voting control** (S016). Drove the "Alphabet" restructuring (separating moonshots/Other Bets from Google) and the long-horizon bets (self-driving, life sciences). **Does adult conduct match the formed values?** Strongly on the engineering/moonshot axis; the **disengagement from operations since 2019** is the notable *departure* — the builder became a **largely passive controller**, which is itself a signal (the long-term agenda is now set more by Pichai than by Page day-to-day, even though Page can still veto via votes).

*Dark-arc (required):* (1) **Entrenchment by design** — Page (with Brin) holds **>51% of votes on <12% of the economics** (S004, S018); outside owners cannot check him. The structure that protects patience also **removes accountability** for any mistake. (2) **Disengaged controller** — stepping out of operations in 2019 while keeping the votes creates an **accountability vacuum**: the person with ultimate power is not the person running the company. (3) **Communication/visibility retreat** — Page has been notably reclusive and largely absent from public engagement since ~2019, raising "is the controller still steering?" questions the public material cannot answer. (4) **Moonshot capital drain** — the same moonshot appetite has historically funded large, loss-making Other Bets (S001 shows Other Bets operating loss $(1,020)M in Q1'24 alone).

*Reliability axis:* unchooseable facts (East Lansing birth, Carl Page's MSU professorship, Montessori, Michigan BS, Stanford PhD-unfinished, founding 1998) are **high-reliability** (S016, cross-validated). The "Montessori made me question authority" framing is **founder-narrated** — directionally credible but discount the polish. The voting-control mechanics are **primary** (S004/S018), not narrative.

*Guardrails:* no determinism. Direction of departure: formation predicts an engaged engineer-builder; the adult **departed toward passive control** — the risk is not value-drift but a **controller who no longer operates**.

**Sergey Brin** *(co-founder / controller)*

*Formation (unchooseable → value formation; highest weight):*
- **Family & home:** b. **August 21, 1973, Moscow, USSR**, to a **Jewish family that faced Soviet antisemitism** (his father was blocked from physics/astronomy doctoral paths and career advancement because he was Jewish). **Father Michael Brin became a mathematics professor at the University of Maryland; mother Eugenia was a researcher at NASA Goddard.** The family **emigrated from the USSR in 1979** (when Sergey was ~6) after a hard, uncertain exit process. A **refugee-from-an-authoritarian-state** origin in a **mathematician household**. (S017)
- **Place & era:** early childhood under Soviet repression, then an immigrant kid in 1980s Maryland — the **émigré experience of building from nothing in a new country** plus a family memory of **what an unaccountable state does to talented people**. (S017)
- **Schooling primary → high:** **Montessori** elementary (like Page — a shared pedagogical DNA), then Eleanor Roosevelt High School (Maryland); a math-prodigy track from early on. (S017)
- **University & education:** **University of Maryland, BS in mathematics & computer science** (where his father taught), then **Stanford CS PhD program — left unfinished** to build Google with Page, whom he met at Stanford. (S017)
- **First job / first venture:** like Page, **Google itself** was effectively his first venture — the BackRub research, built in the lab pre-audience. He behaved as a **mathematician-hacker** drawn to data-mining and large-scale problems. (S017)
- → **What this likely formed** (cautious): a **mathematician's love of elegant, large-scale systems**; an **immigrant/refugee "build-from-nothing, distrust-arbitrary-authority" frame** (consistent, ironically, with later building a structure where *he* holds unchecked control — the émigré who escaped an unaccountable state and then engineered his own insulation from accountability is a tension worth naming, not resolving). Montessori + math-household + Stanford = same engineering-first, question-rules pattern as Page.

*Adult track (compressed):* co-founded Google 1998; long the technical/research-leaning founder (worked on Google X / moonshots, Glass); **President of Alphabet from its 2015 creation until December 2019**, when he **stepped down alongside Page while retaining voting control** (S017). **Pre-cutoff**, he **re-engaged on AI/Gemini work** — reported by the **WSJ in July 2023** (S017) and around the Dec 2022 **"Code Red"** when founders were reportedly pulled back into AI-response meetings (S024). **Does conduct match formation?** Yes on the technical/research axis (he returned to *hands-on AI*, not to management). The departure from formation is the same as Page's: a refugee-from-unaccountable-power who **built and kept an unaccountable control structure**.

*Dark-arc (required):* (1) **Co-equal entrenchment** — half of the >51% voting bloc; same accountability critique as Page (S004, S018). (2) **Disengaged-then-selectively-re-engaged** — out of operations since 2019, dipping back into AI only when the company faced an existential competitive scare (Dec 2022 "Code Red," S024); a controller who appears on his own terms. (3) **Personal-conduct / governance history** — Brin's well-documented (pre-2019) workplace-relationship episode is part of the public record and a culture/governance optics item for a controller (recorded as a dated fact-of-record, **no psychological diagnosis**). (4) **Moonshot losses** — co-owns the Other-Bets / Glass-style loss history.

*Reliability axis:* unchooseable facts (Moscow birth, Jewish émigré 1979, Michael Brin at Maryland, Montessori, Maryland BS, Stanford PhD-unfinished, founding 1998, Alphabet President to Dec 2019) are **high-reliability** (S017). The July-2023 AI re-engagement is **WSJ-sourced** (S017); the Dec-2022 "Code Red" founder-recall is **news-lead** (S024), used directionally. **The post-cutoff 2025 "in the office every day" quote and I/O 2025 appearance are EXCLUDED** (case_control §6).

*Guardrails:* no determinism. The émigré-distrust-of-arbitrary-authority origin **co-exists** with building a personally unaccountable control structure — flag the tension, do not adjudicate it.

*Formation (unchooseable → value formation; highest weight):*
- **Family & home:** b. **June 10, 1972, Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India**. **Father a senior electrical engineer at the British conglomerate GEC (ran a component-manufacturing plant); mother a stenographer** who stopped working after children. **Modest means:** the family of four reportedly lived in a small two-room home in Chennai, the boys slept in the living room, and the household did not own a car (a Lambretta scooter) or a refrigerator/telephone until Sundar was a teen. He reportedly had a prodigious memory for phone numbers. A **technical but financially constrained** Indian middle-class upbringing. (S014)
- **Place & era:** Chennai/Madurai in 1970s–80s India — scarcity-shaped, education-as-escalator, pre-liberalization economy. **Earned-not-given, resource-constrained** baseline. (S014)
- **Schooling primary → high:** local schools in Chennai (Jawahar Vidyalaya, then Vana Vani at IIT Madras); a strong-student track in a competitive, exam-driven system. (S014)
- **University & education:** **IIT Kharagpur, B.Tech in metallurgical engineering** (one of India's most selective institutions; reportedly earned a scholarship), then **Stanford (MS, materials science) — for which the airfare was a stretch for the family** — and later a **Wharton MBA** (Siebel Scholar / Palmer Scholar). The **IIT → Stanford → Wharton** path is the classic high-talent Indian-immigrant escalator. (S014)
- **First job / first venture:** **Applied Materials** (semiconductor/materials, engineering & product management) and **McKinsey** (management consulting) before Google — i.e. he layered **consulting/management polish on an engineering base** before any public audience. He behaved as a **consensus-building, execution-and-product operator**, not a charismatic founder-evangelist. (S014)
- → **What this likely formed** (cautious): **frugality and earned-progress discipline** (constrained Chennai childhood); **engineering-first credibility** (metallurgy + materials science + Applied Materials); a **diplomatic, consensus-driven, low-ego management style** (consulting + a culture that rewarded getting along) — well suited to running a sprawling, regulated, politically-scrutinized platform, and a recurring question for whether he is *aggressive enough* in a winner-take-all AI race.

*Adult track (compressed):* joined **Google in 2004**; built/shipped **Toolbar, then Chrome** (a defining product win that proved his product judgment), then **Chrome OS and Android**, rising to product chief; **named CEO of Google in 2015** and **CEO of Alphabet in December 2019** when the founders stepped back (S014). Track record ≤ as-of: led the **Jan 20 2023 ~12,000-job layoff** (~6% of staff) and the **"durably re-engineer the cost base"** discipline that shows up directly in Q1'24 (margin to 32% from 25%, lower severance; S001, S020); mobilized the company's AI response after the Dec 2022 **"Code Red"** (S024) and shipped Gemini + AI Overviews by mid-2024. **Does conduct match formation?** Strongly: the frugal, consensus-builder, engineering-credible operator shows up as cost discipline + steady platform stewardship rather than founder-style swashbuckling.

(2) **Pay-vs-layoff optics** — **~$226M 2022 total comp** (S019) landing alongside a mass layoff is a real morale/credibility flag. (3) **"Too consensus / too slow?"** — the same diplomatic temperament that stabilizes a regulated giant is the standing critique that he reacts rather than leads in fast platform shifts; the **AI-Overviews-launched-imperfect** episode (S008–S010) is dated evidence the speed-vs-quality tension is live. (4) **He does not control the votes** — Pichai runs the company but **answers to founders who hold >51%**; his strategic latitude is bounded by controllers who are themselves disengaged.

*Reliability axis:* unchooseable facts (Madurai birth, father at GEC, IIT KGP metallurgy, Stanford MS, Wharton MBA, Applied Materials/McKinsey, joined Google 2004, CEO 2015/Alphabet CEO 2019) are **high-reliability** (S014, cross-validated). The vivid childhood-poverty specifics (two-room home, sleeping in the living room, no fridge/phone) trace to **paywalled profiles relayed via the bio** — treat as **character-pattern**, directionally consistent, not EVIDENCE-tier. Comp/layoff facts are **news-primary** (S019/S020).

*Guardrails:* no determinism; the main open question is **temperament-vs-task fit** (steady steward vs. aggression in an AI race), recorded, not adjudicated.

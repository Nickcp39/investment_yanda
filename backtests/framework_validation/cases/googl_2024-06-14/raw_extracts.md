## C. Share-class & voting / governance — primary (S004 DEF 14A; corroborated by S018)

- Verbatim from the proxy (a shareholder-proposal supporting statement, describing the structure): "In our company's multi-class voting structure, **Class B stock has 10 times the voting rights of Class A**. As a result, **Mr. Page and Mr. Brin currently control over 51% of our company's total voting power while owning less than 12% of stock** – and will continue to retain voting control even though they have stepped down from leading the company."
- Class C is a non-voting class (0 votes); Class A (GOOGL) carries 1 vote.
- Corroborating mechanics (S018, Fortune 2019): Page and Brin together held 84.2% of Class B, equal to ~51.2% of all votes; the five Class B holders were Page (42.9%), Brin (41.3%), Eric Schmidt (8.6%), John Doerr (2.4%), David Drummond (0.03%).

## E. Dated narrative / overhangs (news — context only, public ≤ 2024-06-14)

### Generative-AI-in-Search rollout and its early stumbles (S008, S009, S010)
- After Google I/O (2024-05-14), Google rolled out **AI Overviews** (AI-generated summaries atop Search results) to US users in mid-May 2024.
- The rollout drew criticism: users reported nonsensical/erroneous answers (widely cited examples: suggesting glue on pizza; "eat a rock"). Google's own blog (S008, 2024-05-30) acknowledged the feature produced odd results on some queries and described fixes / scope limits.
- This is the live, as-of "generative-AI-vs-Search" overhang: the core Search experience is being reshaped by generative AI, and the first broad consumer deployment was visibly imperfect. (Recorded neutrally; no judgment on monetization impact.)

### Competitive AI context (S007 framing; S012 peer)
- The Q1'24 cycle was framed around AI competition (ChatGPT/Microsoft Copilot vs Google Gemini). Google positioned Gemini and its AI infrastructure as differentiators (management framing per S007).
- Peer read (S012): Microsoft FY24-Q3 (qtr ended 2024-03-31) revenue $61.9B (+17%); **Microsoft Cloud $35.1B (+23%)**, with Azure + Copilot/AI emphasized — competitive backdrop for Google Cloud and AI.

### Capex / AI-spend curve (S001)
- The capex ramp is already visible in the primary data: **Q1'24 capex $12,012M vs $6,289M in Q1'23** (~+91% YoY), reflecting AI/infra investment. (Recorded as fact from S001; no post-as-of capex guidance used.)

### Digital-advertising environment (S013 peer)
- Peer read (S013): **Meta Q1'24 ad revenue +27% YoY to $35.64B** (its fastest growth since 2021), indicating a broad 2024 re-acceleration in digital advertising — the same environment in which Google's ad lines re-accelerated (Search +14%, YouTube ads +21% YoY in Q1'24 per S001).

### DOJ search-antitrust case — PENDING as of the as-of date (S011)
- DOJ v. Google (search monopoly): a ~10-week bench trial began September 2023; **closing arguments were heard May 2-3, 2024**; the case is **pending before Judge Amit Mehta** as of 2024-06-14 (no ruling issued by the as-of date). DOJ alleged Google holds ~90% of general search share. (The eventual ruling is post-as-of and is NOT included.)

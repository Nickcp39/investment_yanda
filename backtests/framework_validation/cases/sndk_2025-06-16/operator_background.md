### Team dark-arc / fracture inversion

Where could the team break → kill criteria (for the Runner's M5):
- **Governance / oversight failure** — Chair+CEO duality + a brand-new board + a large non-strategic seller (WDC, 19.9%) overhanging the register = **weak external check** early. **Watch:** any value-destructive capital move (e.g. overpaying to grow into the cycle, or diluting) that a stronger independent board would have resisted.

- **CEO/CFO-pair instability** — an untested ~4-month pairing; **watch** for early executive departures (Visoso's historically short tenures are a specific flag) that would reset the standalone leadership clock again.
- **Architect's-sunk-cost bias** — Goeckeler personally designed the spin; **watch** for reluctance to cut/retrench if the standalone economics disappoint (defending the separation thesis past the evidence).
- **Spin-off morale/talent leakage** — a sub-scale, loss-making new entity in a trough risks losing engineering talent. **Watch:** senior NAND/SSD engineering retention.

### Verdict → durability ceiling

- **Team life-arc grade (1–5): 3/5.** A pair of **individually experienced, technically/financially credible operators** — a CEO who is a genuine deep-tech systems builder *and* the architect of this very separation, plus a serial big-tech CFO — but assembled as a **brand-new standalone team (~4 months)** with **no shared crucible**, **adjacent-not-native memory-cycle fit**, a **Chair+CEO governance concentration**, and a cost base **shared with a JV they don't control**. Competent individuals; unproven *as this team, in this business, independently*.
- **Ceiling effect:** the operator read is **neutral — it neither raises nor lowers the ceiling much, but it caps the high end.** It does **not** support the 15–25% Core tier on operator grounds (no long-arc, battle-tested, values-proven team; governance concentration; JV-bounded control).

## 3. Normalized Earnings

As of 2025-06-16, normalized earnings cannot be known. The runner should use a range:

- Bear / no turn: revenue stays around $1.7B-$1.8B per quarter, gross margin remains low 20s, EPS near breakeven or loss.
- Base / mid-cycle turn: revenue grows toward $7B-$8B annualized, gross margin recovers into high 20s / low 30s, operating income becomes meaningfully positive.
- Upside / pricing turn: enterprise SSD demand and price increases push margins well above the trough, creating large incremental earnings power.

At $6.4B equity value, the market did not need a heroic software-like multiple for the stock to work. It needed credible evidence that normalized earnings were not permanently impaired.

## 4. Balance Sheet And Survival

- Cash: $1.507B.
- Long-term debt: $1.927B, with $20M current portion.
- Revolver: company had a $1.5B revolving credit facility from separation; 10-Q notes the term loan facility and covenants.
- Flash Ventures: material fixed-cost, lease guarantee, and funding obligations.
- Immediate solvency risk: not high.
- Cycle / fixed-cost risk: real.

## 5. Red Flags

- Sequential revenue decline in all end markets.
- Cloud revenue still small at $197M.
- Non-GAAP EPS negative.
- Memory remains highly cyclical.
- Sandisk depends heavily on Kioxia Flash Ventures for wafer supply.
- Secondary offering pressure may signal shareholder overhang.

## 6. Handoff

- M5 should not hard-veto unless margin guide fails or balance-sheet obligations become unsustainable.
- M6 should underwrite a small starter with strict add triggers.
- Required next confirmation: revenue above guide midpoint, gross margin at or above guide, and signs that cloud / enterprise demand is growing.

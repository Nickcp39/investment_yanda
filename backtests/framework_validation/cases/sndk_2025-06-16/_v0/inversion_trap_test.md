## 3. False-Cheap Checklist

- Low P/E based on trailing EPS? no; trailing EPS is negative.
- Low P/B as margin of safety? not primary; goodwill and JV obligations make book value hard to rely on.
- High dividend yield? no.
- Peak-cycle earnings being capitalized? no as of 2025-06-16; earnings are trough-like.
- One-time windfall treated as recurring? no.
- Reported EPS diverges from owner earnings? yes, because goodwill impairment dominates GAAP loss.

## 4. Structural Decline Test

- Revenue quality: mixed; cloud growth high year over year but small and sequentially down.
- Unit economics: weak current gross margin, but guided improvement.
- Pricing power: early positive; management said price increases began.
- Market share: not enough evidence to assert gain.
- Customer behavior: enterprise SSD demand likely improving per TrendForce.
- Substitute / bypass risk: HDD and hybrid architectures can compete in bulk storage.
- Profit-pool migration: possible toward suppliers if supply remains tight, but not durable enough to call a moat.

## 5. Survival / Balance Sheet Test

- Net debt: roughly $0.44B if comparing cash to long-term debt, before considering obligations.
- Debt maturity: term loan due 2032; current portion modest.
- Required capex / commitments: material due to Flash Ventures.
- FCF after maintenance capex: not proven; nine-month OCF near breakeven and capex $159M.
- Dilution risk: no primary issuance in the June secondary; Sandisk did not receive proceeds.
- Liquidity risk: acceptable for small starter, but monitor if cycle worsens.

## 6. Kill Criteria

If any of these happen, M6 must cut size or exit:

1. Q4 FY2025 revenue misses guidance or gross margin fails to reach guided range.
2. Management reverses price increases or reports worsening inventory / utilization.
3. Cloud / enterprise revenue fails to grow over the next two quarters.
4. Debt or Flash Ventures commitments force cash burn or capital raise.
5. The stock rises above the add/hold band before margin evidence arrives.

# M3 Profit Pool / Durability - SNDK 2025-06-16

## 0. Module Verdict

- M3 signal: +1
- Profit-pool owner: NAND producers with scale, technology, customer access, and disciplined supply.
- Durability: medium-low as a moat, medium as a cycle-inflection profit pool.
- Moat type: scale, technology ramp, Kioxia JV manufacturing access, brand / channel, enterprise relationships.

## 1. Value Chain

| participant | role | pricing power | risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hyperscalers / enterprise customers | Buy high-capacity storage for AI and cloud infrastructure. | High in weak supply periods; lower when supply tightens. | Can delay, dual-source, or switch mix. |
| Sandisk | Designs, sells, and supplies NAND flash products and SSDs. | Improves when industry supply tightens. | Commodity exposure, execution, Kioxia dependency. |
| Kioxia / Flash Ventures | Major wafer production partner. | Critical input and capacity source. | JV obligations and partner execution risk. |
| PC / client / consumer channels | Large volume demand pools. | Cyclical and price sensitive. | Weak demand can offset enterprise strength. |
| Competitors | Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron, Kioxia-related supply, others. | Industry behavior determines ASP cycle. | Overproduction can collapse margin. |

## 2. Profit Pool Claim

The profit pool is not a permanent monopoly. It is a cycle-sensitive NAND margin recovery:

- Suppliers had been reducing utilization and bit growth.
- AI infrastructure and enterprise SSD demand were beginning to tighten supply.
- If ASP and mix improved, Sandisk's margin recovery could be large because the revenue base was already around $1.7B-$1.8B per quarter.
- A newly independent company could be valued on normalized earnings rather than a conglomerate / carve-out discount.

## 3. Durability Tests

| test | evidence | result |
|---|---|---|
| Customer ROI | AI and enterprise infrastructure requires storage capacity and performance. | pass, but not company-specific enough |
| Supply constraint | Sandisk reduced utilization and TrendForce saw enterprise SSD undersupply risk. | pass |
| Technology position | BiCS 8 ramp and broad NAND portfolio. | pass, needs execution proof |
| Pricing power | Management commenced price increases; TrendForce saw up to 10% Q3 enterprise SSD price increase. | pass, early |
| Substitute risk | HDD and other storage architectures compete on cost; SSD wins performance use cases. | mixed |
| Moat permanence | NAND remains competitive and cyclical. | fail as quality-compounder moat |

## 4. Operator / Capital Allocation

- CEO David Goeckeler came from WDC and is directly tied to the separation strategy.
- Management's early actions were rational for a commodity cycle: reduce supply, restrain capex, begin price increases.
- The secondary offering was not Sandisk raising cash; it was WDC monetizing retained shares. That matters because dilution is not the issue, but technical supply is.
- Operator record as a standalone public company is too short to award high confidence.

## 5. Risks For M5

- Profit pool can disappear if NAND supply ramps too fast.
- Kioxia / Flash Ventures obligations can force cash needs or fixed-cost absorption.
- Client and consumer weakness can overwhelm AI / cloud strength.
- If the thesis is only "AI needs storage," competitors may capture the upside.

## 6. Handoff

- M4 must verify that gross margin and revenue are starting to turn.
- M5 must test whether this is a false cyclical inflection.
- M6 should size as a cyclical / spin-off starter, not a durable compounder core.

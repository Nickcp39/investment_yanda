## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation

### Evidence

- The company was a very recent spin-off with only one standalone reported quarter.
- The Form 10/A and S-1/A created a fuller audit trail for separation mechanics, retained WDC stake, and resale overhang.
- Reported GAAP earnings were deeply negative, but the largest loss item was a non-cash goodwill impairment.
- Management had already reduced supply and commenced price increases.
- Independent industry data before the as-of date pointed toward AI / enterprise SSD demand and potential NAND undersupply.
- Peer evidence from Micron supported the same broad memory-cycle setup while confirming the picture was mixed, not a clean all-clear.
- A WDC secondary created likely technical selling pressure near $38.50.

### Interpretation

- The correct question is not whether trailing GAAP EPS looks cheap. It does not.
- The better question is whether a newly separated NAND business was priced like a weak cyclical while industry supply / demand was starting to improve.
- The evidence supports an asymmetric setup, but not a high-confidence full position.

### Sentiment / Narrative Only

- AI-storage language from management supports the thesis direction, but the core proof must come from supply discipline, pricing, margin, and enterprise SSD demand.

## 4. Handoff

- M2 can use AI / enterprise SSD demand, supply discipline, and forced-seller spin-off setup.
- M3 must avoid overstating moat; Sandisk has scale / technology / channels but no monopoly.
- M4 must normalize GAAP loss and track gross margin recovery.
- M5 must test memory-cycle downside, Kioxia / Flash Ventures obligations, and false turnarounds.
- M6 must convert uncertainty into small starter size plus explicit confirmation triggers.

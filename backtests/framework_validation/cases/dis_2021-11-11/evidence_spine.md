## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation (neutral)

### Evidence (as reported)
- Reported revenue grew, GAAP profit returned at the company level, and the full-year DTC loss
  narrowed YoY — but the most-watched metric (Disney+ net adds) decelerated sharply, and the
  quarterly DTC operating loss widened sequentially/YoY.
- Linear Networks remained large and profitable in absolute terms ($1.6B quarterly operating
  income) but was flat-to-down; domestic-channel operating income fell 14% in the quarter.
- Parks rebounded hard off COVID lows, with management citing strong per-capita spend and a new
  paid upcharge (Genie+); the segment still carried ~$1B of fiscal-2021 COVID-related costs and
  reduced operating capacity.
- The balance sheet shows ~$54B total borrowings against ~$16B cash and ~$93B equity, plus ~$78B
  goodwill (largely from the 2019 Fox acquisition) and ~$9B redeemable NCI tied to Hulu.

### Open questions (carried into the future test, not answered here)
- Is the Disney+ deceleration a temporary lull (markets + content timing, per management) or the
  start of a structurally lower growth path?
- How fast can DTC losses turn toward the stated FY2024 profitability target, and at what content cost?
- Is linear-network decline gradual/manageable or accelerating, and how much does it subsidize DTC?
- How durable is the parks per-capita / Genie+ pricing strength versus COVID-driven pent-up demand?
- What is the right normalized earnings base given COVID distortions on both DTC (peak engagement)
  and Parks (suppressed then recovering)?

### Sentiment / narrative only (not core proof)
- Management's "non-linear" and "dam will break on content" framing and the reiterated FY2024
  target are company optimistic framing; treated as commentary, not verified fact.

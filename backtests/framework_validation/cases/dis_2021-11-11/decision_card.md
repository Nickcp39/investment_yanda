## How the 2.5/5 operator grade affected the durability ceiling (explicit)

METHODOLOGY §2 makes the position ceiling a function of **durability**, and durability **explicitly includes the founder/core-team life-arc grade**: a proven, cohesive, values-consistent long-arc team *raises* the ceiling; a fragile / one-person / misaligned team *lowers* it. Here the grade is a **LOW 2.5/5 ("fragile transition" band)**, and it acts as a ceiling DEPRESSANT at the worst possible moment:

- The **architect and dealmaker (Iger) exits by end-2021** — peak key-man transition risk precisely as the hardest DTC-execution year begins; institutional memory and the franchise-M&A relationships leave with him.
- The **incoming CEO (Chapek) lacks the content pedigree** an IP-driven company's moat depends on; his edge is ops/monetization/distribution, not the creative engine that feeds DTC.
- **Active creative-vs-business culture friction** (the Oct-2020 DMED reorg stripped content chiefs of P&L/greenlight authority under loyalist Daniel; senior content execs exited) is a direct **IP-pipeline risk** — the thing that justifies the entire streaming bet.
- A **loyalist-heavy inner circle + insider CFO** means limited independent counterweight to a determined CEO once Iger is gone, with chairman succession unsettled at as-of.

Concretely: even if I were otherwise inclined to a starter, this grade (a) reinforces the WATCH-over-STARTER call by widening the execution/governance discount, and (b) caps any *future* position small — it removes the "durable, cohesive long-arc team" justification that would be required to size a Disney-scale franchise toward a Core tier. Where GOOGL's 4/5 team *raised* its ceiling to a 12% Confirmed tier, DIS's 2.5/5 team is a reason the ceiling stays at tracking now and ~2% even on a future re-rate. The people line here **caps upside and widens the discount**, consistent with the operator dossier's "ceiling effect: LOWER" conclusion.

At the as-of price this is **UNRESOLVED**, and the Q4 print (subscriber deceleration + widening quarterly DTC loss + ARPU -9%) leans the wrong way, while ~71x ex-items / ~146x GAAP leaves no margin of safety. This holds new money at **WATCH** — explicitly **not** a structural-zero veto, because the IP moat is intact and there is no bankruptcy path. The discipline is to require **either a normalized-base entry price or a confirmed DTC-economics inflection** before committing capital.

## Downside if wrong / upside if right

- **Downside (the trap realizes):** the FY2024 DTC target slips, linear erodes faster than DTC can replace its profit, and the ~71x/146x multiple compresses toward a normalized media multiple on a distorted base — a meaningful **drawdown-to-fair-value / dead-money** risk. Survivable (no bankruptcy path; fortress IP + profitable linear + high-pricing Parks) — this is a de-rate risk, not a permanent-total-loss risk, which is exactly why M5 warns rather than vetoes.
- **Upside (the compounder thesis wins):** DTC unit economics bend to the FY2024 target, the IP library + Parks pricing power compound, and the platform shift converts to a durable, profitable streaming annuity — at which point the thesis can be re-underwritten and a real (still operator-capped) position built on confirmation.

## Triggers

- **Re-rate-to-buy trigger (new money):** DTC economics visibly inflect — sequential operating-loss narrowing + ARPU stabilization + net-add re-acceleration toward the 230-260M path — AND a stabilized post-Iger creative/governance structure; OR price into the low-$100s that discounts a normalized base.
- **Trim trigger (existing holder):** the four methodology triggers — thesis wrong / price absurd vs value / a better idea needs the capital / thesis disconfirmed. At as-of, the rich multiple on a distorted base + the operator/transition risk already justify trimming into strength; high weight alone is never the reason.

## Kill criteria (stay out / cut existing exposure / re-underwrite)

1. Disney+ net adds stay decelerating / ARPU keeps falling and the FY2024 230-260M / DTC-profitability path is pushed out → the platform shift is value-destructive.
2. Quarterly DTC operating losses keep widening rather than narrowing while content spend rises → the land-grab is not converting to a profitable annuity.
3. Linear Networks decline accelerates (rev/OI falling faster than mid-single digits) → the cash cow funding the transition erodes faster than DTC can replace it.
4. Parks per-cap / Genie+ strength proves to be pulled-forward COVID demand that fades as capacity normalizes → the one clearly-working engine was a reopening pop, not durable pricing.
5. The post-Iger creative/governance structure produces a visible exodus of top franchise talent or a thinning slate → the IP engine that justifies the DTC bet is impaired (operator-risk realized).
6. Multiple compresses toward a normalized media multiple on a de-rate → confirms there was no trailing MOS at the as-of price (a foreseeable drawdown-to-fair-value).

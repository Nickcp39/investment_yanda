## S002 - Q4 FY2021 Earnings Call Transcript (2021-11-10)

- Chapek (prepared remarks): "...confident we are on the right trajectory to achieve the guidance that we provided at last year's Investor Day - reaching between 230 and 260 million paid Disney+ subscribers globally by the end of fiscal year 2024, and with Disney+ achieving profitability that same year."
- Chapek (Q&A, on subscriber cadence): "we're real pleased with where we're sitting. But again, it's not going to be a linear rate quarter-to-quarter." He said the recovery in the growth rate "is really going to come in the third and the fourth quarters" [of fiscal 2022], driven first by roughly doubling the number of Disney+ markets "to more than 160 by FY23," then by the content slate announced "last December," with "a very strong cadence of content, which will now hit the pipeline in the second half of this year."
- Analyst (Ben Swinburne, Morgan Stanley) framed the two issues weighing on the stock this year as "the Disney+ net adds" and "parks margins," and asked management to address both.
- Chapek on Parks demand: "we are very bullish. We're seeing incredible 30% increases in per caps... at per caps that are much higher than we've traditionally seen." On Genie+: "1/3 of our guests at Walt Disney World are buying the Genie+ upgrade at $15... per-guest per-day... a very, very material increase for us in per caps, but also in margins."
- Parks operations: "Attendance trends continued to strengthen at our domestic parks, with Walt Disney World Q4 attendance up double digits versus Q3, and Disneyland attendance continuing to strengthen significantly from its reopening."
- McCarthy (CFO): fiscal 2021 capital expenditures were $3.6 billion, ~$400 million lower than fiscal 2020's $4.0 billion. Goal "to more than double the number of countries we are currently in to over 160 by fiscal year '23."

Use: management framing of the streaming-deceleration cause (timing/markets/content), the
reiterated FY2024 subscriber + profitability target, and the parks-recovery economics.

## S005 - Hollywood Reporter, Disney Q4 2021 earnings (2021-11-10)

- Revenue $18.53B and EPS $0.37 versus Wall Street expectations of **$18.79B revenue and $0.51 EPS** (both missed).
- Disney+ ~118M with net adds of only ~2M, "falling well short of analyst expectations around 126 million."
- Chapek had "forecast at a Goldman Sachs conference in September that the company expected to end the quarter with 'low-single-digit' subscriber growth."
- Disney+ ARPU down 9% YoY to $4.12; Hulu +700K to 39.7M; ESPN+ +2.3M to 17.1M.
- Parks & experiences +99% to $5.45B as attendance normalized at Disneyland and Walt Disney World.
- Chapek: "the single most effective way to grow our streaming platforms worldwide is with new content."

Use: precise consensus-vs-actual figures (revenue, EPS, subscribers) and the September pre-signal.

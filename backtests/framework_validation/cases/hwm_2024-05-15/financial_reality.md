## 2. The valuation / normalization reality (the warning half — pulls to 0)

This is the crux for a `quality_compounder` bought at a 52-week high, and where M4 earns its keep:
- **As-of multiple is rich:** at $82.75, **trailing P/E ≈ 45×** (FY2023 adj EPS $1.84) and **forward P/E ≈ 35×**
  (FY2024 adj-EPS guide midpoint ~$2.35). On EV/EBITDA, ~$33.8B equity + ~$3.1B net debt ≈ **~$37B EV / ~$1.75B
  FY2024E EBITDA ≈ ~21× forward EV/EBITDA.** These are premium, growth-stock multiples for an industrial.
- **Is the margin a cyclical peak?** Record 24% EBITDA margin *could* be cyclically elevated (aftermarket-rich mix +
  the GTF spares bulge + tight supply supporting price). Part of the margin is **structural** (aftermarket mix shift,
  operating leverage) and likely persists; part may be **cyclical** (the GTF grounding peaks Q1'24 then declines —
  S008 — and supply tightness eases as ramps catch up — S009). The honest read: margins are **somewhat elevated**,
  not purely structural — so forward EPS should not assume the record margin compounds indefinitely.
- **Normalized-earnings frame:** even crediting durable mid-cycle margins, ~35× forward **prices in years of
  high-teens earnings growth**. The business can plausibly deliver that *if* the aftermarket upturn runs and MAX
  recovers — but the price leaves **little margin of safety** if growth merely normalizes to mid-single-digits. The
  multiple is the risk, not the financials.

## 4. Cycle / normalization check (the `quality_compounder` discipline)

- **Where in the cycle?** This is **not a trough** (the `cyclical_inflection` mistake to avoid). It is **mid-to-later
  in a recovery + a special spares bulge** — earnings and margins are at/near records, the stock at its high. The
  correct discipline is therefore the *opposite* of trough-buying: **do not extrapolate peak margins, and demand a
  sensible entry** (M6).
- **But not a peak to short either:** the secular backlog (~11–12yr, S015), the installed-base aftermarket annuity,
  and the *conservatively-assumed* MAX rate (~20/mo, with recovery as un-assumed upside) mean the earnings stream is
  **more durable than a classic cyclical peak**. The risk is *paying too much for durable-but-not-accelerating-
  forever* earnings, not *buying a cliff*.

## 5. Financial-reality verdict

- **Accounting/cash quality: strongly confirms the thesis** (real, accelerating, clean GAAP-vs-adjusted, capex-light,
  high FCF conversion, IG balance sheet, counter-cyclical capital returns). On its own → **+1 to +2**.
- **Valuation vs possibly-elevated margins: warns** (~45× trailing / ~35× forward / ~21× EV/EBITDA at a 52-week high,
  with margins partly cyclical and little margin of safety if growth normalizes). On its own → **−1**.
- **Net M4 = 0**, and — per the `quality_compounder` gate — **the warning is expressed as an ENTRY/SIZE discipline,
  not a veto.** The balance sheet removes any survival concern, so M4 does **not** fire a hard veto; it says: *own the
  quality, but let price and the margin-durability evidence govern how much and at what level* (→ M6).

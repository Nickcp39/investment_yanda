## C. Q1 2024 call — management quotes (S005, 2024-05-02; two corroborating mirrors)

- **Plant (737 MAX):** "*a further reduction in build to approximately 20 aircraft per month average for the year is
  a more secure assumption than that previously reported of 34 aircraft per month*"; "*the prospect of going up to
  rate 42 and rate 47 per month is now unlikely in 2024.*"
- **Plant (net replan):** "*we net all of this replanning out to an overall increase of approximately $200 million of
  revenue for 2024*"; "*we've increased the guide by $200 million of revenue at midpoint and the margin rate from 23%
  to 24%.*"
- **Plant (wide-body):** cut the wide-body assumption "*from six aircraft per month down to five… we've not taken it
  down to three*," citing "*higher wide-body build especially in preparing to move into 2025.*"
- **Giacobbe (drivers):** growth "*supported by demand for new more fuel-efficient aircraft… and increased spares
  demand for engines*"; "*defense aerospace was also strong, up 12%, driven by fighter programs and engine spares
  demand*," with **F-35 "up 27% year-over-year."**
- **Giacobbe (margins):** "*Incremental flow-through of revenue to EBITDA was a healthy 35%… EBITDA margin was a
  record at 24%.*"
- **Plant (capital allocation):** dividend "*40% increase… from $0.05 per share to a total of $0.07 per share*";
  "*The majority of the other uses of free cash flow… will be share buyback in 2024.*"
- **Giacobbe (rating):** "*Moody's Q1 ratings upgrade to investment grade… we are now rated as investment grade with
  all 3 rating agencies.*"
- *Note (candor):* management did **not** use the word "supercycle" on this call; they framed it as broad-based
  spares/aftermarket + defense + IGT strength. Recorded so the term is not over-attributed.

## E. Aerospace-industry backdrop (dated ≤ 2024-05-15)

- **GTF powder-metal crisis (S008, 2023-09-11):** RTX ~$3B Q3'23 charge (net share); program cost up to ~$7B;
  ~600–700 incremental engine shop visits 2023→2026; **avg ~350 A320neo aircraft grounded/yr through 2026, peak in
  Q1 2024.** Structural pull for replacement hot-section parts and spare engines.
- **Engine aftermarket hot (S009 GE Q1'24, 2024-04-23):** commercial services +39%, internal shop-visit revenue
  +35%, **spare-parts sales +25%** "from improved material availability." (S010 Safran FY2023: civil aftermarket
  +32.9%. S011 RTX Q1'24: P&W +23%.)
- **Air traffic recovered (S012 IATA, 2024-01-31):** FY2023 RPK 94.1% of 2019; Q4'23 98.2%; domestic 103.9%.
- **Narrow-body ramp up (S013 Airbus Q1'24, 2024-04-25):** "*progress towards rate 75/mo [A320 family] in 2026*";
  backlog 8,626.
- **Record backlog (S015, 2024-01-15):** combined Airbus+Boeing YE2023 backlog ~14,814 aircraft (~11.7–11.8 years).
- **Boeing near-term cut (S014 Boeing Q1'24, 2024-04-24):** Q1'24 deliveries 83 (67×737), lowest since mid-2021; ran
  **below the FAA 38/mo cap** after the Jan-5-2024 Alaska MAX-9 door-plug event; Q1 net loss $355M. → near-term
  narrow-body new-build headwind (HWM assumes ~20/mo MAX for 2024).
- **Defense steady (S016, 2024-01-26):** F-35 2024 plan 75–110 jets; build held ~156/yr (TR-3 = storage/timing).
- **Truck down-cycle (S017, 2024-03):** FTR March-2024 forecast ~248,800 Class 8 units, **down ~26% vs 2023** →
  headwind for Forged Wheels.
- **Titanium constraint (S018):** Boeing/Airbus historically ~80%/~60% Russian-titanium-dependent (VSMPO);
  deliberately excluded from sanctions; Airbus diversified to ~20% by 2023. System ramp constraint — caps volume but
  supports pricing/share for qualified suppliers.

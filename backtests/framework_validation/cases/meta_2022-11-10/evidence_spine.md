## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation (descriptive only)

### Evidence (what the sources establish)

- The core Family-of-Apps business remained large and profitable ($9.3B Q3 segment operating income) even as total revenue declined for the first time YoY and total operating margin compressed to 20% from 36%.
- The reported earnings decline was driven by a 19% rise in total costs and expenses on a 4% revenue decline, plus a large and growing Reality Labs operating loss ($(3,672)M in the quarter).
- The balance sheet showed net cash (~$31.9B), modest long-term debt ($9.9B), and large ongoing buybacks ($21.1B over nine months).
- Two dated events bracket the as-of date: the Q3 print (2022-10-26/-27) and the Nov 9 layoff + cost-discipline announcement.
- Independent peer data (Snap, Alphabet) corroborate a broad digital-advertising deceleration in the same period, i.e., the revenue pressure was not purely company-specific.
- The 2023 expense/capex guidance issued on Oct 26 implied rising spending; the Nov 9 message then signaled an intent to lower the cost trajectory relative to that guidance.

### Items that are management framing (not verified fact)

- Zuckerberg's statements that the company is "deeply underestimated" and that the core business is "among the most profitable ever built." (Recorded as framing per S003.)
- Transcript characterizations of 2023 expenses "roughly flat" and headcount "flat to down" (S004) are paraphrased, not verbatim, and are lower-confidence.

### Sentiment / narrative only

- Analyst price-target cuts (S007) and the "investment year" framing are sentiment context, not core proof.
- The Oct 27 and Nov 10 price moves (S006, S011) are market reactions, not fundamentals.

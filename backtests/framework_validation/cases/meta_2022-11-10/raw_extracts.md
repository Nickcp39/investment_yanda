## S003 — Meta Nov 9 2022 Layoff Announcement (8-K Exhibit 99.1, 2022-11-09)

Verbatim load-bearing excerpts from "Mark Zuckerberg's Message to Meta Employees," dated November 9, 2022:

- "I've decided to reduce the size of our team by about 13% and let more than 11,000 of our talented employees go."
- "We are also taking a number of additional steps to become a leaner and more efficient company by cutting discretionary spending and extending our hiring freeze through Q1."
- "I want to take accountability for these decisions and for how we got here."
- On cause: "Not only has online commerce returned to prior trends, but the macroeconomic downturn, increased competition, and ads signal loss have caused our revenue to be much lower than I'd expected. I got this wrong, and I take responsibility for that."
- On priorities: "We've shifted more of our resources onto a smaller number of high priority growth areas — like our AI discovery engine, our ads and business platforms, and our long-term vision for the metaverse."
- On cost actions: "We've cut costs across our business, including scaling back budgets, reducing perks, and shrinking our real estate footprint. We're restructuring teams to increase our efficiency."
- US severance: "16 weeks of base pay plus two additional weeks for every year of service, with no cap"; six months of healthcare cost coverage; RSU vesting through November 15, 2022.
- "We're also extending our hiring freeze through Q1 with a small number of exceptions."
- "This will give us the ability to control our cost structure in the event of a continued economic downturn. It will also put us on a path to achieve a more efficient cost structure than we outlined to investors recently."
- "I'm currently in the middle of a thorough review of our infrastructure spending. As we build our AI infrastructure, we're focused on becoming even more efficient with our capacity ... I believe we can achieve this while spending less."
- "We're making all these changes for two reasons: our revenue outlook is lower than we expected at the beginning of this year, and we want to make sure we're operating efficiently across both Family of Apps and Reality Labs."
- "I believe we are deeply underestimated as a company today ... Our core business is among the most profitable ever built with huge potential ahead."

Use: the second anchor event; documents the scale of the workforce reduction, the cost-discipline pivot, and management framing of cause (macro, competition, ATT signal loss). Management's optimistic self-characterization is recorded as management framing, not as verified fact.

## S004 — Meta Q3 2022 Earnings Call (2022-10-26) [paraphrase; verbatim precision limited]

Management commentary, paraphrased (treat as management framing, not verified fact; the retrieval of this transcript returned paraphrase rather than verbatim quotes — see material_audit.md):

- Zuckerberg framed the company around "doing more with less" and becoming "a leaner and more efficient company," with plans to slow headcount growth materially versus prior years.
- CFO (Dave Wehner) indicated 2023 total expenses would be roughly flat versus 2022, with headcount approximately flat to slightly down as efficiency was prioritized.
- Reality Labs operating losses were stated to be expected to grow in 2023.
- Revenue pressure was attributed to Apple's iOS/ATT privacy changes (signal loss), macroeconomic uncertainty / weaker advertiser demand, intensifying competition (short-form video / Reels), and FX.
- Reels engagement was growing while monetization remained below Feed levels and was described as ramping over time.
- AI-driven discovery / recommendations were cited as contributing to engagement growth.

Use: management context for cost discipline, RL loss trajectory, and ad headwinds. Lower source-quality confidence than S001-S003 because extracts are paraphrased, not verbatim.

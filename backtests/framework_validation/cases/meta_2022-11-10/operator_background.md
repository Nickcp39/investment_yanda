### Per-person life-arc — formation first (the unchooseable years are the most truthful)

**Mark Zuckerberg** — Founder, Chairman & CEO, controlling shareholder

*Formation (unchooseable → value formation; highest weight):*
- **Family & home:** Born 1984-05-14 in White Plains NY, raised in **Dobbs Ferry**, an affluent Westchester suburb. Father **Edward Zuckerberg, a dentist** who ran his practice from the home; mother **Karen, a psychiatrist** who later managed the practice. Three sisters (Randi, Donna, Arielle). Intact, professional, comfortable, education-prizing Jewish-American household (S019).
- **Place & era:** 1980s–90s suburban Northeast US; the PC-then-internet generation. He had a home computer and was encouraged into it early — at ~12 he wrote **"ZuckNet,"** a messaging program linking the house to his father's dental office (S019). Formative fact: software as a tool that *connects people*, built for a real user (his father), before any audience existed.
- **Schooling primary → high:** Ardsley public schools, then transferred to **Phillips Exeter Academy** (elite New Hampshire boarding school), where he excelled in classics and won science/math prizes (S001-search/S019). Exeter is a status-and-rigor environment; he was already shipping software (a music player, AIM-plugin) noticed by Microsoft/AOL before college.
- **University & education:** **Harvard** (entered 2002), studied computer science and psychology — the psychology + CS combination maps directly onto a social-network product. Built "TheFacebook" in his dorm in **February 2004** at age 19; **left Harvard in 2004 (sophomore year)** to run it (S012 confirms "attended Harvard," S019/search confirm the dorm-founding and dropout). No formal mentor structure — self-directed from the start.
- **First venture:** Facebook *is* the first venture — there is no pre-Facebook employer. He has never worked for anyone else. The unchooseable read: he learned to operate by *being the controller from day one*, never as a subordinate.
- → **What this likely formed (cautious, not determinism):** engineering-first product instinct; comfort with total control and conviction over consensus; long-horizon "connect the world" mission framing; and — because he was never anyone's employee — a low instinct to defer to outside authority (boards, markets). The dual-class structure is consistent with formation, not a late invention.

*Adult track (compressed, ≤ as-of):* Scaled Facebook from a dorm to ~3.7B monthly users and a profitable ad franchise (S001); made several correct platform pivots (mobile, video/Reels, AI discovery, S004). Crucible behavior: in the Nov 9 2022 layoff message he publicly owns the misjudgment of revenue/ATT — "I got this wrong, and I take responsibility" (S003) — and executes the first large cost-cut in company history. **Adult conduct broadly matches formation** (engineer-founder, conviction-led, mission-framed). Motivation/fire: clearly still building (the metaverse bet, S001/S003). Skin in the game: maximal — ~$294B-cap company, ~13% economic / 56.9% voting (S012).

*Dark-arc (required):* (1) **Governance:** he has engineered and defended a structure under which **no shareholder can remove him or stop him** — the 2022 ballot's dual-class and independent-chair proposals (S012) exist precisely because outside owners cannot otherwise constrain him. (2) **Candor/harm:** the Cambridge Analytica saga produced a **record $5B FTC penalty** and a 20-year order that had to **strip "unfettered control"** from him at the privacy-committee level (S017); the Frances Haugen "Facebook Files" (S018) allege the firm knew of platform harms and prioritized growth. (3) **Over-confidence:** simultaneously guiding rising 2023 capex/opex into a money-losing segment while the core revenue declined YoY (S001), framing the company as "deeply underestimated" (S003) — a pattern of large conviction bets others cannot veto.

*Reliability axis:* unchooseable facts (Dobbs Ferry, dentist/psychiatrist parents, Exeter, Harvard CS, 2004 dorm-founding) are documented and dated (S019, S012) → trust most. His own later origin narrative (the "connect the world" mission story) is polished → discount. Third-party records (FTC order S017, Haugen testimony S018, proxy S012) are weighted above self-narrative.

*Guardrails:* no determinism — formation explains the *origin* of his control instinct, not its outcome. Note the **direction of departure**: nothing in his formation departed from it; if anything he doubled down on founder-control as the company grew (the tell is the dual-class defense), which is itself the signal.

**Andrew "Boz" Bosworth** — CTO & Head of Reality Labs (owns the metaverse bet)

*Formation (unchooseable → value formation; highest weight):*
- **Family & home:** Born 1982-01-07 in Santa Clara County, California; **grew up on a farm in Saratoga, CA**, and did **4-H** as a child (S014). Farm + 4-H is an unchooseable formation of hands-on, build-and-maintain, agricultural-work-ethic values — notable for someone who became a hardware/infrastructure leader.
- **Place & era:** Silicon Valley itself, 1980s–90s — geographically inside the computing industry as it formed.
- **Schooling → University:** **Harvard, class of 2004, computer science** (S014). The load-bearing relationship: he **met Zuckerberg there as a teaching assistant in an AI class** — i.e., the CTO–founder bond predates Facebook and is a teacher/peer relationship, not a hire-off-the-street one.
- **First job:** **Microsoft, ~2 years on Visio** after graduating (S014) — he behaved, pre-audience, as a working production engineer at a big software company before joining a startup. Then **joined Facebook in January 2006 as one of the first ~15 engineers** (S014).
- → **What this likely formed:** builder/maker ethos (farm + 4-H + production engineering); deep loyalty/trust line to Zuckerberg (Harvard AI class → 16-yr colleague); willingness to own unglamorous infrastructure and long-horizon hardware.

*Adult track (compressed, ≤ as-of):* Built the original **News Feed**, Messenger, Groups, Video Calling; **led the 2012 desktop→mobile advertising transition** (the move that saved the ad business); took the ads & business-platform group's revenue from ~$4B to ~$40B/yr; founded/runs **Reality Labs** (AR/VR since 2017, renamed 2020); became **CTO in 2021/2022** (S014). Conduct matches formation: a builder repeatedly handed the hardest infra/product problems and the company's biggest long-horizon bet.

*Dark-arc (required):* (1) He is the operational owner of **Reality Labs, which lost $(3,672)M in a single quarter on $285M revenue** (S001) — the single largest discretionary money-drain, and his judgment is heavily staked to a bet with no proof of payoff as-of. (2) Historically associated with internal "growth at all costs" framing inside Facebook's engineering culture (the milieu the Haugen documents, S018, put under scrutiny). (3) Key-man overlap: concentrating the ad-platform architect *and* the metaverse champion in one person raises single-point dependency.

*Reliability axis:* unchooseable facts (Saratoga farm, 4-H, Harvard CS 2004, Visio, ~10th-engineer hire date) are third-party-documented (S014) → trust. His public blogging ("Boz" memos) is self-narrative → discount.

*Guardrails:* formation (farm builder + Harvard CS) is consistent with the infra/hardware path; no notable departure from formation observed.

### Team dark-arc / fracture inversion

- **Where could the team break?**
  1. **Controller error with no brake:** Zuckerberg directs an unprovable multi-year metaverse bet (RL loss $(3.67)B/qtr, S001) and **no board or shareholder can stop or remove him** (S012). → *kill criterion:* if the central capital-allocation bet keeps scaling losses with no engagement/revenue proof and there is no governance mechanism to force a reallocation, the control structure converts from a long-termism asset into an unhedgeable risk.
  2. **Loss of the counterweight:** Sandberg's exit (S016) removes the operational/external adult-in-the-room; if the replacement layer (Olivan/Li) proves to be executors rather than challengers, the **"yes-to-the-founder" failure mode** intensifies. → *kill criterion:* visible absence of any internal voice that can push back on the controller.
  3. **Culture/candor:** the Haugen-type internal-harm pattern (S018) plus the morale shock of the first-ever 13%/11,000 layoff against a base that had grown +28% YoY (S001/S003). → *kill criterion:* A-player attrition or a second credible whistleblower wave indicating bad news cannot travel up.
  4. **Governance escalation:** repeated shareholder proposals on dual-class and independent chair (S012) signal sustained owner discontent that the structure permanently overrides. → *kill criterion:* none available to minority holders — which is itself the risk.

### Verdict → durability ceiling

- **Team life-arc grade (1–5): 3.**
  - *Raises the ceiling:* a proven, long-tenured, complementary, repeatedly-crucible-tested core team with real bench depth and a near-perfect founder-market-fit engineer-founder who has been right on multiple platform pivots and who has just demonstrated error-ownership and cost-correction (S003).
  - *Lowers the ceiling:* **maximum governance/control risk** (56.9% voting, controlled company, founder = chair = CEO, S012) with no mechanism for owners to constrain a wrong long-horizon bet; **extreme key-man concentration** on the controller; the **simultaneous loss of the #2 counterweight** (Sandberg, S016) and a **CFO handoff** mid-storm to an untested internal promotee (S015); and a **candor/harm dark-arc** (FTC $5B order S017; Haugen S018). Net: a strong operating team capped by a hard control discount — a 3, not higher.
- **Ceiling effect:** the team would support a raised durability ceiling *if* it were a normal-governance company; the dual-class control structure pulls the net effect back to neutral-with-a-discount. Do not let founder-quality alone raise the position ceiling without pricing the control risk.
- **Feeds Duan IC lens ("管理层是否靠谱"):** management is *capable and aligned with the mission* but *structurally unaccountable to outside owners*; trust is in the operator's judgment, not in any check on it.

- [x] **Maximum governance/control risk:** Zuckerberg holds 56.9% of total voting power via 10-votes-per-share Class B; Meta is a Nasdaq "controlled company"; he is Founder + Chairman + CEO simultaneously; minority holders cannot remove him or stop the metaverse spend (S012).

- [x] **CFO handoff during the downturn:** Wehner → Chief Strategy Officer and Susan Li (untested-at-top, 14-yr internal promotee) → CFO effective 2022-11-01 (S015) — orderly but concentrating finance leadership in an insider unlikely to brake the controller.
- [x] **Candor / harm history predating as-of:** record $5B FTC penalty + 20-year privacy order that had to strip "unfettered control" from Zuckerberg (S017, 2019); Frances Haugen "Facebook Files" Senate testimony alleging known harms (S018, 2021).
- [x] **Key-man concentration:** controller, chief product visionary, and ultimate capital allocator are effectively one person.
- [x] **Sustained owner discontent the structure overrides:** 2022 ballot Proposals Four (dual-class) and Five (independent chair) (S012) — non-binding and unwinnable over his votes.

## Verdict

- **Operator grade:** strong operating engine (founder-market fit, technical judgment, long-tenured complementary team, demonstrated error-ownership) carrying a **hard governance/control discount** — net ~25/40, "needs the control risk explicitly priced." Indicative only; the decision is deferred to a later blind Runner.
- **Governance discount required? YES** — explicit and material. This is the single most important operator finding: a controlled company where a sole founder-chairman-CEO with 56.9% voting power directs an unprovable multi-year capital bet that no owner can stop (S012), with the #2 counterweight exiting (S016) and finance leadership handed to an untested insider (S015).
- **Evidence that would change view (recorded, not sought beyond as-of):** a governance change limiting super-voting or adding an independent chair; an independent CFO/COO with demonstrated willingness to challenge the controller; evidence the central bet is constrained by a real reallocation mechanism. None of these is present as-of 2022-11-10.

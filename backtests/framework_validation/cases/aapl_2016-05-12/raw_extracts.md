## S001 - Apple FY2015 Form 10-K (filed 2015-10-28; fiscal year ended 2015-09-26)

Selected financial data (in millions, except per-share):

- FY2015 net sales: $233,715 (+28% vs FY2014 $182,795).
- FY2015 net income: $53,394 (vs FY2014 $39,510).
- FY2015 diluted EPS: $9.22 (basic $9.28).
- FY2015 gross margin: $93,626; gross-margin percentage 40.1% (FY2014 38.6%, FY2013 37.6%).
- FY2015 R&D expense: $8,067 million (+34% YoY).
- Cash + cash equivalents + total marketable securities at FY2015 end: $205,666 million.

Net sales by product (FY2015, with YoY change):

- iPhone: $155,041 (+52%); 66% of total net sales (FY2014 56%, FY2013 53%).
- iPad: $23,227 (-23%).
- Mac: $25,471 (+6%).
- Services: $19,909 (+10%).
- Other Products: $10,067 (+20%).

Unit sales by product (FY2015, thousands):

- iPhone: 231,218 (+37% YoY); iPhone ASPs up ~11% in 2015 vs 2014.
- iPad: 54,856 (-19%).
- Mac: 20,587 (+9%).

Management/MD&A notes:

- FY2015 growth "driven by a 52% year-over-year increase in iPhone net sales"; iPhone net sales and units rose in all reportable segments.
- "The Company anticipates gross margin during the first quarter of 2016 to be between 39% and 40%."
- "In general, the Company believes gross margins will remain under downward pressure" due to industry-wide pricing pressure, competition, compressed product life cycles, product transitions, component cost increases, and a potentially stronger U.S. dollar.
- Capital-return history disclosed: in April 2014 the company increased share-repurchase authorization to $90B and raised the quarterly dividend to $0.47, lifting the overall program above $130B; during 2014 it used $45B on buybacks and paid $11.1B of dividends/dividend equivalents.

Use: baseline FY2015 economics, iPhone concentration (66% of revenue), Services line, gross margin, cash pile, capital-return framework.

## S003 - Apple Q2 FY2016 Earnings Release (2016-04-26; quarter ended 2016-03-26)

Headline: "Apple Reports Second Quarter Results - Capital Return Program Expanding to $250 Billion."

- "quarterly revenue of $50.6 billion and quarterly net income of $10.5 billion, or $1.90 per diluted share. These results compare to revenue of $58 billion and net income of $13.6 billion, or $2.33 per diluted share, in the year-ago quarter."
- Gross margin 39.4% (year-ago 40.8%). International sales 67% of revenue.
- Tim Cook: "Our team executed extremely well in the face of strong macroeconomic headwinds. We are very happy with the continued strong growth in revenue from Services ... our growing base of over one billion active devices."
- Capital return: Board authorized a $50B increase; cumulative program raised to $250B of cash by end of March 2018. Share-repurchase authorization increased to $175B from $140B. From program inception (Aug 2012) through March 2016, Apple returned over $163B to shareholders, including $117B in repurchases.
- Luca Maestri: "We generated strong operating cash flow of $11.6 billion and returned $10 billion to shareholders through our capital return program during the March quarter."
- Dividend: Board approved a 10% increase; declared $0.57/share, payable 2016-05-12 to holders of record 2016-05-09.
- Q3 FY2016 guidance: revenue between $41 billion and $43 billion; gross margin 37.5%-38%; opex $6.0-6.1B; tax rate 25.5%.

Condensed statement of operations (three months ended 2016-03-26 vs 2015-03-28, $M):

- Net sales: 50,557 vs 58,010.
- Cost of sales: 30,636 vs 34,354; Gross margin: 19,921 vs 23,656.
- R&D: 2,511 vs 1,918; SG&A: 3,423 vs 3,460; Total opex: 5,934 vs 5,378.
- Operating income: 13,987 vs 18,278.
- Net income: 10,516 vs 13,569. Diluted EPS: $1.90 vs $2.33.
- Diluted shares: 5,540,886 thousand (year-ago 5,834,858) — share count down YoY from buybacks.

Six months ended 2016-03-26: net sales $126,429M (vs $132,609M); net income $28,877M (vs $31,593M); diluted EPS $5.19 (vs $5.39).

Use: the anchor event — first YoY revenue decline; margin compression; sharply expanded capital-return; management framing of Services strength.

## S006 - Apple Q2 FY2016 Earnings Call Transcript (2016-04-26)

iPhone / upgrade cycle:

- Tim Cook: "We sold 51.2 million iPhones in the quarter, consistent with the range of our own expectations but lower than the exceptional year-ago quarter."
- Tim Cook: "The upgrade rate for the iPhone 6s cycle has been slightly higher than what we experienced in the iPhone 5s cycle two years ago, but it is lower than the accelerated upgrade rate we saw with iPhone 6."
- Luca Maestri: "It was a particularly challenging comparison to the record quarter a year ago when iPhone sales grew 40%."

Macro:

- Tim Cook: "Despite the pause in our growth, our results represent excellent execution by our team in the face of ongoing macroeconomic headwinds in much of the world."
- Tim Cook: "We saw continuing currency weakness in the vast majority of our international markets."

Greater China:

- Tim Cook: "The vast majority of the weakness in the Greater China region was in Hong Kong."
- Tim Cook: "If you look at mainland China, we're down 11 percent on a reported basis. On a constant currency basis we're only down 7."
- Tim Cook: "I see China as ... may not have the wind at our backs that we once did, but it's a lot more stable than what I think is the common view of it."

Services / installed base:

- Tim Cook: "Services revenue jumped 20% to 6 billion dollars."
- Tim Cook: "The services business is powered by our huge installed base of active devices which crossed 1 billion units earlier this year."
- Luca Maestri: "The purchase value of services tied to our installed base was a record 9.9 billion dollars in the March quarter, up 27% over last year."
- Luca Maestri: "The App Store generated 90% more global revenue than Google Play in the March quarter."

Switchers / emerging markets:

- Tim Cook: "We added more switchers from Android and other platforms in the first half of this year than any other six month period ever."
- Tim Cook: "In India our iPhone sales were up 56% from a year ago."
- Tim Cook: "India is where China was maybe 7-10 years ago, from that point of view."

iPhone SE:

- Tim Cook: "iPhone SE became available on March 31st ... Demand has been very strong and exceeds supply at this point."

Use: management's own framing of the decline (cycle comparison, macro/FX), the Services/installed-base annuity narrative, and emerging-market switcher growth. Treat as management commentary, not independently verified fact.

## S010 - Engadget, "Apple iPhone sales and revenue finally decline" (2016-04-26)

- Apple sold 51.2M iPhones, -16% from 61M a year ago — "the first time iPhone sales have ever declined year-over-year." Revenue $50.6B, -13% from $58B.
- "First quarterly revenue decline in 13 years"; company had warned in January this was likely.
- Cook attributed the slowdown to iPhone 6s upgrades not matching the accelerated iPhone 6 cycle; cited record switchers and +56% iPhone sales in India.
- Services ~$6B (exceeded Mac and iPad revenue); App Store +35% YoY; Apple Music >13 million subscribers.
- iPad -19% to 10.3M units; Mac -12% to 4.03M units.

Use: dated, independent recap of the anchor event and the "is iPhone past peak?" framing. News context.

## S012 - Strategy Analytics, Q1 2016 Global Smartphone Shipments (2016-04-27)

- Dateline BOSTON, 2016-04-27. Global smartphone shipments 334.6M in Q1 2016 vs 345.0M in Q1 2015, -3.0% YoY — "the first time ever since the modern smartphone market began in 1996 that global shipments have shrunk on an annualized basis."
- Samsung 79.0M (23.6% share, -4% YoY), #1. Apple 51.2M (15.3% share, -16% YoY; down from ~18% share).
- Linda Sui: "Smartphone growth is slowing due to increasing penetration maturity in major markets like China and consumer caution about the future of the world economy."
- Neil Mawston: Apple faces "iPhone fatigue and pressure is mounting for Apple to innovate a new wow design beyond its standard rectangle formfactor."

Use: independent industry evidence that the smartphone market itself had stopped growing as of the as-of date — context for the maturing-hardware question. Industry source.

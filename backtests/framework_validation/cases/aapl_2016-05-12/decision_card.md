## The decision question

> Is Apple a quality compounder mispriced as "ex-growth" after its first revenue decline in 13 years, or a maturing-hardware value trap whose iPhone franchise has peaked?

**Answer: a quality compounder mispriced as ex-growth — at a starter size, with the iPhone-trajectory question explicitly unresolved.** Not a REJECT, not WATCH/0%. But the durability ceiling is capped below full Core because the *next-category / growth* line is unproven post-Jobs and iPhone saturation in a no-growth smartphone market is a genuine risk.

## Binding constraint

**iPhone trajectory.** Is the unit decline a cyclical iPhone-6-supercycle comparison effect (→ maturity bridged by the 1B installed base + Services annuity, the bull) or the onset of structural saturation (→ a slow melt dragging normalized earnings)? This one variable governs whether normalized earnings hold and whether Services can carry a re-rate. China (transitory HK/FX vs deteriorating competitive/regulatory position) is the secondary swing factor.

## Hard veto?

**No.** M5 fires a −1 warning (size haircut), not a veto. The structural_decline / false_cheap_value_trap labels were tested and rejected: moat intact, normalized earnings only modestly below trailing, fortress net cash, downside survivable.

## Main reasons

- **To own:** durable ecosystem/brand moat + 1B installed base + compounding Services, bought at ~6.7x EV/earnings with ~$153B net cash and an aggressive buyback shrinking the share count; owner-minded, low-governance-risk operator team.
- **Not to own (size discipline):** iPhone is 66% of revenue and just posted its first-ever unit decline into a smartphone market that itself stopped growing; China is a supply-and-demand single point; margins are compressing; the post-Jobs design-soul / new-category line is unproven.

## Triggers & monitor

- **Add trigger:** next iPhone cycle shows the decline was the 6s-vs-6 compare (units stabilize / re-grow) AND Services keeps compounding ~20%+ → scale toward max (~11%).
- **Trim trigger:** only on a broken thesis (below), never on weight.
- **Kill criteria:** (1) iPhone units decline a 2nd consecutive cycle with no installed-base/Services offset (saturation confirmed structural); (2) Services decelerates toward flat; (3) Greater China deteriorates beyond HK/FX into sustained mainland share/regulatory loss; (4) gross margin breaks durably below the high-30s with no Services mix offset; (5) capital allocation tilts to EPS-engineering (buyback up while R&D / product cadence falls).
- **Next monitor event:** Q3 FY2016 result vs the $41–43B guide and the gross-margin 37.5–38% guide — the first read on whether the decline is stabilizing or deepening.

## Existing-position note (HOLD, not ADD-with-conviction)

A holder owns a durable, cash-generative franchise at ~7x EV/earnings with a large buyback retiring shares — there is no reason to EXIT or TRIM. It is not a high-conviction ADD only because the maturity question (binding constraint) is genuinely unresolved as-of; HOLD and let the buyback compound while watching the iPhone-trajectory tell.

## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation

### Evidence (as-of facts)

- The anchor event is unambiguous and documented in Apple's own filings: Q2 FY2016 is the first revenue decline in 13 years and the first-ever YoY iPhone unit decline.
- The decline is broad across hardware (iPhone, iPad, Mac all down YoY) and concentrated geographically in Greater China (-26%).
- Two lines grew: Services (+20%) and Other Products (+30%); Services exceeded Mac and iPad revenue in the quarter.
- The balance sheet is exceptionally strong: ~$233B cash + securities, ~$153B net cash, and an enlarged $250B capital-return program with a raised buyback and dividend.
- Valuation arithmetic from the primary statements puts the trailing P/E near 10x and EV/earnings near ~6.7x at the as-of close.
- Independent industry data confirm the smartphone market stopped growing in Q1 2016 - i.e., this is partly an industry-maturity event, not only an Apple event.
- iPhone is ~66% of revenue (FY2015), so the franchise's trajectory dominates the financial picture.

### Interpretation (LEFT OPEN - for the future runner)

- Whether ~10x trailing earnings with ~$153B net cash represents a mispriced quality franchise or correctly prices a peaking hardware business is exactly the decision question and is NOT resolved here.
- Whether Services + installed base can carry the narrative, and whether iPhone declines are cyclical (tough 6-vs-6s compare) or structural (saturation), are open and must be argued in M2-M5.

### Sentiment / Narrative Only (use as leads, not proof)

- Management's framing (Services annuity, switchers, India, "China more stable than the common view") is company optimism and must be tested, not accepted.
- The Icahn exit (S011) and Money.com demand-worry narrative (S009) are bearish sentiment leads, not verified fundamentals.

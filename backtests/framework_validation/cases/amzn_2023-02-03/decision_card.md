## Kill criteria (cut back toward tracking / do not add / re-underwrite)

1. AWS YoY growth keeps stepping down past "mid-teens" into sustained low-double/single digits AND margin keeps compressing below the low-20s% with no stabilization → the sole profit engine re-rates to a durably lower baseline (not an optimization pause).
2. NA and/or Intl operating margin does NOT recover off the trough once the one-time charges roll off → retail losses were structural unit-economics impairment, not cyclical over-build → re-underwrite, likely trim.
3. Capex does NOT moderate and TTM FCF stays materially negative while OCF stalls → the negative-FCF setup was empire-capex, not a digestible build → don't add.
4. Consumer-demand environment deteriorates further (paid-unit growth negative, mix keeps shifting to value) AND operating-income guidance stays near breakeven → the cyclical trough deepens into something structural.
5. Operator/transmission risk realizes: Bezos–Jassy authority conflict, a Jassy/AWS-leadership exit, capital-allocation discipline breaking (open-ended capex with no return), or the "Day 1"/cost-discipline culture visibly hollowing under the first non-founder CEO → durability was over-credited.
6. Evidence AWS deceleration is SHARE LOSS to Azure/Google Cloud rather than industry-wide cyclical digestion (peers re-accelerate while AWS does not) → the moat read was wrong.

## S001-S005 - Amazon Q4 2022 Earnings Press Release (issued 2023-02-02, "for its fourth quarter ended December 31, 2022")

### Headline Q4 2022 (from the release narrative)

- Net sales increased 9% to $149.2 billion in Q4 2022, compared with $137.4 billion in Q4 2021. Excluding a $5.0 billion unfavorable FX impact, net sales increased 12%.
  - North America segment sales increased 13% YoY to $93.4 billion (+14% ex-FX).
  - International segment sales decreased 8% YoY to $34.5 billion (+5% ex-FX).
  - AWS segment sales increased 20% YoY to $21.4 billion.
- Operating income decreased to $2.7 billion in Q4 2022, compared with $3.5 billion in Q4 2021. Q4 2022 operating income includes approximately $2.7 billion of charges for changes in estimates related to self-insurance liabilities, impairments of property and equipment and operating leases, and estimated severance costs. These charges primarily impacted the North America segment.
  - North America segment operating loss was $0.2 billion (vs operating loss of $0.2 billion in Q4 2021).
  - International segment operating loss was $2.2 billion (vs operating loss of $1.6 billion in Q4 2021).
  - AWS segment operating income was $5.2 billion (vs operating income of $5.3 billion in Q4 2021).
- Net income was $0.3 billion in Q4 2022, or $0.03 per diluted share, compared with $14.3 billion, or $1.39 per diluted share, in Q4 2021. (All per-share figures reflect the 20-for-1 stock split effected May 27, 2022.)
  - Q4 2022 net income includes a pre-tax valuation loss of $2.3 billion in non-operating income (expense) from the common stock investment in Rivian Automotive, Inc., compared with a pre-tax valuation gain of $11.8 billion from that investment in Q4 2021.

### Headline Full Year 2022 (from the release narrative)

- Net sales increased 9% to $514.0 billion in 2022, compared with $469.8 billion in 2021. Excluding a $15.5 billion unfavorable FX impact, net sales increased 13%.
  - North America segment sales increased 13% YoY to $315.9 billion.
  - International segment sales decreased 8% YoY to $118.0 billion (+4% ex-FX).
  - AWS segment sales increased 29% YoY to $80.1 billion.
- Operating income decreased to $12.2 billion in 2022, compared with $24.9 billion in 2021.
  - North America segment operating loss was $2.8 billion (vs operating income of $7.3 billion in 2021).
  - International segment operating loss was $7.7 billion (vs operating loss of $0.9 billion in 2021).
  - AWS segment operating income was $22.8 billion (vs operating income of $18.5 billion in 2021).
- Net loss was $2.7 billion in 2022, or $0.27 per diluted share, compared with net income of $33.4 billion, or $3.24 per diluted share, in 2021.
  - 2022 net loss includes a pre-tax valuation loss of $12.7 billion in non-operating income (expense) from the Rivian common stock investment, compared with a pre-tax valuation gain of $11.8 billion in 2021.
- Operating cash flow increased 1% to $46.8 billion for the trailing twelve months, compared with $46.3 billion for the TTM ended December 31, 2021.
- Free cash flow decreased to an outflow of $11.6 billion for the trailing twelve months, compared with an outflow of $9.1 billion for the TTM ended December 31, 2021.
- Free cash flow less principal repayments of finance leases and financing obligations was an outflow of $19.8 billion (vs $20.4 billion outflow TTM 2021).

### CEO statement (Andy Jassy, in the release)

- "...we're appreciative of all our customers who turned to Amazon this past holiday season... we're also encouraged by the continued progress we're making in reducing our cost to serve in the operations part of our Stores business. In the short term, we face an uncertain economy, but we remain quite optimistic about the long-term opportunities for Amazon."

## S007 - Amazon Q4 2022 Earnings Call (2023-02-02) — management commentary

- AWS growth: CFO Brian Olsavsky indicated AWS year-over-year revenue growth was "in the mid-teens" as of the early part of January 2023, and that the company expected optimization efforts to continue to be a headwind to AWS growth "in at least the next couple of quarters."
- Customer behavior: CEO Andy Jassy described enterprises "acting cautiously" and looking to "spend less money," noting customers may temporarily use less compute, while framing the long-term trends as intact. He stated Amazon was "going to help our customers find a way to spend less money" and was "not focused on trying to optimize in any one quarter."
- Charges: Olsavsky attributed the ~$2.7 billion of Q4 charges to (a) a $640 million severance cost tied to eliminating "just over 18,000 roles, primarily impacting our stores and device businesses as well as our human resources teams"; (b) ~$720 million of property/equipment impairments (primarily related to Amazon Fresh and Amazon Go stores); and (c) ~$1.3 billion of self-insurance reserves for product and automobile liability claims.
- Capex / fulfillment: Olsavsky noted the company had roughly doubled its fulfillment-center footprint in a couple of years and that the focus had shifted toward optimizing and improving productivity of existing assets rather than aggressive expansion. (No specific 2023 capex dollar guidance was given in the captured commentary.)
- Stores cost: Jassy framed reducing "cost to serve in our operations network" as the "#1 priority."
- Rivian: Olsavsky described the pre-tax valuation loss of $2.3 billion (Q4) from the Rivian common stock investment as "not related to Amazon's ongoing operations."
- Demand environment: Olsavsky noted consumers were "very careful about how they allocate their resources" and were shifting toward "lower-priced items and value brands."

Use: management framing for AWS deceleration/optimization, the cost-cut/charge composition, and the demand backdrop. Treat optimistic framing as management view, not verified fact.

## S009 / S010 - ~18,000 Role Reductions (dated 2023-01-04 / 2023-01-05)

- In a message dated early January 2023, CEO Andy Jassy stated Amazon planned to eliminate "just over 18,000 roles."
- The reductions consolidated cuts begun in fall 2022 and represented roughly 1.2% of Amazon's ~1.544 million full- and part-time employees.
- The majority of eliminations were in the Stores and PXT (People, Experience, and Technology) organizations.
- Jassy said the news was shared earlier than planned after an external leak, with notifications to affected employees beginning January 18, citing a "more uncertain" economy.

Use: dates and frames the headcount-reduction program that underlies the Q4 severance charge (cross-referenced in S007).

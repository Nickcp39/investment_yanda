## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation

### Evidence (reported, as-of)
- The print was released after market close 2023-02-02; the FY2022 10-K was filed 2023-02-03.
- Reported FY2022 swung to a net loss, but the largest single negative item was a non-operating, non-cash $12.7B Rivian valuation loss; reported operating income remained positive at $12.248B.
- Both retail segments (North America full-year, International full-year) reported operating losses in FY2022; AWS remained the sole large operating-profit pool ($22.8B FY).
- AWS revenue growth decelerated sequentially through 2022 and AWS operating margin compressed (Q4 standalone 24.3%); management said growth was "mid-teens" into January and flagged a continued optimization headwind.
- Free cash flow was negative for the TTM while capex stayed near $60B+; operating cash flow was roughly flat.
- A ~18,000 role-reduction program and ~$2.7B of Q4 charges were in place; management framed cost-to-serve reduction as the top priority.

### Interpretation note (boundary)
- The evidence shows a margin/earnings down-cycle (retail segment losses + AWS deceleration + charges + negative FCF) co-existing with a still-dominant, profitable AWS and double-digit consolidated revenue growth ex-FX. Whether this is a trough to be looked through or a structural impairment is exactly the deferred decision question and is NOT resolved here.

### Sentiment / Narrative Only
- Management's "optimistic about the long-term" framing and the "long-term trends are still there" AWS comment are management views; they are recorded as framing, not as verified fact.
- S013 is a narrative lead only for the direction of the post-print stock reaction; its price/market-cap figures are not used.

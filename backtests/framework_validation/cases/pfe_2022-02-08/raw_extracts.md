## S001 / S002 - Pfizer Q4 and Full-Year 2021 Results (release dated 2022-02-08)

Reported pre-market on 2022-02-08 (8-K Exhibit 99.1; figures cross-checked against the BioSpace mirror of the same release).

Headline revenue:
- Q4 2021 total revenues: $23,838 million, up 105% reported (106% operationally) versus the prior-year quarter.
- Full-year 2021 total revenues: $81,288 million, up 95% reported (92% operationally) versus 2020.

Earnings:
- Q4 2021 reported (GAAP) diluted EPS: $0.59. Q4 2021 adjusted (non-GAAP) diluted EPS: $1.08.
- Full-year 2021 reported (GAAP) diluted EPS: $3.85. Full-year 2021 adjusted (non-GAAP) diluted EPS: $4.42.

2022 full-year company guidance (issued in this release):
- Total revenues: $98.0 to $102.0 billion (~23% YoY growth at the midpoint).
- Adjusted diluted EPS: $6.35 to $6.55 (~46% YoY growth at the midpoint).

2022 COVID-19 product guidance (within the company total above):
- Comirnaty: anticipated ~$32 billion of revenue in 2022.
- Paxlovid: anticipated ~$22 billion of revenue in 2022.
- Both figures reflect doses / treatment courses expected to be delivered "under supply contracts signed [or signed or committed] as of late-January 2022."

Ex-COVID base-business signal:
- "Excluding the revenue contributions of Comirnaty and Paxlovid, revenues for the full year [2021] grew 6% operationally."

Dividend:
- Full-year 2021 dividends declared were $1.56 per share of common stock, an increase of 3% versus 2020 (see also S010 for the raised Q1 2022 rate).

Use: primary financial/guidance anchor for the case. Establishes both the optical cheapness (record earnings) and the size of the COVID dependency.

## S003 - Pfizer Q4 2021 Earnings Call Transcript (2022-02-08)

Participants included Albert Bourla (Chairman & CEO), Frank D'Amelio (CFO), Mikael Dolsten (CSO), Angela Hwang (Group President, Biopharmaceuticals), Aamir Malik (Chief Business & Innovation Officer).

COVID-19 durability / endemicity (management framing, not verified fact):
- Bourla: "Our scientists continue to monitor the SARS-CoV-2 virus and believe it is unlikely that it will be fully eradicated in the foreseeable future" — framing an endemic-management model with continued mutation and limited durable natural immunity.
- D'Amelio on Paxlovid: "Unlike the situation for Comirnaty, demand for Paxlovid should have upside from these levels depending on the outcomes of discussions with certain governments and potential purchases for stockpiling against future coronavirus pandemics."

Capital deployment / business development:
- Bourla: "The strength of our balance sheet and cash flows allow us to pursue new business development opportunities going forward that could add at least $25 billion of risk-adjusted revenues to our 2030 top-line expectations."
- Malik: "We're going to be incredibly flexible... Acquisitions are obviously very much in the cards, but strategic partnerships and alliances as well."

Non-COVID base growth / 2025 ambition (management framing):
- D'Amelio: "Excluding Comirnaty direct sales... 2021 operational revenue growth was 6%. This is consistent with our projected revenue CAGR of at least 6% from 2020 through the end of 2025."
- Hwang: the "6% confidence is really driven by two things. First of all, the launches... that will take place between now and 2025," plus share-gain and under-diagnosis headroom in existing brands.

Paxlovid manufacturing / order book:
- Bourla: "Overall, we expect to produce 30 million courses in the first half of 2022 and 120 million courses for the full year... depending on the global need." "We are in discussions with governments around the world and expect that as the number of authorizations increase, so will the number of contracts."

Capital allocation / dividend / buybacks:
- Bourla: "We expect to do this while still maintaining our growing dividend, as well as flexibility for other uses of our cash."
- D'Amelio: "2022 guidance... assumes no share repurchases... Pfizer did not repurchase shares in either 2020 or 2021... we do not expect to repurchase shares in 2022."

Use: management's intended use of the COVID cash and its claimed non-COVID growth bridge. Treat as company optimistic framing / leads, not verified outcomes.

## S008 - FDA Paxlovid Emergency Use Authorization (2021-12-22)

- The FDA issued an Emergency Use Authorization for Pfizer's Paxlovid (nirmatrelvir tablets + ritonavir tablets, co-packaged for oral use) on 2021-12-22.
- Authorized for treatment of mild-to-moderate COVID-19 in patients 12 years and older (>=40 kg) with positive SARS-CoV-2 test who are at high risk of progression to severe disease, including hospitalization or death.
- Described as the first oral antiviral pill authorized for COVID-19.

Use: regulatory milestone underpinning the new Paxlovid revenue line in the 2022 guide.

## S009 - Paxlovid EPIC-HR Efficacy (Pfizer release, Nov 2021)

- In the Phase 2/3 EPIC-HR study, Paxlovid reduced the risk of COVID-19-related hospitalization or death by 89% versus placebo in non-hospitalized high-risk adults treated within three days of symptom onset.
- Through Day 28, no deaths in the Paxlovid arm versus 10 deaths in the placebo arm (overall study population, per final-results reporting).

Use: clinical basis for Paxlovid demand assumptions. Reported efficacy from company release; treat the headline figure as company-reported.

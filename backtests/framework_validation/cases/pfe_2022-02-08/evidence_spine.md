## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation

### Evidence (as-of, verifiable)
- 2021 was a record revenue and earnings year, driven overwhelmingly by Comirnaty (~45% of revenue), with a new Paxlovid franchise just authorized (Dec 2021).
- 2022 guidance points to a second record year (~$100B revenue, ~$6.45 adjusted EPS midpoint), but ~$54B of the increase rests on two COVID products (~$32B Comirnaty + ~$22B Paxlovid).
- The ex-COVID base grew 6% operationally in 2021 and spans a diversified set of ~$1-6B products (Eliquis, Ibrance, Prevnar, Xeljanz, Vyndaqel, Xtandi, Inlyta, Enbrel intl).
- The stock screens optically very cheap (~8x guided 2022 earnings) and yields ~3.1%, with no buybacks planned.
- The market reaction was negative despite the EPS beat, because the revenue guide came in under consensus.

### Forward-looking / framing (NOT settled fact as of the date)
- Whether the 2022 COVID guidance is a durable run-rate or a temporary peak is explicitly unresolved in the as-of record (management argues endemicity + Paxlovid upside; the analyst note flags post-2022 normalization risk).
- Management's "at least $25B of risk-adjusted 2030 revenue from BD" and "6% non-COVID CAGR to 2025" are stated ambitions, not verified results.
- Paxlovid 2022 volume (~120M courses) depends on still-pending government contracts and authorizations.

### Sentiment / narrative only
- "$100 billion pharma goliath" (S007) is media/analyst framing, useful as context for what the market already expected, not as proof of durability.

## 4. Open Questions (for the future runner, not answered here)

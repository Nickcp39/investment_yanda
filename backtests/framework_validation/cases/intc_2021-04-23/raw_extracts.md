## S001 / S002 / S006 - IDM 2.0 Strategy Announcement (2021-03-23)

Source: Intel press release + 8-K exhibit 99.1 + BusinessWire wire copy, all dated 2021-03-23.

- New CEO Pat Gelsinger (CEO since 2021-02-15) outlined "IDM 2.0," described as an evolution of
  Intel's integrated device manufacturing model, on the "Intel Unleashed: Engineering the Future" webcast.
- IDM 2.0 has three components:
  1. Intel's internal/global factory network for at-scale manufacturing.
  2. Expanded use of third-party foundry capacity, including for advanced process technologies,
     for "a range of modular tiles" beginning in 2023.
  3. A new standalone business unit, Intel Foundry Services (IFS), to build a foundry business.
- Arizona expansion: ~$20 billion investment to build two new fabs at the Ocotillo campus in
  Chandler, Arizona. Stated job impact: >3,000 permanent high-tech jobs, >3,000 construction jobs,
  ~15,000 long-term local jobs.
- Intel Foundry Services: led by Dr. Randhir Thakur, reporting directly to Gelsinger; targets U.S.-
  and Europe-based capacity for global customers; offers leading-edge process and packaging plus an
  IP portfolio including x86 cores as well as Arm and RISC-V ecosystem IP.
- Process / 7nm: Intel said it expected to tape in the compute tile for its first 7nm client CPU
  (code-named "Meteor Lake") in the second quarter of 2021, with increased use of EUV lithography.

Use: anchor for the IDM 2.0 / foundry / capex strategy and the dated 7nm-progress statement.

## S003 - Intel Q1 2021 Earnings Release (reported 2021-04-22)

Headline financials (Q1 2021 vs Q1 2020):

- GAAP revenue: $19.7 billion ($19,673M), down 1% YoY.
- Non-GAAP revenue: $18.6 billion, flat YoY (non-GAAP excludes the divested NSG/memory business and a Corporate VLSI item).
- GAAP EPS: $0.82. Non-GAAP EPS: $1.39 (exceeded January guidance by $0.29).
- GAAP gross margin: 55.2%, down 5.4 points vs 60.6% in Q1 2020.
- Non-GAAP gross margin: 58.4%, down 6.1 points vs 64.5% in Q1 2020.
- Q1 GAAP revenue exceeded January guidance by $1.1 billion, led by strong PC demand; PC unit
  volumes up 38% YoY and notebook volumes set a new Intel record.

Segment revenue (Q1 2021, YoY):

- Client Computing Group (CCG): $10.6 billion, up 8%.
- Data Center Group (DCG): $5.6 billion, down 20%.
- Internet of Things Group (IOTG): $914 million, up 4%.
- Mobileye: $377 million, up 48% (new record).
- Non-Volatile Memory Solutions Group (NSG): $1.1 billion, down 17%.
- Programmable Solutions Group (PSG): $486 million, down 6%.
- DCG was characterized as soft on Enterprise & Government and on cloud-service-provider digestion.

Platform volume / ASP table (Q1 2021 YoY, from the release):

- Desktop platform volumes (19)%; desktop platform ASP +4%.
- Notebook platform volumes +54%; notebook platform ASP (23)%.
- (Lower notebook ASP attributed to a higher mix of consumer and education PCs.)

FY2021 guidance (as given 2021-04-22):

- Revenue: $77.0 billion (GAAP) / $72.5 billion (non-GAAP).
- GAAP EPS $4.00; non-GAAP EPS $4.60.
- Gross margin: 54.5% (GAAP) / 56.5% (non-GAAP).
- Full-year capital spending: $19.0-20.0 billion.
- Free cash flow (non-GAAP): ~$10.5 billion.

Capital return (Q1 2021): paid dividends of $1.4 billion; used $2.3 billion to repurchase stock.

Use: core financial evidence — revenue mix, gross-margin compression off highs, DCG decline,
notebook ASP pressure, elevated 2021 capex guide, dividend.

## S005 - Intel Q1 2021 Earnings Call (2021-04-22)

Management commentary (CEO Pat Gelsinger and CFO George Davis), quotes/paraphrases:

IDM 2.0 / Intel Foundry Services:
- Gelsinger: "IDM 2.0 utilizes our internal factory network to reliably deliver leadership products
  and provide the industry another source of foundry capacity."
- Gelsinger: starting with a ~$20 billion investment for first large-scale foundry operations in Arizona;
  establishing Intel Foundry Services as a major provider.
- Gelsinger: engaged with "well over 50 potential customers" for foundry services.

7nm / process:
- Gelsinger: "7 nanometers is progressing well... puts us on a path to restore process performance leadership."
- Gelsinger: within the next couple of weeks, Intel would tape in the compute tile for Meteor Lake,
  its first 7nm CPU (for 2023).

Data Center Group:
- Characterized cloud-digestion impacts as beginning to subside, with enterprise/government momentum,
  in the forward characterization.
- Davis attributed much of the YoY DCG decline to a difficult comparison against a very strong Q1 2020.

Gross margin:
- Davis: 7nm start-up costs ramping through the year were a major factor pressuring margins.
- Davis cited factory start-up costs, higher opex, and the unit-cost impact of ramping 10nm on DCG margin.

Capital spending:
- Davis: "$20 billion for this year is a good number" on capex.
- Davis framed 2021 as an "investment phase" to support demand growth and build initial infrastructure
  for future foundry volumes.

Capital return:
- Davis: "We remain committed to growing the dividend," with lower stock repurchases as the company
  enters an investment phase.

Use: management framing for IDM 2.0, 7nm, DCG, the margin trajectory, capex, and the dividend. Company
optimistic framing is recorded as management commentary, not as verified fact.

## S007 / S008 / S009 - 7nm Delay Context (July 2020)

Source: Engadget (2020-07-23), Tom's Hardware (2020-07-24), AppleInsider (2020-07-23).

- On 2020-07-23/24, Intel disclosed that 7nm product timing was slipping ~6 months relative to prior
  expectations; 7nm process yield was reported as trending ~12 months behind Intel's internal target.
- 7nm-based CPUs were then expected in late 2022 / early 2023.
- Intel signaled it could consider outsourcing more manufacturing to third parties as a contingency.

Use: dated background establishing the process-node / 7nm-delay context that pre-dates IDM 2.0; explains
why "restore process leadership" is a stated goal and why the foundry/outsourcing question existed by the as-of date.

## 2. False-Cheap / Expensive-Trap Diagnosis

- **This is NOT a false_cheap_value_trap.** NBIS does not look statistically cheap; it looks expensive on trailing numbers. The bull case is explicitly a forward-ARR/asymmetry case, so the "low P/E is a trap" failure mode does not apply.
- **The relevant trap is the inverse: an "expensive-growth trap."** The danger is paying up for a hyper-growth narrative that (a) cannot be funded without ruinous dilution, or (b) over-builds into a glut. The discipline against this is *price/size* (M6: buy with margin of safety vs. financing milestones, not at any price) plus hard ARR/financing kill gates.
- **Structural-decline check:** there is no structural decline -- revenue and ARR are accelerating, the core is margin-positive, and demand is sold out. The bear case is *over-success risk* (over-building, over-diluting), not erosion.
- **Balance-sheet-trap check:** net-cash at 9/30/25 with low-coupon, long-dated converts -> no near-term solvency trap. The risk is *future* financing terms, not present fragility.

## 3. What Would Prove the Thesis Wrong (falsifiers)

1. Core AI-infra ARR growth decelerates materially or the $7-9B 2026 exit target is walked back.
2. Connected/active power prints lag the guided path (220MW connected by CYE2025; 800MW-1GW by CYE2026), signaling conversion failure.
3. A marquee customer (Microsoft/Meta) renegotiates, delays, or cancels.
4. The next major financing is equity-heavy at a *lower* price than the $92.50 Sept-2025 print, or asset-backed debt fails to materialize -> dilution path confirmed.
5. Core adjusted-EBITDA margin reverses back toward break-even.
6. Industry-wide ASP/utilization deterioration signals a capacity glut.

## 4. Inversion Summary

- **Could a careful skeptic lose permanently here?** Yes -- via K1 (dilution) or K2 (glut) compounding K4 (conversion lag). These are real and justify a *position that is small relative to its volatility, with explicit gates*, not a maximum bet.
- **But is any kill path *confirmed* at the as-of date?** No. The balance sheet is sound, demand is sold out, two blue-chip contracts are signed, and the core is margin-positive. The risks are forward and probabilistic, not present and structural.
- **Does M5 veto?** No. M5 emits **-1**: it caps size and hard-wires kill criteria, but it does not override the M2/M6 asymmetry. The correct expression of this risk is a disciplined starter with add-only-on-confirmation and a wide, defined price band -- not REJECT, and not WATCH/0%.

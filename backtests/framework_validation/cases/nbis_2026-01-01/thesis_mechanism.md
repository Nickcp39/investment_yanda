## 1. Integrated thesis (template fields)

- **Theme:** The AI build-out's binding constraint has moved from chips to **power + ready data-center capacity**.
  Hyperscalers and AI labs cannot build enough of their own fast enough, so they **rent dedicated GPU capacity** from
  specialist "neocloud" operators. NBIS is a pure-play on that rented-capacity bottleneck.
- **Why now:** NBIS spent 2024–25 converting from the ex-Yandex remnant into a focused AI-infra builder, and in a
  single quarter (Q3'25, reported 2025-11-11) printed **two blue-chip anchor contracts** — Microsoft (~$17.4–19.4B
  through 2031) and Meta (~$3B/5yr) — while declaring itself **"sold out of capacity."** The constraint is demand-
  exceeds-supply for *its* capacity, not the reverse. (S007, S008, S012, S013)
- **Market narrative:** Bull — "the next CoreWeave, blue-chip-anchored, hypergrowth ARR, net-cash, founder-led."
  Bear — "capex furnace, customer-concentrated, dilution machine, ex-Yandex governance, 15.5% short interest, no
  index bid." Both are loud; the stock is **contested** (S022).
- **What the market may be OVER-emphasizing:** (a) the optical valuation (~$21.1B cap on ~$146M quarterly revenue
  looks extreme on trailing numbers); (b) headline GAAP losses; (c) the Yandex stigma. These are the classic
  `exceptional_bottleneck` failure-trigger — "missing the starter because valuation looks optically expensive."
- **What the market may be UNDER-emphasizing:** (a) the **rate of change** — core ARR ~$220M → ~$551M in three
  quarters, core already adj-EBITDA **positive at ~19% margin** (S013); (b) the **contract backlog is real and
  blue-chip** (counterparty credit quality enables cheaper asset-backed debt); (c) the **team actually built this
  category before** (ex-Yandex cloud/AI infra — see operator dossier); (d) it was **roughly net-cash at 9/30/25**.
- **Capital-flow evidence:** ~$4.2–4.3B raised Sept-2025 (converts @ ~$138.75 conv. + equity @ $92.50); NVIDIA +
  Accel + Orbis put in $700M @ $21.00 (Dec-2024) — i.e., the **chip vendor itself** validated the platform. (S009,
  S010, S011, S015)
- **Demand shock:** the generative-AI training/inference capex super-cycle; IEA projects data-center power demand
  to **more than double to ~945 TWh by 2030**, with **~20% of planned projects at delay risk** — so whoever can
  actually *deliver connected power* captures scarcity rents. (S020)
- **Scarce resource / bottleneck:** **contracted → connected → active power** (secured land+power, then built DC,
  then energized racks). NBIS guides contracted power to **~2.5GW by CYE2026** with **800MW–1GW connected**. The
  scarce input is energized capacity, and lead times (transmission, turbines) are multi-year. (S013, S020)
- **Who pays:** hyperscalers (Microsoft) and AI labs / frontier-model owners (Meta), plus a diversified core base of
  AI-native companies. Blue-chip, creditworthy demand.
- **Unit of value:** a GPU-hour / a megawatt of dedicated, energized, networked AI capacity delivered under a
  multi-year take-or-pay-style contract.
- **Unit of cost:** GPUs (NVIDIA), data-center construction, **power**, and the **cost of capital** to pre-fund all
  three ahead of revenue.
- **Profit pool:** the spread between contracted GPU-capacity pricing and the all-in cost of energized capacity +
  financing, over the asset life (NBIS depreciates hardware over 4 yrs, called "conservative"). Whether that spread
  is fat or thin is the whole question (→ M3/M4).
- **Why this company captures it:** (1) **first-mover anchor contracts** with the two best possible counterparties;
  (2) **builder-team pedigree** (ex-Yandex built national-scale cloud/AI infra); (3) **vertical integration** (own
  software stack + hardware + DC); (4) **NVIDIA as both supplier and shareholder**; (5) **(briefly) net-cash** start
  position that most neoclouds lack.
- **One metric that will force a market update:** **connected power (MW) actually energized vs the 220MW CYE2025 /
  800MW–1GW CYE2026 guide**, and the **realized ARR trajectory toward the $7–9B CYE2026 target.** If connected MW
  and ARR track the guide, the trailing-multiple bears are wrong; if connection slips (à la CoreWeave's flagged DC
  delays, S019), the capex-furnace bears are right.
- **Catalyst:** each quarter's connected-MW + ARR print; new anchor-contract announcements; the first asset-backed
  financing that proves the funding model; (negatively) any dilution surprise or a delivery slip.
- **Kill condition:** capacity-to-revenue conversion stalls (connected MW lags guide); OR financing turns punitive /
  heavily dilutive; OR a major contract is shown to be low-margin; OR a customer concentration shock (Microsoft or
  Meta pause/renegotiate).
- **Initial context label:** `exceptional_bottleneck` (a real demand-exceeds-supply bottleneck with an identified
  scarce resource, a named mechanism, blue-chip payers, and a forcing metric — *not* a `quality_compounder` yet
  because durability of the spread is unproven, and *not* a `cyclical_inflection` because the driver is secular
  build-out, not a cycle trough).

## 2. Why `exceptional_bottleneck` and not a trap

The trap contexts are explicitly tested in M5. Here the M2 case for "real opportunity" rests on:
- a **demand shock** that is independently evidenced (IEA, hyperscaler capex), not company spin;
- a **scarce resource** (energized power) with structural lead-time scarcity;
- **blue-chip counterparties** signing multi-year, multi-billion contracts (not vendor-financed vapor);
- a **forcing metric** (connected MW / ARR) that will falsify or confirm within ~4 quarters.

The `exceptional_bottleneck` playbook (template §6) warns the dominant failure is *under-sizing the starter because
the multiple looks expensive* — flagged here for M6.

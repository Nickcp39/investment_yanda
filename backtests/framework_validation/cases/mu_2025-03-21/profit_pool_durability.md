# M3 Profit Pool / Durability — MU as of 2025-03-21

Role: conviction. Signal: **+1**. Confidence: **med**.

## Value chain
AI accelerator value chain: foundry (TSMC) → GPU/accelerator designer (e.g. accelerator vendors) → board/system → hyperscaler. **Memory (HBM + DRAM) is a required co-component**: every high-end accelerator needs multiple HBM stacks; every AI server needs more DRAM content. Micron sits at the memory node alongside Samsung and SK Hynix.

## Where the profit pool sits — split explicitly (template requirement)

**A. HBM + data-center DRAM (scarce, AI-driven) — the durable sleeve.**
- HBM revenue >$1B in FQ2-25; data-center DRAM at a record; data center surpassed 50% of total revenue (Q1) and tripled YoY. [S1, S2]
- Priced on performance/scarcity, qualified into customer platforms (switching cost, multi-quarter qualification), capacity-constrained. This sleeve has genuine, if temporary-to-medium-term, pricing power and a real bottleneck.
- CNBU op income swung to $980M (10% margin) in FY2024 from a -$585M loss in FY2023. [S3]

**B. Commodity DRAM / NAND (cyclical, low-moat) — the structural-floor risk.**
- NAND/storage (SBU) ran an operating LOSS of -$362M even in the FY2024 recovery year, and -$1,887M at the FY2023 trough. [S3]
- Customers "reluctant to enter into long-term, fixed-price purchase contracts"; pricing renegotiated to market — i.e., $/bit commodity dynamics with no durable moat. [S3]
- This is where prior cycles destroyed Micron's earnings: ~$1.8B NRV inventory write-down in FY2023, facilities underutilized 2023–2024. [S3]

## Moat vs short-term bottleneck
- **Moat (structural):** membership in a rational 3-player DRAM oligopoly; leading-edge process (1-gamma DRAM); HBM qualification + IP; scale capex that few can match. This is a durable *industry-structure* moat, weaker than a software/network moat but real.
- **Bottleneck (temporary-to-medium):** HBM capacity scarcity is the current super-normal-profit driver. It is contestable — Samsung and SK Hynix are racing the same HBM ramp, and HBM premium can compress as all three add capacity. So the HBM premium is a *bottleneck rent*, not a permanent moat.

## Pricing power
Real and elevated in HBM/data-center today; structurally weak in commodity bits. Blended pricing power is cyclical with a rising structural floor as mix shifts to data center.

## Operator / capital allocation
- CEO Sanjay Mehrotra; consistent strategy messaging (technology leadership, data-center mix, AI positioning). [S1, S2]
- Capital allocation is capex-heavy by necessity: net capex $3.085B in a single quarter (FQ2-25), ~$3.1–3.2B/quarter run-rate, against OCF ~$3.9B → adjusted FCF only $857M. CHIPS-Act-supported US fabs are a multi-year capital commitment. [S1, S3]
- Pays a small dividend ($0.115/qtr) — token relative to capex; balance sheet, not yield, is the capital story.

## Durability verdict
**Mixed-but-improving.** The company is structurally better than it was one cycle ago because the data-center/HBM pool is growing as a share of the mix and carries real (if bottleneck-driven) pricing power. But ~half the company is still commodity bits with no moat and a demonstrated ability to go deeply loss-making at the trough. Durability is a function of (a) how long the HBM bottleneck rent persists and (b) how high the mix shift lifts the mid-cycle margin. **M3 = +1** (conviction present, capped by the commodity half and the contestable nature of the HBM premium).

## The pool is bifurcated — analyze the two halves separately

### Half A — the NEW pool: HBM + leading-node data-center DRAM (the AI bottleneck)

- **Size & momentum (as-of facts):** HBM revenue **crossed $1B in FQ2 FY25**; **data-center DRAM hit a
  record**; **data-center revenue tripled YoY** and (per FQ1) **surpassed 50% of total revenue**. This pool
  is growing fast and re-weighting Micron's mix toward its highest-margin products. [S1, S2]
- **Why the economics persist (the moat of THIS half):**
  - **3-player oligopoly:** only Micron, Samsung, SK Hynix make HBM/leading-node DRAM at scale. [S3 context]
  - **Capacity-allocated / contracted:** HBM in this period is sold largely on contract and is
    capacity-constrained — it does NOT price like spot commodity DRAM. (Direction inferred from Micron's
    DC/HBM disclosures; no independent dated HBM index in-pack — M1 open-question #4, treat as a lead.)
  - **Technical gate:** HBM requires advanced packaging + leading-edge DRAM yield; Micron's 1-gamma node +
    DeBoer's 29-yr process org are the entry ticket. [S1, O05]
- **Durability verdict (Half A):** **genuinely higher-quality than commodity memory** — scarcer, contracted,
  higher-margin, technically gated. BUT durability across a FULL cycle is **unproven**: HBM is young, the
  competitors are formidable (the leader has been ahead on HBM qualification), and capacity-allocated
  pricing can normalize as all three add HBM capacity. A real, but not yet bulletproof, moat. **This half is
  the only thing that can raise Micron's structural ceiling above "commodity cyclical."**

### Half B — the OLD pool: commodity DRAM + NAND

- **Size:** DRAM ≈70% / NAND ≈29% of revenue (FY24); the majority of the business is still commodity. [S3]
- **Economics:** an oligopoly (rational-ish capital discipline) but fundamentally **cyclical and
  spot-exposed** — ASPs swing violently (FY22 peak → FY23 −$5.8B loss with a $1.8B NRV write-down →
  FY24 recovery). Fixed manufacturing cost means underutilization punishes margin hard. [S3]
- **Durability verdict (Half B):** **low — this is commodity memory.** No moat beyond scale + oligopoly
  discipline; the profit pool is real on the up-leg and disappears on the down-leg. It is NOT eroding
  structurally (this is not Intel-DCG losing share) — it is *cyclical*, which is different. But it earns no
  durability premium.

## Folding the two halves: who captures the economics, and how durable

- Micron **captures** a meaningful and growing slice of the high-quality Half-A pool (HBM >$1B, DC record,
  DC >50% of revenue) — that is the bull case and the source of any re-rating.
- But the **blended** business is still ~majority commodity (Half B), so the *consolidated* durability is a
  weighted average: a high-quality, unproven-durable AI pool growing on top of a low-durability commodity
  base. The mix is shifting the right way; the question is how far and how durably.

## Operator grade → durability ceiling (per METHODOLOGY + `operator_background.md`)

- **Operator/team life-arc grade: 4/5** — a near-ideal-fit, candid, long-term-oriented, **cycle-tested
  cohesive team** (Mehrotra founder-grade CEO ~8 yrs + ~28-yr SanDisk co-founder track; Sadana P&L /
  Bhatia ops imported with him; DeBoer 29-yr technical owner of the HBM/DRAM roadmap; Murphy capital),
  clean one-share-one-vote governance. The team has **already executed through a full memory cycle** (FY23
  trough → FY24/FY25 recovery) and into the HBM window. [operator_background.md]
- **Ceiling effect — the key interaction:** a 4/5 team **raises the operating-durability read and lifts
  conviction WITHIN the cyclical band** — this is a reason to take Micron's HBM execution seriously and not
  dismiss it as a commodity also-ran. **BUT** METHODOLOGY is explicit that **the ceiling is set by
  DURABILITY of the BUSINESS, not just by operator quality or by confirmation**: a confirmed *cyclical* caps
  around the Confirmed tier (~10–15%), and the 15–25% Core tier is reserved for 5–10-yr-holdable secular
  compounders. **The operator grade does NOT by itself convert commodity memory into a secular compounder.**
  Only durable HBM/AI-memory differentiation (Half A persisting and re-weighting the mix across a full
  cycle) could raise that structural ceiling — and that is the open thesis question, not an as-of fact.

## Net M3 signal

**+1 (mild positive), med confidence.** Micron captures a real, growing, higher-quality AI-memory pool
(Half A) on top of a low-durability commodity base (Half B), run by a strong, cycle-tested 4/5 team. That is
genuinely better than a pure commodity cyclical — enough for a positive conviction signal and a Confirmed-tier
ceiling within the cyclical band. It is **not +2**, because (a) the majority of the business is still
commodity, (b) HBM durability is unproven across a full cycle against formidable competitors, and (c) the
operator grade lifts conviction within — but does not lift the structural ceiling above — the cyclical band.
The durability ceiling is therefore **Confirmed-tier (~10–15%), not Core-tier**, until HBM differentiation
proves durable.

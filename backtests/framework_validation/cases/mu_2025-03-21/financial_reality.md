## 3. Normalized owner earnings — do NOT capitalize the up-cycle number

- **The trap:** at $94.72, the stock is ~**15x** the ~$6.2 run-rate non-GAAP EPS — optically cheap. But
  $6.2 is an **up-leg** number (rising ASPs + recovering utilization). Capitalizing it as permanent is the
  cardinal cyclical error.
- **Normalization (modeled range, NOT a disclosed figure — M1 open-question #3):** averaging across the
  cycle (FY22 peak $8.7B NI ↔ FY23 trough −$5.8B loss, with FY24 $0.78B as the recovery midpoint) yields a
  crude through-cycle net income well below the current run-rate. The M1 spine modeled **normalized EPS
  ≈ $2.7–4.5**. The **HBM/data-center mix shift is a legitimate reason normalized margin may be structurally
  HIGHER this cycle than last** (more contracted, higher-margin revenue) — but the magnitude is an estimate,
  unproven across a full cycle.
- **On normalized EPS, valuation is very different:** at ~$3.5 mid-normalized, $94.72 is ~**27x** — i.e., NOT
  cheap on normalized earnings; you are paying a full multiple for the *expectation* that HBM raises the
  through-cycle floor. P/B ≈ **2.17x** (a more cycle-robust anchor) is mid-range for memory, neither
  trough-cheap (memory has bottomed near ~1x book) nor peak-rich.

## 4. Accounting quality / red-flag scan

- **No trailing-earnings distortion games:** the YoY recovery is real cash (OCF $3.94B), not accrual
  manufacture. The FY23 $1.8B NRV write-down was taken honestly at the trough, and $987M of lower costs
  from selling previously-written-down inventory *benefited* FY24 (disclosed) — a normal cyclical mechanic,
  flagged for what it is, not a quality flag. [S3]
- **Inventory $9,007M** is elevated (up from $8,705M) — worth watching in a cyclical (rising inventory into

- **Capex ≈ OCF:** $3.085B net capex vs $3.94B OCF this quarter, sustained multi-year for the fab build —
  the classic memory capital intensity; adj FCF only $857M. In a down-cycle this flips negative fast. A
  structural caution, not a current red flag (balance sheet survives). [S1]

## 5. Net M4 signal

**+1 (mild positive), with a normalization caveat.** Accounting **confirms** the thesis and shows **no
trailing-earnings trap of the fraudulent kind** — the recovery is real, cash-backed, balance-sheet-safe, and
the HBM/data-center mix is genuinely lifting margins YoY. M4 is **+1, not +2**, because: (a) we are on an
**up-leg**, so the optically cheap ~15x run-rate multiple is on flattered earnings — on **normalized** EPS

soft signals; (c) **capex ≈ OCF** keeps FCF thin and would turn negative in a down-cycle. **The discipline:
size off NORMALIZED, cycle-aware economics — not the up-leg run-rate.** This is the module that keeps the
position from being over-sized on a cheap-looking trailing multiple.

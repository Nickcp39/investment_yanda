## First-principles read

Strip the narrative and two facts remain: **(1)** memory is a brutally cyclical, capital-intensive,
fixed-cost commodity business where the cardinal sin is capitalizing up-cycle earnings as if permanent
(FY22 $8.7B NI → FY23 −$5.8B loss proves the amplitude). **(2)** AI has carved out a *genuinely scarce*
sub-pool (HBM / leading-node data-center DRAM) that behaves less like a commodity — contracted,
capacity-allocated, higher-margin — and Micron is one of only three firms on Earth that can supply it, with
that pool now >$1B/qtr and the data-center mix already past 50% of revenue.

The investment question is therefore **not** "is Micron cheap on $6.2 run-rate EPS?" (that is the
up-cycle-EPS trap). It is: **"how much of the new HBM/AI pool is durable, and does it raise Micron's
through-cycle margin enough to justify paying for a cyclical at a mid-cycle price?"** That is a
conviction-and-size question with real asymmetry, not a reject and not a chase.

## Net M2 signal

The theme is real and dated (a positive), the dislocation (~8% drop on a mix blip) is a mild opportunity
(positive), BUT the context is a *cyclical* whose up-leg earnings flatter the optics and whose HBM
durability is unproven across a full cycle (a check on conviction). **Net = +1 (mild positive), med
confidence** — a genuine cyclical-inflection-with-secular-overlay opportunity, not yet a high-conviction
secular compounder. The label routes weighting to **M2/M4/M5/M6** per the template's `cyclical_inflection`
emphasis, with M3 carrying the HBM-durability question.

# M6 Decision Card — MU as of 2025-03-21

Role: price + output. Context: `cyclical_inflection` (with embedded `exceptional_bottleneck` HBM sleeve).
As-of price: **$94.72** (2025-03-21 close, [S5]). Market cap ≈ **$105.6B**; EV ≈ $110.4B; net debt ≈ $4.76B.

## Business verdict vs price verdict
- **Business verdict: good (not exceptional).** A top-3 DRAM oligopolist with a real, growing HBM/data-center bottleneck sleeve, leading-edge process, and a survivable balance sheet — but ~half the company is commodity bits with no moat and a demonstrated ability to lose billions at the trough.
- **Price verdict: fair, not a layup.** $94.72 is ~15× up-leg run-rate but ~21–35× normalized through-cycle earnings. After an ~8% post-print de-rate it is *reasonable* for a starter, but it is NOT a deep-value, trough-multiple entry. A good company is not automatically a good buy-point — here it is an acceptable starter-point with adds reserved for lower.

## Verdicts
- **New-money verdict: STARTER** (small).
- **Existing-position verdict: HOLD** (thesis intact; do not chase; add only into weakness).
- **Suggested initial size: 1.5%** of equity portfolio.
- **Suggested max size: 4.0%** (reached only by adding lower and/or as the HBM/data-center mix-shift durability is confirmed).

## Price bands (anchored to normalized economics + cycle risk, not the run-rate multiple)
- **Buy-below (starter): ≤ $95** — i.e., at/around the as-of price; the post-print level is an acceptable starter entry.
- **Starter zone: $85–$95.**
- **Add zone: $70–$85** (a deeper de-rate toward ~12–13× run-rate / closer to normalized; this is where the asymmetry improves and you build toward max size). For reference the stock traded ~$87 on 2025-03-10. [S5]
- **Hold zone: $95–$110.**
- **Trim / no-chase zone: > $115–$120** (run-rate P/E pushing ~19×+ on still-elevated cyclical earnings → reduce or stop adding; let it work, don't add into a hot cyclical).
- **Reject / avoid-adding zone:** any level where the entry implies paying a clear premium to a *peak*-earnings multiple.

## Reasons
- **Main reason to own:** structural mix shift to data-center/HBM is raising Micron's earnings floor and pricing power within a rational DRAM oligopoly; bought above a survivable balance sheet during an up-cycle, with an embedded scarce-bottleneck (HBM >$1B/qtr) sleeve. [S1, S2, S3]
- **Main reason not to own (and why size is capped):** it is a commodity-heavy memory cyclical bought *above* mid-cycle earnings; the HBM premium is a contestable bottleneck rent, not a permanent moat; FCF is thin against heavy capex; the up-cycle could roll over.
- **Binding constraint:** **M5 cyclical-peak risk anchored by M4 normalization** — paying ~21–35× normalized for a cyclical whose up-leg could reverse is what caps this to a small starter with adds staged lower. (Not M1 confidence; not a veto.)
- **Evidence confidence haircut:** modest. M1 is high-confidence on facts; the haircut is for the unproven normalized-earnings step-up and undisclosed HBM margin → keeps initial size at a starter rather than a medium position.

## Asymmetry
- **Downside if wrong (cyclical roll-over to ~normalized/trough):** plausibly -30% to -45% (toward ~$55–$65) if the up-cycle reverses and the multiple re-rates against falling EPS — survivable at 1.5% starter; this is exactly why the size is small and adds are reserved.
- **Upside if right (mix shift durable, HBM rent persists, cycle extends):** the market re-rates the earnings *floor* higher; a structurally-higher mid-cycle EPS on a re-rated multiple supports a materially higher price. Asymmetry is favorable *at a starter size with adds lower*, not at a full position chased here.

## Triggers / monitor
- **Add trigger:** price into $70–$85 AND data-center/HBM revenue + gross margin still rising; OR confirmation over 2+ quarters that blended GM holds structurally above prior-cycle peaks.
- **Trim trigger:** price > $115–$120 on still-cyclical earnings; OR two consecutive quarters of declining data-center/HBM revenue and GM (begin reducing).
- **Exit / kill criteria:**
  1. Two consecutive quarters of falling data-center/HBM revenue AND gross margin (up-cycle has rolled over before the structural floor is proven).
  2. HBM ASP/premium visibly compressing as competitor capacity floods the socket.
  3. Industry capex signalling 2026 oversupply + Micron inventory building faster than revenue (NRV-write-down regime returning).
  4. Balance-sheet deterioration: net debt rising materially and FCF turning durably negative through the cycle.
- **Next monitor event:** FQ3 FY2025 print (guided record revenue $8.80B; watch realized GM vs the 36.5% non-GAAP guide and the data-center/HBM trajectory).

## Runner dissent
Mild. The mechanical harness logic (real bottleneck + survivable balance sheet + growing profit pool, no veto) supports at least a STARTER, and I agree. My personal hesitation is that the entry is *above* mid-cycle earnings on a contestable HBM premium, so I deliberately kept initial size small (1.5%) and put the real accumulation zone $10–$25 lower. If anything, a strict cyclical-discipline investor could defend WATCH-until-$85; I chose a small STARTER because the structural data-center mix shift gives this cycle a credibly higher floor and the downside is survivable at this size. No outcome knowledge informed this.

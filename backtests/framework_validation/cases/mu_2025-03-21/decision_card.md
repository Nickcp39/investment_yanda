## Binding constraint

**Cycle position + HBM durability (M4/M5/M3).** You are buying a majority-commodity cyclical on a confirmed
**mid-cycle up-leg** at a **full normalized multiple** (~27x on ~$3.5 normalized EPS; the optically cheap

but size into the ceiling must be **earned** by HBM differentiation + blended-margin durability proving out
(and the GM step-down NOT extending into a cycle top). A **size + cycle-timing constraint, not a veto.**

## Downside if wrong / upside if right

- **Downside:** the GM step-down is the start of a cyclical top, not a mix blip — commodity (and eventually
  HBM) ASPs decline together while capex stays elevated → glut; or HBM fails to differentiate durably and
  re-commoditizes. Multiple de-rates to normalized; you lose ~40–60% to normalized fair value. **Survivable
  at 4–12%** (balance sheet absorbs a cycle, no bankruptcy path) — a drawdown-to-fair-value risk, not a
  permanent-impairment-of-the-whole risk.
- **Upside:** HBM/AI-memory demand persists and structurally re-weights the mix toward higher-margin,
  contracted revenue, lifting the through-cycle margin floor; the commodity half rides the up-leg; a
  cycle-tested team executes the node/HBM roadmap. The cyclical re-rates and the position earns its way to
  the Confirmed-tier ceiling (and the structural ceiling itself rises if HBM durability proves out).

## Triggers

- **Add trigger:** price weakness into ~$70–$85 (a better normalized/book entry), OR a clean print
  confirming HBM + DC-DRAM revenue still growing with blended gross margin holding/re-expanding (step-down
  was mix, not a top) AND inventory not ballooning.
- **Trim trigger:** only the four methodology triggers — thesis wrong / price absurd vs **normalized** value

  trim reason.

## Kill criteria (cut back toward tracking / do not add / re-underwrite)

1. **Cyclical rollover confirmed:** DC/HBM demand softens OR commodity AND HBM ASPs decline together, GM
   steps down *further* beyond the FQ3 guide, while capex stays ~$3B/qtr → glut.
2. **HBM differentiation fails:** HBM share/qualification slips vs the leader, or HBM pricing normalizes fast
   as all three flood capacity → the Half-A premium evaporates.
3. **Capex destroys FCF:** sustained ~$3B/qtr capex into a down-cycle with FCF deeply negative + balance-sheet
   stress → the build was mis-timed.
4. **Inventory glut signal:** inventory keeps rising into softening ASPs (early glut tell).
5. **Normalized-margin thesis breaks:** evidence the HBM mix does NOT structurally lift the through-cycle
   margin floor (blended margin reverts to prior-cycle levels) → the full normalized multiple is unjustified.

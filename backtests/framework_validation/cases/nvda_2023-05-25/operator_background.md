**Jensen Huang (黄仁勋)**

*Formation (unchooseable → value formation; highest weight):*
- **Family & home:** b. Feb 17, 1963, **Taiwan** (sources vary Tainan/Taipei). **Father a chemical engineer; mother a grade-school teacher** — a technical-and-education household, professional but not wealthy or connected. Mother drilled English vocabulary into the boys (10 dictionary words a day) **before** the family had any US foothold — preparation-under-uncertainty was a family habit. (OP03, OP10)
- **Place & era:** moved to **Thailand at age 5** (~4 years), then, with the Vietnam War making the region unstable, the parents made a hard, forward-looking bet: **sent Jensen (≈9) and his older brother alone to the US in 1973** to an uncle, ahead of the parents themselves. An immigrant child of the 1970s, displaced twice before age 10. (OP03, OP10)
- **Schooling primary → high:** the uncle enrolled the boys at **Oneida Baptist Institute, Kentucky** — understood by the family as a prep school, in fact a **religious reform academy for troubled rural youth** in Appalachia. Jensen, ~10, **cleaned the bathrooms daily, shared a dorm with much older boys, and taught his illiterate 17-year-old roommate to read** (in exchange for weightlifting lessons). Became a **nationally ranked junior table-tennis player** in the same window. Family reunited and settled in **Beaverton, Oregon**; **Aloha High School**, where he skipped grades and graduated ~16. Worked the **graveyard/back-of-house at Denny's (busboy, dishwasher, waiter)** as a teenager. (OP03, OP10)
- **University & education:** **Oregon State University, BSEE, 1984** (graduated with high honors at ~20); met **Lori Mills**, his future wife, as his **engineering lab partner**. Later **Stanford MSEE, 1992**, earned **at night/weekends while already working** at LSI Logic. (OP03, OP04, OP10)
- **First job / first venture:** **AMD — microprocessor designer (~1984)**, then **LSI Logic** (rose to run a division; the LSI/Sun relationship is where he met the future co-founders' world). He behaved, pre-audience, as a **hands-on chip engineer who also climbed into management** — builder + operator, not pure scientist. (OP02, OP03)
- → **What this likely formed** (cautious pattern, not determinism): **engineering-first identity** (chip-designer father-analog career, EE degrees); **extreme resilience / comfort at the bottom** (reform-school bathrooms, Denny's graveyard shift, displaced immigrant child — "30 days from going out of business" as a *native* worldview, not a slogan); **preparation-under-uncertainty** (the family's whole arc); **earned-not-given frame** (worked menial jobs young, paid his own way through Stanford at night). The Denny's-founding choice 20 years later **rhymes** with the Denny's where he bussed tables — high internal consistency.

*Adult track (compressed):* AMD → LSI Logic → **co-founds NVIDIA April 5, 1993, at age 30**, at a Denny's in East San Jose, with Malachowsky and Priem; seed-backed by **Don Valentine / Sequoia** (Valentine reportedly vouched via LSI's Wilf Corrigan) (OP13). Named President/CEO on day one and **has held it ~30 years** (OP02). Crucible behavior is the core evidence: the **NV1 architecture bet failed** (chose quadrilaterals; Microsoft's Direct3D standardized on triangles), and Huang executed a **bet-the-company pivot to the RIVA 128 (1997), cutting ~50% of staff** with "enough money to cover salaries for the following month" (OP11); separately, on the **Sega Dreamcast contract he flew to Japan to tell Sega the contracted tech would not work, asked to be released from the contract, AND asked Sega to still pay** — a ~$5M payment that helped the company survive (well-documented pre-2023 founder lore; OP11-adjacent). **Does adult conduct match the formed values?** Strongly: the reform-school-and-Denny's resilience and engineering-first frame show up directly in the crucible record. Motivation + fire at as-of: a 60-year-old still running the company he founded, ~3.77% owner (large absolute, modest %) — legacy/mission-shaped skin in the game, not a cash-out posture (OP01).

*Dark-arc (required):* (1) **Key-man singularity** — he is strategy, culture, and public face; **no disclosed successor**; a flat org routes everything through him (OP01, OP14). (2) **The survivorship trap in his own legend** — the celebrated pre-1998 judgment was *twice nearly fatal*; the same decisiveness that saved the company is the decisiveness that almost killed it, and the saved version is the one that gets retold (discount the polished crucible narrative). (3) **Intensity/demanding-culture** — the flat-org "everything in the open, I'll say it to the group" style (OP14, lead only) is widely framed as demanding; as a material pack we record this as a **lead, not a verified culture finding**, because the load-bearing quotes are post-as-of. (4) **Cult-of-founder optics** — a 30-year founder-CEO who is the brand invites personality-cult dynamics the board must counterweight.

*Reliability axis:* unchooseable facts (Taiwan birth, immigrant displacement, Oneida Baptist Institute, Denny's jobs, OSU/Stanford degrees) are **high-reliability** (OP03/OP04/OP10, cross-validated). The dramatic crucible stories (Sega, "30 days from going out of business") are **founder-narrated lore** — directionally corroborated but **discount the polish**. Avoid the post-as-of accolade list ("world's best CEO") entirely.

*Guardrails:* no determinism. Direction of any departure: the formation predicts an engineering-first, bottom-comfortable builder; the adult record matches rather than departs — the main risk is not value-drift but **over-concentration of those values in one person**.

### Team dark-arc / fracture inversion

Where could the team break → kill criteria (for a FUTURE Runner, not a decision here):
- **Founder key-man event** (Huang departs/incapacitated, age 60) → flat org + no named successor = the clearest fracture line. **Kill/trim trigger** if he exits without a credible, pre-built succession.
- **Strategic over-concentration** — if Malachowsky/Kress are *carriers/amplifiers* rather than independent checks, the team's judgment is effectively one person's; a wrong founder bet has no internal brake. **Watch:** evidence of genuine internal dissent vs. founder.
- **Capital-allocation regime change** — a 35-year-operating-CFO's discipline is the counterweight to founder ambition; **watch** a CFO transition or a shift from operating-finance discipline to empire-funding (NOT graded here; flagged for the cycle).
- **Culture dilution at scale** — the flat/open culture is founder-dependent; rapid scaling without the founder's presence could dilute it. **Watch.**
- (Governance gives **partial comfort**: one-share-one-vote + 12/13 independent board + independent Lead Director means a value-destroying founder *can* in principle be checked by shareholders/board — unusual for a 30-year founder-CEO. The risk is operational indispensability, not voting entrenchment.)

### Verdict → durability ceiling

- **Team life-arc grade (1–5): 4/5.** A rare, battle-tested, **30-year founder core** (Huang + Malachowsky) with proven crucible-survival, complemented by a long-tenured operating CFO, governed by a genuinely independent one-share-one-vote board. The single deduction from 5 is **acute key-man/succession concentration on a 60-year-old founder with no disclosed heir**, plus an under-documented (here) capital-allocation record and a thin public formation record on the CFO.
- **Ceiling effect:** the proven cohesive long-arc team and clean governance **support a higher durability read** than a typical founder-dependent tech company (because the board is not captured); **but** the unresolved succession/key-man question and (in this pack) the un-graded capital-allocation cycle **cap** how far that can be pushed. Net: a **team that raises, not lowers, the durability ceiling — with the cap set by succession.**

- Tax rate 14.0% +/- 1%.

- Data Center: record $4.28B, up 14% Y/Y, up 18% Q/Q.

Capital return: returned $99M in cash dividends in Q1 FY24; next quarterly dividend $0.04/share (record date 2023-06-08, payable 2023-06-30).

## S002 - NVIDIA Q1 FY2024 CFO Commentary (Colette Kress, 2023-05-24)

Revenue by reportable segment (Q1 FY24):

- Compute & Networking: $4,460M (up 21% Q/Q, up 21% Y/Y).
- Graphics: $2,732M (up 15% Q/Q, down 41% Y/Y).
- Total: $7,192M.

Revenue by market platform (Q1 FY24, with prior periods):

- Data Center: $4,284M (Q4 FY23 $3,616M; Q1 FY23 $3,750M) — up 18% Q/Q, up 14% Y/Y.
- Gaming: $2,240M — up 22% Q/Q, down 38% Y/Y.
- Professional Visualization: $295M.
- Automotive: $296M.
- OEM and Other: $77M.

Demand narrative (paraphrase / quotes from commentary):

- "Data Center revenue was a record, up 14% from a year ago and up 18% sequentially, led by growing demand for generative AI and large language models using GPUs based on our NVIDIA Hopper and Ampere architectures."
- "The revenue growth reflects strong demand from large consumer internet companies and cloud service providers." Enterprise demand for GPU platforms was described as strong; general-purpose networking solutions declined.
- Gaming: Y/Y decline attributed to macroeconomic slowdown and channel-inventory normalization; sequential increase driven by the ramp of GeForce RTX 40 Series (Ada Lovelace).

Gross margin bridge (Q1 FY24):

- GAAP gross profit $4,648M; GAAP gross margin 64.6%.
- Non-GAAP gross profit $4,802M; non-GAAP gross margin 66.8%.
- Sequential margin increase attributed to lower Gaming costs and higher Data Center margins as the Hopper architecture ramps.

Supply / obligations and balance-sheet commentary:

- "Cash, cash equivalents and marketable securities were $15.32 billion, down from $20.34 billion a year ago and up from $13.30 billion a quarter ago."
- Year-on-year cash decrease reflects $8.04 billion in stock repurchases.
- Inventory $4.61B (DSI 165); accounts receivable $4.08B (DSO 52).
- "Gross inventory purchase and long-term supply and capacity obligations were $7.27 billion and prepaid supply agreements were $3.40 billion."
- Other purchase obligations $3.26B, inclusive of $2.43B of multi-year cloud-service agreements.
- Operating cash flow $2.91B; free cash flow $2.64B.
- Starting fiscal 2024, useful lives of most servers/storage/network equipment extended from three years to a range of four to five years.

Q2 FY2024 outlook detail (as given):

- GAAP gross margin 68.6%; non-GAAP 70.0%.
- GAAP operating expenses $2,710M; non-GAAP $1,900M.

Use: segment granularity, the explicit generative-AI demand attribution, supply-capacity obligations, and balance sheet for material spine.

## B. Q1 2025 earnings call — management commentary [S004] (2025-05-01)

- **Spares ~20% of total revenue in Q1; up ~33% overall.** Within **aerospace & defense, spares +40%+**;
  IGT/oil & gas spares ~+15%.
- **Defense aerospace +19% YoY**, driven by engine-spares demand; **F-35 spares growth "notable."**
- **LEAP / LPT (low-pressure-turbine) parts destocking:** prior-year over-production is a current headwind;
  management expects to be **beyond the destocking effect by Q3/Q4** as LEAP production rises (~450+ engines/qtr).
- **Guidance raised** on (a) commercial-aero spares strength and (b) **Boeing 737 build-rate assumptions being raised**.
  Wide-body recovery assumed **slower** (787 ramp delayed ~3 months; A350 ~5.5 vs 6); A320 mid-50s.
- **Incremental flow-through of revenue to EBITDA "excellent at more than 100%"** in Q1; Structures margin +720 bps to 21.3%.
- Balance sheet: **net debt / trailing EBITDA at a record-low ~1.4x**, expected to improve **toward ~1.1x by year-end**.
  **Fitch upgraded HWM from BBB to BBB+ ("three notches into investment grade")** in Q1.
- Tariffs: gross worst-case ~$80M; **net impact <$15M in 2025** after mitigation (duty drawback, bonded warehouse,
  free-trade zones; force-majeure letters issued).
- Buyback authorization remaining **~$2.0B as of April**.
- Forged Wheels: despite −13% revenue, **27% EBITDA margin** held.

## C. FY2024 results — press release [S001] + 10-K [S002] (2025-02-13)

- **FY2024 revenue $7.43B (+12% YoY)**; aerospace ~68% of revenue, commercial transportation 17%, industrial/other 15%.
- FY2024 segments: **Engine Products $3.735B (+14%)**, Fastening $1.576B (+17%), Engineered Structures $1.065B (+21%),
  Forged Wheels $1.054B (−8%).
- **FY2024 commercial aerospace revenue +20%**, supported by rising OEM production rates and **healthy engine-spares demand**.
- FY2024 operating income **$1.633B**; **adjusted EBITDA $1.914B (25.8% margin)**; **FCF $977M** (~88% of net income excl. special items).
- **COGS 68.9% of sales (down from 71.9% in FY2023)** — margin expansion from volume + favorable pricing (10-K MD&A).
- Net income **$1.155B**; GAAP diluted EPS **$2.81**; FY24 avg diluted shares ~410M.
- Balance sheet 12/31/24: cash **$564M**, total debt **~$3.315B**; **gross debt reduced $365M in FY24** (~$37M annualized interest saving).
- FY2024 capital return: **$500M buybacks** (avg ~$86.65); dividend raised. Initial **FY2025 guide** (Feb): adj EBITDA
  $2.105–2.155B, adj EPS $3.13–3.21, FCF $1.025–1.125B — **subsequently raised at Q1** (see A).
- 2025 outlook (10-K): commercial aerospace growth (wide- and narrow-body) expected to continue; commercial transportation
  expected to recover in H2 2025; defense aerospace to increase.

## D. Industry context — Boeing/Airbus build rates [S007] (2025-04-10, March 2025 data)

- **Boeing Q1 2025 deliveries 130** (Jan 45 / Feb 44 / Mar 41); **737 MAX still below the FAA-capped 38/month** (March 33).
- **Airbus Q1 2025 deliveries 136**; 2025 official delivery target ~820 (Forecast Int'l ~840); ramp constrained by supply chain + tariffs.
- Backlogs are vast: **Airbus 8,720 aircraft (~10.4 years), Boeing 6,319 (~11.1 years)**; A320 family 88.9% of Airbus
  unfilled orders, 737 MAX 75.4% of Boeing's — i.e. a multi-year narrowbody up-cycle still ramping toward (not past) peak rates.

## E. Operator / structure [S008] (2020-04-01) + [S004]

  stated capital-allocation playbook: disciplined volume/margin growth, debt reduction, buybacks, measured dividend.

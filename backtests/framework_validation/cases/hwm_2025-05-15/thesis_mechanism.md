## 1. Integrated Thesis (template fields)

**Theme.** The post-COVID commercial-aerospace up-cycle: airlines are flying record traffic on an installed
base that was under-maintained through 2020–22, and OEMs (Boeing, Airbus) are ramping narrow-body production
toward — but still **below** — target rates. Both forces drive demand for the engineered metal content that
HWM makes: turbine airfoils/blades, fasteners, and structures. (S007: Boeing/Airbus backlogs ~10–11 years;
737 MAX still under the FAA-capped 38/mo in Q1'25 — the ramp is mid-stream, not finished.)

**Why now.** Two things crystallized by 2025-05-15: (1) HWM **raised its full-year 2025 guide one quarter in**
(adj EBITDA $2.13B→$2.25B, adj EPS $3.17→$3.40, FCF $1.075B→$1.15B; S003 vs S001), explicitly on **spares
strength + rising Boeing 737 build-rate assumptions** (S004); and (2) the company crossed into a **record-low
~1.4x net-debt/EBITDA with a Fitch upgrade to BBB+** (S004) — the balance-sheet repair is essentially done, so
incremental cash now flows to buybacks/dividends rather than deleveraging. The business is inflecting on both
the income statement and the capital-return story at the same time.

**Market narrative.** "Best-in-class aerospace supplier, secular demand, fortress execution — but the stock is
expensive (≈47x forward earnings) and sitting at an all-time/52-week high after a post-earnings pop." The bull
case is quality + cycle; the bear case is purely valuation/timing.

**What the market may be over-emphasizing.** (a) The optical multiple in isolation — ~47x P/E on a business
compounding EPS ~50% YoY with >100% incremental margins is less extreme than it looks on a forward-two-year
view, but the *headline* number scares value buyers. (b) Boeing's troubles as if they cap HWM — HWM offsets
OEM lumpiness with a 40%+ -growing, higher-margin **spares** stream and rising **defense** (F-35) content.

**What the market may be under-emphasizing.** (a) The **spares mix shift** (now ~20% of revenue, +33%, aero/
defense +40%): spares are a higher-margin annuity tied to the *flying installed base*, not to OEM build rates,
so they de-risk the cycle and lift through-cycle margin. (b) The **>100% incremental EBITDA flow-through** and
+500 bps YoY operating-margin step — pricing power + yield gains that may prove partly structural. (c) The
**de-levered, IG-upgraded balance sheet** funding an open-ended ~$2.0B buyback into rising FCF.

**Capital-flow evidence.** Company is a **net buyer of its own shares** ($125M Q1 + $100M April; ~$2.0B
authorization remaining; S003/S004) and **doubled the dividend** — management is voting with the balance sheet.

**Demand shock.** Structural, not a spike: global air traffic back above pre-COVID, an aging in-service fleet
needing turbine spares, OEMs ramping to clear ~10–11-year backlogs (S007). LEAP/LPT destocking (S004) is the
one near-term air-pocket — a working-capital timing effect mgmt expects to clear by Q3/Q4, **not** a demand loss.

**Scarce resource / bottleneck.** Qualified, certified production of single-crystal turbine airfoils and
aero-grade fasteners/structures. These are flight-safety-critical, decades-qualified on specific engine
platforms (LEAP, GTF, GE9X, F135), with long requalification cycles — a textbook switching-cost moat (developed
in M3).

**Who pays.** Engine OEMs (GE/Safran via CFM, Pratt & Whitney/RTX, Rolls-Royce), airframers (Boeing, Airbus),
and — for spares — the MRO/aftermarket chain ultimately funded by airlines' flight-hours.

**Unit of value.** A shipset/engine-set of qualified airfoils + fasteners + structures per aircraft, *plus* a
recurring spares stream per engine flight-hour over a multi-decade service life.

**Unit of cost.** Specialty alloys (nickel superalloys, titanium), energy, skilled labor, and qualified capacity.
Pricing has been **favorable** (COGS 68.9% of sales in FY24 vs 71.9% FY23; S002) — HWM is passing through and then some.

**Profit pool.** The high-margin nodes of the aero value chain are (1) the engine-airfoil content (Engine
Products, HWM's largest + fastest segment, +13–14%) and (2) the **aftermarket spares annuity** layered on top.
HWM sits squarely on both.

**Why this company captures it.** Sole/dual-source qualified positions on the highest-volume engine programs;
a pure-play focus since the 2020 spin-off; an operator (Plant) who has driven +500 bps margin and a record-low
leverage ratio. (Developed in M3.)

**One metric that will force a market update.** **Commercial-aerospace + spares organic growth vs. the raised
guide** — concretely, whether adj EBITDA tracks the new $2.225–2.275B FY25 range and spares stays +30–40%.
A miss (or a Boeing rate cut) re-rates a 47x stock hard; a beat extends the compounding.

**Catalyst.** (1) Boeing 737 MAX rate breaking above the FAA-capped 38/mo and the 787 ramp resuming → step-up
in OEM volume; (2) continued spares strength + LEAP destocking clearing in H2'25; (3) buyback shrinking the share
count into rising EPS.

**Kill condition.** A demand rollover (air-traffic shock / recession cutting flight hours and spares), a
structural Boeing/Airbus rate cut, OR a self-inflicted capital-allocation error (overpriced M&A, leverage
re-expansion). At this price, **a "good but not great" result is enough to de-rate the multiple** — so the kill
condition includes *the thesis merely decelerating*, not only breaking.

## 2. Context-Label Reasoning

This is a **`quality_compounder`** riding a **`cyclical_inflection`** — and the distinction drives the sizing
trap. As a pure cyclical bought near a price high, the danger is paying peak multiple on peak earnings. But three
facts argue this is **mid-cycle quality, not late-cycle commodity**:

1. **Build rates are still below target** (737 MAX < 38/mo; wide-body delayed; backlogs ~10–11 years; S007) —
   the OEM-volume ramp is unfinished, so FY25 is unlikely to be peak earnings.
2. **The spares annuity** (~20% of revenue, +40% in aero/defense) is **counter-cyclical to OEM lumpiness** and
   structurally higher-margin — it raises the floor and the through-cycle multiple a pure airframe-cycle name deserves.
3. **Margins + balance sheet are still improving** (op margin +500 bps YoY, leverage 1.4x→target 1.1x, IG upgrade) —
   the operating leverage and capital-return flywheel have room to run.

So the methodology's named `quality_compounder` failure — *"selling or avoiding because the classic multiple
looks high"* — is the primary trap to resist. **But** the equally real risk specific to *this entry* is the
mirror image: **chasing a 52-week high on a richly-valued cyclical**. The honest synthesis (carried into M6) is
**own the quality, but discipline the price** — a starter with a defined buy-below, not a full-size chase at $161.

**M2 = +2:** the mechanism is real, multi-year, evidenced, and captured by the right company. The reservation
is entirely about *price*, which is M6's job, not M2's.

## 0. Module Verdict

- Role: conviction / warning (dual)
- M4 signal: **0** (net of two strong but opposing reads)

**Normalized read.** The forward numbers are *guided and credible*, but they are **up-cycle** numbers. Two-year-out
EPS (if the build-rate ramp + spares continue) could be meaningfully higher, pulling the forward P/E toward the
high-30s — which is how a bull justifies the entry. The bear note: if FY25 is closer to a cycle peak than a
mid-point, ~47x on near-peak EPS is the **double-count risk** (peak multiple × peak earnings) that defines a
cyclical top.

## 3. Cycle / Trough / Peak Check (the decisive M4 question for a cyclical-at-a-high)

**Evidence we are MID-ramp, not at peak (supports owning):**
- **OEM build rates are still below target:** Boeing 737 MAX **below the FAA-capped 38/mo** in Q1'25 (March 33);
  787 ramp delayed ~3 months; A350 ~5.5; backlogs **~10–11 years** (S007). Volume has further to climb.
- **Spares are still inflecting** (+40% aero/defense) and the **LEAP/LPT destocking headwind has yet to clear**
  (expected H2'25; S004) — i.e. a *suppressant* on current numbers, not a tailwind that has already peaked.
- **Margins + leverage still improving** (28.8% and 1.4x→1.1x) — operating-leverage runway remains.

**Evidence of peak-ish risk (warns):**
- **Incremental EBITDA flow-through >100%** is, by definition, not a sustainable run-rate — current-quarter
  profitability is being flattered by exceptional operating leverage.
- The stock is at a **52-week high (−0.27% off high, +110% off low; S006)** — the *market* is already pricing a
  durable up-cycle. Sentiment, not just earnings, is elevated.
- HWM remains **cyclical**: a recession / air-traffic shock would cut flight hours (spares) and OEM volumes together.

**Synthesis:** the *business* is mid-ramp (earnings likely still rising), but the *price* already assumes the
ramp continues smoothly. The asymmetry at $161 is therefore **narrower than the business quality alone implies** —
which the −1 cycle/valuation read captures, netting M4 to **0**.

## 4. Balance Sheet & Solvency

- 3/31/25: cash **$536M**, LT debt **$3,317M** → **net debt ~$2.79B**; **net debt/EBITDA ~1.4x** (record low).
- **Fitch upgrade to BBB+** (three notches into IG; S004); gross debt cut $365M in FY24 (S002).
- **No solvency risk; ample flexibility** to fund the ~$2.0B buyback + dividend from FY24 FCF $977M / FY25 guide
  $1.15B. This is the opposite of a balance-sheet trap — and the strongest single argument that downside is
  *survivable* (the business won't be forced; only the multiple is at risk).

## 5. Why M4 = 0 (not +1)

The accounting, margins, cash conversion, and balance sheet are **excellent and confirm the thesis** — on a
quality axis this is a +1 business. But M4's job in a `cyclical_inflection / quality_compounder` at a price high
is to test **normalized economics and cycle position**, and that test says the **current earnings power is
up-cycle and the price already embeds it**. The two reads offset: M4 emits **0**, handing the decision to price
discipline (M6) and the inversion/cycle risk (M5), rather than adding conviction at $161.

## 2. Evidence vs Interpretation

### Evidence (as-of, audited)

- Headline revenue was only modestly down YoY, but the company swung to a GAAP operating
  and net loss, and non-GAAP operating margin collapsed to ~0.2%.
- The single largest internal drag is Intel Foundry: a $2.83B quarterly operating loss and
  a $7.0B full-year-2023 loss, with management's own framing that 2024 is the trough and
  break-even is years out (~2027).
- The company simultaneously announced three capital-allocation actions: a >15% headcount
  cut, a >$10B cost-reduction plan, and suspension of a decades-old dividend — explicitly
  to prioritize liquidity for the foundry/IDM 2.0 buildout.
- Balance sheet shows large gross liquidity (~$29.3B cash + ST investments) but also ~$53B
  of total debt, and H1 operating cash flow ($1.07B) far below H1 gross capex ($11.65B),
  with the gap bridged by partner contributions and debt.
- Segment mix: CCG (PC) is the profit engine and grew on AI-PC units; DCAI (data center)
  revenue and profit fell YoY; the AI-accelerator and data-center-GPU momentum in the
  contemporaneous peer set sits with Nvidia and AMD.
- The market repriced the equity ~26% lower in one session to $21.48 on record volume.

### Interpretation (framing only — NOT a decision)

- The cleanly verifiable tension for the future decision pass is between (a) a self-funded
  turnaround/cyclical-reset reading (cost cuts + dividend suspension building liquidity,
  process leadership claimed to return via 18A, PC unit strength) and (b) a
  structural-decline reading (data-center share/AI-accelerator miss, a foundry that loses
  billions with a multi-year path to break-even, and heavy capex against thin operating
  cash flow).
- This pack does not adjudicate that tension. It only assembles the as-of facts on both
  sides.

### Sentiment / Narrative Only (lead, not proof)

- News framing ("worst day in ~50 years," "$25-32B value erased") and management's forward
  optimism (18A "regain process leadership," Gaudi 3 "2x perf/$ vs H100") are recorded as
  context/claims, not as verified outcomes.

## 4. Open Questions for the FUTURE Decision Pass (not answered here)

- Is H1 2024's low operating cash flow vs high gross capex bridgeable without further
  dilution or leverage, given the dividend is now suspended and capex is being cut?

  or is it a signal of deeper cash strain?
- How much of the DCAI decline is cyclical (inventory, China, TAM shift to AI servers) vs
  structural (share loss, missing the AI-accelerator wave)?

  how should an outsider weight a management-stated timeline?

  implied — a question for M4/M6 in the future test, deliberately left open here.

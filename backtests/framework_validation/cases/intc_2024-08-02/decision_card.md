## Main reason to own / not to own

- **Main reason to own (the bull case being declined):** a once-dominant franchise repriced ~26% to ~$92B (sub-book optics), with ~$29B gross liquidity and a still-profitable PC engine, executing a self-funded reset (dividend suspension + capex/opex cuts) that *extends* runway while management claims process leadership returns via 18A and a ~2027 foundry break-even.
- **Main reason NOT to own:** the richest profit pool in semis (AI/data-center compute) is being captured by Nvidia (~90%+ accelerator share) and AMD (record DC quarter, >$1B MI300X) while Intel's DCAI shrinks; the foundry loses ~$2.8B/quarter (~$7B/yr, >85% pre-EUV wafers) with break-even years out; and H1 operating cash flow (~$1.07B) covers less than a tenth of gross capex (~$11.65B), with the gap bridged by partner contributions + debt against ~$53B total debt. Optical cheapness is the trap signature, not a margin of safety.

## Binding constraint

**The foundry cash-burn-vs-thin-operating-cash-flow gap.** The multi-year, largely irreversible fab buildout is being funded by financial engineering (partner contributions + debt), not by the business, while the data-center profit pool migrates to competitors. This holds the position at 0%/WATCH — a near-veto SIZE constraint. Real money is unlocked only when foundry losses are demonstrably past trough AND cash covers a materially larger share of capex, or the data-center franchise stabilizes.

## Evidence confidence haircut

Evidence quality is **high** (primary filings, verified price) — so confidence does NOT add a haircut. But high confidence does not rescue the case: the verdict is driven by *negative financial reality* (M4) and *trap risk* (M5), which high-quality evidence makes more, not less, certain.

## Downside if wrong / upside if right

- **Downside if wrong (we own and it's a trap):** open-ended — continued foundry losses, further cash strain, possible equity dilution, no dividend cushion; a sub-book stock can still halve again if owner earnings stay negative. Not survivable-with-upside at a real position.
- **Upside if right (we tracked and it's a bottom):** if the foundry troughs in 2024 and cash coverage improves, the re-rate from ~$92B on a stabilizing data-center + cheap PC engine could be large. The discipline is to capture that by re-rating to a STARTER *on confirmation*, foregoing only the first leg — an acceptable cost to avoid catching a falling knife.

## Triggers

- **Add trigger:** forward confirmation of (a) foundry past trough + (b) cash covering more of capex without financial-engineering offsets + (c) DCAI stabilizing → re-rate to STARTER 3–4%.
- **Trim trigger (for an existing holder):** immediate — the thesis of a self-funding turnaround is unconfirmed; trim toward tracking now.
- **Exit / kill criteria:**
  1. Foundry losses do NOT trough in 2024 (flat/widening into 2025) → central premise fails.
  2. OCF stays far below gross capex with the gap still bridged by partner contributions + new debt, OR an equity raise / further duress-driven capex cut → cash strain is structural.
  3. DCAI revenue/share keeps eroding as Nvidia/AMD extend their lead → data-center pool permanently lost.
  4. CCG (the lone profit engine) weakens materially → the cash cow funding the bet is impaired.
  5. 18A milestones slip or independent (non-management) evidence contradicts "regain process leadership" → manufacturing thesis broken.
  6. Total debt rises / leverage deteriorates / any rating or covenant stress → survival (not just returns) in question → converts the near-veto into a hard zero (REJECT).

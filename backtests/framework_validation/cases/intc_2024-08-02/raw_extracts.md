Header / news summary (verbatim figures):

- Headline: "Intel Reports Second-Quarter 2024 Financial Results — Announces $10 Billion Cost
  Reduction Plan to Increase Efficiency and Market Competitiveness." Dated "SANTA CLARA,
  Calif., Aug. 1, 2024."
- Second-quarter revenue of $12.8 billion, down 1% year over year.
- Second-quarter GAAP EPS attributable to Intel was $(0.38); non-GAAP EPS was $0.02.
- Forecasting Q3 2024 revenue of $12.5 billion to $13.5 billion; expecting Q3 GAAP EPS of

Cost-reduction plan (four priorities, verbatim points):

- Reducing Operating Expenses: cut non-GAAP R&D and MG&A to ~$20B in 2024 and ~$17.5B in
  2025, with further reductions in 2026. "Intel expects to reduce headcount by greater
  than 15% with the majority completed by the end of 2024."
- Reducing Capital Expenditures: reduce gross capital expenditures in 2024 by more than
  20% from prior projections, to between $25B and $27B in 2024; net capital spending in
  2024 of $11B-$13B. For 2025, gross capex $20B-$23B and net capital spending $12B-$14B.
- Reducing Cost of Sales: expects ~$1 billion in savings in non-variable cost of sales in
  2025; "Product mix will continue to be a headwind next year, contributing to modest YoY
  improvements to 2025's gross margin."
- Maintaining Core Investments: continues to advance process and product roadmap; total
  opex + capex reductions of more than $10 billion in 2025 vs previous estimates.
- "Intel is taking the added step of suspending the dividend starting in the fourth
  quarter, recognizing the importance of prioritizing liquidity to support the investments

## S002 - Intel Q2 2024 Earnings Call (held Aug 1 2024)

Management commentary (close paraphrase / quoted phrases):

- On weak Q2 / gross margin: profitability was below expectations "due in part by our
  decision to more quickly ramp Core Ultra AI CPUs"; additional headwinds from the
  decision to "accelerate transition of Intel 4 and 3 wafers from our development fab in
  Oregon to our high volume facility in Ireland, where wafer costs are higher in the near
  term," plus "higher-than-typical period charges related to noncore businesses and charges
  associated with unused capacity," and "unfavorable product mix and more competitive
  pricing."
- On headcount / cost plan: "headcount reduction of greater than 15% by the end of 2025,
  with the majority of this action completed by the end of this year"; total cost savings
  "more than 10 billion in 2025"; operating expenses targeted at ~$17.5 billion in 2025.
- On the dividend: "suspending the dividend at the beginning of the fourth quarter,
  recognizing the importance of prioritizing liquidity to support the investments needed to
  execute our strategy."
- On Intel Foundry: Q2 foundry operating loss of ~$2.8 billion expected to continue "at
  approximately the same rate in Q3"; over "85% of wafer volume still coming from pre-EUV
  nodes with an uncompetitive cost structure"; management reiterated a foundry break-even
  objective in the 2027 timeframe.
- On the weaker second half: "weaker spending across consumer and enterprise markets,
  especially in China"; "continued focus on AI server investments in the cloud have
  reduced our TAM expectations for 2024"; elevated customer inventory; export-control
  impact on the China client business.
- On data center / AI: Sierra Forest e-core showing ">25% TCO value" early in ramp;
  Granite Rapids launching in the quarter; Gaudi 3 expected to deliver "roughly 2x
  performance per dollar in both inference and training versus H100."
- On liquidity: actions expected to "significantly improve our liquidity in 2025 and
  position us to begin the process of meaningfully decreasing our leverage"; 2025 adjusted
  free cash flow targeted to be positive.

Use: management's own framing of why results were weak, the foundry loss run-rate, and the
liquidity rationale for the dividend suspension. Treat optimistic forward statements as
management framing, not verified fact.

## S004 / S012 - Intel Foundry Financial Framework (8-K, Apr 2 2024)

- Intel Foundry had an operating loss of $7.0 billion in 2023, compared with an operating
  loss of $5.2 billion in 2022.
- Management said Intel Foundry operating losses are expected to peak in 2024 ("2024 is the
  trough for foundry operating losses") as Intel completes its five-nodes-in-four-years
  journey.
- Break-even target for the foundry business in the 2027 timeframe; longer-term operating
  margin goal of ~30% by ~2030.
- Almost all foundry revenue is internal under the new operating model.

Use: sizing the foundry drag and management's stated path/timeline — context for both the
"turnaround" and "structural-decline" framings of the decision question.

## S011 - AMD Q2 2024 Peer / Competitive Context (reported Jul 30 2024)

- AMD reported record data-center segment revenue for Q2 2024, including more than $1
  billion of MI300X AI-accelerator sales in the quarter, with data-center revenue up
  sharply year over year.
- AMD raised its 2024 data-center GPU revenue expectation (to well above $4 billion) on
  stronger-than-expected demand.
- Contemporaneous industry commentary placed Nvidia at well above ~90% share of
  data-center AI accelerators, with AMD as the main alternative and gaining server-CPU
  share, while Intel's Xeon remained the unit leader in server CPUs but under pressure.

Use: independent, contemporaneous peer evidence on the competitive backdrop (AI-accelerator
dominance by Nvidia, AMD momentum in data-center GPU and server CPU) against which Intel's
DCAI decline and foundry investment must be read. Context, not core proof of any verdict.

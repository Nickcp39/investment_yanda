# Simple Position Rules Draft

Date: 2026-06-17

Status: draft note only. Do not treat this as the final pipeline or portfolio policy.

Purpose: preserve the simpler Duan Yongping / Munger-style position-management direction after the SNDK sizing discussion. The goal is a one-page, memorable system: simple, concentrated, patient, and cash-disciplined.

## 1. Why This Direction

The prior portfolio-controller idea became too complex. The user preference is closer to early Duan Yongping / Munger:

- Only buy what is understood.
- Concentrate when odds are unusually favorable.
- Hold cash when nothing is cheap enough.
- Use cash-secured puts or limit orders to get paid for waiting.
- Avoid leverage, shorting, and complicated tactical overlays.

Buffett's large cash position at Berkshire is not a clean template because Berkshire has insurance float and liability-driven liquidity needs. The better analogy is a private investor with no forced-liability cash need.

## 2. Source Notes And Confidence

### Duan Yongping

Useful mechanism:

- Concentrated value investing based on understanding the business.
- Cash is acceptable when there is no clear opportunity.
- Sell puts can turn waiting into cash flow if the strike is a price the investor genuinely wants to buy.

Evidence notes:

- Multiple public profiles describe Duan buying NetEase around the 2001/2002 distress period, when the stock traded below or around $1 and faced delisting / legal pressure.
- Exact entry price, share count, and holding-period return vary by source. Do not hard-code a precise 100x claim in formal rules without primary verification.
- The robust lesson is not the exact multiple. It is: he sized heavily only because he believed he understood NetEase's business and downside better than the market.

### Charlie Munger

Useful mechanism:

- Big money is made by waiting for rare, obvious opportunities and betting meaningfully when the odds are favorable.
- Excessive diversification dilutes best ideas.
- The hard part is temperament: doing nothing most of the time.

Evidence notes:

- Public quotes are often recycled through secondary sources. Use cautiously, but the theme is consistent across Munger's public record.
- The cleanest rule to borrow: when odds are clearly favorable, size must matter; otherwise do not bet.

### Cboe PutWrite / Cash-Secured Put

Useful mechanism:

- Duan-style sell-put waiting can be institutionalized as a fully collateralized put-writing discipline.
- The crucial rule is not "sell puts." It is "never sell more puts than cash / T-bills can fully cover."

Evidence notes:

- The Cboe PUT Index sells monthly at-the-money SPX puts over a Treasury-bill account.
- Cboe's published material describes lower volatility and drawdown versus the S&P 500 over long histories, but put-writing can lag in strong bull markets and has equity downside exposure.
- For individual stocks, only use cash-secured puts at prices where assignment is genuinely welcome.

## 3. One-Page Rule Card Draft

### Position Tiers

| Tier | Weight | Use Case | Requirements |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Core heavy position | 20%-35% | Rare long-duration idea | Understandable, durable, cheap or fair, willing to hold 5-10 years, no balance-sheet kill risk |
| Normal position | 8%-15% | Attractive but less certain | Understandable, positive asymmetry, still needs confirmation or valuation is only fair |
| Test position | 3%-5% | New thesis / convex setup | High upside but unconfirmed; must have explicit add and kill triggers |
| Zero | 0% | Outside circle or thesis broken | Too hard, too levered, no downside control, or better opportunity cost |

### Five Rules

1. Buy only what is understood. If it is outside the circle of competence, the position is 0%.
2. Size by conviction, not by formula. Move from test to normal to core only when understanding and evidence improve.
3. Cash is a residual with a floor. If nothing is cheap enough, hold cash / T-bills. Keep enough cash to avoid forced selling.
4. Sell only for three reasons: the thesis is wrong, price is absurd versus value, or a clearly superior opportunity needs capital.
5. No leverage, no naked options, no shorting, no constant market timing.

### Cash / Put Rule

Cash can wait in T-bills or be used for cash-secured puts:

- Only sell puts at prices where assignment is welcome.
- Treat assigned shares as a planned buy, not an accident.
- Fully reserve cash for the maximum assignment obligation.
- Do not sell puts on a true fat pitch; buy the stock directly.

## 4. SNDK Implication

The old SNDK output was directionally right but under-sized.

Under this draft card:

- At 2025-06-16, SNDK would likely start as a test position, not 1.5%.
- A reasonable draft range would be 3%-5% if the investor truly understood the spin-off / NAND-cycle setup.
- It could move toward a normal position only after gross margin, revenue, and enterprise / cloud demand confirmed.
- It should not become a core heavy position unless the business became durable beyond a cyclical NAND recovery.

This is a sizing lesson, not a retroactive recommendation.

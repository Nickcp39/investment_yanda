## Sub-sector themes (top-down)

1. **Clinical volume is HEALTHY across US labs — reference-lab consolidation is compounding it.** Quest (+9.2% rev, requisition vol +10.9%) and Labcorp (+5.8%) both raised FY26 guidance on strong volume + hospital-outreach M&A (Quest–Corewell JV ~$250M, Fresenius $80–100M). The "labs buy hospital outreach + regional independents" flywheel is the durable growth engine — scale + automation/AI lowers unit cost, wins more insurer/health-system deals.

2. **Molecular / NGS / liquid biopsy is the fastest-growing layer AND the M&A magnet.** NeoGenomics NGS +26%, RaDaR ST + PanTracer LBx MolDX coverage opening new TAM. The blockbuster: **Abbott closed its ~$21B ($105/sh cash) acquisition of Exact Sciences on 2026-03-23**, buying Cologuard + Oncotype DX + Cancerguard (MCED) — strategics are paying up for screening/precision-oncology franchises. Liquid biopsy + multi-cancer early detection is where the story (and the premium multiples) live.

3. **Animal health = the quality-compounder outlier; and AI is now a real in-clinic diagnostic vector.** IDEXX put up +11.2% organic with instrument revenue +28% organic, led by the **inVue Dx slide-free AI cellular analyzer** — a deep-learning imaging/cytology device trained by board-certified pathologists giving 10-min reference-lab-quality reads in-clinic. Direct read-across to Nick's imaging + DL edge. Contrast: **QGEN cut FY26 to +1–2% CER (from ≥5%)** — sample-tech/QuantiFERON softness (immigration testing collapse) shows the tools/consumables side is NOT sharing the clinical-volume boom.

## Per-company

### IDXX — IDEXX Laboratories (animal health / vet diagnostics)
- **Q1 2026 (reported):** Revenue $1,140.8M, +14.3% reported / **+11.2% organic**. EPS $3.47 (+15% comparable). Op margin +~100bps. CAG $1.05B (+11.6% org); CAG Dx recurring +11.0% org. Instruments +28% organic, 4,650 premium placements incl. 1,100 inVue Dx units.
- **Guidance (RAISED):** FY26 revenue $4.675–4.76B (+7.7–9.7% org, ~+70bps midpoint bump); EPS $14.45–14.90.
- **Notable / hot:** **inVue Dx** slide-free AI cytology/blood-morphology analyzer driving instrument placements + downstream consumables/recurring pull-through; AI diagnostic suite (ImageVue DR50 Plus imaging, Cancer Dx Panel). This is the story.
- **Valuation (UNVERIFIED):** fwd P/E ~37–38x — premium compounder multiple, richest of the peer set ex-EXAS.
- **Nick-edge fit:** HIGH — inVue Dx is literally AI-on-imaging/cytology hardware + DL models; cleanest imaging+DL+medtech read in the sub-sector (vet, but same tech thesis).

### LH — Labcorp Holdings (clinical lab + biopharma services)
- **Q1 2026 (reported):** Revenue $3.54B, +5.8%. Adj EPS $4.25 (+10.6%); GAAP EPS $3.35. Adj op margin 14.4%. Diagnostics +5.0%, Biopharma (CRO) +8.2%. OCF $191.5M, FCF $70.5M (vs negative yr-ago).
- **Guidance (RAISED):** FY26 revenue $14.65–14.80B (+5.0–6.1%, +20bps midpoint); Adj EPS $17.70–18.35; FCF $1.24–1.36B.
- **Notable / hot:** Steady volume + hospital/health-system outreach acquisition pipeline; margin expansion + FCF inflection. Lowest-multiple compounder.
- **Valuation (UNVERIFIED):** fwd P/E ~15–16x — cheapest in the set.
- **Nick-edge fit:** LOW-MED — scale lab automation; some AI in workflow but not imaging-centric.

### DGX — Quest Diagnostics (clinical lab)
- **Q1 2026 (reported):** Revenue $2.90B, **+9.2%**. DIS revenue +9.4%; requisition vol +10.9%, organic vol +10.8%. Adj EPS $2.50 (+13.1%); GAAP EPS $2.24 (+15.5%). Productivity from automation + AI.
- **Guidance (RAISED):** FY26 revenue $11.78–11.9B; Adj EPS $10.63–10.83; OCF ~$1.75B.
- **Notable / hot:** M&A-fueled volume — **Corewell Health JV (~$250M, consolidated), Fresenius ($80–100M)** = ~3.3% rev impact; double-digit growth in brain-health + cardiometabolic testing. Best organic volume print of the two big labs.
- **Valuation (UNVERIFIED):** fwd P/E ~18x.
- **Nick-edge fit:** LOW-MED — automation/AI for productivity, not imaging.

### QGEN — Qiagen (molecular sample-tech / tools & consumables)
- **Q1 2026 (reported):** Revenue $492.3M (MISS vs ~$496M est); adj EPS $0.54 (in line). (One source cites $0.33 EPS drop headline — flag conflicting figures, UNVERIFIED.)
- **Guidance (CUT):** FY26 lowered to **+1–2% CER** (from ≥5%); adj EPS ≥$2.43 (from ≥$2.50); FY revenue ~$2.1B — both below consensus.
- **Notable / hot:** **QuantiFERON (TB) −5% CER** as US + Middle East immigration-related testing demand collapsed — the negative surprise / thesis-breaker. Sample tech >50% of sales. Tools/consumables side decoupled from the clinical-volume boom.
- **Valuation (UNVERIFIED):** not pulled cleanly; low-growth tools multiple implied.
- **Nick-edge fit:** LOW — molecular consumables/instruments, no imaging angle.

### EXAS — Exact Sciences → **NOW PART OF ABBOTT (ABT)**
- **STATUS:** **Acquired by Abbott, deal CLOSED 2026-03-23, ~$21B ($20.6B), $105/share cash.** No longer an independent public equity. Portfolio: Cologuard / Cologuard Plus, Oncotype DX, **Cancerguard (MCED, launched 2H2025)**. ~$3B incremental sales to Abbott in 2026; ~$0.20 EPS dilution to ABT.
- **Q1 2026 "EXAS" figures circulating** (rev $1.62B +12%; screening $815M +15%; Precision Onc $647M) are **UNVERIFIED and likely pre-close / mis-attributed** given the March close — treat as directional only, do NOT rely on as standalone EXAS reporting.
- **Notable / hot:** The deal itself — validates screening + liquid-biopsy/MCED as strategic must-haves; a diagnostics-consolidation signpost. To play the thesis now you'd analyze **ABT**, not EXAS.
- **Nick-edge fit:** MED (via molecular screening, not imaging). Actionable only through ABT.

### NEO — NeoGenomics (oncology testing / NGS + liquid biopsy)
- **Q1 2026 (reported):** Total revenue $187M, **+11%**; clinical +14%; **NGS +26%**. Gross profit $81M (+10%). Net loss $17.1M (−$0.13), narrowed from −$25.9M (−$0.20) yr-ago — still loss-making.
- **Guidance (RAISED):** FY26 revenue $797–803M, lifted on strong Q1 + earlier-than-assumed CMS/MolDX coverage of PanTracer LBx (Mar 2026).
- **Notable / hot:** **RaDaR ST + PanTracer LBx MolDX approval** opening large liquid-biopsy TAM; NGS mix-shift is the growth story. Small-cap, pre-profit turnaround.
- **Valuation (UNVERIFIED):** not pulled; unprofitable, revenue-multiple name.
- **Nick-edge fit:** LOW-MED — genomics/liquid biopsy, no imaging; but MRD/liquid-biopsy is a hot adjacent lane.

## Candidates worth a full pipeline dossier (and why)

1. **IDXX (IDEXX)** — top pick for a full dossier. Best-in-class organic growth + margins + raised guide, AND the **inVue Dx AI slide-free cytology/imaging analyzer** is the strongest direct fit to Nick's imaging + deep-learning + medtech edge in this whole sub-sector. Key question a dossier must answer: is ~37x fwd P/E already pricing the AI-instrument cycle, and how big is the recurring pull-through from placements?

2. **DGX (Quest)** — best "GARP-y" scan lead. +9.2% rev with double-digit organic volume, M&A flywheel (Corewell/Fresenius), raised guide, ~18x — cheaper than IDXX with accelerating volume. Dossier angle: durability of the hospital-outreach consolidation engine + automation/AI margin runway. (LH is the cheaper-but-slower alternative at ~15x.)

3. **NEO (NeoGenomics)** — speculative/optional watch-lead. Liquid-biopsy/NGS TAM unlock (PanTracer MolDX, RaDaR ST) + turnaround from losses. Higher risk, no imaging fit — include only if the panel wants MRD/liquid-biopsy exposure; the Abbott–EXAS deal shows strategics will pay premiums here.

**Skip for now:** QGEN (guidance CUT, tools-side decoupled, thesis-breaker on QuantiFERON). **EXAS is off the board** — acquired by Abbott; route any screening/MCED interest through ABT.

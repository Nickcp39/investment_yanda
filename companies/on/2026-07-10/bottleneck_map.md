# Bottleneck Map — ON · as_of 2026-07-10

## The batch question: is onsemi a "demand-exploded + supply-choked + already-earning" robotics bottleneck like NVDA-in-compute?
**No.**

| Test | NVDA (compute) | onsemi (robotics) |
|---|---|---|
| Demand already exploded? | Yes — DC +92% Y/Y, $75B/qtr | **No** — robotics rev ≈ $0; auto/industrial only just inflected +5% off a trough |
| Supply choked / hard to fill? | Yes — CoWoS/CUDA/full-stack | **No** — power/sensor content is commodity, 4+ rivals, SiC oversupply built |
| Already earning the cash? | Yes | **No** — robotics = design-wins/demos; core GM only 38.5% |
| Falsifiable pricing power? | Yes (74.9% GM) | **No** (38.5% GM = commodity) |

## Where onsemi *does* have a mild bottleneck
- **In-house SiC substrate (IDM):** genuine cost/supply control — a MED barrier, but being competed and in oversupply.
- **Auto design-in qualification:** a switching-cost bottleneck *inside* a won platform, re-competed each cycle.
Neither is a robotics bottleneck; both are core-auto/industrial and cyclical.

## Real bottleneck vs concept (verdict)
- **Robotics/embodied-AI: CONCEPT.** onsemi supplies commodity content *into* robots; the bottleneck node in robots is compute/actuation/software, not onsemi's parts.
- **Core auto/industrial power+sensing: REAL but MED and CYCLICAL** — switching cost + SiC IDM, not monopoly pricing.
- **AI-datacenter POWER: REAL, growing, COMMODITY** — dated cash (~$500M 2026E) but contested by Infineon/Vicor/MPS/TI.

**One line:** the price is buying a cyclical power/sensing recovery + a real-but-commodity AI-DC-power ramp; the robotics "bottleneck" is a narrative option worth ~0 in cash today.

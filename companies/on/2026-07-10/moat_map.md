## Falsifiable moat test (PLAN §1d)
**Claim:** onsemi has a defensible pricing bottleneck in robotics/embodied AI.
**Falsification:** it does not. (1) Robotics revenue ≈ $0 — no pricing observable. (2) The components it *would* sell into robots (power, position/image sensors) are **commodity-competitive** with 4+ credible rivals and **38.5% gross margin** — the number itself falsifies "pricing power bottleneck." (3) No CUDA-like switching cost. **Verdict: commodity/concept in robotics; MED cyclical switching-cost moat in core auto power/sensing.** It is NOT an NVDA-style monopoly bottleneck.

## Durability grade
**MED, cyclical.** Secular content growth is durable; onsemi's *pricing capture* is not. Durability caps sizing **below Core** (cyclical rule, METHODOLOGY §2).

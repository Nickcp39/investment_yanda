## Where onsemi sits
- **IDM** (owns fabs + SiC substrate) → cost/supply-chain control in SiC, a real differentiator vs fabless peers.
- **Position in the robot value chain (the batch question):** onsemi would supply **power + position/image sensing content** *into* robots — the same commodity-competitive components it sells into cars. It is **not** a bottleneck node in embodied AI (that is compute/actuation/software). Robot content ≈ a downstream extension of existing auto/industrial parts.

## Profit-pool shift
- Value migrating toward **SiC traction + AI-DC power + higher-margin Treo analog + edge-AI (Synaptics)**. Real, but each pool is contested:
  - SiC: Wolfspeed, Infineon, STMicro, Chinese entrants; oversupply built in the up-cycle.
  - CIS (image sensors): **Sony intensifying competition** (S009) against onsemi's ~1/3 advanced-sensing share.
  - AI-DC power: Infineon, Vicor, MPS, TI all chasing the same XPU power tree.

## Bargaining power
- **Suppliers:** moderate (SiC substrate in-house helps).
- **Customers:** strong Tier-1/OEM concentration → pricing pressure; auto design-in lock partly offsets.
- **Substitutes/new entrants:** rising (Chinese SiC/power) → structural margin cap.

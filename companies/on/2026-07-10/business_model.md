# Business Model — ON (onsemi) · as_of 2026-07-10

## One-line business
onsemi sells **power semiconductors (incl. silicon carbide) and image/depth sensors** into cars and factories — a cyclical analog/power chipmaker levered to vehicle electrification, industrial automation, and (newly) AI-datacenter power, currently inflecting out of a multi-quarter auto/industrial downcycle.

**End-market mix:** automotive ~53% (**$797M**, +5% — first growth after 7 down quarters), industrial + other ~47%. Auto+industrial ≈ **80%** of revenue. **AI-datacenter POWER** is the fast-growing sliver: ~$250M (2025) → ~$500M (2026E) — real cash, but ~7–8% of revenue and it is *power for datacenters, not robots*.

## What today's price is actually buying (thesis split — PLAN §1e)
1. **Core cyclical (auto/industrial power + sensing)** — ~92% of revenue, the real value; inflecting off a trough.
2. **AI-datacenter POWER option** — real, dated, growing cash (~$500M 2026E), commodity-competitive power content.
3. **Robotics / Physical-AI option** — **~0% of revenue; design-wins + the Synaptics narrative only.** Pure anticipation.

## 10-year test
Will people buy more power + sensing silicon in cars/factories/datacenters in 10 years? **Yes** — secular content growth (EV, electrification, automation, DC power) is durable. But onsemi's *slice* is **commodity-competitive** (Infineon, STMicro, NXP, Wolfspeed, Sony in CIS, rising Chinese SiC) and **cyclical** — durable demand ≠ durable pricing power.

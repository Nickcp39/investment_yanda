## The one-paragraph thesis

MELI is Latin America's dominant commerce + fintech ecosystem — marketplace + Mercado Pago + Mercado Envíos + Mercado Crédito — a two-sided flywheel compounding volume at hyperscale (Q1'26 revenue +49%, GMV +42%, TPV +50%) into a hugely under-penetrated market (7 online purchases/yr per Latin American vs 41 per American). It has fallen ~31% from its high to **$1,763.36** on a Q1 2026 "margin miss": operating margin compressed to 6.9% and EPS fell YoY — driven ~two-thirds by IFRS-9 loan-loss provisioning front-loaded on an 87%-growing credit book, plus deliberate free-shipping/1P/cross-border investment to defend share against Shopee and Shein. The debate is whether that margin sacrifice is temporary, high-ROIC investment (bull) or a permanent competition/credit problem (bear). Governance is clean (one-share-one-vote), the founder-culture is intact (Galperin → Executive Chairman, insider CEO), and — critically for this book — MELI is essentially uncorrelated to the BTC/GOOGL/NBIS AI-factor.

## Why STARTER, not WATCH (the inverse of HWM)

Unlike HWM (near-ATH, negative base IRR, PRICE forbids entry), MELI has **de-rated into a range where the math works**. On NORMALIZED (not trough) earnings, the base 5-year IRR clears a 15%+ hurdle at today's price, and even the plain bear is roughly breakeven because 40%+ revenue growth compounds absolute earnings. Only a genuine LatAm credit-cycle **severe-bear** loses materially. The margin of safety is in the GROWTH and the de-rated multiple, not a low absolute P/E (~43x forward). What caps it at Starter is CONVICTION, not price: the bull case hinges on an **unproven** margin-and-credit inflection.

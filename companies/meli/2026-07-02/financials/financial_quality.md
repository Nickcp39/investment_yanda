# MELI Financial Quality (M4)

As-of: 2026-07-02 | As-of price: $1,763.36

> This is a marketplace with a bank bolted inside it. The single most important analytical move is to (a) NORMALIZE free cash flow (raw FCF is inflated by fintech float) and (b) read the margin compression through the lens of IFRS-9 credit provisioning on an 87%-growing loan book. Get those two right and the financials are legible; get them wrong and you either over- or under-state the business badly.

## 5-year trend (hyper-growth WITH margin compression)

| FY | Revenue | Gross margin | Op margin | Diluted EPS | Adj FCF (owner-earnings proxy) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2021 | $7,069M | 42.5% | 6.2% | $1.67 | ~$0.2-0.4B |
| 2022 | $10,780M | 48.2% | 9.9% | $9.53 | ~$0.6B |
| 2023 | $15,107M | 50.2% | **14.6%** | $19.46 | ~$1.0-1.2B |
| 2024 | $20,777M | 46.1% | 12.7% | $37.69 | ~$1.2-1.4B |
| 2025 | $28,893M | 44.5% | 11.1% | $39.40 | **$1.48B** |
| TTM (thru Q1'26) | $31,803M | 43.9% | **9.6%** | **$37.89** | ~$1.4B |

- Revenue ~**4.1x in 4 years (~42% CAGR)** — genuinely rare hyperscale, and STILL accelerating (Q1'26 +49%, fastest in 4 years).
- **BUT operating margin PEAKED at 14.6% (FY23) and has fallen to 11.1% (FY25) -> ~9.6% TTM.** TTM EPS ($37.89) is now BELOW FY25 EPS ($39.40): **trailing earnings are declining while revenue accelerates.** This is the entire bear case in one line — and the entire bull case is that it's deliberate and temporary.
- Gross margin compression (50.2% -> 43.9%) reflects mix shift into lower-margin 1P retail, free shipping, and the credit-card product.

## Q1 2026 (reported 2026-05-07)

- Revenue $8,845M (+49% YoY, +46% FX-neutral). Income from operations $611M, **6.9% margin** (vs 12.9% Q1'25, ~-600bps). Net income $417M (4.7% margin); **diluted EPS $8.23 (vs $9.74 Q1'25 — DOWN YoY).**
- GMV $19.0B (+42%); TPV $87.2B (+50%); 84M buyers (+26%); 83M fintech MAU (+29%); items sold 722M (+47%).

## The margin compression — decomposed (the crux)

Management + sources attribute the ~600bps op-margin decline to THREE things:

| Driver | Nature | Bull read | Bear read |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Credit-loss provisioning (~2/3)** — provision expense doubled to $1,244M (from $603M) as the book grew +87% | IFRS-9 books LIFETIME expected loss at ORIGINATION; a fast-growing book front-loads losses vs. interest earned over 8 months | Accounting artifact of growth; NPLs stable (15-90 day 8.0%), coverage 149% -> understates true profitability | If provisions reflect REAL deterioration (not just growth), margins don't recover and the book is a credit trap |
| **Deliberate investment** — Brazil free shipping (threshold to R$19), 1P retail, cross-border-from-China | Strategic opex/COGS | Defends #1 share vs Shopee/Shein; Brazil items sold +56% proves it works; CFO calls it a "5-6pp" self-imposed hit | Competition is FORCING the subsidy; it may be permanent, not optional |
| **Mix shift** — lower-spread credit card rising (NIMAL 17.8% from 22.7%), more 1P | Structural mix | Card scales the ecosystem; ads (+67%) will re-lift blended margin | Structurally lower-margin business than the 2023 peak |

**My read:** the bull framing is more likely right on the PROVISIONING (IFRS-9 front-loading on stable-NPL growth is real and well-understood) and PARTLY right on the investment (some is genuinely optional/high-ROIC, some is competition-forced and semi-permanent). The honest conclusion: **normalized operating margin is probably materially above the 6.9% Q1 trough but BELOW the 14.6% FY23 peak — call it low-to-mid teens through-cycle.** The valuation must be built on that normalized number, not the trough (too bearish) or the peak (too bullish).

## Fintech credit book — quality assessment

| Metric | Q1'26 | Read |
|---|---:|---|
| Gross credit portfolio | $14.6B (+87% YoY) | Fastest-growing, highest-risk engine |
| Loans receivable, net | $10,735M | Balance-sheet carrying value |
| Allowance for doubtful accounts | $3,865M (~26% of gross) | Heavy reserve — conservative, appropriate for LatAm consumer |
| Provision expense (Q1) | $1,244M (2x YoY) | The margin hit; grows with the book under IFRS-9 |
| **NPL 15-90 day (portfolio)** | **8.0%, broadly stable YoY** | Stable delinquency WHILE book +87% = underwriting holding (the key bull evidence) |
| Credit-card 15-90 day NPL | fell 80bps YoY | Improving in the largest-growth product |
| NIMAL | 17.8% (from 22.7%) | Still a FAT spread after losses; compression is mix + Brazil, not blowup |
| Portfolio mix | card 37% / consumer 38% / merchant 16% / ABL 2% | Diversified; unsecured consumer/card is the risk concentration |

- **The single most important credit fact:** NPLs are STABLE-to-IMPROVING while the book grows 87%. Under IFRS-9, provisions rise with volume regardless of quality, so doubling provisions on stable NPLs is consistent with growth, NOT deterioration. This is the strongest evidence for the bull "temporary" thesis.
- **The single most important credit RISK:** this book has NOT been tested through a full LatAm consumer-credit downturn at $14.6B scale. Provisions are a forward estimate; actual charge-offs through a recession are the real test. Argentina delinquency is already rising system-wide (MELI's held so far).

## Balance sheet & funding

- Cash & equivalents ~$15.1B; total assets ~$46.9B. The credit book ($14.6B) is funded partly by **$2.35B third-party fintech financing** (FY25) + customer deposits (AUM $19.9B) — leverage sits in the loan book / funding stack, not in corporate debt. Brazil Selic easing (14.25%, cutting) is a direct funding-cost tailwind.
- Very low share count (~50.7M), negligible dilution over 5 years — EPS growth is NOT dilution-flattered (the opposite of many hyper-growth names).

## Owner-earnings sanity (the normalization that matters)

- **Do NOT use raw FCF ($10.8B FY25, $11.8B TTM per aggregators)** — it includes fintech float (customer funds, loans-payable inflows) and vastly overstates owner earnings.
- **Use MELI's Adjusted FCF: $1.48B FY25** (after $1.33B capex), which strips changes in loans receivable/payable + customer funds. Q1'26 adjusted FCF was **-$56M** (the credit-book growth consumed cash).
- Reconcile to earnings: FY25 net income $1,997M vs adjusted FCF $1.48B — adjusted FCF runs BELOW net income because MELI is self-funding a fast-growing loan book with equity/retained cash. As book growth normalizes, adjusted FCF should converge up toward net income.
- At $89.4B mcap: **~45x FY25 net income, ~60x FY25 adjusted FCF** — expensive on trailing owner-earnings; the case rests on forward normalized earnings growing into the multiple (see valuation.md).

## Normalization / quality flags

| Item | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Raw FCF ($10.8B) | REJECT for valuation — fintech float. Use adjusted FCF ($1.48B FY25). |
| Op margin 6.9% (Q1 trough) | Do NOT extrapolate — IFRS-9 + investment front-loading. Normalize to low-to-mid teens. |
| TTM EPS $37.89 < FY25 $39.40 | Real — trailing earnings declining on margin compression; a genuine (if likely temporary) headwind. |
| Argentina IAS 29 / FX | Reported USD understates real growth (+23% USD vs +65% FX-neutral); a diminishing drag as inflation falls. |
| Provision expense | Grows with book under IFRS-9; watch CHARGE-OFFS (realized) vs provisions (estimated) for true credit health. |

## Verdict

Financial reality is **MIXED, and that is the honest signal (+1, not +2)**: the top line is an exceptional, accelerating ~42% compounder, the credit book shows stable-to-improving delinquency while growing 87% (genuinely impressive underwriting), and governance/dilution are clean. BUT reported margins and EPS are DECLINING, adjusted FCF is thin ($1.48B) and even negative in Q1, and the entire bull case depends on an UNPROVEN assumption that the margin compression is temporary and the credit book survives a cycle. The financials neither confirm the thesis unambiguously (that would be +2) nor break it (that would be negative) — they say "great growth, real risk, normalized earnings power is the whole ballgame."

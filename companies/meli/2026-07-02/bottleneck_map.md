# MELI Bottleneck Map (M3)

As-of: 2026-07-02

## The bottleneck question: who holds the scarce resource?

In LatAm digital commerce, the scarce resources are (1) **same-day/next-day fulfillment density** in markets with weak public logistics, and (2) **the proprietary transaction data needed to underwrite thin-file consumers/merchants at scale.** MELI is the only player that holds BOTH at continental scale. Unlike HWM's certified single-crystal airfoil (a hard physical/regulatory bottleneck), MELI's is a *systems + scale + data* bottleneck — more defensible than pure distribution, less absolute than a qualification-gated part, because a well-capitalized entrant CAN attack it (Shopee is trying).

## Bottleneck stack

| Layer | Scarce? | Who holds it | MELI position |
|---|---|---|---|
| Demand aggregation (buyers) | Moderate — contestable | MELI, Shopee, Amazon, Shein | #1 but subsidizing to hold share |
| **Fulfillment density (same-day FCs)** | **YES — the hardest to replicate** | MELI (~55% own-FC), few others | **Leading, deepening** |
| Payment rail | Less so (PIX is public) | MELI, Nubank, banks, PIX | Strong, rides PIX |
| **Ecosystem credit data** | **YES — proprietary** | MELI (from commerce+payments) | **Unique underwriting input** |
| Credit funding | No — commodity | Capital markets / deposits | Funded partly by 3rd parties (a dependency, not a moat) |

## Why this matters for the thesis

- The two genuine bottlenecks (fulfillment density + credit data) explain why MELI can grow GMV +42% and a credit book +87% while keeping NPLs stable — economics concentrate where MELI is scarce.
- BUT the bottleneck is being ATTACKED at the demand layer (Shopee/Shein) and DEPENDS on external funding at the credit layer — so it protects the business but does not, right now, protect the *margin* (which management is spending to defend the demand layer). This is the inverse of HWM, where the bottleneck protected the business but the risk was the *multiple*. Here the bottleneck is real but the risk is *whether the margin sacrifice earns its return before a competitor or a credit cycle erodes the moat*.

## Bottleneck risk

- **Fulfillment:** capital-intensive to extend; a mis-timed FC build during a downturn is a risk, but the asset is durable.
- **Credit data:** only as good as the underwriting discipline applied to it — the 87% book growth is the stress test. If standards loosened, the data moat is undermined by its own volume.
- **Demand:** the most contestable layer; Shopee's Brazil momentum is the clearest sign the bottleneck is not absolute.

## One metric that forces a market update

**Consolidated operating margin trajectory + credit NPL/charge-off trend over the next 2-3 quarters.** If operating margin inflects back up (toward the low-to-mid teens) WHILE NPLs stay stable and GMV/credit keep compounding, the "deliberate high-ROIC investment" thesis is validated and the drawdown was a gift. If margin stays depressed AND charge-offs (not just provisions) rise, the market will re-rate this as a contested-moat, credit-cycle-exposed business — the value-trap path.

## Value-capture logic

- **Upstream (sellers/suppliers):** MELI aggregates millions of LatAm SMB sellers + brands + a growing cross-border-from-China channel + its own 1P inventory. It captures a ~21% commerce take plus fast-growing high-margin **advertising** (+67% FX-neutral). Seller lock-in comes from fulfillment (FBA-style) + working-capital loans (merchant financing, 16% of the book).
- **MELI node (the ecosystem):** The value concentrates in owning the **demand aggregation + payment rail + logistics + credit-underwriting data** simultaneously. No LatAm competitor has all four at MELI's scale. This is the choke point — but note it is a *systems/scale/data* moat, not a physical bottleneck like HWM's certified airfoils; it can be attacked by well-capitalized entrants (Shopee is building the same stack).
- **Downstream (buyers/borrowers):** 84M buyers with rising frequency (items sold +47%); habit + loyalty + Mercado Pago balances create switching costs. The credit book turns buyers into borrowers, monetizing the relationship a second time at a fat (if compressing) spread.
- **The prize (credit + data):** Ecosystem data lets MELI underwrite thin-file LatAm consumers/merchants that banks can't, at a 17.8% margin after losses. This is the highest-return, highest-risk layer — it is simultaneously the growth engine and the source of the provisioning drag that caused the drawdown.

## Pricing-power evidence (and its current suspension)

- Commerce take 21.3% (-10bps) and fintech take stable — MELI is CHOOSING not to raise take, subsidizing free shipping (Brazil threshold to R$19) to defend share vs Shopee/Shein.
- NIMAL 17.8% (from 22.7%) — still a fat lending spread even after doubling provisions; compression is mix (more low-spread credit card) + Brazil spread give-back, not an inability to price.
- Advertising +67% is the clearest un-subsidized pricing-power signal and the most likely margin-recovery lever.

## Where the chain is weakest

- **Credit book (the funded, cyclical layer):** $14.6B, +87%, provisions doubled to $1.24B; funded partly by 3rd-party financing. A LatAm consumer-credit downturn hits here first (see inversion_map).
- **Commerce vs Shopee/Shein:** the marketplace layer is where the free-shipping subsidy is being spent; the moat is real (logistics) but the *margin* at this layer is under active competitive attack.
- **Argentina (19% of rev):** most profitable on direct contribution (35.7%) but FX/IAS 29 makes reported USD results the highest-variance line.

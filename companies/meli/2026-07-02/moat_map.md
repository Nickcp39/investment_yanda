# MELI Moat Map (M3)

As-of: 2026-07-02

## Moat sources (ranked)

| Source | Strength | Evidence | Durability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Logistics network (Mercado Envíos)** | **Strong** | ~95% of shipments touch MELI's managed network (from ~8% in 2018); ~55% fulfilled from own FCs; 199M fast shipments (+39%); 50+ FCs, 9 cargo aircraft, 10,000+ pickup points | High — capital-intensive, LatAm infra weak, years+billions to replicate |
| **Two-sided network effect (marketplace)** | Strong but contested | 84M buyers, millions of sellers, rising frequency (items sold +47%) | Real, but Shopee/Shein are credibly attacking with capital |
| **Ecosystem credit-underwriting data** | Strong | Proprietary repeat-transaction + seller cash-flow data underwrites thin-file borrowers; NIMAL 17.8% after losses; NPL 15-90 stable at 8.0% while book +87% | Structural IF underwriting holds through a cycle (unproven at this book size) |
| **Payments scale / Mercado Pago** | Moderate-strong | TPV $87.2B (+50%), 83M fintech MAU, off-marketplace acceptance | Rides PIX rails (tailwind); Nubank is a bigger banking franchise |
| **Brand / habit / loyalty** | Moderate | #1 e-commerce brand in most LatAm markets; loyalty program | Sticky but not absolute |
| **Advertising flywheel** | Emerging-strong | Ad revenue +67% FX-neutral; high-margin | The key future margin lever |

## Moat test by layer

- **Logistics — DEEP & DURABLE (the best moat).** Self-built LatAm fulfillment at same-day density is genuinely hard to replicate; this is MELI's most defensible asset and it is deepening (~55% FC penetration, rising). Closest in spirit to a real bottleneck.
- **Marketplace network effect — REAL but CONTESTED.** MELI is #1 and gaining items-sold share, but Shopee (Brazil Mall GMV 2x, now ~15% of Brazil GMV) and Shein/Temu are well-capitalized attackers. The proof the moat is under pressure: MELI must subsidize free shipping rather than raise take. Not an unassailable moat.
- **Credit/data — POWERFUL but UNPROVEN AT SCALE.** The data advantage is real and NPLs are stable-to-improving, but a $14.6B book growing 87% has not been tested through a full LatAm consumer-credit downturn. High return, real tail risk.
- **Fintech/payments — GOOD, differentiated by commerce embedding.** PIX is a tailwind not a threat; Mercado Pago's edge over Nubank is the marketplace-embedded wallet + BNPL. But Nubank out-scales it in core banking.

## What could erode it

- **Shopee replicating the full stack in Brazil** (commerce + SeaMoney lending +250% + logistics) — the single biggest structural threat.
- **Shein/Temu cross-border** compressing marketplace economics (partly mitigated by Brazil import-tax changes + MELI's own China channel).
- **Credit-underwriting failure** — if the 87% book growth was bought by loosening standards, a cycle would expose it (provisions already doubled). This is the risk that turns the drawdown into a value trap.
- **Nubank expanding from banking into commerce/marketplace adjacencies.**

## Verdict

Moat is **deep and durable in LOGISTICS, real-but-contested in the MARKETPLACE, and powerful-but-unproven-at-scale in CREDIT.** This is a genuine wide-moat ecosystem — but, unlike a certified-airfoil bottleneck (HWM), it is a *systems/scale/data* moat that well-funded entrants are actively attacking, which is precisely why MELI is spending margin to defend it. Justifies a quality_growth_compounder label and a premium multiple — the open question (valuation.md / inversion_map.md) is whether ~43x forward on temporarily depressed earnings adequately compensates for the contested-moat + credit-cycle risk.

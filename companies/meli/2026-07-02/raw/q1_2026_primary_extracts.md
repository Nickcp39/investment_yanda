## Credit book (fintech)
- **Total credit portfolio $14.6bn, up 87% YoY** — "the largest ever quarterly increase in nominal terms."
- 10-Q balance sheet: Loans receivable, net $10,735M (Mar'26) vs $9,365M (Dec'25); Allowance for doubtful accounts $3,865M.
- **Provision for doubtful accounts (Q1 expense) $1,244M, vs $603M Q1'25 — doubled (+106%).**
- Portfolio mix: credit card 37%, consumer loans 38%, merchant financing 16%, asset-backed lending 2%.
- Asset quality (PRIMARY, from the Letter): **portfolio 15–90 day NPL = 8.0%, "broadly stable YoY"**; credit-card 15–90 day NPL "fell by 80bps YoY" (improving). Provision coverage ~149% of 15+ day past due (secondary). [CORRECTION: an investing.com slide recap mislabeled buckets as 15–90=17.6% / 90+=8.0%; the primary release text states 15–90 = 8.0% portfolio-wide. Use 8.0% for 15–90 day. 90+ day not separately stated in the Letter.]
- **NIMAL 17.8% (from 22.7% YoY)** — "driven by the higher mix of the lower-spread credit card and some compression of spreads in Brazil due to higher provisions."
- Mgmt: "While the financial system in Argentina is seeing rising delinquency, our 15-90 day NPL fell sequentially in Q1'26 while spreads increased. This demonstrates the strength of our underwriting and our agility."
- AUM (fintech, incl. remunerated accounts) $19.9B, up 77% YoY.
- Brazil loan-duration extension: from ~5 months to ~8 months "to reach new segments," requiring higher upfront provisioning (near-term NIM/earnings drag).

## Management framing (verbatim excerpts)
- "When your business is behaving like this, we believe the right response is not to harvest – it is to invest. We have a once-in-a-generation opportunity to transform how hundreds of millions of Latin Americans shop, pay and access financial services."
- "The average American makes 41 online purchases per year whereas the average Latin American makes just 7. Our own buyers average 11."
- "We continued to invest over five years in Brazil because the short-term signals — consistent evolution of NIMAL to breakeven and profitability, market share gains in credit card TPV, and positive ecosystemic impact — were clear, and validated our business plan. This successful track record gives us the confidence to pursue a similarly ambitious and patient plan in Mexico and Argentina."
- Brazil GMV growth accelerated to 38% YoY FX-neutral; items sold +56% YoY (up from 45% Q4'25, 42% Q3'25, 26% Q2'25) — "volume growth has doubled from a high base in just nine months."

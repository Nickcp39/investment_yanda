## Competition — LOGISTICS (most durable moat)
- ~95% of shipments originate from / flow through MELI managed network (up from ~8% in 2018).
- ~55% of shipments FULFILLED from MELI fulfillment centers (deeper 1P/FBA layer); 50+ FCs.
- Same/next-day: 199M fast shipments Q1'26 (+39%); ~76% of fast shipments <48h region-wide; Mexico FC penetration ~80% Q4'25.
- Assets: 30+ FCs, 90+ logistics centers, 9 cargo aircraft, 3,600+ EVs, 10,000+ Meli Places pickup points.
- [ANALYSIS] Most durable moat. LatAm weak public logistics => self-built network extremely hard/capital-intensive to replicate; Shopee/Amazon need years+billions to match same-day density. ~55% FC penetration (vs 95% touches) is the durability number, and rising.

## Moat durability — take rates & flywheel
- Commerce 3P take rate 21.3%, DOWN 10bps YoY (slight give-back from free shipping).
- Fintech take rate stable YoY (credit growth offset lower acquiring).
- Advertising revenue +67% YoY FX-neutral (Q4'25) — high-margin offset to shipping subsidy.
- [ANALYSIS] Flywheel STRENGTHENING operationally (items sold +45-56% Brazil, logistics penetration up, MAU +29%, TPV +50%, ads +67%, AUM +77%) but WEAKENING on reported margin (6.9% from ~14%, EPS falling). Network protects share+volume, NOT currently margins — because mgmt CHOOSES to reinvest pricing power into share defense vs harvest. Durable moat in reinvestment/defense phase, not harvest. Margin recovery "when" not "if" per bulls, but no timeline given.

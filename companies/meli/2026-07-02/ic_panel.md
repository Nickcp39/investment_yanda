# MELI IC Panel — Five Souls

As-of: 2026-07-02 | As-of price: $1,763.36

> No fabricated quotes. Each "voice" is a LENS — the documented investing principles of each investor applied
> to MELI. Where a principle is paraphrased, it is labeled (lens); no primary quote is attributed.

## 1. Duan Yongping (段永平) — chief judge (lens)

- **本分 / circle of competence:** Is this a business I understand and would own for 10 years? *Mostly yes — a dominant LatAm commerce+fintech ecosystem with a real logistics moat and a huge underpenetrated market (7 online purchases/yr vs 41 in the US) is comprehensible. The credit book is the part that stretches the circle — it is a bank inside the marketplace, and banks are harder to underwrite from outside.*
- **管理层是否靠谱:** Founder-culture owner-operator, ~7% skin in the game, one-share-one-vote, orderly insider succession, 25-yr efficient track record. **靠谱.** The honest caveat: Galperin is a privileged, finance-trained builder, not the scrappy engineer archetype — but the conduct (survival, reinvestment discipline, clean governance) is what matters, and it is strong.
- **价格 / "好生意 + 好价格":** Unlike HWM, this one does NOT obviously fail on price — it has DE-RATED 31%, the base 5y IRR clears the hurdle, and the bear is ~breakeven. 段's discipline is to buy a good business when the price is fair and the future is understandable. Here the business is good and the price is fair; the UNDERSTANDABILITY of the margin/credit inflection is the open question. Verdict: **STARTER — 看得懂大部分,价格合理,先买一点,看利润率和坏账拐点确认再加.**

## 2. Warren Buffett (lens)

- "Wonderful company at a fair price." MELI is wonderful-ish (moat, 40%+ growth, owner-operator) and the price is now fair-to-attractive after the de-rate. BUT Buffett is wary of businesses with large, fast-growing UNSECURED CONSUMER CREDIT books he can't fully see through — "only when the tide goes out do you discover who's been swimming naked." The credit book is the reservation. Verdict: **admire the franchise; a measured starter, sized for the credit tail; not a full-conviction Core until the book proves through a cycle.**

## 3. Charlie Munger (lens)

- "A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price." MELI at a de-rated ~43x forward on temporarily depressed earnings is closer to "great business, fair price" than HWM's "great business, terrible price." Munger would like the moat + the reinvestment-over-harvest discipline (delayed gratification). He would **invert on the credit book** — the way to lose is a LatAm consumer-credit cycle exposing loose underwriting. Verdict: **a good business at a fair price — own some, but respect the one thing that can kill it (credit).**

## 4. Howard Marks (lens)

- **Where are we in the cycle?** The STOCK is post-drawdown (-31%), sentiment washed out on the margin miss — the good news is NOT in the price (the opposite of HWM). But the LatAm CREDIT cycle is a separate axis: Brazil rates easing (tailwind) but a large book untested through a downturn. "The most important thing is buying when others are fearful, IF the risk is understood." Marks: **the price-cycle is favorable; the credit-cycle is the risk to size for.** Verdict: **constructive — a starter at a fearful price, sized for the credit uncertainty.**

## 5. Seth Klarman (lens)

- **Margin of safety is the cardinal rule.** Here the MOS is in the GROWTH and the de-rated multiple, not in a low absolute P/E — a base 5y IRR of ~24% with a ~breakeven bear IS a margin of safety, provided the severe-bear (credit blowup) is a genuine tail and sized accordingly. Klarman would demand the credit book be stress-tested and the position sized so the severe-bear is survivable. Verdict: **acceptable as a SIZED starter; the discipline is position size, not abstention — the price already embeds real pessimism.**

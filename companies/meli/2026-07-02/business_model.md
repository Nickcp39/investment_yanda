# MELI Business Model (M2 / M3 input)

As-of: 2026-07-02 | Pipeline: lean-6module-v1.1

## One sentence

MercadoLibre is Latin America's dominant digital-commerce + fintech ecosystem — a marketplace (Mercado Libre), a payments/wallet/credit franchise (Mercado Pago + Mercado Crédito), and a self-built logistics network (Mercado Envíos) — whose four engines feed each other into a two-sided flywheel, and which is currently trading ~31% below its high because it is deliberately spending its pricing power on share and scale instead of near-term margin.

## Revenue engines (2 reporting segments; Q1 2026)

| Segment | Q1'26 rev | % of rev | What it is | Why it earns / the debate |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| **Commerce** | ~$4.9B (FY25 $16.3B) | ~55% | 3P marketplace (take rate ~21.3%), 1P retail, advertising (+67% YoY), classifieds, shipping | High-margin ads + marketplace take, but take rate flat/-10bps as free shipping subsidizes GMV to defend share vs Shopee/Shein |

## The flywheel (why the four engines compound)

1. **Marketplace** brings buyers + sellers + first-party demand data.
2. **Mercado Pago** monetizes checkout (acquiring), then extends the wallet off-marketplace (TPV $87.2B, +50%; fintech MAU 83M, +29%).
3. **Mercado Envíos** (logistics) makes the marketplace faster/cheaper (199M fast shipments, +39%; ~55% fulfilled from own FCs) — a hard-to-replicate LatAm moat.
4. **Mercado Crédito** uses proprietary ecosystem data (repeat transactions, seller cash-flow) to underwrite credit incumbents can't (credit portfolio $14.6B, +87%).
Each engine lowers the other's CAC and raises switching costs. The data from commerce+payments underwrites credit; credit + free shipping drive more commerce.

## Demand drivers

1. **Secular LatAm e-commerce + fintech penetration** — mgmt: average American makes 41 online purchases/yr, average Latin American just 7 (MELI's own buyers 11). Long runway.
2. **Brazil (54% of rev)** — GMV growth ACCELERATING (+38% FX-neutral; items sold +56% YoY, up from 26% three quarters ago) as free shipping + credit cards win share.
3. **Credit-card & consumer lending** — card TPV +90%, 2.7M cards issued in Q1; the fastest-growing, but lowest-spread and most provision-heavy, product.
4. **Advertising** — +67% YoY FX-neutral; the high-margin monetization lever that can offset shipping subsidy over time.
5. **Argentina recovery** — +65% FX-neutral (Milei disinflation) but peso still eats USD reported growth (+23%).

## 10-year test (would you own it for a decade?)

- **For the business: yes, with a caveat.** Genuine super-app moat, 40%+ revenue CAGR, huge underpenetrated TAM, founder-culture continuity, clean governance. The caveat: the moat is CONTESTED (Shopee/Shein/Nubank) and the economics now depend on a credit book that must be underwritten through a full LatAm credit cycle.
- **At this price: plausibly, if the margin thesis is right.** ~43x forward on TEMPORARILY depressed earnings is not obviously cheap, but unlike HWM/GEV the multiple has already de-rated and the growth is real — the question is normalized earnings power (see valuation.md), not "is it a bubble."

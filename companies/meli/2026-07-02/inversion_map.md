# MELI Inversion / Trap Test (M5)

As-of: 2026-07-02 | As-of price: $1,763.36

> Invert: how does an owner LOSE money buying MELI here? Separate permanent business impairment / credit-book
> solvency risk (would trigger a veto) from valuation/margin-timing risk (sizing, not veto).

## Veto check (hard veto = thesis durability break OR the credit book / balance sheet cannot survive)

- **Durability:** the ecosystem moat (logistics deepest, marketplace contested, credit powerful-but-unproven) is intact and the flywheel is compounding VOLUME (+42% GMV, +50% TPV). The moat is being ATTACKED (Shopee/Shein) but is not broken. **No durability veto.**
- **Credit-book solvency:** the $14.6B book carries a $3.9B allowance (~26% of gross) and 149% coverage of 15+ day past-due; NPLs are stable (15-90 day 8.0%) while the book grows 87%; funded partly by 3rd-party financing + $19.9B AUM/deposits; parent holds ~$15.1B cash. A stress scenario hurts earnings badly but does NOT plausibly bankrupt the company. **No solvency veto** — but this is the closest thing to a veto risk and must be sized for.
- => **Hard veto does NOT fire.** This is a margin-timing + competition + credit-cycle risk, not a structural-death or solvency trap.

## Why F2 (credit) is the one that matters most

The bull case REQUIRES that the doubled provisions ($1,244M) are IFRS-9 front-loading on a healthy, fast-growing book — and the evidence supports it (NPLs stable at 8.0% while book +87%, credit-card NPL improving, 149% coverage). **But provisions are a forward ESTIMATE; realized charge-offs through a full downturn are the untested reality.** This book has never been $14.6B through a LatAm recession. If F2 fires, margin compression becomes permanent AND the severe-bear (-12.5%/yr) is the outcome, not the base. This is the single risk that converts the favorable asymmetry into a loss, and it is why the position must be sized for the tail rather than the base.

## Quantified downside (5y, from valuation.md)

- Severe bear (-12.5%/yr): credit blowup + growth collapse + deep de-rate.
- Bear (+0.7%/yr): margins stuck at trough, growth halves — still ~breakeven (growth compounds).
- Base (+24%/yr) / Bull (+38%/yr): margin inflects, growth durable.
- **The asymmetry is FAVORABLE at $1,763**: material upside (base/bull), limited downside except in the severe-bear tail. The inverse of HWM (where base was already negative).

## What would prove the thesis WRONG (and flip from STARTER)

- **Bearish confirmation (-> TRIM/EXIT):** operating margin stays at/below ~7% for 2-3 more quarters WHILE realized charge-offs rise and NPLs break above ~10% (15-90 day) — the credit-deterioration + permanent-compression path.
- **Competitive confirmation (-> re-underwrite):** Shopee overtakes MELI on Brazil GMV growth for consecutive quarters, or MELI's commerce take rate falls materially below ~20%.
- **Bullish confirmation (-> ADD):** operating margin inflects back toward low-to-mid teens WHILE GMV/credit keep compounding and NPLs stay stable -> the "deliberate high-ROIC investment" thesis validated.

## Verdict

No permanent-impairment or solvency veto; **signal -1 on M5** — a real but sized credit-cycle + competition + margin-timing risk, NOT a business kill and NOT a valuation-forbids-entry trap. The business is a keeper being tested; the price has already de-rated to where the base case works. Size for the severe-bear credit tail (cap at Confirmed ~8-12%, start as a Starter), and let the next 2-3 quarters resolve the margin/credit inflection.

## Why WATCH, Not STARTER

IDXX's own base-case scenario model — which assumes management's already-strong guidance is essentially
achieved and the growth algorithm merely moderates from its current mid-teens acceleration toward a still-
healthy ~9-10% CAGR — produces only **+2.8% IRR at 3 years and +5.6% at 5 years**, both below the 8%
hurdle. Only the bull case (AI-diagnostics flywheel proves durable, growth sustains near current pace,
multiple holds) clears the hurdle (+16.5%/+15.2%). At ~30x EV/EBITDA and ~41x trailing P/E — a 1.5-3x
premium to the closest animal-health peer, Zoetis — there is very little room for anything less than
continued near-best-case execution to still work out for a new buyer today.

## Why WATCH, Not AVOID

Every business-quality module supports ownership: FY2025 revenue growth accelerated to +10% (Q1 2026
+14.3%), operating margin expanded to 31.6% (Q1 2026 31.8%), FY2026 guidance was **raised twice** in six
months (the opposite signal from GEHC's guidance cut), FCF conversion ran ~99% of net income, the balance
sheet is clean (net debt <1x FCF, only ~16% goodwill/intangibles of total assets), and the CEO succession
(15-year internal veteran, previously ran the exact AI-diagnostics business line, promoted with a 16-month
lead time) reads as a deliberate, continuity-focused positive signal. None of the identified structural
risks (Zoetis AI competition, Mars/Antech-Heska's captive hospital network) currently show up in IDXX's own
numbers.

## Career Note: Razor/Blade + AI Diagnostics (Nick's imaging/DL lens)

IDXX runs one of the cleanest razor/blade models reviewed in this project — ~79% of total revenue is
recurring consumables/reference-lab annuity on an installed analyzer base. The AI layer (inVue Dx: "AI
models trained by IDEXX board-certified pathologists" performing slide-free cytology in ~10 minutes) is
embedded directly IN the point-of-care hardware, not a separate enterprise-software upsell — a genuinely
different AI-integration architecture than GEHC's Intelerad/MIM approach. Unlike GEHC's AI moat (zero
monetization evidence found anywhere), IDXX shows a real, quantified adoption flywheel: >1,900 inVue Dx
units placed in Q4 2025 alone, record pace, and instrument revenue +51.8% FY2025. The new CEO (Michael
Erickson) ran this exact business line before promotion — a strong signal of strategic priority.

**The unresolved, career-relevant question**: Zoetis's Vetscan OptiCell (AI-powered hematology, launched
2024-09) proves AI-in-point-of-care is now contested, not IDXX-exclusive — and no third-party benchmark of
inVue Dx vs OptiCell accuracy/economics exists in any source found this run. This is the single most
valuable open research item for next refresh, and sits squarely in Nick's domain expertise to evaluate if
primary technical materials (FDA submissions, peer-reviewed validation studies, or investor-day technical
deep-dives) become available.

## Bottom Line

IDXX is arguably the best BUSINESS reviewed in this medical batch so far — an accelerating, high-ROIC,
low-leverage, well-managed razor/blade compounder with a genuinely evidenced AI-diagnostics growth engine.
It is not, at $557.80, the best current INVESTMENT in the batch, because the price already assumes that

a genuine, independent resolution of the AI-durability question to justify paying today's price for the
bull case.

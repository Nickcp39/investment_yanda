## Margin Trend — EXPANDING, NOT COMPRESSING (contrast with GEHC exemplar)

| Period | Revenue | Growth | Gross Margin | Op Margin | EPS (dil.) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FY2022 | $3,367M | +4.73% | 59.52% | 26.69% | $8.03 |
| FY2023 | $3,661M | +8.72% | 59.82% | 29.97% | $10.06 |

**Verdict**: Both revenue growth AND margin are ACCELERATING across the most recent two periods

periods of margin compression). The one blemish (FY2024's operating-margin dip to 28.95% from FY2023's
29.97%) resolved positively and sharply the following year — consistent with either a deliberate
investment year (inVue Dx R&D/launch costs) that then paid off, or normal short-term margin noise, not a
structural deterioration signal.

## Earnings Quality — Litigation One-Off Is Cleanly Disclosed, Comparable Growth Still Very Strong

- FY2025 diluted EPS +23% reported vs +14% comparable — the 9pt gap is explained by a single, explicitly
  quantified, cleanly-disclosed litigation-accrual item (E31-E32): a ~$80M judgment paid 2026-04-17,
  concluding a multi-year breach-of-contract matter, with EPS impacts explicitly stated for both the 2024
  accrual (−$0.56) and the Q1 2025 partial release (+$0.08).
- **Critically, the COMPARABLE (ex-item) growth rates are still very strong**: +14% comparable EPS growth
  FY2025, +15% comparable EPS growth Q1 2026. This is NOT a case where a one-off is doing most of the work
  to make a mediocre underlying trend look good (contrast with a hypothetical where comparable growth would
  be low-single-digit) — the underlying business is genuinely accelerating even after stripping the one-off.
- No other one-off items (impairments, restructuring charges, discontinued operations) identified in sources
  reviewed this run.

## Free Cash Flow Quality — Exceptionally High Conversion

- FY2025 FCF $1,057.1M on net income ~$1,059M — **~99% FCF/NI conversion** [E29]. This is an
  unusually high conversion ratio (many high-quality compounders run 70-90%); it indicates minimal
  working-capital drag and modest capex intensity relative to earnings (capex only $124.7M vs $1,057.1M
  FCF, i.e., capex is only ~10.5% of operating cash flow).
- Q1 2026 FCF $234.3M vs net income $278.4M (~84% conversion) — Q1 is seasonally likely to run below the
  full-year rate (consistent with typical working-capital seasonality in many businesses; not confirmed
  IDXX-specific this run), and FY2026 guidance explicitly targets 85%-95% FCF/NI conversion for the full
  year [E33] — i.e., management's own guide is consistent with, not contradicted by, the Q1 print.
- **This is a materially stronger FCF quality profile than the GEHC exemplar** (GEHC FY2025 FCF/NI ~70%,
  with a Q1 2026 FCF collapse to $112M flagged as an open question in that dossier). IDXX shows no comparable
  red flag.

## Balance Sheet — Clean, Low Leverage, Not M&A-Funded Growth

- Total debt $949M (FY2025), down slightly from $965M (FY2024) [E43] — debt is NOT growing to fund
  acquisitions (contrast directly with GEHC, whose debt rose from $8.95B to $10.17B in barely more than a
  year, driven by the debt-funded Intelerad acquisition)
- Cash declined from $288.3M to $180.1M [E44] — but this decline is explained by the buyback program
  ($1,227.6M repurchased in FY2025 [E30]), a capital-RETURN use of cash, not a distress signal or an
  M&A cash outlay
- Net debt ~$769M against >$1B annual FCF — under 1x FCF, an extremely conservative leverage position
  [E47]
- Debt-to-equity 0.60, debt-to-EBITDA 0.69x [E50] — low leverage by any conventional standard
- Goodwill + intangibles ~$524M ($414M + $109.84M) is only ~16% of total assets ($3,351M) [E45-E46] — a
  MUCH cleaner balance sheet than GEHC's ~40%-goodwill profile, indicating growth has been overwhelmingly
  organic rather than built through large acquisitions requiring goodwill capitalization

## Returns on Capital — Among the Highest-Quality Profiles Reviewed in This Project to Date

- ROE: 69.3% (current) / 66.2% (FY2025 basis) [E48]
- ROIC: 46.8% [E49]
- ROA: 33.4% [E49]
- ROCE: 65.6% [E49]

**Context**: These returns are extraordinarily high in absolute terms. Part of the ROE figure is
mechanically inflated by a THIN equity base (only $1,605M equity against $1,059M net income — a byproduct of
years of aggressive buybacks reducing the share count/equity base, per E30 and E48's own note that ROE
declined slightly from 66.2% FY2025 to 69.3% "current," implying continued buyback-driven equity
reduction). This does not mean the returns are illusory — ROIC (46.8%, which nets out the buyback-driven
equity distortion better than ROE) is still exceptionally high and consistent with a genuinely
capital-light, high-margin, high-recurring-revenue business model — but the ROE figure specifically should
be read with the buyback-driven-equity-shrinkage caveat in mind, not as a pure signal of increasing
capital efficiency.

## Guidance Credibility — Raised Twice in Six Months (Contrast with GEHC's Cut)

FY2026 guidance was given 2026-01-29 (revenue $4,632M-$4,720M, EPS $14.29-$14.80) and then RAISED after
Q1 2026 results (~2026-05-04) to revenue $4,675M-$4,760M, EPS $14.45-$14.90 [E33-E34]. This is the single
clearest guidance-credibility contrast with the GEHC exemplar in this batch (GEHC cut guidance after one
quarter). A raised guide after one quarter is a genuinely positive signal, though it is only one data point
(two quarters of actual FY2026 results are needed to confirm the full-year trend holds).

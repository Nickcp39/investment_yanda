# IDXX Business Model (M2 companion)

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E8-E19, E56-E62

---

## One-Sentence Description

IDEXX sells diagnostic instruments to veterinary practices and reference laboratories at low/breakeven
margin, then earns a multi-decade recurring annuity on every consumable, test, and software subscription
those instruments consume — a classic razor/blade model, now being extended into AI-enabled point-of-care
diagnostics (inVue Dx) that raise the ASP and stickiness of each new placement.

---

## Segment 1: Companion Animal Group (CAG) — ~92% of FY2025 revenue ($3,953.3M of $4,303.7M) [E16]

The dominant, career-relevant segment. Three sub-lines:

### 1a. CAG Diagnostics Recurring Revenue — $3,407.2M FY2025 (~79% of TOTAL company revenue) [E17]
- **VetLab consumables** ($1,496.8M, +14.8%/+13.7% organic) — slides, cartridges, reagents consumed by
  IDEXX's own installed analyzer base (Catalyst, ProCyte, SediVue, inVue Dx). This is the purest razor/blade
  line: an installed analyzer is worthless without IDEXX-branded consumables, and consumable revenue scales
  with test volume, not just instrument count.
- **Reference Laboratory Services** ($1,424.1M, +6.6%/+5.9% organic) — IDEXX's own network of reference labs
  runs more complex assays that in-clinic analyzers cannot. This monetizes veterinary diagnostic demand even
  when the practice does NOT own an IDEXX in-clinic instrument — a second, complementary annuity.
- **Rapid Assay products** ($349.0M, −3.0%/−3.3% organic — the one shrinking line) — SNAP-test-style
  point-of-care immunoassays; the lowest-tech, most commoditized product in the CAG Diagnostics recurring
  mix, and the most exposed to Zoetis/generic competition (facts.md O8).

### 1b. CAG Diagnostics Capital / Instruments — $200.2M FY2025 (+51.8%/+49.3% organic) [E18]
The "razor" itself. Includes the AI-enabled **inVue Dx Cellular Analyzer** (>1,900 units placed in Q4 2025
alone), plus legacy hematology/chemistry/urinalysis analyzers. Instrument revenue growing 5x faster than
total company revenue is the leading indicator of future consumable-annuity growth — this quarter's
instrument placement is next year's (and next decade's) recurring VetLab-consumables customer.

### 1c. Veterinary Software / Diagnostic Imaging / Services — $345.9M FY2025 (+10.6%/+10.2% organic) [E19]
Practice-management software (Cornerstone, ezyVet, IDEXX Neo) and the VetConnect PLUS connectivity
platform, which pipes diagnostic results directly into a practice's workflow/EMR. This is the software
layer that raises switching costs on TOP OF the hardware/consumables lock-in — a practice that has
digitized its workflow around IDEXX software has an additional reason not to switch analyzer vendors.

## Segment 2: Water — $201.1M FY2025 (4.7% of revenue, +8.7%/+8.0% organic) [E16]
Water-quality/safety testing products (Colilert, Quanti-Tray). A smaller, unrelated-diversification
business — steady growth, not career-relevant, minimal further research this run.

## Segment 3: Livestock, Poultry and Dairy (LPD) — $131.8M FY2025 (3.1% of revenue, +8.0%/+6.1% organic) [E16]
Diagnostic products/services for production-animal health (as distinct from companion-animal/pet health).
Smallest segment; modest steady grower; not career-relevant, minimal further research this run.

## The Flywheel (mechanism)

```
New instrument placement (incl. AI-enabled inVue Dx)
        |
        v
Practice adopts IDEXX consumables + software workflow (VetConnect PLUS)
        |
        v
Switching cost rises (retraining, workflow re-integration, data migration)
        |
        v
Recurring VetLab consumable + reference-lab pull grows with test volume
  (independent of vet-visit VOLUME, dependent on tests-PER-visit and installed-base growth)
        |
        v
High-margin recurring revenue funds further R&D (new test menus, new AI models)
        |
        v
New test menus increase utilization on EXISTING installed base -> new instrument cycle
```

This is structurally similar to GEV's turbine-services annuity and GEHC's installed-base-plus-PDx model,
but with two distinguishing features relevant to Nick's career lens:
1. **The "razor" (analyzer) itself is now AI-enabled** (inVue Dx uses AI models trained by IDEXX
   pathologists to interpret cytology images) — the AI is embedded directly in the point-of-care hardware,
   not a separate software upsell layer bolted onto legacy equipment (contrast with GEHC's Intelerad/MIM,
   which are enterprise-imaging software acquisitions layered onto existing imaging hardware).
2. **The recurring revenue base (~79% of total company revenue) is unusually large relative to total
   revenue** — even GEHC's PDx consumables (its strongest annuity) is a much smaller share of GEHC's total
   mix. IDXX's business is overwhelmingly annuity, not equipment-sale, revenue.

## Growth Algorithm Under Macro Pressure (career/inversion-relevant)

US veterinary clinical visits have declined for 16 consecutive quarters [facts.md E63], yet IDXX guided
(and then RAISED) FY2026 revenue/EPS guidance above prior Street consensus [E33-E34]. The growth algorithm
management is explicitly running is:

```
flat-to-declining visit VOLUME
        +
rising diagnostic INTENSITY per visit (aging pet population, expanding test menus)
        +
premium-instrument attach/upgrade cycle (inVue Dx flywheel, +51.8% instrument revenue)
        +
price/mix
        =
double-digit total revenue growth despite a shrinking visit base
```

This is a fundamentally different growth mechanism than a pure volume story, and it is currently working
(both FY2025 and Q1 2026 show accelerating, not decelerating, growth) — but it depends on two levers
(diagnostic intensity + instrument attach) continuing to work simultaneously. If either lever stalls while
visit volume keeps declining, there is no third lever visible in the evidence gathered this run. This is the
single most important mechanism to monitor going forward (see inversion_map.md).

## Outgoing CEO: Jonathan (Jay) Mazelsky — Track Record 2019-2026

### Background
Joined IDEXX in August 2012 as EVP; appointed President & CEO on 2019-10-23. Prior career: Philips
Healthcare (GM, Computed Tomography/Nuclear Medicine/Radiation Therapy Planning, 2001-2012), Hewlett-Packard
and Agilent Technologies (1988-2002, including leading the integration of Agilent's Healthcare Group into
Philips). MBA University of Chicago Booth, BA Mathematics University of Rochester [E14]. This is a genuine
medical-device-industry career operator, not a first-time or outside-industry CEO — directly comparable in
background depth to GEHC's Arduini (prior Integra LifeSciences CEO) and arguably deeper on the
imaging/diagnostics-specific technical side given the Philips CT/Nuclear Medicine background.

### What Went Right (2019-2026, ~7-year tenure)
- **"IDEXX nearly doubled both its revenue and stock price during his time as CEO"** [THEORG-MAZELSKY, B2
  secondary characterization, directionally consistent with the filed financial trend: revenue $3,367M
  FY2022 -> $4,304M FY2025, a period covering only part of his tenure but showing sustained double-digit
  compounding]
- Successfully navigated the company through a genuinely difficult multi-year macro (16 consecutive
  quarters of declining US vet visits) [E63] WITHOUT the growth algorithm breaking — FY2025 revenue growth
  actually ACCELERATED to +10% (from +6.46% FY2024), and Q1 2026 accelerated further to +14.3% [E15, E35]
- Guided FY2026 conservatively in January 2026, then RAISED guidance after Q1 2026 beat — the opposite
  credibility signal from GEHC's Arduini, who cut guidance after one quarter [E33-E34; contrast with GEHC
  exemplar E36]
- Oversaw the inVue Dx launch and its record adoption pace (>1,900 units/quarter) [E18, E60] — a genuine,
  successful new-product execution track record, not just steady-state management
- Clean, orderly, long-lead-time succession planning: announced 16 months before the effective transition
  date, with a 12-month post-transition advisory runway as Executive Chair before intended retirement
  [E12-E13] — this is a textbook example of GOOD governance practice (contrast with abrupt or
  crisis-driven CEO exits)

### What Is Less Clear / Not Verified This Run

- The FY2024 operating-margin dip (28.95%, down from FY2023's 29.97%) before the sharp FY2025 recovery to

## Incoming CEO: Michael (Mike) Erickson, PhD — Effective 2026-05-12

Services (2014-2018), GM of Corporate & Strategic Accounts (Oct 2018-Dec 2022), GM of Global Point of Care
Diagnostics (Jan-Dec 2023), then EVP & GM of **Global Point of Care Diagnostics AND Telemedicine**
immediately pre-promotion [E13]. Pre-IDEXX: Associate Principal at McKinsey & Company advising
pharmaceutical/biotech/health-services clients — a strategy-consulting background rather than a

concern.

### Why This Succession Choice Is Meaningfully Career-Relevant
Erickson's immediate pre-promotion role ran the EXACT business line housing inVue Dx and the broader

outgoing CEO see the company's strategic center of gravity: not manufacturing scale, not reference-lab
logistics (though he did run software/accounts earlier in his career, giving him breadth), but specifically
the AI-enabled point-of-care diagnostics + telemedicine growth vector. This is a materially different
signal than GEHC's situation (Arduini himself, not a successor, is driving the AI/software bet via M&A;
no comparable "AI-line-of-business GM promoted to CEO" signal exists in the GEHC dossier).

## Capital Allocation

- **Buybacks, not dividends**: IDEXX pays no ordinary dividend [E7] and instead returns cash via share
  repurchases — $1,227.6M in FY2025 for 2.427M shares at an average price of $505.74/share [E30], a price
  ~9.3% BELOW the current $557.80. This is a genuinely disciplined capital-return program executed at a
  reasonable (not obviously overpaying) price point, funded from a business generating ~99% FCF/NI
  conversion [E29] — a materially stronger cash-generation and capital-discipline profile than GEHC (whose
  FCF/NI conversion was ~70% and whose major capital deployment was a large debt-funded M&A deal, Intelerad,
  rather than opportunistic buybacks).
- **Balance sheet discipline**: net debt only ~$769M against >$1B annual FCF [E47], debt/EBITDA just 0.69x
  [E50] — IDEXX has NOT grown via large debt-funded acquisitions the way GEHC has (Intelerad $2.3B cash) or
  many roll-up med-tech peers have. Growth is overwhelmingly organic (instrument placements, consumable
  volume, price/mix) plus presumably smaller bolt-on tuck-ins not separately itemized this run.
- **Litigation handling**: the ~$80M litigation judgment [E31] was paid and closed cleanly and transparently,
  with the EPS impact explicitly quantified and disclosed both when the expense was accrued (2024, −$0.56
  EPS) and when partially released (Q1 2025, +$0.08 EPS) [E32] — no evidence of obscured or ambiguous
  accounting treatment.

## Grade: 4/5

**Rationale**: Mazelsky's actual track record (2019-2026) is strong and directly evidenced: accelerating
(not decelerating) growth through a genuinely adverse multi-year macro, a successful major new-product
launch (inVue Dx) with record adoption, RAISED (not cut) guidance, disciplined buyback-funded capital return
at reasonable prices, and — critically — an orderly, well-telegraphed, internally-sourced succession that
explicitly signals continuity in the exact strategic area (AI-enabled point-of-care diagnostics) that
matters most for this dossier's thesis. This is a genuinely stronger evidenced operator grade than GEHC's
Arduini (3/5 in the GEHC exemplar, docked for a guidance cut after one quarter and two unexplained weak
segments). Docked from 5/5 only because: (a) the incoming CEO (Erickson) has literally zero track record yet
as CEO — the grade necessarily blends a strongly-evidenced outgoing operator with an unproven (though
well-positioned and thoughtfully-selected) incoming one, and (b) full compensation/incentive-alignment
detail was not verified this run (a mechanical, not judgment-based, gap).

**What would move this to 5/5**: Erickson's first 2-3 quarters as CEO showing continuity of the growth
algorithm (guidance held or raised again, inVue Dx/AI-diagnostics momentum sustained), plus confirmed
CEO compensation structure showing majority equity-linked incentive alignment.

**What would move this DOWN**: Any guidance cut or strategic reversal within Erickson's first year that
suggests the transition disrupted operating discipline, or evidence that the AI-diagnostics strategic
emphasis (signaled by his promotion) fails to translate into sustained inVue Dx-driven growth.

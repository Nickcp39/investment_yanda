## Founder & Core Team Life-Arc — John C. Plant

### Formation (unchooseable -> value formation; highest weight)

- **Family & home:** British; details of upbringing not well-documented publicly (discount the narrative; weight the documented facts).
- **Place & era:** Postwar/late-20th-century Britain; came up through UK industrial manufacturing.
- **Schooling / university:** **University of Birmingham** — Bachelor of Commerce in **economics, accounting & law** (later Honorary Doctorate). **Fellow of the Institute of Chartered Accountants.** -> Formation is *accounting/finance-first, numbers-disciplined* — the tell of a capital-allocator CEO, not an engineer-founder.
- **First career:** UK auto/industrial supply (Lucas Varity Automotive, managing director Electrical & Electronics division, 1991-97) -> learned global component manufacturing economics from the inside.
- **-> Likely formed:** cost/margin discipline, balance-sheet conservatism, an allocator's instinct for buybacks/portfolio surgery over empire-building. Consistent with his actual HWM conduct (margin expansion + capital return + A- rating).

### Adult track (compressed)

- **TRW:** EVP from the 1999 Lucas Varity acquisition; co-member of TRW Inc.'s Chief Executive Office 2001-03; President & CEO of TRW Automotive 2003-11, then CEO/Chairman 2011-15 — **sold TRW to ZF for ~$13.5B in 2015** (a clean, well-timed exit; ~65,000 employees, top-10 global supplier).
- **Arconic/Howmet:** joined board 2016, Chair 2017, CEO 2019-20, **engineered the 2020 separation** of Arconic into Howmet (aero) + Arconic (rolled products), Co-CEO 2020-21, CEO 2021-present.
- **Crucible behavior:** ran complex global suppliers through downturns; the HWM turnaround (op margin 15%->25%, debt to A-, $2.45B buyback) is the adult conduct **matching** the formation.

### Dark-arc (required)

- **Empire-vs-discipline tension:** the CAM ($1.8B) + Brunner ($120M) deals after years of debt paydown, plus **buying back $300M of stock at ~$230 (>50x earnings) in Q1 2026**, are the bear case — a great allocator can still overpay near a cycle/valuation peak. If buybacks continue at 60x+ P/E, that is capital destruction even by a disciplined hand.
- **Comp / age:** Plant has been very highly paid across his career; at ~71 the **succession gap** is the sharpest risk — the thesis leans on one allocator with no clearly groomed successor in public view.

### Reliability axis

Documented facts (Birmingham degree, ICA fellowship, TRW/ZF sale, Arconic split) > the polished origin narrative. The track record is verifiable and strong.

## Veto check (hard veto = thesis durability break OR balance sheet cannot survive)

- Durability: moat deep (Engine Products + spares), recurring aftermarket floor -> intact. **No veto.**
- Balance sheet: net debt ~0.7x, A- rated, $2.4B cash, strong FCF -> survives any plausible cycle. **No veto.**
- => **Hard veto does NOT fire.** This is not a structural-decline or balance-sheet trap.

## Why F3 dominates

At 65x trailing / 57x forward earnings, **most of the expected return depends on the multiple staying elevated**, not on the (excellent) business. History of aero-component compounders shows multiples this high mean-revert; a de-rate to a *still-premium* 30x with flat earnings is ~-45%. This is the GEV-shaped risk: an exceptional business where the price has borrowed years of future returns.

## Quantified downside (5y, from valuation.md)

- Bear (-13.8%/yr): aero stall + de-rate to 22x.
- Base (-1.6%/yr): solid execution but de-rate to 32x — **still negative**.
- The asymmetry is unfavorable at $280.36: limited upside (bull +4.2%/yr) vs material downside.

## What would prove the thesis WRONG (and flip from WATCH)

- **Bullish flip (-> STARTER):** price falls toward ~$174-200 on a cycle scare while spares + build rates hold -> margin of safety appears.
- **Bearish confirmation (-> AVOID):** commercial-aero build-rate cuts + spares deceleration + margin reversal — the cyclical-peak trap materializing.

## Verdict

No permanent-impairment veto; **signal -1 on M5 (valuation/cycle trap), -2 on M6 (price forbids entry)**. The business is a keeper; the price is the problem.

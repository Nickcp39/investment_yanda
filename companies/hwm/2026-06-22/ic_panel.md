# HWM IC Panel — Five Souls

As-of: 2026-06-22 | As-of price: $280.36

> No fabricated quotes. Each "voice" is a LENS — the documented investing principles of each investor applied
> to HWM. Where a principle is paraphrased, it is labeled (lens); no primary quote is attributed.

## 1. Duan Yongping (段永平) — chief judge (lens)

- **本分 / circle of competence:** Is this a business I understand and would own for 10 years? *Yes — a bottleneck components compounder with a recurring spares annuity and an A- balance sheet is comprehensible and durable.*
- **管理层是否靠谱:** Plant is a verifiable, elite allocator (TRW->ZF sale, Arconic split, 15%->25% margin, A-). 靠谱 — but watch the late-cycle M&A and the buyback at >50x P/E, and the succession gap.
- **价格 / "好生意 + 好价格":** This is where it fails. 段's discipline is to *wait for the fat pitch*. At 65x trailing / 57x forward earnings with a negative base-case 5y IRR, **this is a good business at a bad price — 看不懂的贵**. Verdict: **WATCH; 看着,等回调到 ~$174-200.**

## 2. Warren Buffett (lens)

- "Wonderful company at a fair price." HWM is wonderful (moat, returns on capital, spares annuity). But "**price is what you pay, value is what you get**" — at ~57x forward earnings the margin of safety is absent. Buffett buys quality but is multiple-disciplined. Verdict: **admire, wait.** HOLD existing; no new money.

## 3. Charlie Munger (lens)

- "A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price." HWM is a great business — but **"no matter how wonderful, it's not worth an infinite price."** The 57x multiple borrows years of return. Munger would also flag **incentive-caused bias** in buybacks at >50x. Verdict: **great business, price too rich — invert and wait.**

## 4. Howard Marks (lens)

- **Where are we in the cycle?** Commercial aero is mid/late up-cycle; the multiple is at the optimistic end. "**The most dangerous thing is paying a price that assumes everything goes right.**" HWM's base case requires flawless execution AND a permanent premium multiple. Marks: **second-level thinking says the good news is in the price** -> asymmetry is poor. Verdict: **WATCH; risk is to the downside of the multiple.**

## 5. Seth Klarman (lens)

- **Margin of safety is the cardinal rule.** A negative base-case 5y IRR is the opposite of margin of safety. Klarman would not own it here regardless of quality. Verdict: **REJECT new money until price < ~$174;** the business quality does not substitute for a margin of safety.

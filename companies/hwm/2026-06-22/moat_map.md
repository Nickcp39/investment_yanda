# HWM Moat Map (M3)

As-of: 2026-06-22

## Moat sources (ranked)

| Source | Strength | Evidence | Durability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Qualification / certification barrier** | Very strong | Superalloy airfoils require multi-year FAA/OEM qualification per engine program; re-sourcing is slow and risky | 10-20+ yr per program |
| **Process / metallurgical IP & yield** | Very strong | Single-crystal investment casting is a hard, low-yield process few can do at scale; tacit know-how | Structural |
| **Installed-base aftermarket lock-in** | Strong | Spares pull off engines in service 20-30 yr; HWM's growing base compounds it | Multi-decade |
| **Switching cost / cost-of-failure** | Strong | A failed part grounds a multi-hundred-$M asset; customers don't chase price on spec parts | Structural |
| **Scale / share** | Moderate-strong | Leading positions in airfoils, aero fasteners, forged wheels (#1) | Cyclical in wheels |
| **Customer concentration (two-edged)** | — | GE/Safran/RTX/Rolls/Boeing/Airbus: sticky once qualified, but powerful on new-part price | — |

## Moat test by segment

- **Engine Products — DEEP & DURABLE.** The certification + metallurgy + aftermarket triple-lock is as strong a components moat as exists in aerospace. 36.6% EBITDA confirms it.
- **Fastening Systems — DEEP.** Spec-in + cost-of-failure asymmetry; 31.8% EBITDA.
- **Engineered Structures — MODERATE.** Capital/scale moat, more commoditized, 22.4% EBITDA.
- **Forged Wheels — GOOD but CYCLICAL.** #1 share and 30.5% EBITDA, but tied to the truck cycle (volumes -11% in Q1).

## What could erode it

- A genuine commercial-aero down-cycle compresses volumes (cyclical, not structural — the moat survives).
- OEM in-sourcing of airfoil capacity (slow, capital-heavy, historically limited).
- Additive/new manufacturing eventually lowering the casting barrier (long-dated, not near-term).
- Substitution risk is low for certified hot-section parts.

## Verdict

Moat is **deep and durable for the profit-dominant Engine Products + Fastening core**; cyclical-but-good for Wheels. This justifies a quality_compounder label — and a premium multiple — but NOT the 57x forward multiple at which the stock currently trades (see M5/M6).

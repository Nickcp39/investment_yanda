Core thesis

Howmet is a top-tier pure-play on the scarce, certified nodes of the jet-engine supply chain — investment-cast single-crystal superalloy turbine airfoils (Engine Products, 54% of revenue, 36.6% segment EBITDA), aerospace fasteners, Ti structures, and forged truck wheels — riding a genuine multi-year commercial-aero up-cycle plus a high-margin recurring engine-spares annuity. Q1 2026 evidences it: revenue +19%, commercial aero +20%, defense +10%, gas turbines +39%, adj EBITDA margin 32.0% (+320bps). Balance sheet is a fortress (net debt ~0.7x, A- rated); operator John Plant is an elite allocator (TRW→ZF sale, Arconic→Howmet split, op margin 15%→25%, $2.45B buyback). Business quality = exceptional.

The problem is price, not the business. At $280.36 the stock is 65x trailing / 57x forward earnings and ~37x EV/EBITDA, near an all-time high after a +65%-off-low run. The multiple embeds the whole up-cycle AND a permanently elevated quality re-rating.

Runner dissent

Higher conviction in the business than the mechanical −2 implies. HWM's airfoil moat + spares annuity is arguably more durable and less cyclical than GEV at a comparable nosebleed multiple, with a better balance sheet and an elite allocator; the bull case may be too conservative and per-share buyback/dividend compounding adds points the price-to-price IRR misses. WATCH is still correct — base IRR is negative and there is genuinely zero margin of safety at $280 — but this is a name to want to own; a 30–40% pullback toward $170–200 flips it to STARTER fast. Swing factor = the exit multiple; if aero-bottleneck quality permanently re-rates to 40x+, today's price is defensible and WATCH would be wrong.

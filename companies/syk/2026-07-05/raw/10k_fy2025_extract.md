## Business Description (as filed)
Stryker Corporation — global medical technology company. Products/services in MedSurg and Neurotechnology, Orthopaedics, and Spine. ~52,000+ employees. Operates in ~75 countries. One of the largest pure-play medtech companies by revenue.

## Segments (as-reported FY2025 structure — TWO reportable segments)
1. **MedSurg and Neurotechnology** (larger segment, ~ 60% of sales): Instruments (surgical power tools, waste management, smoke evacuation), Endoscopy (video imaging, communications, sports medicine), Medical (emergency care / EMS cots & stretchers, defibrillators [Physio-Control/LIFEPAK], patient handling, Sage disposables), Neurovascular (stroke / hemorrhagic & ischemic — coils, stent retrievers), and Neuro Cranial (Neurosurgical, ENT).
2. **Orthopaedics and Spine** (~ 40% of sales): Knees, Hips, Trauma & Extremities, Other Ortho, Spine (spinal implants + enabling technology), and — critically — **Mako SmartRobotics** (robotic-arm-assisted surgery platform, razor that pulls Stryker-brand implant "razorblades").

NOTE: Stryker in prior years reported "Orthopaedics" and "Spine" more separately and MedSurg/Neurotech as its other pillar; current 10-K aggregates into the two reportable segments above. Mako sits within Orthopaedics.

## Named Competitors (10-K risk factors / competition section)
Medtronic, Johnson & Johnson (DePuy Synthes / Ethicon), Zimmer Biomet, Smith+Nephew, Intuitive Surgical, Globus Medical (+ NuVasive, now part of Globus), Boston Scientific, Olympus, Penumbra (neurovascular), among others.

## China / VBP Risk Factor (paraphrased)
Company identifies volume-based procurement (VBP) / centralized government tendering in China, and increased competition from domestic manufacturers, as risks that can pressure pricing and volume. China ortho VBP has repriced knee/hip/trauma/spine implants downward in prior tender rounds. Domestic surgical-robot entrants (e.g., Tinavi, MicroPort MedBot/OrthoBot) named in industry context as emerging competition to Mako in-market.

## Financial Headline (FY2025, cross-checked stockanalysis.com + IR press release)
- FY2025 net sales: ~$25.1B (organic growth high-single-digit; reported growth reflects M&A + FX)
- Adjusted diluted EPS: ~$13.63 (FY2025)
- Active bolt-on M&A cadence; goodwill + intangibles large as a % of assets (M&A-driven)
- Two-segment split: MedSurg & Neurotech ~60% / Orthopaedics & Spine ~40%

## Mako SmartRobotics (as filed + IR/industry data)
- Cumulative Mako installed base: 3,000+ systems globally (growing double digits YoY)
- Mako indications: total knee, partial knee, total hip; Mako Spine and Mako Shoulder are the newest applications extending the platform
- Razor/razorblade: a Mako placement effectively commits the hospital to Stryker knee/hip implants for procedures performed on the robot — high implant pull-through + switching cost once surgeons are trained and workflow is built

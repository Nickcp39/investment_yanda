Capex is modest relative to revenue (3.03%), and R&D investment (6.46% of revenue, $1,623M) is substantial and consistent with a genuine innovation-driven strategy (funding the Mako platform roadmap: Hip Revision, Spine, Shoulder, RPS). This is consistent with (not a red flag against) Stryker's stated serial-acquirer strategy, but it does mean free cash flow AFTER acquisitions (a "owner earnings ex-growth-capex" lens) is much thinner than headline OCF suggests — explicitly flagged as a modeling consideration for valuation.md.

**Read**: Like GEHC, SYK's identified one-offs this run are predominantly on the COST/disruption side (cyber incident, tariffs) rather than earnings-INFLATING one-offs requiring normalization (contrast with the GEV exemplar's tax-release/M&A-remeasurement gains). This means current reported Q1 2026 figures likely UNDERSTATE normalized run-rate earnings power, and the key test of whether this is truly transient (as management maintains) rather than a sign of deeper issues is whether H2 2026 results show the deferred cyber-incident revenue materially recaptured (facts.md O6) and margin recovering from the tariff drag as guided.

## Financial Quality Verdict

**GOOD, with a real but well-contained and well-explained single-quarter disruption.** FY2025 was a strong year on every headline metric (double-digit organic growth, margin expansion, strong OCF/NI conversion at ~1.55x). Q1 2026's adjusted EPS decline (−8.5% YoY) and organic growth deceleration (10.3%→1.0%) are real and should not be waved away, but they trace almost entirely to a single, dated, quantified event (the cyber incident) plus a front-loaded, guided-to-fade tariff headwind — not a multi-period structural deterioration pattern like GEHC's three-consecutive-quarter margin compression. The balance sheet carries real, M&A-driven leverage (goodwill+intangibles >50% of assets) that is moderate in absolute terms (D/E 0.64x) but structurally embeds integration/impairment risk as a recurring feature of the business model. This financial profile supports a "strong underlying business absorbing one confirmed, well-explained disruption" read — materially more constructive than GEHC's "solid business showing a multi-quarter negative trend" verdict, though the H2 2026 recapture of the deferred $375M (facts.md O6) remains the single most important near-term validation point.

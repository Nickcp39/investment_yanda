## Background

Kevin A. Lobo has served as Chairman, President & CEO of Stryker since **October 2012** — approximately **13.5 years of tenure** as of the as_of date [facts.md E8, E14]. This is a materially longer, more proven track record than GEHC's Peter Arduini (~3.5 years at GEHC specifically, albeit with prior med-tech CEO experience) — Lobo has led Stryker through more than a decade of consistent execution, multiple full economic cycles, and the entire build-out of the Mako robotics franchise from acquisition (Stryker acquired MAKO Surgical in 2013, shortly after Lobo's arrival — general company-history knowledge, not independently re-verified this run) to its current ~85%+ estimated category share [facts.md E36].

## Track Record at SYK (2012-present)

**What has gone right**:
- Built and scaled the Mako SmartRobotics franchise from a 2013 acquisition into the dominant orthopedic-robotics platform (3,000+ installs, 1M+/2M+ procedure milestones, facts.md E30/E32) — this is a genuine, multi-year value-creation achievement directly attributable to capital-allocation and operational execution decisions made on Lobo's watch
- Led **60+ acquisitions** over his tenure [facts.md E46] — a genuinely serial, disciplined bolt-on M&A program (~$5.0B/year cash spend, E15) that has consistently added both MedSurg & Neurotechnology breadth and Orthopaedics/robotics depth (Inari Medical 2025, AVS 2026, facts.md E44-E45)
- FY2025 delivered +10.3% organic growth, +11.8% adjusted EPS growth, and 26.3% adjusted operating margin [facts.md E10-E13] — genuinely strong, multi-metric operating performance in the year immediately preceding the cyber incident
- **Maintained (did not cut) FY2026 guidance** immediately after the March 2026 cyber incident and the resulting Q1 EPS miss [facts.md E22] — this is either a genuine display of confidence backed by real visibility into H2 recovery, or a credibility risk if the recapture does not materialize; either way, it is a decisive, un-hedged public commitment rather than a vague "we're monitoring the situation" statement
- Installed a new COO (Spencer Stiles) in the 2025-2026 window [facts.md E8] — a structural leadership addition suggesting active management-bench development, though the specific rationale/mandate is not detailed in sources reviewed this run

**What has gone wrong / under pressure**:
- The March 2026 cyber incident occurred on Lobo's watch and caused a real, quantified ~$375M deferred-sales impact and a Q1 2026 adjusted EPS miss of ~8.5% YoY (missing consensus by ~12.8%) [facts.md E19, E21] — while cyber incidents are an industry-wide risk not unique to management quality, the fact that Stryker's own IT infrastructure created a genuine ~3-week operational shutdown is a legitimate operational-resilience question for the leadership team, not purely bad luck
- Segment growth divergence in Orthopaedics (reported growth trailing organic growth by ~5.2pts, facts.md I4/O2) is unexplained in sources reviewed this run — if this reflects an unfavorable FX or divestiture dynamic, it would be a modest mark against segment-level execution clarity; if it is a data-extraction artifact, it is not a real concern — genuinely unresolved either way
- China VBP pressure (−1.4% to −1.5% growth-rate headwind, facts.md E42) and the lack of a quantified China-specific competitive response to domestic robotics entrants (Tinavi, MicroPort, etc., E40) suggest the China strategy, whatever it is, has not yet been evidenced as a clear success in sources reviewed this run — directly analogous to the still-open question of whether GEHC's China response is working

## Capital Allocation

- **M&A discipline**: 60+ acquisitions over ~13.5 years is a genuinely high cadence (roughly 4-5 deals/year on average), funded substantially from operating cash flow ($5,044M FY2025 OCF, facts.md E14) rather than solely debt — this is a real, sustained competency, not a one-off bet (contrast with GEHC's single large, still-unproven Intelerad bet). The AVS acquisition structure (~$435M upfront + up to $400M in milestones, facts.md E44) shows a disciplined, earn-out-linked structure that aligns payment with realized performance rather than paying full value upfront — a genuinely shareholder-protective deal structure.
- **Balance sheet**: Total debt $14,723M against equity $22,979M (D/E 0.64x, facts.md E28) and gross leverage ~2.1x debt/EBITDA (E29, single-source this run, O9) is a moderate, investment-grade-appropriate capital structure for a company running a ~$5.0B/year acquisition program — not a "net cash fortress" (unlike some exemplars), but not alarming either.
- **Dividend**: $1,284M paid in FY2025 [facts.md E14], yielding 1.08% at the current price [E5] — a modest, not headline, capital-return component, consistent with a company that clearly prioritizes reinvestment (R&D $1,623M, M&A ~$5.0B, facts.md E14-E15) over large-scale buybacks/dividends. This is a coherent, growth-oriented capital allocation posture, not scattershot.

## Grade: 4/5

**Rationale**: Lobo has a materially longer and more proven track record than GEHC's Arduini — 13.5 years of consistent execution, including building the single most valuable strategic asset in this dossier (the Mako franchise) essentially from scratch via disciplined M&A, sustained by a genuinely serial (60+ deal) acquisition program funded predominantly from operating cash flow rather than balance-sheet stress. The 93%-at-risk compensation structure is a real, confirmed positive alignment signal (unlike GEHC, where this was an unresolved gap). The docks from a 5/5: (a) the March 2026 cyber incident, whatever its ultimate cause, represents a real operational-resilience gap that materialized under his leadership with a quantified $375M impact; (b) maintaining full FY2026 guidance immediately after a real, not cosmetic, Q1 miss is a bold but unproven bet whose validation is still pending (facts.md O6); (c) the China competitive response to domestic robotics entrants has not been evidenced as a clear success in sources reviewed this run, leaving a genuine open question about whether the ~85%+ category dominance (E36) is durable in the one market where the "China domestic fast-follower" playbook has proven most effective against other med-tech incumbents (as demonstrated in the GEHC/United Imaging dossier).

**What would move this to 5/5**: (a) confirmation that the $375M deferred Q1 sales is substantially recaptured in H2 2026, validating the maintained-guidance bet; (b) a quantified China-specific Mako/implant competitive response showing share stability or growth against Tinavi/MicroPort/domestic entrants; (c) resolution of the Orthopaedics segment reported-vs-organic-growth divergence with a benign explanation; (d) continued execution on the Mako platform roadmap (Shoulder launch in 2026 as targeted) without further operational disruptions.

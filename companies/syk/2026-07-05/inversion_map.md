## Inversion Question: How Does This Investment Permanently Impair Capital?

Working backward from failure — what would have to be true for a Stryker investment at $326.54 to be a genuine, permanent loss (not just a volatile drawdown)?

---

## Kill Path 1: Cyber-Incident Recovery Fails to Materialize (HIGHEST NEAR-TERM TEST)

**Mechanism**: Management maintained FY2026 guidance (organic growth 8.0-9.5%, adjusted EPS $14.90-15.10, facts.md E22) immediately after the March 2026 cyber incident and a real Q1 EPS miss. If the ~$375M deferred Q1 sales (E21) are NOT substantially recaptured in H2 2026 — because customers ordered from competitors during the ~3-week disruption window, or because the shortfall reflects genuine demand loss rather than pure timing — the maintained guidance would prove to be an overconfident management commitment, and FY2026 results would miss the guided range, likely triggering a real re-rating.

**Evidence for**: This is a genuinely untested claim as of the as_of date — only Q1 2026 results have been reported; H2 2026 recapture cannot yet be confirmed either way (facts.md O6).

**Evidence against / mitigant**: Cyber incidents of this type are, per general industry pattern, typically operational/order-to-shipment disruptions rather than genuine demand destruction — once systems are restored (as they were by early April 2026), deferred orders for medical devices (which hospitals generally still need, rather than substitute away from during a temporary vendor outage) are plausibly recoverable. Management's willingness to maintain (not hedge) guidance one quarter after the event is itself a signal of genuine internal visibility into order backlogs, though this is inherently a claim that can only be verified in arrears.

**Verdict**: MODERATE risk, testable within 1-2 quarters. **Q2 2026 results (expected ~late July 2026) are the single most important near-term validation/falsification point in this entire dossier** — directly comparable in importance to GEHC's Q2 2026 tariff-fade test.

## Kill Path 2: China Domestic Robotics Replicates the United Imaging Playbook Against Mako (HIGHEST CAREER-RELEVANCE, LEAST-RESOLVED)

**Mechanism**: China's domestic orthopedic-robotics entrants (Tinavi/Tirobot NMPA-cleared 2023 for knee, MicroPort MedBot's OrthoBot/Honghu, plus implant makers Weigao/AK Medical/Chunli, facts.md E40) could replicate the exact playbook that has already worked against GEHC's China imaging position: VBP-tender alignment, local-manufacture procurement preference, and rapid NMPA-cleared product iteration at a lower price point. If this dynamic compounds and Stryker's China robotics/implant position erodes the way GEHC's China imaging position demonstrably has (−15% YoY, per the GEHC dossier), this would directly threaten the ~85%+ estimated global category share (facts.md E36) that underpins much of the Mako investment thesis, at least within the China market.

**Evidence for**: The mechanism is not hypothetical — a directly comparable global medtech incumbent (GEHC) has already experienced this exact erosion pattern in China via an analogous domestic-fast-follower dynamic (United Imaging). Stryker's own China VBP growth-rate headwind (−1.4% to −1.5%, facts.md E42) confirms SOME version of this pressure is already present, even if not yet quantified at the robotics-specific or dollar level.

**Evidence against / mitigant**: No source reviewed this run confirms that Stryker's China robotics position has ALREADY eroded the way GEHC's China imaging position has — the evidence gap here (facts.md O3) means this kill path is currently UNCONFIRMED, not evidenced, unlike the GEHC case where the erosion was a demonstrated financial fact. Stryker's global Mako installed base (3,000+ systems, well ahead of any single disclosed domestic Chinese competitor's installed base) plus its multi-generation platform roadmap (Hip Revision already shipping, Spine/Shoulder in the pipeline) suggest a genuinely larger global scale advantage than GEHC had relative to United Imaging at a comparable stage.

**Verdict**: REAL but CURRENTLY UNCONFIRMED risk — the single most important research gap in this dossier (facts.md O3), and the top priority for the next refresh. Given the directly analogous GEHC precedent, this should be tracked with real diligence, not dismissed as low-probability simply because it is currently unquantified.

## Kill Path 3: Implant Pull-Through Thesis Is Wrong (or Overstated)

**Mechanism**: If the analyst thesis that Mako placements drive implant "pull-through" (facts.md I5, O4) is significantly overstated — i.e., if hospitals with Mako robots do NOT preferentially buy Stryker implants at a meaningfully higher rate than hospitals without Mako, or if a growing share of Mako-adjacent procedures use non-Stryker implants — then the Mako franchise's economic value to Stryker would be substantially smaller than the installed-base-and-procedure-volume metrics alone (facts.md E30, E32) might suggest, since the ROBOT alone (without confirmed implant attach) is a lower-margin, more competitively exposed capital-equipment sale.

**Evidence for**: This is genuinely untested in the evidence gathered this run — the pull-through thesis has never been confirmed with a company-disclosed figure (facts.md O4), meaning it is equally possible the thesis is directionally correct, meaningfully overstated, or not a significant economic factor at all.

**Evidence against**: The general medtech "razor/razorblade" pattern is well-established across the industry (this is not a novel or implausible mechanism), and Stryker's continued heavy investment in expanding the Mako platform (Hip Revision, Spine, Shoulder, RPS, facts.md E34) is consistent with management believing the installed-base investment generates durable downstream value, even if the specific implant-pull-through mechanism is not the only channel through which that value is realized (e.g., instrument/disposable attach, service, or simply defending against a competitor's robotic platform gaining a foothold, might also be sufficient rationale for the investment even without confirmed implant pull-through).

**Verdict**: LOW-MODERATE risk to the THESIS as a whole (since the installed-base moat, Moat 1 in moat_map.md, stands independently of the pull-through question), but a genuine unresolved question for anyone trying to model Mako's TRUE economic contribution with precision. Not a basis for a negative verdict on its own, but should temper any valuation approach that assumes aggressive Mako-specific monetization.

## Kill Path 4: Balance-Sheet / M&A-Driven Impairment Risk

**Mechanism**: With goodwill + intangibles exceeding half of total assets (facts.md E27) and a continuing ~$5.0B/year acquisition cadence (E15), any individual large deal (AVS, Inari, or a future acquisition) that underperforms expectations would flow through as a goodwill impairment charge against a large intangible base. If Stryker's M&A discipline (60+ deals under Lobo, generally viewed favorably, facts.md E46) were to slip — e.g., overpaying for a deal amid competitive pressure to expand into the soft-tissue robotics adjacency (E39) — this could crystallize a real, if likely non-fatal, earnings/book-value hit.

**Evidence for**: The mechanical exposure is real and quantifiable (goodwill+intangibles $24.7B against $22.98B total equity, facts.md E26-E27) — a large impairment on even one sizeable deal could meaningfully move book value.

**Evidence against**: 13.5 years and 60+ deals of demonstrated M&A discipline under the same CEO (facts.md E46) is a meaningfully long track record without an apparent major, disclosed impairment event flagged in sources reviewed this run (though a systematic impairment-history search was not performed this run — flagged as a gap). Leverage ratios (D/E 0.64x, ~2.1x gross debt/EBITDA) are moderate, providing some balance-sheet cushion against a moderate impairment shock.

**Verdict**: LOW-MODERATE, structurally embedded but not acute risk — monitorable via future 10-K impairment disclosures, not an immediate concern.

## What Would NOT Kill This Thesis (Noise, Not Signal)

- **The Q1 2026 stock-price weakness that set the 52wk low ($281.00 on 2026-05-11)** — this reflects the cyber-incident-driven Q1 miss, which is real but explicitly characterized by management (and, per the maintained guidance, believed by management) to be a timing disruption, not a permanent demand or competitive-share loss. The current price ($326.54, +16.2% above that low) reflects a partial market re-rating consistent with this interpretation, not a data error (independently confirmed against 2 sources, facts.md E1).
- **Analyst sentiment (27 Buy ratings, $386.80 average price target, +18.5% upside, facts.md E6/S1)** — provides some independent corroboration that Wall Street views the cyber-incident disruption as transitory, though sell-side consensus is not independently verified evidence and should be weighted lightly.
- **The mere existence of China domestic robotics competitors (Tinavi, MicroPort, etc.)** — their EXISTENCE is confirmed and named (facts.md E40), but their competitive IMPACT on Stryker specifically is not yet quantified (unlike the confirmed GEHC/United Imaging erosion) — existence of a competitor is not itself evidence of share loss, and should not be treated as such until better evidence emerges.

## Inversion Summary

No single kill path reviewed rises to "high-confidence imminent thesis-killer." The most immediately testable (cyber-incident recovery, Kill Path 1) resolves within 1-2 quarters via Q2 2026 results. The highest career-relevance risk (China domestic robotics replicating the United Imaging playbook, Kill Path 2) is REAL in mechanism (directly analogous to an already-realized precedent in a comparable peer) but CURRENTLY UNCONFIRMED in magnitude for Stryker specifically — a genuine evidence gap, not a dismissed risk. The two lower-priority paths (implant pull-through overstatement, M&A/impairment risk) are structurally embedded, low-probability-in-the-near-term considerations rather than acute threats. This supports a measured, evidence-disciplined verdict: **a fundamentally stronger financial-quality and moat-evidence position than the GEHC exemplar, with one real near-term test (cyber recapture) and one genuine, unresolved long-term research priority (China robotics competitive dynamics)** — see valuation.md for the price-side conclusion.

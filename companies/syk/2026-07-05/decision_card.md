## Career Note: Mako Robotics + China VBP / Domestic Robotics (AI + Robotics Competition Lens)

Stryker's Mako SmartRobotics franchise is the closest orthopedic-specific analog to the "installed-base robotic moat" thesis tracked across this coverage universe — **3,000+ cumulative installed systems, 1M+ robotic knee procedures, 2M+ total robotic procedures, and an estimated ~85%+ share of the orthopedic-robotics category** (facts.md E30, E32, E36 — the last an analyst estimate, not company-disclosed). The platform continues expanding: Mako Hip Revision (FDA-cleared March 2025 — the first robotic-assisted hip-revision feature in the market), Mako Spine, and Mako Shoulder (targeted 2026 launch) all extend the addressable procedure base beyond the original knee/hip indications, with Q1 2026 delivering the **best-ever Q1 for Mako installs** even amid the cyber-incident disruption (facts.md E31) — a genuine signal of underlying demand strength.

Two China-related competitive dynamics deserve close tracking. First, **China domestic orthopedic-robotics competitors are real and named**: Tinavi (天智航, Tirobot platform, NMPA-cleared for knee application in 2023), MicroPort MedBot (OrthoBot/Honghu), plus adjacent implant makers Weigao, AK Medical, and Chunli (facts.md E40). This is structurally the SAME playbook — NMPA-cleared, price-competitive, VBP-tender-aligned domestic alternatives — that has **already worked once**, demonstrably, against GEHC's China imaging position (United Imaging's 31% revenue CAGR 2018-2024 and −15% YoY GEHC China revenue decline, per the GEHC dossier). **Unlike the GEHC case, however, Stryker's disclosure here is thinner**: only a growth-RATE VBP headwind is confirmed (−1.4% in 2024, −1.5% guided 2025, facts.md E42), with no China-specific revenue dollar figure, percentage of total, or robotics-specific competitive-impact data found in sources reviewed this run. **This is the single most important open research question in this dossier** — is Stryker's China robotics position holding, or is it following GEHC's China imaging trajectory with a lag? The evidence gathered this run cannot answer this, and closing this gap should be the top priority for the next refresh.

Second, **Stryker's own management has flagged an evaluation of the soft-tissue robotics adjacency** (~50 identified startups, facts.md E39) — a potential future move into Intuitive Surgical's (da Vinci, ~9,000 installs) and Medtronic's (Hugo) territory. This is a forward-looking strategic signal worth tracking: it demonstrates confidence that Stryker's robotics competency (built via Mako) is extensible beyond orthopedics, but it would also mean competing against a soft-tissue incumbent (ISRG) with an arguably deeper, longer-established moat than Stryker currently enjoys even in its own dominant orthopedic category — a genuinely different competitive proposition than defending home turf.

## Bottom Line

Stryker is a genuinely strong, category-defining medtech business — a well-quantified Mako orthopedic-robotics installed-base moat sitting atop a diversified, faster-growing MedSurg & Neurotechnology franchise, currently absorbing one confirmed, dated, well-explained disruption (the March 2026 cyber incident) with maintained full-year guidance and a base-case valuation that already clears an 8% hurdle with real margin of safety at today's price. The verdict is STARTER, not because the business or the price require caution, but because two genuinely open questions — whether China domestic robotics competition will replicate its already-demonstrated success against GEHC, and whether the implant "pull-through" thesis is real — remain unresolved and deserve to be closed before sizing up further. The single fact worth remembering: Mako's ~85%+ estimated category share and 3,000+ installed systems are genuinely strong, well-evidenced facts — but whether that dominance holds in China, where the exact same domestic-fast-follower playbook has already worked once against a comparable global medtech incumbent, is the one question this dossier cannot yet answer.

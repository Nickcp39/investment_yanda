# SYK Moat Map (M3)

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E7-E51

---

## Moat Summary: DOMINANT ROBOTIC-SURGERY FRANCHISE, GENUINE BUT PARTIALLY-UNQUANTIFIED PULL-THROUGH, FACING TWO EMERGING FLANKS

Business quality grade: **GOOD-TO-VERY-GOOD** — Stryker has built a genuinely category-defining position in orthopedic robotics (Mako, ~85%+ estimated share, facts.md E36) with a real, multi-year installed-base switching-cost moat, sitting atop a diversified, faster-growing MedSurg & Neurotechnology base (+10.7% organic FY2025). This is a stronger moat foundation than GEHC's (which was explicitly "good, not exceptional" with a visibly eroding China position) — but SYK's moat carries its own two distinct, unresolved flanks: (a) the implant "pull-through" economics that would make Mako a true annuity are analyst-estimated, not company-confirmed, and (b) China domestic robotics entrants + Stryker's own stated evaluation of expanding into ISRG's soft-tissue territory both signal the current category boundaries may not hold indefinitely.

---

## Moat 1: Mako Installed-Base + Surgeon-Training Switching Cost — REAL AND SUBSTANTIALLY QUANTIFIED

**Evidence**:
- 3,000+ cumulative installed systems globally, milestone reached Q4 2025, with **best-ever Q1 installs** in Q1 2026 even amid the cyber-incident disruption [facts.md E30-E31]
- 1M+ cumulative robotic knee procedures, 2M+ total robotic procedures across all indications [E32]
- Clear multi-generation platform roadmap: Mako 4 (AAOS 2025), Mako Hip Revision (FDA-cleared March 2025 — first robotic hip-revision feature in the market), Mako Spine, Mako Shoulder (2026 launch target), Mako RPS (handheld, expanding beyond the cart-based form factor) [E34]
- US utilization mix (~2/3 knees, ~1/3 hips) and international mix (~50% knees, ~20% hips) show genuine, ongoing procedure-volume growth on the existing base, not just unit placements [E33]

**Durability**: Genuinely strong — once a hospital has trained surgeons and redesigned OR workflow around Mako, switching to a competing platform (Zimmer ROSA, ~1,500 installs — roughly half of Mako's base, E38) involves real retraining cost, workflow disruption, and (per the analyst thesis) potential implant-supply disruption. This is a materially harder-to-replicate moat than GEHC's service-annuity moat, which explicitly competes against third-party service organizations — there is no equivalent "independent Mako servicer" undermining this lock-in in sources reviewed this run.

**Gap vs a fully quantified standard**: The installed-base and procedure-volume figures are well-evidenced (A1/A2 tier, IR-sourced), but Stryker does not disclose Mako as a standalone reporting segment — direct Mako revenue (~$1.0B FY2024, E35) and market share (~85%+, E36) are both analyst estimates, not audited disclosures. This is a real but smaller completeness gap than GEHC's service-revenue-mix gap (which had literally zero quantification), since the UNIT/PROCEDURE metrics here are genuinely disclosed even if the DOLLAR attribution is not.

## Moat 2: Implant "Pull-Through" (Triathlon Knee, etc.) — PLAUSIBLE, ANALYST-ESTIMATED, NOT COMPANY-CONFIRMED

**The thesis** (facts.md I5, O4): A Mako placement is hypothesized to commit the hospital to Stryker-brand knee/hip implants for on-robot procedures, creating a recurring, procedure-linked "razorblade" revenue stream layered on top of the robot "razor." This is structurally consistent with the general medtech playbook and would, if true and quantifiable, be the single most valuable moat fact in this dossier — a genuine annuity tied 1:1 to the installed base.

**Why this is NOT yet treated as EVIDENCE**: No source reviewed this run — including the 10-K — states an implant attach-rate, an ASP premium for on-robot procedures, or any other quantified pull-through metric. This is explicitly flagged (not glossed over) as the single most important unresolved moat question in this dossier, directly analogous to GEHC's unquantified AI-monetization gap. **Until this is confirmed with a company-disclosed figure, "Mako drives implant pull-through" should be treated as a coherent, probable hypothesis — not an audited fact.**

**Comparison to GEHC's AI-monetization gap**: Structurally similar (both are "the strategic narrative is coherent, but the dollar evidence doesn't exist yet" pattern), but SYK's version is arguably lower-risk to the thesis, because even WITHOUT confirmed pull-through, the underlying installed-base moat (Moat 1) and the diversified MedSurg & Neurotechnology base independently support the business — pull-through would be an accelerant/confirmation, not the sole pillar the whole investment case rests on (unlike GEHC, where AI monetization was closer to load-bearing for the bull case).

## Moat 3: Diversified MedSurg & Neurotechnology Base — GENUINE, FASTER-GROWING, LESS SINGULARLY DEPENDENT

**Evidence**:
- $15,647M FY2025 (62% of total revenue), +10.7% organic — growing faster than Orthopaedics (+9.5% organic) [facts.md E16]
- Genuinely broad portfolio: Instruments, Endoscopy, Medical (incl. Physio-Control/LIFEPAK defibrillators), Neurovascular, Neuro Cranial — a diversified base spanning surgical tools, emergency/critical-care equipment, and neurovascular devices, none of which depend on the Mako robotics narrative

**Durability**: This segment provides genuine diversification value — Stryker is not a single-product-thesis company the way a pure-play robotics name would be. Named competitors here (Medtronic, Boston Scientific, Olympus, Penumbra, facts.md E37) are large, well-resourced incumbents, meaning this segment's moat is more "scaled, diversified incumbent" than "category-defining dominant share" — a genuinely different (and somewhat lower) moat character than the Mako franchise, but a real one nonetheless given its consistent double-digit organic growth.

---

## Moat 4: Regulatory Approval Barrier — TIME-BASED, NOT PERMANENT

FDA/CE-mark/NMPA clearance cycles create a genuine lag for fast-followers on any single robotic indication (Mako Hip Revision cleared March 2025; Mako Shoulder targeting 2026; Mako Spine in the pipeline, facts.md E34). But — as with GEHC's imaging-clearance moat — this is a rolling, not permanent, barrier: China's Tinavi already secured NMPA clearance for a knee-robotics application in 2023 (facts.md E40), meaning the "first-mover regulatory lead" dynamic is already contested in at least one major market, not a clean Stryker-only advantage globally.

---

## Moat Weaknesses / Where the Moat Faces Genuine Emerging Pressure

1. **China domestic robotics entrants are real, named, and NMPA-cleared** (facts.md E40): Tinavi (Tirobot, knee-cleared 2023), MicroPort MedBot (OrthoBot/Honghu), Weigao, AK Medical, Chunli. Unlike GEHC's United Imaging (which has a clean, quantified 31% revenue CAGR and 18.2% share data point), **no comparably quantified China-specific competitive-share or revenue-impact figure exists for Stryker's Mako franchise in sources reviewed this run** — this is a genuine evidence gap (facts.md O3), not a confirmed absence of risk. The directional signal (VBP growth-rate drag of −1.4% to −1.5%, E42) confirms SOME China pricing/competitive pressure exists, but its magnitude relative to Mako specifically is unknown.
2. **Stryker's own stated evaluation of soft-tissue robotics adjacency (~50 startups, facts.md E39) is a double-edged signal**: On one hand, it demonstrates management confidence that the Mako-honed robotics competency is extensible — a genuine strategic option value. On the other hand, it implicitly concedes that orthopedic robotics alone (where Stryker already dominates) may not be a sufficient long-term growth vector on its own, and any move into ISRG's/Medtronic Hugo's territory would put Stryker up against Intuitive Surgical's own, arguably deeper, soft-tissue moat (~9,000 da Vinci installs, decades of surgeon-training lock-in, E38) — a much harder competitive position than the one Stryker currently enjoys in orthopedics.
3. **The cyber incident (facts.md E21) reveals a genuine operational-resilience gap** — a moat is not just about competitive positioning but about the ability to reliably execute; a ~3-week shutdown and $375M deferred-sales impact from an internal IT incident is a real, realized vulnerability, distinct from (but analytically comparable in spirit to) GEHC's tariff/supply-chain cost shocks.
4. **Segment growth divergence (facts.md I4/O2)** — the unexplained pattern where Orthopaedics' reported growth trails its organic growth by ~5.2pts is unresolved and could, in principle, reflect an unfavorable FX/divestiture dynamic specific to the Mako-containing segment; flagged for verification, not assumed benign.

**Overall moat verdict**: Stryker's moat is **real and, in its core installed-base dimension, better-evidenced than GEHC's** — the unit/procedure-volume metrics (3,000+ installs, 1M+/2M+ procedures) are genuine, IR-disclosed, multi-period-consistent facts, not just a strategic narrative. However, the moat's MOST VALUABLE potential dimension (implant pull-through economics) remains an unconfirmed analyst thesis, and two emerging flanks — China domestic robotics entrants (quantification gap, O3) and Stryker's own contemplated expansion into ISRG's soft-tissue territory (E39) — mean the current category dominance should not be assumed to be permanent or uncontested. This caps the moat at "good-to-very-good," not "exceptional/unambiguous," and is the primary driver of the DECISION_DRAFT (not COMPLETE) audit ceiling.

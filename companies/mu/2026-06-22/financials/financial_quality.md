## 2. Owner-earnings bridge (latest reported quarter, FQ2-26)

- GAAP net income $13,785M; non-GAAP $14,021M (the ~$236M gap is stock-comp/one-offs — clean, no large
  non-cash distortion à la NVDA's equity-gain inflation).
- Operating cash flow $11,900M; net capex $5,000M → **adjusted FCF $6,900M** for the quarter (FCF is REAL
  cash, not accrual — a key quality check). H1 FY26 OCF ~$20.3B funded ~$11.8B capex + $4.37B debt paydown.
- **Quality verdict: earnings are cash-backed.** Unlike a trailing-accounting trap, the record GAAP earnings
  are corroborated by record operating cash flow and FCF. There is no aggressive revenue-recognition or
  one-off gain doing the work. The issue is NOT accounting quality; it is **cycle position** (are these
  through-cycle or peak earnings?).

## 3. Balance sheet — genuinely de-risked vs prior cycles

- Cash + marketable + restricted investments **$16.7B** (FQ2-26); total debt ~$10.1–10.8B → **net-cash
  positive ~$6B** (vs net debt ~$4.76B at the 2025-03-21 prior). H1 FY26 prepaid $4.37B of debt.
- P/B ~17.6x at $1,185 (book value has NOT kept pace with the price; equity ~$55-65B implied).
- **Survivability: high.** Micron absorbed a -$5.8B FY23 loss and -$6.1B FCF *with a weaker balance sheet*
  and survived without distress. Today's net-cash position means a future down-cycle is a drawdown-to-fair-
  value risk, not a solvency risk. This is a real positive that caps the downside *floor* (no zero).

## 4. Normalized / through-cycle earnings (the central modeling problem)

This is where the valuation lives or dies. The trailing/run-rate numbers are up-leg-flattered:

- **Run-rate (annualizing FQ2-26 non-GAAP ~$12.20/qtr): ~$49 EPS.** Q3 guide ($19.15) annualizes to ~$76.
  Full-year FY26 consensus is even higher.
- **Normalized mid-cycle EPS: unknown and much lower.** History says memory gross margin mean-reverts toward
  30–45% over a full cycle, not 74–81%. A reasonable normalized estimate must blend the AI-memory uplift
  (HBM may permanently raise the floor) against commodity reversion. Rough band:
  - **Bear-normalized (margins revert toward historical mid-cycle, HBM premium partly competes away): ~$8–14 EPS.**
  - **Base-normalized (HBM structurally lifts the floor; through-cycle GM ~45–55%): ~$18–28 EPS.**
  - **Bull-normalized (AI demand keeps HBM scarce for years; GM holds 55%+): ~$30–45 EPS.**
- At $1,185: ~24–60x bear-normalized, ~42–66x base-normalized, ~26–40x bull-normalized. **On any reasonable
  NORMALIZED number, the stock is expensive** — the only way it is "cheap" (~11x) is on peak run-rate EPS
  treated as permanent. That is precisely the false_cheap trap pattern for a cyclical.

## 5. Capital intensity — the structural drag

- ~$5B/qtr net capex (recently); the AI-memory build (fabs + advanced packaging for HBM) is multi-year and
  large. In an up-cycle, OCF ($11.9B/qtr) dwarfs capex → big FCF. In a down-cycle, capex this size against
  collapsing OCF turns FCF negative fast (the FY23 -$6.1B FCF precedent).
- CHIPS Act incentives ($2.26B H1 FY26) subsidize part of the US fab build — a positive, but the bet is still
  large and only partly de-risked.

## 6. Watch-items (early warning of cycle roll)

- DRAM/NAND ASP direction (commodity portion); HBM contract pricing on renewals.
- Inventory trend (a rise into softening ASPs = the classic early glut tell).
- Gross-margin trajectory beyond the 81% Q3 guide — does it hold or step down in Q4 guidance?
- Capex vs OCF (if capex stays ~$5B/qtr while OCF falls → FCF compression).
- Industry capacity additions (all 3 suppliers' DRAM→HBM mix shift + new fabs → oversupply timing).

## Net financial-quality verdict

**High earnings QUALITY (cash-backed, clean, fortress balance sheet) at a high CYCLE POSITION (peak-leg
economics).** The accounting confirms the thesis — there is no fraud or distortion — but the financials must
be valued off NORMALIZED, not run-rate, economics, and on that basis the current price embeds a very
optimistic durability assumption. M4 signal: **+1 on confirmation/quality, but with a strong cycle-position
warning** that the run-rate is not the through-cycle number.

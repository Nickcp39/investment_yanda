# MU Inversion Map — as_of 2026-06-22

> Inversion: how does an owner who buys at ~$1,184.88 (all-time high, ~53x trailing, peak-cycle margins) lose
> money — and is any loss path PERMANENT (veto) vs a recoverable drawdown-to-fair-value (size cap)?

## Failure paths (ranked by likelihood × severity)

### 1. Cycle rollover / oversupply — the classic memory killer (HIGH likelihood over the horizon)
- **Mechanism:** the three suppliers' combined HBM + DRAM capacity additions (DRAM→HBM mix shift + new fabs)
  eventually outrun demand; ASPs fall; utilization drops; gross margin compresses from 74–81% back toward
  the historical 30–45% mid-cycle band. Earnings normalize down hard.
- **Precedent:** FY2022 peak ($8.7B NI) → FY2023 trough (-$5.8B NI, -$6.1B FCF) in one year. This is not
  hypothetical — it is the defining pattern of the business.
- **Loss type: RECOVERABLE drawdown-to-fair-value, NOT permanent.** A cyclical buyer at the peak can lose
  60–86% to mid-cycle fair value (see valuation) but the company survives (net-cash) and the cycle eventually
  turns again. This is a SIZE/PRICE problem, not a zero. → caps size and is the binding constraint, not a veto.

### 2. HBM4 share loss / qualification stumble (MEDIUM likelihood, HIGH severity to the thesis)
- **Mechanism:** Micron is #3 in HBM (~11–20%). If SK Hynix (leader) and/or Samsung win the HBM4/HBM4E
  transition on yield/qualification/pricing, Micron's differentiated premium erodes and it re-commoditizes —
  removing the one thing that could justify a structurally higher margin floor.
- **Loss type:** thesis-impairing but the company still has the commodity DRAM/NAND oligopoly base — so a
  severe de-rate toward commodity-cyclical valuation, not a zero. → escalates toward the durability question;
  a kill criterion, not an immediate veto.

### 3. AI-capex digestion (MEDIUM likelihood, HIGH severity — the upstream demand kill)
- **Mechanism:** MU's demand rests on hyperscaler AI capex. The repo macro file (M2) documents that capex is
  being funded by drawing down FCF (AMZN TTM FCF -95%, META buybacks $0, GOOGL still raising) — a "growth →
  digestion" switch in any one hyperscaler (a single quarter of revenue decel + capex held) would ripple into
  memory demand. AI-memory demand is the load-bearing assumption.
- **Loss type:** demand-shock cyclical drawdown — recoverable if AI is secular, severe if it was pull-forward.
  → kill monitor.

### 4. Paying peak multiple on peak earnings (HIGH likelihood of poor RETURN, even if business is fine)
- **Mechanism:** even if Micron executes perfectly, buying at an ATH above the average analyst target on
  run-rate earnings that the market is treating as durable means the *return* can be poor or negative as the
  earnings normalize and the multiple compresses — the business can be great and the stock still a bad
  new-money entry. This is the GOOGL/NVDA-at-$210 "great business, no MOS" pattern.
- **Loss type:** opportunity cost + drawdown. → this is precisely why M6 caps new money.

### 5. China new-entrant (LOW near-term, MEDIUM long-term)
- CXMT/YMTC scaling at leading-node/HBM would add a 4th+ supplier and erode the oligopoly over years. Not a
  near-term factor; a long-run structural watch.

## What is NOT a failure path here (the prior cycle's risks that are now MILDER)
- **Bankruptcy / balance-sheet death: ABSENT.** Net-cash positive, $16.7B liquidity, survived a worse setup
  in FY23. There is no solvency path. (This is why nothing here is a hard veto — the harness reserves veto for
  thesis-durability-break OR balance-sheet-can't-survive; neither holds.)
- **Structural decline / moat erosion (à la INTC-DCG): ABSENT.** The pool is growing, not shrinking; the moat
  is intact/widening. The risk is cyclical reversion, not secular decline.

## Permanent-impairment vs recoverable-drawdown verdict

**The dominant loss paths are RECOVERABLE (cyclical reversion, demand digestion, multiple compression), not
PERMANENT (no bankruptcy, no structural decline).** Therefore M5 = a strong SIZE/PRICE cap, NOT a hard veto.
The "permanent" risk is only the partial/relative one: paying a peak price means you may *never recover* your
specific entry cost even as the company thrives — i.e. the risk is the ENTRY PRICE, not the business.

## Kill criteria (cut back to cash/tracking)
1. **Cycle rollover confirmed:** DRAM/NAND/HBM ASPs decline together, gross margin steps down materially below
   the ~81% Q3 guide trajectory, while capex stays ~$5B/qtr → glut forming.
2. **HBM4 share/qualification slips** vs SK Hynix/Samsung, or HBM pricing normalizes fast as all three flood
   capacity → the differentiated premium evaporates.
3. **AI-capex digestion:** any major hyperscaler prints a quarter of revenue decel + holds/cuts capex guide
   (the macro-file switch signal) → upstream demand kill.
4. **Inventory glut tell:** inventory rises into softening ASPs.
5. **Multiple stays stretched with no estimate support:** price holds far above normalized fair value on
   run-rate earnings the market treats as permanent → poor risk/reward persists (already the case).
6. **Management over-builds at the top:** capex guided sharply higher into peak demand without a demand-
   durability case → the cycle-long-bias dark-arc materializing.

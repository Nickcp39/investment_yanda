# MU Investment Memo v1 — as_of 2026-06-22 (LIVE, DECISION_DRAFT)

## One-paragraph thesis

Micron is now an **exceptional but fundamentally cyclical** business at an **all-time-high price with no
margin of safety**. The 2025 thesis (a scarce AI-memory bottleneck supplier with HBM optionality) was right —
and the payoff vastly exceeded expectations: gross margin went 36.8% → 74.4% in a year, a single quarter's
net income ($13.79B) eclipsed the entire prior-cycle peak, and the balance sheet flipped to net-cash. But the
stock re-rated ~11.5x off its 52-week low to ~$1,185, trading at ~53x trailing earnings, **above the average
analyst target (~$965) and above even a bull-case normalized fair value (~$950)**, with fiscal Q3 earnings due
2026-06-24 (2 days after as_of). On the honest basis for a cyclical — normalized through-cycle EPS (~$11 bear
/ ~$22 base / ~$38 bull) — the price is ~2.7x base fair value. **New money: WATCH** (no MOS, late-cycle, peak
margins that have never survived a full cycle). **Existing position: HOLD**, with a real lean to **TRIM** into
the parabolic move. Business is not the constraint; price is.

## The core question: durability vs cycle-peak

- **Bull:** HBM's multi-year contracts + sold-out capacity + technical gating structurally lift the
  through-cycle margin floor; AI memory demand is a multi-year secular step-change; ~11x forward is cheap.
- **Bear:** 74-81% gross margins are peak-cycle; memory has never sustained them through a cycle; Micron is #3
  in HBM and the share/pricing erodes as all three suppliers flood HBM4 capacity; AI capex is FCF-funded and a
  digestion phase is the upstream kill; this is the same cycle with a bigger amplitude.
- **My read:** the bull case is *plausible and partly proven* (the AI-memory mix shift is real and durable to
  a degree), but the price already pays for the *fully-permanent* bull case and then some. At 2.7x base-
  normalized fair value, you are not paid to take the durability bet here. The asymmetry is wrong.

## INTERPRETATION (my read — not fact)

- **This is no longer the 2025-03-21 "cheap cyclical starter."** The prior case (P0) was right directionally — HBM/AI memory was a real bottleneck — but the *magnitude* of monetization (GM 36.8% → 74.4% in one year; quarterly NI exceeding the entire prior-cycle peak) vastly exceeded that thesis. The stock has re-rated ~11x off the 52wk low.
- **The whole investment debate sits in the trailing-vs-forward P/E gap (53x vs ~11x).** Forward ~11x is "cheap" ONLY if the ~$76–108 annualized forward EPS is durable. The bull says HBM's multi-year contracts + sold-out capacity structurally lift the through-cycle margin floor; the bear says these are peak-cycle earnings and the right multiple is on normalized (mid-cycle) EPS far below the run-rate. **Memory has never sustained 74% gross margins through a full cycle** — the burden of proof is on durability.
- **The margin of safety question is the binding one.** At ~$1,185, the price is above the average analyst target ($965) and at an all-time high, with Q3 earnings 2 days out. Even on a bullish forward number there is little room for error; on any normalization the downside to mid-cycle fair value is severe (the FY2023 trough is the cautionary mirror).
- **Balance sheet has genuinely strengthened** (net-cash positive, $16.7B liquidity) — survivability through a down-cycle is much better than in 2023. This caps *bankruptcy* risk, not *drawdown-to-fair-value* risk.
- **HBM #3 position is a real durability caveat.** MU captures the AI-memory pool but as the smallest of three suppliers; HBM4 share and qualification vs SK Hynix/Samsung is the swing variable for whether the premium persists.

# MU Business Model — as_of 2026-06-22

## One-line business

Micron designs and manufactures **memory and storage semiconductors — DRAM (including HBM), NAND flash, and
related products** — selling into data center (AI), mobile, client/PC, automotive, and embedded markets. It
is one of only **three scaled DRAM makers worldwide** (with SK Hynix and Samsung) and one of a handful of
NAND makers. Economics are driven by **bit supply/demand and ASP (a cyclical commodity dynamic)**, now
overlaid by a **structurally scarcer, higher-margin AI-memory pool (HBM + leading-node data-center DRAM)**.

## Revenue engine

- **Bits × ASP.** Revenue = bits shipped × average selling price, per product (DRAM/NAND). Historically the
  ASP is set quarterly in a near-commodity market → violent cyclicality (FY22 peak → FY23 -$5.8B loss →
  FY26 record).
- **The AI-memory overlay changes the engine for part of the mix.** HBM is sold **under multi-year (3–5yr)
  contracts**, capacity-allocated, and sold out for 2026 — a *structural* departure from quarterly commodity
  pricing. Leading-node data-center DRAM is similarly scarce. This is the higher-quality, higher-margin slice.
- **New 4-BU reporting (FQ2-26):** Cloud Memory (HBM + cloud DRAM), Core Data Center, Mobile & Client,
  Automotive & Embedded — a market-facing reorg that signals the data-center pivot. In FQ2-26, Cloud Memory
  + Core Data Center = ~$13.4B (~56% of revenue) at ~74% GM; DRAM ~79% of total revenue.
- **Capital intensity is the structural cost.** ~$5B/qtr net capex (recently) funds fabs + advanced packaging
  for HBM. CHIPS Act incentives ($2.26B netted in H1 FY26) partly subsidize US fab builds.

## 10-year test (would I want to own this for a decade?)

- **The business franchise: YES, with a cyclicality asterisk.** Memory is an enduring, consolidating
  oligopoly (3 DRAM players) with a genuine, deepening AI-demand tailwind. Micron has a real technical
  franchise and a cycle-tested team. Over 10 years, AI compute almost certainly needs vastly more memory.
- **But the *earnings stream* is cyclical, not a smooth compounder.** You would be underwriting multiple
  peak-to-trough swings. The right way to own a memory company for 10 years is to buy near trough/normalized
  economics, not at a cycle-peak all-time high on 74% gross margins.
- **The durability upgrade is the open question.** IF HBM's contracted, scarce, technically-gated economics
  permanently lift the through-cycle margin floor (e.g. 45–55% normalized GM vs 30–40% historically), Micron
  becomes a higher-quality, semi-secular business worth a higher multiple. That conversion is **plausible but
  unproven** — it has not survived a full cycle yet.

**Net: a GOOD, possibly improving, but fundamentally CYCLICAL franchise** — a durable oligopoly with a
genuine AI tailwind and a strong team, whose earnings power is real but cycle-dependent, and whose moat is
solid-but-#3 in the one differentiated product (HBM). Quality is rising with the AI-memory mix shift, but
not yet proven across a cycle → durability ceiling stays in the cyclical band (caps below Core per

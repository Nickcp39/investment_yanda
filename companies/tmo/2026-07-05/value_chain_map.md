## Upstream → TMO → Downstream

**Upstream inputs**:
- Electronic components, precision optics, superconducting/vacuum-system components (mass spectrometry, electron microscopy instruments)
- Specialty chemicals / biological raw materials (cell-culture media, reagents, bioproduction consumables — subject to biological/agricultural supply-chain considerations)
- Contract manufacturing and component suppliers subject to tariff exposure (the ~$375M tariff-impact estimate cited in facts.md E46, unconfirmed against a primary source this run)
- Skilled scientific/clinical labor (PPD clinical-research operations require large trained clinical-operations and monitoring staff across many countries)

**TMO internal value-add**:
- R&D / instrument engineering (Orbitrap mass-spec platform — a genuinely proprietary, decades-refined technology franchise; electron microscopy; chromatography systems)
- AI/software layer (Proteoform Studio, Proteome Discoverer, BioPharma Finder — increasingly bundled with instruments; NVIDIA collaboration for lab automation at scale [facts.md E47-E49])
- Manufacturing (instruments + consumables/reagents — large-scale, quality-system-heavy given regulated end-markets)
- Bioproduction media/consumables manufacturing (single-use bioreactor components, cell-culture media — a genuine, hard-to-replicate manufacturing-scale + quality-system barrier)
- Clinical trial operations (PPD) — global clinical-site network, regulatory/GCP expertise, data management (increasingly including Clario's imaging/eCOA/cardiac-safety endpoint data specialty)
- CDMO / pharma development services — drug-substance and drug-product manufacturing on behalf of biopharma customers
- Direct + channel sales force, instrument service/field-engineer network

**Downstream customers**:
- Pharma / biotech companies (across the full spectrum — Big Pharma to small biotech — for both instruments/reagents AND clinical-research/CDMO services)
- Academic and government research institutions (a currently SOFT channel — the direct driver of Analytical Instruments' −2% organic Q1 2026 [facts.md E29])
- Industrial / applied-markets customers (environmental testing, food safety, forensics — a diversifying, less R&D-cycle-exposed customer base)
- Clinical laboratories / hospital labs (Specialty Diagnostics customers)
- Government/public-health agencies (partially overlapping with the academic/government soft channel)

| Value chain link | Profit pool depth | Notes |

| Capital equipment sale (Analytical Instruments — mass spec/chromatography/EM) | Medium | Highest ASP, highest technology differentiation (Orbitrap franchise), but currently the SLOWEST-growing/only-DECLINING segment (−2% organic Q1 2026) — most exposed to academic/government capex cycle in US + China |

## Bottleneck Candidates (developed further in bottleneck_map.md)

1. **Orbitrap mass-spectrometry technology franchise** — decades of proprietary R&D refinement in high-resolution mass spectrometry; genuinely difficult for a fast-follower (Chinese or otherwise) to replicate at the high end in the near term. This is TMO's closest analog to GEHC's photon-counting-CT detector IP.
2. **BIOSECURE-driven CRO/CDMO reshoring** — a genuine, evidenced, currently-materializing tailwind (not a bottleneck TMO controls, but a policy-driven channel shift working IN TMO's favor, the mirror image of GEHC's China-VBP bottleneck working against it).
3. **Bioproduction consumables scale + quality systems** — single-use bioreactor components and cell-culture media require significant manufacturing scale and regulatory quality-system investment; a real, if not absolute, barrier to new entrants.
4. **Academic/government research funding cycle** — this is a bottleneck WORKING AGAINST TMO currently (soft demand in Analytical Instruments), analogous to GEHC's PCS weakness, though here the CAUSE is explained by management (funding cycle + China macro) rather than left unexplained.
5. **China as end-market vs. China as displaced CRO/CDMO supplier** — TMO sits on both sides of a China-related bottleneck simultaneously: soft as an instruments END-MARKET in China, but a beneficiary of reshoring AWAY FROM Chinese CRO/CDMO SUPPLY. This dual exposure is a distinctive feature not present in the GEHC (single-direction China exposure) exemplar and must be tracked as two separate variables, not netted into one.

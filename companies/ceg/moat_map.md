## ROIC–WACC Lens

- **Current ROIC: positive** — unlike NBIS, the franchise earns real, growing owner earnings (FY2025 adj EPS $9.39, FCF ~$1.3B, ~95% CF on an already-built fleet). (C002–C004)
- **Estimated WACC:** moderate (regulated-adjacent cash flows, PTC floor, but merchant power-price exposure + post-Calpine leverage) — call it high-single to low-double digits.
- **Spread: positive today**, and structurally so on the nuclear fleet (scarce asset + ~$0 fuel + PTC floor). The *incremental* return question is Calpine accretion + new PPAs.
- **Reinvestment runway:** Crane restart (DOE-funded), uprates, 2nd restarts, new PPAs — high-return reinvestment on the *existing* asset base, **without a capex treadmill** (the core fleet is already built). This is the key contrast with NBIS.
- **Competitive advantage period:** long for the nuclear fleet (the un-buildability is durable to 2032+ on the PTC and effectively permanent on the physics).

## Moat verdict

**A genuine, wide-ish moat on the nuclear fleet — durable scarcity rent.** CEG owns the *physical* scarce asset (existing carbon-free baseload that cannot be newly built on any relevant horizon), earns a structurally wide, PTC-floored margin, and is converting it into contracted hyperscaler annuities. This clears the "persistent excess returns" bar that NBIS does **not** — CEG's ROIC is positive and structurally protected, not a forecast.

**The honest qualifications** (why it's a STARTER, not a layup):
1. The **flagship's upside is already contracted to MSFT** (Crane, undisclosed/likely-fixed price) → caps CEG's own upside there; the marginal *new* PPA is the swing. (C013)
2. **Calpine dilutes carbon-free purity (90%→58%)** and adds leverage (~3.8x) — a real, if mitigated, drag on the "pure" moat story. (C023, C011)
3. The moat secures the **business**; the **equity** re-rate still needs a new PPA + clean deleverage.

## Moat Falsification — the moat is weaker than we think if:

- [ ] A new SMR / new-build / restart wave credibly adds carbon-free baseload faster than expected (erodes scarcity). *Low probability this decade.*
- [ ] Power curves sit at the ~$25 PTC floor for years in CEG's open volume (margin floored but thin; upside gone). (C026)
- [ ] Calpine deleverage stalls and the gas mix re-rates the whole entity toward a merchant-gas multiple (purity + leverage drag dominates). (C011, C023)
- [ ] FERC's final PJM co-location definition turns hostile in a way that reaches front-of-meter PPAs (low prob after the 5-0 order; flagship insulated). (C016)
- [ ] No *new* hyperscaler PPA signs for an extended period → the moat exists but the equity is dead money at ~24x (the "quality trap" path).

## Notes

Per the guardrail — don't call it a moat unless it causes *persistent* excess returns. **CEG clears that bar** (positive structural ROIC on a scarce, un-buildable, PTC-floored asset). What it does *not* yet have is uncapped equity upside, because the first great contract (Crane→MSFT) is locked and Calpine deleverage is unproven.

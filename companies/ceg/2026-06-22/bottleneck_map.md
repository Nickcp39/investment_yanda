# Bottleneck Map — CEG

Last updated: **2026-06-22**

Purpose: identify where scarcity creates pricing power, and answer the LEAD question: **is CEG's existing nuclear fleet a DURABLE scarcity rent, or is the AI-power premium already fully priced and hostage to a single regulatory switch (FERC co-location)?**

---

## TL;DR — the LEAD question, answered

**The rent is durable and real; it is NOT primarily hostage to FERC; the −33% de-rate is mostly multiple normalization (>35x → ~24x fwd) + Calpine digestion — not a thesis break.** The premise's "or" is a false binary: the scarcity is structural *and* the regulatory switch already flipped favorable.

1. **The scarce asset is physical and un-buildable.** ~32 GW of existing carbon-free nuclear baseload, ~95% CF, ~$0 marginal fuel. **No new large US reactor has come on time/budget in a generation; greenfield permit+build ≈ 10–15 years.** The supply curve cannot respond to AI demand → the scarcity rent is **structural**. (C022, C004)

2. **The scariest pre-registered kill — FERC blocking co-location — is empirically DEFUSED.** A **5-0 FERC order (2025-12-18, 193 FERC ¶61,217, Docket EL25-49) directs PJM to ENABLE co-located load** — the opposite of the Nov-2024 Talen/AWS BTM rejection that originally framed this as *the* swing risk. No reversal as of 2026-06-22. (C015)

3. **CEG's flagship sidesteps the fight anyway.** Crane→Microsoft is **front-of-the-meter** (Crane injects to the grid; MSFT matches its PJM load) — not behind-the-meter. So even the residual definitional risk in PJM compliance largely does not touch CEG's marquee deal. (C013)

4. **The legislated downside floor survived OBBBA.** §45U PTC intact: ~$25/MWh gross-receipts floor to 2032. 2027 open nuclear is priced **~$34/MWh** — above the floor. (C019, C026)

5. **What's actually left is a capped flagship — and the balance sheet is no longer the worry it looked like.** Crane's upside is **already sold to MSFT**, so the *equity* re-rate hinges on the *next* premium PPA (the one new deal found, CyrusOne 380 MW, is gas). Calpine leverage, feared at ~3.8x in the seed, is **~2.25x pro-forma on the primary 10-Q** with LS Power's $5B cash in — materially de-risked. (C011r, C013, C030)

> **One-line answer:** the rent is durable and the two scariest kills (FERC, PTC) are resolved favorably; the price paid merely normalized; the live risk is now mainly a flagship whose upside is already spoken for — not the existence of the moat, and decreasingly the balance sheet.

## The "is it priced / FERC-hostage?" question, dissected

| If the worry is… | Verdict for CEG | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Is the AI-power premium fully priced? | **Partly — the froth, not the moat, was cut** | >35x → ~24x is multiple normalization; ~24x vs 16–18x peers = fair, not cheap (C027r, C028r) |
| Is it hostage to the FERC co-location switch? | **No — the switch flipped favorable** | 5-0 enabling order; front-of-meter flagship sidesteps it (C015, C013) |
| Is the downside open-ended (power prices)? | **No — legislatively floored** | §45U ~$25/MWh to 2032; 2027 open ~$34/MWh (C019, C026) |
| Is the balance sheet a thesis risk? | **Less than the seed feared** | ~2.25x pro-forma on the 10-Q; LS Power cash in (C011r) |

**Does CEG own the bottleneck or merely depend on it?** It **owns** it — the scarce input (existing carbon-free baseload) sits on CEG's balance sheet. The key contrast with NBIS (which *depends on* a build-and-energize chain it doesn't own).

## Risk — where the bottleneck is still a vulnerability

1. **The flagship's upside is capped.** Crane→MSFT is contracted at an undisclosed (likely fixed) price → CEG's own upside on its marquee asset is limited. The re-rate depends on the **next** premium PPA, which is unsigned (CyrusOne is gas). (C013, C030)
2. **Power-price compression + a residual FERC definition.** Curves toward the ~$25 floor thin the premium (downside floored, upside gone); the final PJM co-location *definition* is still litigated (a minor industry-BTM risk; CEG's front-of-meter flagship is insulated). (C026, C016)
3. **Calpine execution (now secondary).** Still a forecast to ~2.0x, but the primary 10-Q (~2.25x, LS Power cash in) makes a stall less likely than the seed assumed. (C011r)

**Bottom line:** the bottleneck is a **durable, self-owned scarcity rent** — the LEAD answer is "durable, not FERC-hostage." The −33% cut the froth and the Calpine overhang, not the moat. The honest reason this is a STARTER and not a layup: the *equity* re-rate now leans on an unseen next premium PPA, and at ~24x you are paying fair, not cheap.

## BOTTOM LINE

CEG is a **high-quality, cash-generative merchant generator** whose economics rest on ~22 GW of nuclear running ~95% capacity factor at near-zero marginal cost. The accounting is clean and the franchise beats its own adj-EPS guide repeatedly. The two things that make the financials *interesting* (and the de-rate explicable) are: (1) **2026 guidance is "light"** vs the froth embedded at the $413 high, and (2) **Calpine has temporarily levered the balance sheet to ~3.8x** with a credible-but-unproven deleverage path to ~2.0x by 2027. The downside is **legislatively floored** by the §45U PTC (~$25/MWh to 2032). Reported FCF (~$1.3B FY2025) is modest against a ~$98B cap — the forward FCF story is contracted, PTC-floored hyperscaler PPAs, not the trailing number.

## E. The legislated downside floor — §45U PTC [S7, PRIMARY statute]

- **§45U nuclear production tax credit survived OBBBA (P.L.119-21 §70510) intact:** ~$15/MWh max value, **~$25/MWh gross-receipts floor**, sunset **2032**. Only narrow **FEOC** (foreign entity of concern) rules added — irrelevant to US-owned CEG. (C019)
- **2027 open (un-hedged) nuclear volume is priced ~$34/MWh** — i.e., *above* the ~$25 floor. So the power-price kill is **live but floored**: there is a legislated backstop, and current marks sit above it. (C026)
- This is a rare thing in a merchant generator: a **statutory downside backstop** on the commodity. It is why the bear case compresses *upside* more than it threatens *downside*.

## THE QUESTION (verbatim from step0_plan)

> Is CEG's existing nuclear fleet a **DURABLE scarcity rent** — carbon-free baseload you physically cannot build new, sold to datacenters at premium PPAs with a PTC price floor — **or** is the AI-power premium already fully priced and exposed to a single regulatory switch (FERC behind-the-meter co-location)?

Thesis lens: **"瓶颈如何挣钱 / is the scarcity rent durable."**

---

## THE ANSWER

**The rent is durable and real; it is NOT primarily hostage to FERC. The −34% de-rate (from the $413 high to $274) is mostly multiple normalization (>35x → ~24x fwd) + Calpine digestion — not a thesis break.** The premise's "or" is a false binary: the scarcity is structural *and* the regulatory switch already flipped favorable. What the market actually re-priced was the *froth* on top of a real moat, plus the temporary leverage from Calpine.

Decompose the LEAD into its four sub-claims:

### Sub-claim 1 — "carbon-free baseload you physically cannot build new" → TRUE, HIGH
- No new large US reactor on time/budget in a generation; greenfield permit+build ~10–15 yrs; SMRs not yet commercial-scale; restarts are scarce one-offs. The supply curve cannot respond to AI demand. **Durable.** (C022)

### Sub-claim 2 — "sold to datacenters at premium PPAs" → TRUE in mechanism, but UPSIDE PARTLY CAPPED
- The model works: bespoke 15–20yr PPAs convert merchant volatility into contracted premium-to-wholesale annuities. (C021)
- **But the flagship (Crane→MSFT, ~835 MW) is already contracted at an undisclosed (likely fixed) price** → it caps CEG's *own* upside on that unit. (C013) So the *equity* re-rate depends on the *next* PPA, which is unsigned. This is the single most important nuance.

### Sub-claim 3 — "with a PTC price floor" → TRUE, HIGH (survived OBBBA)
- §45U intact: ~$25/MWh gross-receipts floor to 2032. 2027 open nuclear priced ~$34/MWh (above floor). A legislated downside backstop. (C019, C026)

### Sub-claim 4 — "exposed to a single regulatory switch (FERC BTM)" → FALSE as a *live* risk
- The switch **already flipped favorable**: FERC 5-0 order 2025-12-18 directs PJM to *enable* co-location. (C015) And Crane→MSFT is **front-of-meter**, sidestepping the BTM fight entirely. (C013) Residual: PJM compliance definition still litigated (C016) — a re-open risk for the *industry's* BTM deals, minor for CEG's flagship.

---

## "Is it priced?" — the de-rate, decomposed

The −34% from $413 → $274 is **not** the market pricing in a thesis kill. It is three things, in order:
1. **Multiple normalization** (the largest): >35x fwd at the $413 high → ~24x at $274. The AI-premium got too rich and unwound. (C027)
2. **Calpine digestion:** ~3.8x leverage + purity dilution + integration overhang. (C011, C023)
3. **"Light" 2026 guide:** $11–12 adj EPS reaffirmed — a normalization vs froth, read as a soft signal. (C009)

**None of these three is "the moat broke."** Two of the three *biggest* tail risks (FERC, PTC) were resolved *favorably* over the same window. So the variant view is: **the rent is more secure than the de-rated price implies** — the market cut the froth and the Calpine overhang, but did not (and should not) cut the moat.

## Why this matters for the decision

- The LEAD answer supports a **constructive** read: durable moat + two scariest kills defused + accelerating demand.
- But it does **not** support "back up the truck," for one disciplined reason: the *equity's* re-rate now leans on an **unseen next PPA** (Crane's upside is spoken for) and a **clean Calpine deleverage** — both forecasts. At ~24x (vs 16–18x peers) you are paying a fair, not cheap, price.
- → **STARTER**, scaling on (a) a confirmed *new* hyperscaler PPA, or (b) weakness toward $230–250 (bear multiple, moat intact). See `../memo-v1.md`.

## The honest inversion (what would make this answer wrong)

- FERC's final PJM co-location *definition* comes out hostile in a way that reaches front-of-meter structures (low probability given the 5-0 enabling order, but the definition is genuinely still open). (C016)
- Calpine deleverage stalls + no new PPA signs → dead money at 24x even with the moat intact (the "quality trap at a fair price" outcome).
- Power curves fall toward the ~$25 floor in CEG's open years → upside compresses (downside floored, but the premium thins). (C026)

**Net:** The scarcity rent is durable and the FERC/PTC tails are cut. The premise's binary is false — it's a real moat whose *price* normalized, with the *equity* re-rate now gated by the next PPA and Calpine execution. That is a STARTER, not a layup, and not a pass.

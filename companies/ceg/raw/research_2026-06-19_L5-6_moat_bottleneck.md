## BOTTOM LINE (the moat/bottleneck question, answered)

**The bottleneck is real and durable; it is NOT primarily hostage to FERC; and the −34% de-rate is mostly multiple normalization, not a thesis kill.**

1. **The scarce asset is physical and un-buildable.** CEG owns ~22 GW of existing carbon-free nuclear baseload. **No new large US reactor has come on time/budget in a generation; a greenfield permit+build runs ~10–15 years.** The supply curve physically cannot respond to AI demand on any relevant horizon → the scarcity rent is structural. (C022)

2. **The scariest pre-registered kill — FERC blocking co-location — is empirically DEFUSED.** A **5-0 FERC order on 2025-12-18 (193 FERC ¶61,217, Docket EL25-49) directs PJM to ENABLE co-located load** via three new transmission services — the *opposite* of the Nov-2024 Talen/AWS behind-the-meter rejection that originally framed this as the swing risk. PJM compliance is ongoing (FERC accepted-in-part/rejected-in-part 2026-04-16; the co-location *definition* is still litigated), but the **direction is favorable.** (C015, C016)

3. **CEG's flagship deal sidesteps the fight anyway.** Crane→Microsoft is **front-of-the-meter** (Crane injects to the grid; MSFT matches its PJM load across the market), not behind-the-meter. So even the residual definitional risk in PJM compliance largely **does not touch CEG's marquee transaction.** (C013)

4. **The PTC floor — the legislated downside backstop — survived OBBBA.** §45U nuclear PTC intact: ~$15/MWh max, **~$25/MWh gross-receipts floor**, sunset 2032; only narrow FEOC rules added (irrelevant to US-owned CEG). 2027 open nuclear is priced **~$34/MWh** — above the floor. (C019, C026)

5. **What's actually left is execution + a capped flagship.** Calpine has levered the balance sheet to ~3.8x (deleverage to ~2.0x by 2027 is a *forecast*) and **diluted carbon-free purity 90%→58%**; and Crane's upside is **already sold to MSFT**, so the *equity* re-rate hinges on the *next* AI PPA, not on the moat existing. (C011, C023, C024, C013)

> **One-line answer:** the rent is durable and the two scariest kills (FERC, PTC) are resolved favorably; the price paid for the rent merely normalized (>35x → ~24x). The live risks are *execution* (Calpine deleverage) and the fact that the first great contract is already locked — not the existence of the moat.

## A. The bottleneck — un-buildable carbon-free baseload [S1, S7]

- **The asset:** ~22 GW existing nuclear, ~95% CF, near-zero marginal cost, carbon-free, 24/7 (the exact profile AI datacenters need). (C004, C020, C022)
- **Why it cannot be competed away on a relevant horizon:**
  - No new large US reactor has been delivered on time/budget in a generation (Vogtle 3/4 ran years late and billions over).
  - Greenfield permit + build ≈ 10–15 years; SMRs are not yet at commercial scale/cost.
  - Restarts (like Crane) are scarce one-offs — there are only a handful of recently-retired units to bring back.
- **Therefore:** the supply of carbon-free baseload is effectively fixed against a demand curve (AI/datacenter load) that is accelerating. Classic durable scarcity rent. (C022, C025)

## B. The FERC switch — RESOLVED FAVORABLY [S5, PRIMARY regulatory]

| Date | Event | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Nov 2024 | FERC rejects Talen/AWS behind-the-meter (BTM) co-location amendment | Original framing of "the swing risk" — co-location in regulatory limbo |
| **2025-12-18** | **FERC 5-0 order (193 FERC ¶61,217, Docket EL25-49) directs PJM to ENABLE co-located load** via three new transmission services | **DEFUSES the kill** — unanimous, pro-co-location (C015) |
| 2026-04-16 | FERC accepts-in-part / rejects-in-part PJM compliance filing | Direction favorable; co-location **definition still litigated** → residual risk (C016) |

- **Crucially, the order is a 5-0 (unanimous) directive to *enable*, not block.** This is the single most important fact in the case — it converts the pre-registered "FERC kills it" kill from LIVE to DEFUSED.
- **CEG's exposure is doubly limited** because Crane→MSFT is **front-of-meter** — it does not depend on the BTM co-location structure that is the subject of the litigated definition. (C013)
- **Residual:** the PJM compliance *definition* of co-located load is still being worked out; a hostile final definition is a re-open risk for the *industry's* BTM deals, less so for CEG's front-of-meter flagship. → monitor (see `../monitor.md`).

## C. The PTC floor — survived OBBBA [S7, PRIMARY statute]

- **§45U zero-emission nuclear PTC** survived the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (P.L.119-21 §70510): ~$15/MWh max value, **~$25/MWh gross-receipts floor**, **sunset 2032**. Only narrow **FEOC** rules added — irrelevant to US-owned CEG. (C019)
- This is a **legislated downside backstop** on the commodity — rare for a merchant generator. It caps how bad the power-price kill can get.
- **2027 open nuclear priced ~$34/MWh** > the ~$25 floor → cushion exists; the power-price kill is LIVE but FLOORED, and not currently at the floor. (C026)

## D. Calpine — the genuine live risk (partial trigger) [S4, S11]

- Closed 2026-01-07; ~55 GW combined, largest US producer. (C012)
- **Two real costs:**
  1. **Leverage:** standalone ~0.9x → **post-Calpine ~3.8x trailing** (ESTIMATED). Deleverage to ~2.0x by 2027 via ~$5B asset sales (LS Power $5B/4.4 GW signed 2026-03-18, S11) + paydown — a **forecast**, not de-risked. (C011, C024)
  2. **Purity dilution:** carbon-free share **~90% → ~58%** (adds ~23 GW mostly gas). (C023)
- **Mitigants:** guided >20% FY26 EPS-accretive; dispatchable gas firms intermittent renewables and serves 24/7 datacenter load; LS Power sale already signed. Net: **PARTIAL TRIGGER — mitigated by accretion + deleverage, not de-risked.**
- Requires DOJ/FERC divestitures (LS Power is the first) — execution/regulatory-approval risk, not strategy risk.

## E. The PPAs — how the bottleneck monetizes [S12, S6]

- **Crane→Microsoft (20-yr):** restart on track **2027** (pulled fwd from 2028), ~80% staffed; NRC draft EA/FONSI 2026-06-08 (comment to 2026-07-08); ~$1.6B capex, $1.0B DOE loan. Front-of-meter. **Price undisclosed → likely fixed → caps CEG's own upside on Crane.** (C017, C018, C013)
- **Meta→Clinton (1,092 MW):** clean attributes from June 2027. (MED)
- **Interconnection nuance:** Eddystone capacity rights shift to Crane; full PJM deliverability upgrades ~2030 → residual queue/timing risk. (qualitative)
- **The swing variable:** because Crane is already contracted, the **marginal *new* hyperscaler PPA** (unsigned today) is what re-rates the equity. No confirmed new post-Meta PPA exists yet — the single biggest catalyst to monitor.

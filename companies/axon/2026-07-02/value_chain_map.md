## Value-capture logic

- **Upstream (inputs):** cameras, batteries, silicon, contract manufacturing. Some component-cost and tariff exposure on the hardware, but hardware is the *wedge*, not the profit engine — margin sits in software.
- **AXON node (the choke point):** once an agency's evidence, redaction, records, and AI-drafting workflows run on Evidence.com/Axon Cloud, Axon owns the *system of record* for the agency's most sensitive, legally-critical data. That is the scarce position — not the TASER itself (which is a near-monopoly but a smaller pool), but the **data platform the whole agency depends on to do its job and defend itself in court.**
- **Downstream (the buyer):** highly concentrated but sticky customer — a police department. Powerful on price at RFP time, but switching a live evidence platform mid-contract is organizationally near-impossible (training, chain-of-custody continuity, litigation risk). This is why NRR is 125% and RPO is $14.3B.
- **The annuity (the prize):** multi-year OSP/AI-Era bundles convert one-time hardware into subscription; the install base compounds seats + software attach + AI + counter-drone, at ~60% gross margin.

## Pricing power evidence

- Software & Services ~$1.2B at high-50s%/~60%+ gross margin; NRR 125%; ARR +35%.
- AI product revenue +700% YoY, counter-drone +300% — new high-margin layers attach onto the same locked-in base without a new sales motion.
- $14.3B future contracted bookings (+44%) = ~5x annual revenue pre-sold — extraordinary revenue visibility and a sign customers commit years ahead.

## Where the chain is weakest

- **Hardware (TASER + Sensors, ~47% of rev):** lower gross margin, some component/tariff exposure, and — for TASER — recurring product-liability litigation as a managed cost of being a less-lethal weapons maker.
- **Single-buyer concentration:** the entire chain rests on government budgets and procurement, exposing Axon to fiscal austerity, "defund" political cycles, and — increasingly — **AI-ethics/transparency scrutiny** (ACLU/EFF on Draft One; 2022 ethics-board resignation on Taser-drones) that could slow adoption or invite regulation of the highest-margin AI layer.

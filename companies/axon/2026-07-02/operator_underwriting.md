## Founder & Core Team Life-Arc — Rick Smith

### Formation (unchooseable -> value formation; highest weight)

- **Family & home:** Born Santa Monica, CA; family moved to Scottsdale, AZ when he was a teenager. Knew from age 16 he wanted to be an entrepreneur. Self-described "tall and skinny" high-school nose guard who walked on to college football ("didn't go well") — an early, formative pattern of trying hard things and being humbled. **His father "inspired confidence and daring"** and the family later **bet everything they had** on the company, nearly reaching insolvency. [AXON-RICK-SMITH-BIO, AXON-FOUNDERS-DAY]
- **The crucible event (the unchooseable one):** shortly after his 1991 Harvard graduation, **two of his childhood friends, Todd Bogers and Cory Holmes, were shot and killed in a road-rage incident in Scottsdale.** That loss — not a market-sizing exercise — is the origin of the mission: build a less-lethal weapon so conflicts don't end in death. He founded TASER in a **Tucson garage in 1993** with his brother Thomas. [AXON-FOUNDERS-DAY]
- **Schooling:** Harvard, **cum laude in three years** (1991); MBA finance UChicago Booth; MBA international finance Univ. of Leuven, Belgium. -> Unusual combination: elite-credentialed AND mission-obsessed builder. The 3-year Harvard + double-MBA signals raw drive; the garage + family-insolvency signals he is not a credentialed courtier who optimized for a safe seat — he risked ruin on a conviction.
- **-> Likely formed:** a mission-first, high-agency, risk-tolerant builder's temperament; comfort with controversy in service of a mission (less-lethal weapons, then AI); a long-horizon, all-or-nothing orientation (later literalized in the 10-year milestone option award where he took *no salary*).

### Adult track (compressed)

- **1993-2001:** built TASER through near-bankruptcy; survived on family capital; **2001 IPO** rescued the balance sheet. This is the hardship crucible the book prizes — he was nearly wiped out and did not fold.
- **2008-2017:** pivoted from a single-product weapons maker to **body cameras + Evidence.com** — the strategic masterstroke that turned a hardware company into a recurring-revenue software platform. Renamed TASER International -> **Axon Enterprise (2017)** to signal the platform identity.
- **2018-present:** launched the **Elon-style CEO Performance Award** (relinquished salary; options vest only on $1B market-cap-increment milestones + operational gates) and the **employee XSPP** (employees committed their own money on the same milestones) — aligning the whole company to long-run value creation. Extended into **AI (Draft One), counter-drone (Dedrone), robotics, Fusus real-time crime centers, federal + international.** Nine consecutive quarters of 30%+ growth.
- **Crucible behavior:** repeatedly bet the company on a platform shift (weapons -> cameras -> cloud -> AI) ahead of consensus, and repeatedly delivered. This is builder conduct matching the formation.

### Dark-arc (required)

- **Mission-vs-ethics tension — the sharpest one:** the same mission-first temperament that built the company also produced the **2022 Taser-on-drones proposal that triggered the resignation of most of Axon's own AI Ethics Board**, and **Draft One (AI police reports) criticized by ACLU/EFF as "designed to defy transparency/audits."** A founder this convinced of his mission can under-weight external checks. For a company whose single buyer is government and whose social license is contested, this is a genuine risk (feeds M5, K-C).
- **SBC / value-transfer:** the milestone option culture is aligning *and* dilutive — **$634M of SBC in FY2025** is a real transfer from shareholders to the CEO/employees, and it is *why* GAAP operating income is negative. A builder who is hyper-incentivized to grow market cap can tolerate more dilution and richer valuations than an outside shareholder should. Watch share-count creep.
- **Combined Chair+CEO + key-man:** Smith is founder, CEO, and Chairman; the franchise is his vision. Governance is otherwise clean (one-share-one-vote), and he is relatively young (~57), so succession is not urgent — but the concentration of vision/control in one person is the classic founder-firm dependency.

### Reliability axis

Documented facts (garage 1993, near-insolvency, 2001 IPO, the weapons->cloud->AI pivots, 33-year in-seat delivery, no-salary milestone award) > any polished narrative. The track record is verifiable and strongly builder-coded. The formative story (friends killed -> less-lethal mission) is corroborated by the company's own Founder's-Day record and multiple profiles.

## Verdict -> durability ceiling

- **Team life-arc grade: 4.5/5** — this is close to the ideal profile for this book: a **hardship-formed, mission-obsessed, technically-instinctive builder** who nearly went bankrupt on a conviction, repeatedly bet the company on the right platform shift, aligned himself and his employees to long-run value with a no-salary milestone award, and runs clean one-share-one-vote governance. Capped below 5 by (a) the AI-ethics/candor dark-arc (Taser-drones, Draft One transparency), (b) the SBC value-transfer/dilution, and (c) combined Chair+CEO key-man concentration.
- **Ceiling effect:** the operator *raises* the durability ceiling (unlike a courtier/finance-operator, Smith is exactly the builder the book favors) — this supports a Core-tier ceiling for the *business* on quality/operator grounds. The binding constraint is therefore NOT the operator; it is PRICE.
- Feeds Duan IC lens ("管理层是否靠谱"): **靠谱 (reliable) and, better, a true builder — 本分-adjacent mission focus.** Watch the AI-ethics social license and the SBC dilution; do not let the operator's quality talk you into paying any price.

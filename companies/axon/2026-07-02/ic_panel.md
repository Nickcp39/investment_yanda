## 1. Duan Yongping (段永平) — chief judge (lens)

- **本分 / circle of competence:** Is this a business I understand and would own for 10 years? *Yes — a near-monopoly public-safety platform with a system-of-record SaaS lock-in (125% NRR), a founder who built it from a personal-loss mission, and a long runway is comprehensible and durable.*
- **管理层是否靠谱:** Rick Smith is a **rare true builder for this book** — hardship-formed (friends killed -> less-lethal mission), nearly bankrupt on conviction, repeatedly bet the company on the right pivot (weapons->cloud->AI), no-salary milestone alignment, clean one-share-one-vote. 靠谱, and better: 本分-adjacent. Watch the AI-ethics social license and the SBC dilution.
- **价格 / "好生意 + 好价格":** This is where it strains. 段's discipline is to *wait for the fat pitch*. At ~87x non-GAAP / ~70x EV/EBITDA / 16.5x sales **after a +46% 8-session melt-up**, with a base 5y IRR of only ~+2%, **this is a good business (and a good operator) at a stretched price**. Verdict: **WATCH; 看着,等回调到 ~$450 再起步** (want it on a pullback — unlike a courtier-run name, this is one to keep on the list).

## 2. Warren Buffett (lens)

- "Wonderful company at a fair price." AXON is wonderful (moat, NRR, founder). But "**price is what you pay, value is what you get**" — at ~70x EV/adj-EBITDA the margin of safety is absent, and the thin GAAP earnings/FCF would make him uneasy about the SBC. Verdict: **admire, wait.** HOLD existing; no new money at $597.

## 3. Charlie Munger (lens)

- "A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price." AXON is a great business — but **"no matter how wonderful, it's not worth an infinite price,"** and Munger would flag **incentive-caused bias** in a $634M milestone-SBC comp model that masks GAAP profit. Verdict: **great business + great founder, price too rich — invert and wait for the melt-up to unwind.**

## 4. Howard Marks (lens)

- **Where are we in the cycle?** A +46% run in 8 sessions to a fresh local high is late-stage enthusiasm, not a base. "**The most dangerous thing is paying a price that assumes everything goes right.**" AXON's price requires sustained 30% growth AND a permanent premium multiple. Marks: **second-level thinking says the good news (and the momentum) is in the price** -> near-term asymmetry is poor. Verdict: **WATCH; the risk is a multiple/melt-up reversal.**

## 5. Seth Klarman (lens)

- **Margin of safety is the cardinal rule.** A ~+2% base-case 5y IRR at 16.5x sales, with thin FCF, is thin-to-no margin of safety. Klarman would not chase the spike regardless of quality. Verdict: **REJECT new money until price < ~$450-470;** business quality does not substitute for a margin of safety.

# AXON Business Model (M2 / M3 input)

As-of: 2026-07-02 | Pipeline: lean-6module-v1.1

## One sentence

Axon sells the hardware that individual police officers carry (TASER energy weapons + body cameras) as the wedge, then locks the agency into the Evidence.com / Axon Cloud SaaS and AI platform that stores, manages, redacts, and now auto-drafts the evidence — earning a sticky, ~60%-gross-margin, 125%-net-retention recurring-revenue annuity off a near-monopoly install base of US law enforcement, and extending it to federal, international, enterprise, and counter-drone.

## The mechanism (why the flywheel works)

1. **Wedge:** an agency buys TASERs and/or body cameras (hardware, often bundled).
2. **Lock-in:** the footage/data must live somewhere -> Evidence.com. Once an agency's chain-of-custody, redaction workflow, records, and (now) AI report-drafting run on Axon Cloud, migrating is a multi-year, high-risk project. Switching cost is organizational, not just contractual.
3. **Expand:** Axon sells up the stack — more seats, Records, Real-Time Operations, Draft One AI, Fusus, drones, Dedrone — inside the same agency. **NRR 125%** = the average customer spends 25% more each year without a single new logo.
4. **Bundle/annuity:** multi-year "Officer Safety Plan" (OSP) bundles convert one-time hardware into subscription; **$14.3B future contracted bookings (RPO, +44%)** is ~5x annual revenue of pre-sold, high-visibility demand.

## Demand drivers (the runway)

1. **US state/local law enforcement** — the core install base; still expanding seats + software attach.
2. **Federal** — DoD, DHS, federal agencies; a large, under-penetrated TAM.
3. **International (~20% of Q1 rev, >2x YoY)** — EMEA/APAC agencies; early innings.
4. **AI (Draft One + AI Era Plan)** — AI product revenue +700% YoY; the highest-margin, highest-attach layer.
5. **Counter-drone / robotics (Dedrone)** — +300% rev; a genuinely new category riding drone-threat proliferation.
6. **Enterprise** — corporate security, retail, healthcare using the same platform.

## 10-year test (would you own it for a decade?)

- **For the business: yes, with an eye on regulation.** A near-monopoly public-safety platform with a recurring, high-retention SaaS annuity, a founder-CEO, 30%+ compounding, and a multi-vector runway is exactly the kind of durable franchise this book wants — and it *de-correlates from the AI-capex/liquidity factor* (demand = government budgets).
- **At this price: not without a pullback.** ~87x non-GAAP trailing / ~70x EV/adj-EBITDA / ~16.5x sales, after a +46% 8-session melt-up, prices in years of flawless 30% growth + a permanent premium multiple. Base 5y IRR barely clears (see valuation.md).

# TER — Batch Thesis Answer (AI-robotics 4-leaders) — as_of 2026-07-10

> Batch §1 question: is TER a "**demand already exploded + hard bottleneck + already making money**" embodied-AI/robotics leader — or a "humanoid-someday" option → WATCH?
> **Answer: NEITHER a robotics leader NOR making money in robotics. TER is a cyclical semiconductor-test (ATE) duopoly at a probable earnings peak. → WATCH.**

## (a) Robotics revenue % (dated, sourced)
- **Q1 2026: $91M / $1,282.5M = 7.1%** [S001].
- **FY2025: ~$308M / $3,190M = ~9.6%** (robotics derived from disclosed quarters 69/75/75/89 [S005]; O4).
- The other **~86.6%** is **Semiconductor Test** (Q1'26 $1,111M) — an AI-driven but **cyclical** ATE business; Product Test ~6%.
- Of the tiny robotics slice, only **~15% is "AI-related"** (~$14M) [S002].
- **Robotics is ≤10% of the company and shrank Y/Y in 2025** (FY2024 $365M → FY2025 ~$308M).

## (b) Robotics demand: REAL-NOW cash vs ANTICIPATION
- **Neither exploding nor profitable.** Robotics has been a **~$300–400M, flat-to-declining** business for ~5 years amid industrial-automation weakness [S005][S006].
- **It loses money**: Q1'25 operating loss **−$22M**; segment breakeven **~$365M** vs FY2025 revenue **~$308M** → **below breakeven** [S005].
- Recent positive: **4 consecutive quarters of sequential growth**, Q1'26 +32% Y/Y — but off a depressed base; management "cautious about predicting future growth" [S002].
- The embodied/physical-AI upside = **one NVIDIA GTC demo (with "Generalist")** — a **lead, not cash** [S002][S004].
- **Verdict: ANTICIPATION, and not even that in cash terms — it is a loss-making, stagnant segment with an unproven option attached.** The *real* explosive demand is in **semiconductor test** (AI capex), which is cyclical.

## (c) Capital-cycle lens (mandatory; judged on the ROBOTICS segment)
- **applicable**: partial · **stage**: robotics = early / trough-exiting (industrial-automation downturn 2023–25, recovering off a low base); dominant semi-test = late / possible-peak.
- **supply_barrier**: robotics = **low-med** (cobot/AMR commoditizing; Fanuc/ABB/Doosan/Techman/JAKA; UR edge eroding); semi-test = **high** (ATE duopoly).
- **read**: on robotics, supply can respond, there is no hard bottleneck, and the segment is below breakeven → an **option/concept**, not a locked-in bottleneck; we do **not** know if the recovery is a true demand inflection or just cyclical mean-reversion. On the dominant semi-test driver, visible ~70% AI-tied demand at ATH margins is consensus, and the **2026-07 HBM-oversupply selloff + capacity-expansion slowdown reports** are classic late-cycle supply signals.
- **caution**: **PEAK** (semi-test, the value driver) → compress verdict toward WATCH/no-chase. Robotics = **TROUGH-WATCHLIST** only (monitor for a genuine profitable inflection; do not add size — low barrier, loss-making, timing unknowable).

## (d) Real bottleneck vs concept
- **Semi Test = a REAL bottleneck** (falsifiable: leading-edge AI chips must be tested; only TER + Advantest supply the testers; 58–61% GM, qualification switching costs) — **but cyclical.**
- **Robotics = CONCEPT** (no forcing function to buy Teradyne; substitutes; low switching cost; loss-making).
- So TER *does* own a hard chokepoint — just **not the robotics one.** It is semiconductor test on an AI **capex** cycle.

## (e) Split: robotics option value vs core-business value — what is today's price buying?
Of ~$56.29B market cap at $359.60:
- **~95%+ = the cyclical ATE (Semi Test) franchise** at a peak multiple (~40–43× 2026E, ~102× FY2025).
- **~3–5% (generous) = the loss-making robotics "embodied-AI option"** (~$308M revenue × a charitable 5–8× sales ≈ $1.5–2.5B).
- **Today's price is buying a cyclical AI-semiconductor-test winner at peak earnings — NOT an embodied-AI robotics leader.** The robotics story that put TER on this list is a small, currently-negative call option.

## Bottom line for the batch
TER is the **closest** of the 4 to "real deployed robotics revenue" — and it still is **not** a robotics bottleneck making money. This confirms the batch's prior expectation: **no buyable "robotics bottleneck already making money" here → WATCH.** The one genuinely great asset (ATE duopoly) is mispriced for a cyclical peak.

**WATCH → STARTER trigger**: a pullback to **~$250–280** (forward multiple ~28–32× on non-peak earnings) AND continued AI-test demand; a real cycle washout to **~$180–220** opens the add zone.

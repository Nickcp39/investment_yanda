# TER Bottleneck Map — as_of 2026-07-10

**Question**: is there a falsifiable, hard bottleneck TER controls — like NVDA in AI compute?

## Semi Test (ATE): YES — a real bottleneck, but a CYCLICAL one
- **Falsifiable claim**: every leading-edge AI chip must be tested; TER + Advantest are the only two credible suppliers of the testers; you cannot ship untested silicon at volume.
- **Pricing power test (pass)**: 58–61% gross margins sustained across the cycle; duopoly discipline; test content rises with AI complexity.
- **Switching-cost test (pass)**: testers are qualified into production; program/correlation lock-in; multi-quarter re-qualification.
- **But the bottleneck gates a CYCLICAL flow**: revenue is hostage to semiconductor capex (2021 $3.7B → 2023 $2.7B). The bottleneck is real; the *volume through it* is not steady.
- **Capital-cycle read**: the demand side (~70% AI-tied, ATH margins) is the *visible/consensus* surge; the 2026-07 memory-oversupply selloff is the first supply-side signal that flow may be peaking. Bottleneck ≠ non-cyclical.

## Robotics: NO — concept, not bottleneck
- **Falsifiable claim fails**: nothing forces a factory to buy a *Teradyne* cobot/AMR; many substitutes; low switching cost.
- **Pricing power test (fail)**: sub-scale, loss-making, below breakeven — the opposite of pricing power.
- **Physical-AI "bottleneck"**: a narrative (NVIDIA GTC demo), not a controlled chokepoint. If humanoid/embodied AI scales, TER is *a* participant, not the toll booth.

## Verdict
- **One real bottleneck (ATE), cyclical.** **One concept (robotics), no bottleneck, currently loss-making.**
- This is the crux of the batch thesis: TER has a genuine chokepoint, but it is **semiconductor test on an AI *capex* cycle**, not **embodied-AI robotics**. The "robotics leader with a hard bottleneck already making money" does **not** exist here.

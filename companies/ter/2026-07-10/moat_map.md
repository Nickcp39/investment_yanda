# TER Moat Map — as_of 2026-07-10

## Semiconductor Test — MODERATE-TO-WIDE but CYCLICAL
| Moat source | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Duopoly structure** | High | TER + Advantest dominate SoC/memory ATE; rational two-player market |
| **Switching / qualification cost** | High | Testers qualified into a customer's production flow; test programs + correlation data are sticky; re-qualifying a competitor is costly/slow |
| **Know-how / R&D scale** | Med-High | Decades of test IP; leading-edge AI-silicon test is hard; ~50%-of-revenue OpEx model funds R&D |
| **Test-intensity tailwind** | Structural | AI silicon (ASICs, HBM, networking) needs more test per device — raises content even at flat units |
| **Cyclicality (anti-moat)** | — | Demand tracks semi capex; ~30% peak-to-trough revenue swings; a moat around *cyclical* profits |

→ **A genuine, durable oligopoly bottleneck — but on a cyclical revenue stream.** The moat protects share and margin *through* the cycle; it does not remove the cycle.

## Robotics — NARROW / ERODING
| Moat source | Strength | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| UR ease-of-use + UR+ ecosystem | Med (eroding) | UR pioneered accessible cobots; ecosystem of certified peripherals — but rivals have closed the gap |
| Brand / installed base | Med | UR is a category leader by units, but share/pricing pressured |
| Scale economics | **Low/negative** | Sub-scale: ~$308M < ~$365M breakeven → **loss-making** |
| Switching cost | Low | Cobots/AMRs are increasingly interchangeable; integrators multi-source |

→ **Not a hard bottleneck.** A real product with a fading first-mover edge in a commoditizing market. The "physical-AI" upside is optionality, not a moat today.

## Net
The moat that matters is **ATE**. It is real and durable, but it is a **cyclical** moat — which is why the sizing ceiling is capped below Core and why entry price/cycle-position dominate the decision. Robotics adds **no moat**, only an option.

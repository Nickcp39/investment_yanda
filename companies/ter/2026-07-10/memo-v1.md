## The one line
Teradyne is a high-quality but **cyclical semiconductor-test (ATE) duopoly at a probable earnings peak**, with a small, **loss-making** robotics arm. At **$359.60** there is no margin of safety and the capital-cycle points to late/peak. **New money: WATCH (0%). Existing: HOLD.**

## Why the batch looked at TER, and why it doesn't hold
The AI-robotics batch picked TER as "the only one with real deployed robotics revenue" (Universal Robots + MiR). That is technically true but **immaterial to the investment case**: robotics is **7.1% of Q1'26 revenue, loss-making, and below its own breakeven**. The company's value engine is **semiconductor test** (86.6% of revenue), riding an **AI-capex cycle** — a different animal from embodied-AI robotics.

## What's genuinely good
- **ATE duopoly** (TER + Advantest): real switching costs, 58–61% gross margin, structural test-intensity tailwind from AI silicon.
- **Fortress balance sheet**: near net-cash, $0 short-term debt, profitable even in the 2023 trough → no bankruptcy path.
- **Disciplined capital return**: $785M in FY2025 (174% of FCF); buyback raised to up to $1B.

## Why it's still WATCH
1. **Price on peak earnings**: ~40–43× 2026E, ~102× FY2025, ~65×+ normalized. Even the structural-AI **bull case is only +2.3% 5y IRR**; base −15%, bear −26%. **Negative asymmetry.**
2. **Capital-cycle PEAK** on the semi-test driver: ~70% AI-tied revenue at ATH margins is the *visible consensus*; the **2026-07 memory-oversupply selloff (−29% in a week)** + capacity-slowdown reports are late-cycle supply signals.
3. **Robotics thesis fails**: loss-making, stagnant, an unproven physical-AI option — it cannot underwrite the price.
4. **Completeness ~58%** independently caps the verdict at WATCH.

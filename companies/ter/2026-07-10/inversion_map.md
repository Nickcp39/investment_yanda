# TER Inversion Map — as_of 2026-07-10

**Prompt**: how does new money at $359.60 lose, and can the thesis be permanently broken?

## Failure paths
1. **The AI-test surge is a pull-forward peak (base/most-likely case).** 2026 run-rate (~$5B) reflects a bunched AI-capex build; revenue mean-reverts toward a higher-but-lower mid-cycle. At ~40–43× peak earnings, the multiple *and* the E both fall → the −55% base scenario. **This is a de-rate, not a bankruptcy.**
2. **Memory/HBM oversupply downturn (the 2026-07 fear realized).** Reports of chipmakers slowing capacity expansion + HBM oversupply → test-capacity orders slow. Bear −78%. Survivable (net cash) but painful.
3. **Robotics option → zero.** If robotics stays below breakeven / losses widen, the ~3–5% of market cap ascribed to the embodied-AI option is impaired. Small in isolation, but it removes the batch's entire reason to own TER.
4. **Duopoly erosion (the only structural break).** Advantest or a new entrant takes durable SoC/memory-test share → the moat itself cracks. Low-probability but the only path from "cyclical de-rate" to "permanent impairment." **This is the escalate-to-veto watch.**
5. **Multiple compression alone.** Even with fundamentals intact, a semi-sector sentiment reset (already underway, −29% in a week) can halve the multiple from peak levels.

## What would make this permanent (kill) vs temporary (cycle)
- **Temporary (hold/accumulate on weakness)**: cyclical revenue dip, margin lumpiness, robotics losses — all recover with the cycle; balance sheet survives (net cash, $0 short-term debt).
- **Permanent (exit)**: structural ATE share loss to Advantest/entrant; or a technology shift that reduces test intensity per chip. No evidence of either at as_of.

## Balance-sheet survival check
Near net-cash, equity ~$3.14B, $0 short-term debt, FCF-positive even in troughs (2023 was still profitable). **No bankruptcy path.** Downside is de-rate/opportunity cost, not zero.

## Inversion conclusion
The dominant risk is **cyclical + valuation**, not existential. That argues **WATCH now, accumulate on a cycle washout** — NOT reject. The one veto-eligible risk (duopoly erosion) is monitored, not active. No hard veto fires; M5 = −1 (size/no-chase input), consistent with the methodology's "don't over-veto a cleanly-financed franchise" lesson.

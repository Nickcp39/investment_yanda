# TER Business Model — as_of 2026-07-10

## One-sentence business
Teradyne sells **automated test equipment (ATE)** that chipmakers and electronics OEMs must use to test semiconductors and systems, plus a much smaller **industrial robotics** arm (Universal Robots cobots + MiR autonomous mobile robots).

## Revenue engines (Q1 2026 mix [S001])
| Segment | Q1'26 rev | % | What it is | Character |
|---|---:|---:|---|---|
| **Semiconductor Test** | $1,111M | **86.6%** | SoC + memory ATE; testers chipmakers buy to validate every die | **Cyclical duopoly** (TER + Advantest); the value engine |
| **Product Test** | $80M | 6.2% | System-level / defense-&-aerospace / production-board test | Steadier, small |
| **Robotics** | $91M | **7.1%** | UR cobots + MiR AMRs for factories/warehouses | **Loss-making option**; low-med moat |

FY2025 revenue $3,190M; robotics ~$308M (~9.6% on a full-year basis). Either way, **robotics ≤ ~10% of the company and is below its own breakeven.**

## How it actually makes money
- **Semi Test**: sells testers (capital equipment) + recurring service/consumables. Demand tracks semiconductor **capex + device complexity**. Today's surge = AI silicon (accelerator ASICs, HBM, networking) needs far more test intensity → ~70% of revenue is AI-tied. **Cyclical**: revenue swung $3.70B (2021) → $2.68B (2023) → ~$5B run-rate (2026).
- **Robotics**: sells cobots/AMRs through distributors/integrators. Structurally exposed to the **industrial-automation cycle** (weak 2023–25). Sub-scale: ~$308M vs ~$365M breakeven → **operating losses** (Q1'25 −$22M).

## 10-year test (would I want to own the whole thing for 10 years?)
- **Semi Test**: YES on franchise quality (duopoly, rising test intensity per AI chip, high switching costs) — BUT you must **underwrite the cycle**, not the peak. Owning through troughs is the price of admission.
- **Robotics**: **NOT yet** — 5 years of stagnation/losses; the physical-AI upside is a real but unproven option, not a compounder today.
- **Blended**: a **good cyclical franchise with a call option attached**, not an exceptional secular compounder.

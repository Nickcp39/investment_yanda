## ROIC–WACC Lens

- **Current ROIC: negative** — capex ~7× revenue, FCF ≈ −$3.7B; owner earnings negative (C005, C010).
- Estimated WACC: high (capital-intensive, controlled co., early-stage) — call it low-to-mid teens.
- **Spread: negative today.** Excess returns are a *forecast* contingent on utilization scaling, not a current fact.
- Reinvestment runway: very large (demand >> supply), but each dollar is low/negative-return until active power scales.
- Competitive advantage period: short-to-medium — the "edge" is being early + funded into a power-constrained build, which is time-limited.

## Moat verdict

**Not a wide-moat compounder today.** The advantages are **relative, cost/speed-based** (owned capacity, cheap power, NVIDIA access, full-stack) — real and monetizable *now* (sold out, pricing power) but **not yet structural**. The current "moat" is largely *being early and well-capitalized into a power-constrained build-out*, which erodes as the bottleneck eases and hyperscaler silicon scales. It could harden **if** AI Studio software-attach monetizes (raises switching costs) and utilization stays >75%.

## Moat Falsification — the moat is weaker than we think if:

- [ ] Self-service/non-anchor book stays small → demand stays 2-whale concentrated (buyer power, no breadth).
- [ ] Realized revenue/MW or GPU-hour price falls through the GB300→Rubin transition (commoditization).
- [ ] AI Studio never becomes a material, sticky, higher-margin revenue share (no switching-cost build).
- [ ] Capex/revenue stays ~7× → "moat" is just a capital treadmill, not excess returns.

## Notes

Per the guardrail: don't call it a moat unless it causes *persistent* excess returns. NBIS doesn't clear that bar yet (ROIC negative). What it has is a strong *competitive position* in a hot, capital-hungry layer — which is not the same thing. See `bottleneck_map.md` (bottleneck ≠ moat) and `inversion_map.md`.

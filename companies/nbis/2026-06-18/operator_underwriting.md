## Temperament Checklist

- **Candor?** Reasonably — discloses the bottleneck honestly, gives staged power definitions. **But** withholds active-power MW, the most decision-relevant number (C020).
- **Incentives — per-share value or empire?** ⚠️ **Watch.** Comp is **share-price-driven** (premium options at $40/$100; a 6M-option grant vested on a 2025-09-10 share-price condition) with **no disclosed ROIC/capex-efficiency hurdle** (C036). In a capital-intensity story, rewarding scale/stock over capital efficiency is a structural misalignment.
- **Acts when wrong?** Limited history (company is ~18 months old in current form). The Yandex track record shows decisiveness.
- **Culture?** Engineering/builder-led, but still a **conglomerate** (autonomous driving, edtech, data labeling) — attention split; TripleTen is a non-core orphan with a rev-rec material weakness.
- **Trust with 10 years of retained earnings?** **Qualified.** Strong operator, contract-anchored discipline, smart funding — but founder super-voting control + share-price-only incentives + conglomerate sprawl mean this is a "trust but verify, quarterly" operator, not a clean Buffett owner-operator.

## Governance flags

- **Founder voting control (~52–55%) far above economic (~11–13%)** — minority holders have little say. (C035)
- **Russian legacy:** Volozh removed from EU sanctions (2024-03-13); Russia assets sold (2024-07-15). Residual indemnities/contingent liabilities **not confirmed from primary text** — open diligence item. (C037)
- **Comp not tied to capital efficiency** — the one incentive you'd most want aligned in a $20B+/yr-capex business.

## Guardrail check

This is evidence-based (filings, comp design, capital-allocation actions, transcripts), not vibes. The net: **above-average operator and funding discipline, below-average incentive alignment and governance for a minority holder.**

### Per-person life-arc

**Arkady Volozh**

*Formation (unchooseable → value formation; weight this most):*
- **Family & home:** b. 1964 in Guryev (now Atyrau), an oil town on the Soviet periphery; **Russian-Jewish** family — a minority that in the USSR meant real ceilings/quotas, i.e. an outsider from birth. Father an **oil geologist**, mother a **music teacher** → a technical-and-cultural household, not a politically connected one.
- **Place & era:** grew up Soviet, far from the Moscow establishment, coming of age exactly as the USSR decayed and collapsed — a cohort that learned to build *around* failing institutions.
- **Schooling:** a child **math prodigy** → elite selective **Republican School of Physics & Mathematics, Almaty**, where as a teen he formed his ~30-year bond with **Ilya Segalovich** (a fellow prodigy there). Engineering identity *and* his defining partnership were both set before ~18.
- **University:** applied mathematics, **Gubkin State University of Oil & Gas**, Moscow (1986) — applied, not abstract; an oil-and-gas institute echoing his father's field.
- **First work:** a **state pipeline research institute**, then late-perestroika commerce — a **cooperative importing PCs (from Austria), even bartering computers for farm goods**; by his own telling he left in 1989 "with two computers as his stock portfolio" and "felt like a millionaire." → **CompTek (1989)** (computer sales → telecom/networking), **Arkadia (1990)** (search software).
- → **Likely formed** (cautious, not determinism): **engineering/math-first** worldview (technical father + math school + applied-math degree); **outsider pragmatism** (Jewish, peripheral, never establishment → a system-*navigator*, not a system-*belonger*); **building-from-collapse resilience** (first "portfolio" = two bartered PCs in a dying USSR); **durable partnership** (the teenage Segalovich bond).

*Adult track (does conduct match the formed values?):* Yandex 1997 → CEO 2000 → "Russia's Google," IPO 2011 → **2022 EU-sanctioned, steps down** → sanctions lifted 2024-03 → Russian assets force-sold 2024-07 (C037) → **resumes CEO of Nebius 2024**, rebuilt from the non-Russian remnant; renounces Russian citizenship Feb 2026 (Tel Aviv). Crucibles: **Segalovich's death (2013)**; the **2022–24 dismantling**. Motivation: returned at ~60, legacy/mission-driven, not cash-out. Skin: LASTAR super-voting (~52–55% vote on ~11–13% economic; C035). **Reading via formation:** rebuilding value out of a collapsed system at 60 *rhymes with* his 1989–93 origin — high consistency. The much-cited "erased Russia from his bio / Israeli businessman" (Meduza/Kursiv 2023) looks less like a sudden pivot than a **lifelong outsider** who was never fully "of" the Soviet/Russian establishment — the unchooseable formation explains the chooseable adult narrative.

*Departure from formation (flag):* the outsider-engineer nonetheless ran, for two decades, a strategically vital company **entangled with the Kremlin** (state pressure on Yandex search/news). Math-purist → establishment-adjacent operator: a real departure, and the seed of the dark-arc.

*Dark-arc:* super-voting control over minorities; two decades operating inside/around the Putin-era state ("Putin's Oligarchs," Coda); self-curated origin narrative (discount the spin, keep the facts); key-man concentration.

## PART 1 — OPERATOR / CAPITAL-ALLOCATION UNDERWRITING

### 1. Arkady Volozh — operator, sanctions, control

**Background & track record.** Co-founded Yandex (search tech traces to 1993, company 1997), CEO 2000–2022; built "Russia's Google" into a search-dominant (~62-72% Russian share) internet conglomerate spanning ride-hailing, e-commerce, media, cloud, and the Alice assistant. Capital-allocation moves of note: merged Yandex.Taxi with Uber's Russia/CIS ops (2017, ~$3.8bn JV), then bought out Uber's stakes in self-driving/foodtech/grocery (~$1bn, 2021); bolt-ons KinoPoisk, Auto.ru, Foodfox. Reputation is that of a strong builder-operator. He resumed the CEO/Executive Director role at the renamed entity in **August 2024**. [PRIMARY] nebius.com/board-of-directors/arkady-volozh (accessed 2026-06-18); [SECONDARY] Bloomberg 2025-01-10; en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yandex.
- No primary source quantifies his personal IRR; the track-record read is qualitative.

**EU sanctions — added then removed.** Listed **3 June 2022** (EU 6th package) as Yandex founder/CEO "supporting… the Government of the Russian Federation"; he resigned from Yandex within days. **Delisted by Council decision dated 12 March 2024, published in the EU Official Journal 13 March 2024** (one of three names removed that week). The official document **did not state an explicit justification**; context: he publicly called the invasion "barbaric" (Aug 2023), petitioned the General Court, and Reuters-sourced reporting says the legal case weakened given his plan to cut ties with Russia-based Yandex. [PRIMARY—citation] OJ L_202400849, 2024-03-13 (operative text not directly extractable); [SECONDARY] The Moscow Times 2024-03-13; The Register 2022-06-06. Treat the delisting as annulment-driven/political, **not** a written conditional release.

**Ownership & voting control (as of Mar 31, 2026, per 20-F FY2025).** Control runs through **super-voting Class B (10 votes/share)** held by the **LASTAR Trust** (Volozh family; trustee Highvern Cayman): **~51.6% of total voting power on ~11.3% economic interest**. All directors/officers as a group ≈ **52.3% voting / 12.9% economic**; directors+officers+>5% holders ≈ **59.5% voting / 16.8% economic**. **No priority-share/stichting vehicle** — control is purely the founder's Class B. Nebius is a Nasdaq **"Controlled Company" (Rule 5615)** and can opt out of certain independence rules. [PRIMARY] Form 20-F FY2025 (filed 2026-04-30), Items 6/7/10 — sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1513845/000110465926052948/nbis-20251231x20f.htm.

### 4. Capital-allocation discipline vs empire-building

**Funding the buildout (the central question).** FY2026 capex guidance was **raised to $20–25bn** (from $16–20bn) on the Q1-2026 call. It is being funded through a deliberately diversified, **largely non-dilutive-at-the-margin stack**:
- **Customer prepayments** — deferred revenue ~$1.58bn at YE2025 (from ~$0); management is "very focused on generating prepayments to reduce capital needed from equity and debt." [PRIMARY Q1-2026 letter]
- **Debt secured against contracts** — explicitly leveraging Microsoft's/Meta's credit; "raising mid-single-digit billions in the near term." Non-current debt $8.4bn at Q1-2026. [PRIMARY]
- **Equity/converts** — Dec-2024 $700m; **Jun-2025 $1.0bn converts; Sept-2025 ~$4.2–4.3bn (equity at $92.50 + $2.75bn converts); Mar-2026 $4.0bn converts + $2.0bn NVIDIA**. Cumulative converts ≈ **$7.75bn**; an unused ATM (25m shares) sits in reserve. Cash $9.3bn at Q1-2026. [PRIMARY]

**Discipline signals (mixed-positive):**
- *Pro-discipline:* builds capacity **against signed anchor contracts** (Microsoft ~$17.4–19.4bn; Meta up to $27bn) rather than speculatively; **monetizes non-core assets** as funding rather than hoarding them — Toloka (Bezos Expeditions led $72m, May 2025; deconsolidated, ~28% retained), ClickHouse (~28% diluted; Series C ~$6bn May 2025, later marked higher), Avride (up to $375m from Uber+Nebius, Oct 2025; ~83% retained). Management explicitly frames these as "crystallize value to fund core hypergrowth." [PRIMARY PRs]
- *Empire-building flags:* it remains a **conglomerate** (autonomous driving, edtech, data labeling) with management attention split; **TripleTen** is the clearest non-core orphan with no monetization event. The 20-F adjusted EBIT margin was deeply negative in 2025 (see Part 2 #5), partly from inherited Yandex cost base. The share-price-only comp design does not directly reward ROIC.

## PART 2 — INVERSION / FAILURE PATHS ("how does this thesis die?")

### (a) Customer churn / Microsoft contract clawback — **MATERIALIZING (structural), elevated**
- **HSBC's CoreWeave "Reduce" thesis** (init. 17 Jul 2025, analyst Abhishek Shukla, **PT $32**, later ~$41): "increasing commoditization… lower barriers to entry," CoreWeave asset turnover "~32% vs AWS 76%," **Microsoft+OpenAI >72% of revenue**, EBITDA margins ">10pts below consensus," net debt/equity "could approach 10x," "maintenance capex ~35% of revenue from 2030 as GPUs approach end-of-life." [SECONDARY] Investing.com 2025-07-17; MarketScreener. **HSBC has not published a same-named NBIS Reduce note** that could be found — the read-across is made by Morgan Stanley and trade press, not HSBC.
- **Precedent the bears cite:** TD Cowen channel checks (Feb–Mar 2025) said **Microsoft walked away from ~2 GW** of US/Europe data-center capacity ("oversupply position," "forgo some new business from OpenAI"). CoreWeave/Nebius were **not** named as the cancelled counterparties. [SECONDARY] Bloomberg/Yahoo 2025-02-24; DCD 2025-03-26.
- **NBIS-specific contractual hook (verified):** the Microsoft 6-K says that if, **after a grace period, Nebius misses agreed delivery dates and cannot provide alternative capacity, "Microsoft has the right to terminate that GPU Service,"** and the agreement contains "**liquidated damages for late delivery,… indemnities, and limitations of liability**"; either party can terminate for uncured **60-day** material breach. It is **NOT described as take-or-pay / minimum-volume** in the 6-K summary [note: the 20-F financials DO describe MSFT/Meta revenue as take-or-pay "irrespective of utilization" — reconcile these two in claim_ledger]. Combined with the fact that NBIS finances the NJ build with **debt collateralized by that same contract**, this is the cleanest failure vector: a delivery slip → Microsoft termination → refund/LDs → impaired debt collateral. [PRIMARY] SEC 6-K ex-99.1, Sept 2025.
- *Mitigant:* Nebius reported it "delivered on all capacity commitments to Microsoft and Meta in Q1 2026"; the **second Meta deal ($12bn firm + $15bn flexible, Q1-2026)** is explicitly structured with Meta as a demand backstop, softening single-tenant risk.

### (b) GPU depreciation shock — **MATERIALIZING (early signs), high salience**
- **Michael Burry** (Scion, puts disclosed ~Sept-2025): "Understating depreciation by extending useful life of assets artificially boosts earnings — one of the more common frauds of the modern era." On a "2-3 yr product cycle," hyperscalers extending useful lives is illegitimate; he estimated **~$176bn of understated depreciation across the top 5 hyperscalers 2026-2028**. [SECONDARY] Saxo 2025-11-12.
- **Jim Chanos** (named CoreWeave, 16 Dec 2025): "If the chips last for three years, you have to depreciate a third of what you spend… That's the bet you have to make if you're a CoreWeave investor." Data point: **Hopper rental rates already down ~28% YoY**. [SECONDARY] Benzinga 2025-12-16.
- **Direct NBIS read-through (verified, this is the sharpest item):** Nebius **extended server/network useful life from 4 to 5 years effective Q1-2026** — prospectively lowering D&A as a % of revenue. [PRIMARY Q1-2026 letter] Bears read this exactly as Burry frames it: earnings flattery into a B200→GB200→**GB300→Vera Rubin (2H-2026)** cadence. NBIS TTM D&A was reported ~$580m (~66% of TTM revenue). [SECONDARY BanklessTimes 2026-06-15].
- Neither Burry nor Chanos names Nebius specifically; the logic is applied to NBIS by analysts/press.

### (c) Hyperscaler in-house silicon — **DEVELOPING, medium-term**
- Google **TPU v7 "Ironwood"** GA (Cloud Next 2026); **Anthropic up to 1m TPUs, "tens of billions," >1 GW in 2026** — a marquee workload off Nvidia rental. AWS **Trainium2** 500k+ chips; MS **Maia 100** deployed (but capacity maturity lags TPU). Bear data: custom-ASIC market growing ~44.6%/yr vs ~16.1% GPUs; projections of custom chips ~45% of AI silicon by 2028 and Nvidia inference share falling from >90% toward 20-30%; early TPU inference ~50% cheaper. [SECONDARY] CNBC 2025-11-21; SemiAnalysis. Threat to neoclouds: they are ~100% Nvidia-GPU-rental; inference migration to in-house ASICs compresses utilization/pricing.

### (d) Financing / dilution risk — **MATERIALIZING (visible), elevated**
- **~30% dilution since relisting**; cumulative converts ~$7.75bn; bear math: **FY2026 capex $20-25bn vs ~$7bn FCF → ~$10-15bn financing gap.** [SECONDARY Simply Wall St / aggregated]. Circular-financing concern echoes the broader **>$800bn** of AI supply-chain cross-investment (NVIDIA invests in customers who buy NVIDIA) and the **xAI SPV** template ($7.5bn equity + $12.5bn debt buying Nvidia chips, leased back) — the same debt-against-GPU-contract structure NBIS uses. [SECONDARY]
- *Mitigant:* Q1-2026 operating cash flow was **+$2.26bn** (prepayment-boosted), cash $9.3bn, and converts carry low coupons (1.00-3.00%) at 40%+ conversion premiums — a relatively cheap, deep funding stack vs CoreWeave's higher-cost debt.

### (e) Geopolitical / regulatory — **MITIGATED to reputational tail-risk**
- **Russian legacy is a narrative risk, not an active commercial blocker.** Volozh delisted; company published a formal **Trade Controls Policy (Nov 2025)** committing to OFAC/BIS compliance and stating it does not serve Russian customers. **No evidence of export-control restriction on its GPU access** — the opposite: NVIDIA is an equity investor and routes GB300s through Nebius. **No disclosed/active CFIUS review found** (only general commentary that US data centers face rising national-security scrutiny). On the **EU AI Act** (fully applicable 2 Aug 2026), NBIS is positioned as a **beneficiary** of sovereign-AI demand (a top-tier EU-domiciled GPU cloud); heaviest obligations fall on its model-builder customers, not the infra layer. [PRIMARY Trade Controls Policy; SECONDARY eutechreg, Consilium 2026-05-07]. Residual stigma persists in short-seller framing (e.g., "From Russia With Cash" letter) but not in customer behavior. **Open item:** undocumented divestiture indemnities (Part 1 #2).

## RANKED — 3 MOST CREDIBLE FAILURE PATHS + LEADING EARLY-WARNING INDICATOR

1. **Financing/dilution squeeze meets a capex air-pocket (paths d + f).** Most credible because it is arithmetic, not narrative: $20-25bn FY2026 capex against ~$7bn FCF, in a regime where the AI capex cycle is the single most-debated macro risk. A demand wobble removes the prepayment/debt-against-contract funding that makes the model work.
   - **Early-warning indicator:** *quarter-over-quarter change in deferred revenue (customer prepayments) and the equity/ATM share-issuance rate.* Falling prepayment growth + ATM activation = the funding stack flipping from customer-funded to dilution-funded. (Watch the Q2-2026 balance sheet line vs the $1.58bn YE2025 base.)

2. **Microsoft (or Meta) delivery slip triggers the contractual termination/refund clause (path a).** Credible because the clawback is contractually explicit (verified in the 6-K), the counterparty has a demonstrated 2025 willingness to walk from third-party capacity, and the build is debt-collateralized by that contract.
   - **Early-warning indicator:** *on-time activation of New Jersey/Vineland tranches through 2H-2026* — management's own statement that they "delivered on all commitments" is the metric to track; the first disclosed missed/renegotiated tranche, or a Microsoft capex-guidance cut, is the trip-wire.

3. **GPU depreciation shock crystallizes as a write-down / margin reset (path b).** Credible because NBIS just extended useful life 4→5 years into an accelerating Nvidia cadence (GB300, then Rubin in 2H-2026), and Hopper rental rates are already down ~28% YoY — the exact dynamic Burry/Chanos flag, now visible in NBIS's own accounting.
   - **Early-warning indicator:** *realized GPU rental price per GPU-hour and any further useful-life extension or asset impairment in the 20-F/6-K.* A second life extension, or sequential decline in revenue-per-MW, signals residual-value erosion outrunning the depreciation schedule.

*(In-house silicon (c) and geopolitics (e) rank below these: (c) is a 2027+ structural drift rather than a 2026 trigger; (e) is largely mitigated to reputational tail-risk absent a new sanctions/indemnity revelation.)*

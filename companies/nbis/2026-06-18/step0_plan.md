# NBIS Step 0 Research Plan

Last updated: 2026-06-18

## Research Question

What exact decision should this research support?

```text
1. (LEAD) In NBIS's AI-datacenter capacity buildout, is power / "building power
   plants" the binding bottleneck? If not, what is the real gating constraint?
2. (DECISION) Holder already owns NBIS (entry ~$190-195, +~50% as of 2026-06-18).
   Under the new 10-layer evidence-first pipeline: HOLD / TRIM / ADD?
   At what price/evidence would each be right?
3. What would prove the bull thesis wrong (kill criteria)?
```

This is NOT a fresh "should I buy" question — it is (a) a focused industrial-structure
question about the power/buildout bottleneck, and (b) a position-management question for
an existing winner. The old `../../nbis.md` brief (2026-05-04) ran the soul panel on a single
KOL video source and said "Watch"; this rerun replaces that with primary-source evidence.

## Why now

- Holder has a live, in-the-money position and needs a hold/trim frame, not an entry call.
- NBIS just reported Q1 2026 (2026-05-13) with a large guidance raise (ARR exit $7-9B,
  capex $20-25B) and entered the Nasdaq-100 (eff. 2026-06-22) — fresh primary data exists.
- The buildout-bottleneck question is the single biggest swing factor on whether the
  contracted backlog (>$46B) actually converts to revenue.

## Non-Goals

- Do not make a short-term earnings-trade call.
- Do not convert KOL/video claims into thesis (the old brief's failure mode).
- Do not write the memo before the evidence layer (claim_ledger → facts) is built.

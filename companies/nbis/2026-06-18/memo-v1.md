# NBIS Buy-Side Investment Memo

Date: 2026-06-18
Analyst: 10-layer pipeline rerun (replaces old single-source brief `../../nbis.md`)
Version: v1 (new pipeline)

**Verdict: HOLD (existing position) / WATCH for new capital** — quality position, but priced for perfection.

Position rule: **Hold existing; do not add at ~$288. Trim on kill-criteria, not on price alone. New-money buy only <~$150–180.**

---

## 1. One-Line View

NBIS is a top-tier, well-funded AI-neocloud whose real constraint is the **build-and-energize cycle, not power supply** — a genuinely strong competitive position, but at ~$288 the market already prices near-flawless execution of a 2-customer backlog, so the risk/reward for *new* capital is poor while an *existing* +50% position is reasonable to hold with discipline.

## 2. Decision Question

(1) Is power / "building power plants" the bottleneck? (2) Hold/trim/add an existing +50% position? (3) What proves it wrong? See `step0_plan.md`.

## 3. What Must Be True (for the bull case)

- [ ] Contracted power (>3.5GW) converts to **active, revenue-generating MW** on schedule (the bottleneck clears).
- [ ] The 2-customer backlog (RPO $33.6B) is delivered without a slip that triggers MSFT/Meta termination/LDs.
- [ ] Capex/revenue (~7×) **falls** over time so the business stops needing perpetual dilutive financing.
- [ ] Pricing/utilization hold through the GB300→Rubin transition (no commoditization / residual-value collapse).
- [ ] Demand broadens beyond MSFT+Meta (self-service book becomes material in dollars).

## 4. Business Quality — `business_model.md`, `financials/financial_quality.md`

Real, fast-improving (rev +479% FY25; core-segment EBITDA ~45% Q1'26; GM ~69%), but **extreme capital intensity** (capex ~7× rev, FCF ≈ −$3.7B), **weak switching costs**, and **accounting flags** (4→5yr depreciation extension, 2-yr adverse ICFR + PP&E material weakness). Not yet a high-quality compounder.

## 5. Industry / Value Chain — `value_chain_map.md`

Strong-but-squeezed: top-tier neocloud position, but structurally subordinate to **NVIDIA** (supplier power) and **2 concentrated buyers** (MSFT/Meta). Profit pool's most durable economics sit upstream at NVIDIA.

## 6. Moat / Bottleneck — `moat_map.md`, `bottleneck_map.md` (LEAD)

**Power is NOT the bottleneck and NBIS does not build power plants.** It builds data centers, contracts grid power, and uses partner-built behind-the-meter gas (NJ) + Bloom fuel cells to bypass interconnection queues. Power is already locked up (>3.5GW contracted, >75% owned, top-tier). **The binding constraint is converting contracted→active MW** — gated by construction speed and long-lead electrical gear (transformers/gensets), which management calls "the main bottleneck." The bottleneck is a **dependence, not a moat** — and it costs $20–25B/yr to push against. Today's "moat" is being early + well-funded into a power-constrained build; it erodes as the bottleneck eases.

## 7. Operator / Capital Allocation — `operator_underwriting.md`

Above-average operator (Volozh) and smart prepayment-first funding; **but** founder super-voting control (~52–55% vote / ~12% econ), **share-price-only incentives with no ROIC/capex-efficiency hurdle**, and conglomerate sprawl. Trust-but-verify quarterly.

## 8. Inversion — `inversion_map.md`

Three most credible failure paths: **(D)** financing/dilution squeeze meets a capex air-pocket; **(A/B)** delivery slip triggers Microsoft's contractual termination/LDs (the take-or-pay protects NBIS on utilization, but NBIS still carries the delivery obligation); **(C)** GPU depreciation shock (self-inflicted-risk-visible via the 4→5yr change).

## 9. Valuation / Margin Of Safety — `valuation.md`

EV ≈ $72.5B at ~$288. Owner-earnings DCF not usable (FCF negative through ≥2028). On EV/ARR the stock already capitalizes the **$7–9B YE26 exit-ARR target as if achieved**, at a mature multiple. Scenarios: **Bear ~$120–140 (−50%+), Base ~$330–400 (~6–14%/yr), Bull ~$560+**. **No margin of safety at $288.** RPO $33.6B (the real metric) is below the "$46B backlog" headline — and below the current EV.

## 10. Evidence Against The Thesis (steelman the bear)

- [ ] ~90%+ of backlog is 2 customers; non-anchor revenue still tiny in dollars.
- [ ] FCF deeply negative; ~$12.5B raised, +27% dilution, more coming.
- [ ] Depreciation extension + PP&E material weakness = low earnings quality.
- [ ] Active power (the conversion proof) is undisclosed since YE2025.
- [ ] Multiple banks at Neutral/Hold "great company, stretched stock"; stock above most average PTs.

## 11. Final Decision

- **Verdict:** HOLD existing / WATCH for new capital.
- **Position size:** keep current; treat as a high-volatility, high-uncertainty holding (size accordingly — this is not a "back up the truck" name at this price).
- **Buy condition (new money):** <~$150–180 (toward base-case entry with cushion), OR demand breadth + active-MW conversion proven AND multiple de-rates.
- **Add condition:** do **not** add at ~$288. Only on a meaningful pullback *with thesis intact*.
- **Trim / sell condition:** trim into strength (e.g. Nasdaq-100 squeeze) is reasonable given the skew; **trim/exit on kill-criteria** below — not on price alone.
- **Kill criteria (→ `monitor.md`):** active/connected MW stalls vs YE26 target OR a MSFT/Meta tranche is missed/renegotiated; funding flips from prepayment-funded to dilution-funded; a 2nd depreciation extension / GPU impairment; capex confirmed at $31–35B without conversion.

## 12. Monitoring Plan — `monitor.md`

Primary triggers: **active/connected MW** (Q2 2026 6-K, ~Aug), MSFT/Meta tranche delivery, deferred-revenue growth vs ATM issuance, realized price/MW. Resolve open items: customer concentration %, capex actual, Russia indemnities.

---

> **For the holder, plainly:** Your instinct to worry about the power bottleneck was reasonable, but the evidence says power *availability* isn't the gate and NBIS doesn't build power plants — the gate is how fast they turn contracted gigawatts into live, paid megawatts. The company is executing well and the position is fine to hold. The honest catch is that at ~$288 you're no longer being paid for the asymmetric risks (concentration, dilution, depreciation, execution) — so the discipline now is: hold, don't add here, and let the kill-criteria (not the price ticker) decide a trim. The old brief said "Watch"; with primary data the upgrade is to "Hold" — not because it got cheaper, but because it's a better, more-proven business than the single-video brief could see. It's just not cheap.

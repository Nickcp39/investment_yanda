★ Lead question: is power / "building power plants" the bottleneck?

No — the premise is wrong on both counts.

  • NBIS does not build power plants. It builds/owns data centers (>75% owned, 3 GW across 5 sites) drawing contracted grid power, plus partner-built behind-the-meter gas (NJ ~85% gas) and the Bloom Energy fuel-cell deal to bypass grid-interconnection queues.
  • Power availability is not the constraint. Already >3.5 GW contracted (guide >4 GW YE2026), top-tier among pure-play neoclouds. Demand is the opposite of a problem — "everything we build is sold."
  • The real binding constraint is the build-and-energize cycle — turning contracted → connected → active MW. Management's own words: "capacity is the main bottleneck to revenue growth."
  • Hardest sub-link: long-lead electrical gear — "massive shortages in transformers and gas generators" (Volozh). Not GPUs (de-risked), not capital, not demand.
  • The number that proves conversion — active MW — is the one NBIS stopped disclosing after YE2025 (~170 MW). Watch it.

Decision — for the holder

  • Hold the existing +50% position. It's a better, more-proven business than the old "Watch" brief could see on one video source.
  • Don't add at ~$288 — you're no longer paid for the asymmetric risks (concentration, dilution, depreciation, execution). New-money buy only <~$150–180.
  • Trim on kill-criteria, not on price. Trimming into a squeeze (Nasdaq-100 inclusion 6/22) is reasonable given the skew, but let the triggers — not the ticker — drive an exit.

Kill criteria (→ monitor.md)

  • Active/connected MW stalls vs the 800MW–1GW YE26 target, OR a MSFT/Meta tranche is missed/renegotiated.
  • Funding flips from customer-prepayment-funded to dilution-funded (deferred-rev growth stalls + ATM/equity accelerates).
  • A 2nd depreciation-life extension or a GPU impairment; or sequential decline in revenue per MW.
  • FY2026 capex confirmed at the $31–35B high end without commensurate active-MW/ARR conversion.

Caveats & open items

  • Concentration: ~90%+ of contracted backlog is 2 customers (MSFT+Meta); exact % not disclosed.
  • Take-or-pay resolved: revenue is take-or-pay (protects NBIS on utilization) and NBIS carries a delivery obligation with termination/LDs (protects customer) — residual risk is execution/delivery, not customer walk-away.
  • Active power (MW) undisclosed since YE2025 — the key conversion metric.
  • Capex'26: $20–25B (call) vs $31–35B (some secondary) — confirm at Q2 2026 6-K (~Aug).
  • Russia legacy: divestiture indemnities / contingent liabilities not confirmed from primary text.

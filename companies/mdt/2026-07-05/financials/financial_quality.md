| Q4 FY2026 | $9,807M | +9.9% reported / **+6.6% organic** | 65.4% | **19.1% (+300bps YoY)** | **25.5% (down ~230bps YoY)** | $1,243M | $0.96 / $1.55 |

**Read**: Revenue growth ACCELERATED into Q4 (company-wide organic +6.6% vs full-year +5.8%), and this acceleration is broad-based — Cardiovascular (the largest segment) grew EVEN FASTER in Q4 (+10.1%) than for the full year (+9.3%), driven by the PFA-fueled Cardiac Ablation Solutions line (+78% global / +124% US, facts.md E28). This is the OPPOSITE trend shape from the GEHC exemplar (which showed decelerating orders and compressing margin across 3 consecutive periods) — MDT's FY2026 is a genuine multi-quarter ACCELERATION, corroborated by management's own characterization ("highest annual revenue growth in 10 years").

**The margin bifurcation is the one genuine flag**: GAAP operating margin is flat-to-improving (full year flat at 17.8%; Q4 UP sharply +300bps to 19.1%), while non-GAAP operating margin is COMPRESSING in both periods (full year down ~130bps to 24.4%; Q4 down ~230bps to 25.5%), with tariffs explicitly cited as a 50bps (FY) / 80bps (Q4) drag. **This divergence needs a clean explanation that no source reviewed this run provides** (facts.md O4) — plausible candidates include Diabetes-spinoff-preparation costs, restructuring charges, or amortization/purchase-accounting items being excluded from GAAP-favorable treatment while burdening the non-GAAP base, but this is speculation, not confirmed. Flagged as the top financial-quality open item, analogous in spirit to (though structurally different from) the GEHC exemplar's harder-to-explain 3-period adj-EBIT decline.

**Read**: FCF/NI of 1.13x is a GENUINELY STRONG cash-conversion signal — FCF exceeds net income, indicating D&A and non-cash items are running ahead of working-capital/capex consumption, a materially higher-quality profile than the GEHC exemplar's FCF/NI of ~0.70x (where FCF ran meaningfully BELOW net income). This is one of the clearest, most decision-relevant financial-quality data points in this dossier: MDT converts reported earnings into real cash at a rate well above 1:1, supporting both the Dividend King payout (E7, E12) and the ~77% FCF payout ratio (dividends + buybacks $4.2B / FCF $5.426B) without balance-sheet strain.

**Caveat**: This dossier does not have quarterly FCF cadence data (only the full FY2026 figure), so seasonality within the year (e.g., whether Q1 FY26 was unusually weak the way GEHC's Q1 2026 was) is not independently assessed — a minor gap, not a load-bearing one given the strong full-year figure.

**Read**: MDT carries meaningfully MORE absolute debt than the GEHC exemplar ($28.6B vs GEHC's ~$10.2B) but is also a much larger company (market cap ~$106.5B vs GEHC's ~$29.8B) with a materially stronger FCF profile ($5.4B/yr vs GEHC's $1.5B/yr) — net debt / FY2026 FCF is roughly **3.6-3.8x**, a leverage level that is elevated but serviceable for an investment-grade-rated, highly cash-generative med-tech, and NOT currently on a rising trajectory the way GEHC's was (this dossier does not have a prior-year net-debt comparison point for MDT to confirm the trend direction — flagged as a minor gap, since only one period of balance-sheet data was gathered this run). **Verdict**: Leverage is real and higher in absolute dollar terms than the GEHC exemplar, but proportionally reasonable (D/E 59.3% vs total capital) for a company of this scale and cash-generation quality, and is NOT compounding with a deteriorating margin trend the way GEHC's was — MDT's margin (GAAP) is stable-to-improving even as debt sits at a moderate level. This is a materially LESS concerning leverage picture than the GEHC exemplar, chiefly because the FCF engine backing it is so much stronger (FCF/NI >1x vs <1x).

This makes MDT's "normalized earnings power" somewhat HARDER to confidently state than GEHC's, despite MDT's headline trend being much more positive.

## Financial Quality Verdict

**GOOD-TO-VERY-GOOD, with one clean strength (FCF conversion) and two genuine unresolved flags (margin bifurcation, litigation overhang).** Revenue growth accelerated broadly across the largest segment in FY2026 (the best organic growth in a decade), FCF conversion is strong (FCF/NI >1x, materially better than the GEHC exemplar), leverage is proportionally reasonable given the cash-generation strength, and the dividend/buyback program continues uninterrupted (49 consecutive years of increases). However, this is NOT a "clean, no-questions-asked" financial picture: the GAAP-vs-non-GAAP margin divergence is unexplained, and the MiniMed insulin pump litigation represents a real, unquantified, multi-year liability overhang that has not been resolved and carries genuine tail risk, especially given its proximity to the Diabetes spinoff (raising an unaddressed question of how this liability will be allocated between RemainCo and SpinCo). On balance, this financial profile supports a MORE constructive verdict than the GEHC exemplar's "solid but currently absorbing headwinds" read — MDT's headline trend is genuinely accelerating, not decelerating — but the litigation and margin-bridge gaps mean this should not be scored as an unambiguously "exceptional, no-flags" financial quality picture either.

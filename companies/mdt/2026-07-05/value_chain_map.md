# MDT Value Chain Map

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05

---

## Upstream → MDT → Downstream

**Upstream inputs**:
- Semiconductor/electronic components (implantable device electronics — pacemakers, neurostimulators), specialty catheter materials (ablation catheters — PFA electrode arrays), titanium/biocompatible metals (spine implants, structural heart valves), battery technology (implantable device longevity is a genuine engineering differentiator).
- Contract manufacturing and component suppliers subject to tariff exposure — MDT has explicitly diversified manufacturing away from China-sourced components MORE than device-industry peers (facts.md E58: <1% of revenue is directly Chinese-import-tariff-exposed), a structural advantage in the current trade-policy environment relative to peers with heavier China-sourced supply chains.
- Software/firmware development (increasingly embedded in every device category — pacemaker remote monitoring, CGM algorithms, robotic-surgery control software for Hugo).

**MDT internal value-add**:
- R&D / device engineering across four distinct therapeutic domains (cardiac electronics, neuromodulation, surgical robotics, diabetes technology) — genuine platform R&D breadth, though the Diabetes platform is being carved out.
- Regulatory affairs / clinical trial execution (the Expand URO 137-patient IDE study for Hugo, facts.md E42, is a direct example of the clinical-evidence-generation capability required to clear FDA/CE-mark/NMPA in each new therapy category).
- Manufacturing (implantable-grade precision manufacturing — high reliability bar, given implant recall/liability exposure).
- Direct sales force with deep physician relationships (implanting cardiologists, neurosurgeons, urologists) — the switching-cost mechanism for mature categories runs through physician training/preference, not just hospital purchasing departments.
- Field clinical support (many MDT procedures — ablation, DBS programming, robotic surgery — require MDT clinical specialists present or on-call during procedures, a genuine service-layer embedded in the "product").

**Downstream customers**:
- Hospitals / health systems (across all four segments — the common buyer, though the actual END USER/beneficiary is the implanting physician's preference plus the patient's clinical outcome).
- Individual physicians (cardiologists doing ablations, neurosurgeons doing DBS/spine, urologists doing robotic prostatectomies) — in several MDT categories, physician preference/training is AS important as hospital procurement in determining which vendor wins, a genuine and durable form of switching cost.
- Government/public payers (Medicare/CMS in the US — the Jan 2026 ASC reimbursement rule change for cardiac ablation, facts.md E49, is a direct example of a payer-policy tailwind; China VBP tenders — a distinct, price-sensitive procurement channel, structurally similar in spirit to the GEHC exemplar's China dynamic though smaller and less rigorously quantified here).
- Patients directly (Diabetes segment specifically — CGM/insulin pump purchase decisions increasingly involve direct-to-consumer marketing and patient app experience, the exact B2C dynamic cited as the spinoff rationale, facts.md E53).

## Where the Profit Pools Sit

| Value chain link | Profit pool depth | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mature implant categories (CRM/pacemakers, spine, neuromodulation) | **Deep but slow-growing** | High switching cost via physician training/preference; 2-3% organic growth ceiling in the categories reviewed (Neuroscience +3.1% FY26); durable, not exciting |
| Pulsed field ablation (new cardiac category) | **Deep AND fast-growing — currently the best profit pool in the portfolio** | +78% global / +124% US revenue growth (Q4 FY26); MDT holds outright share leadership (48% vs BSX 41%) against a well-resourced peer; reimbursement tailwind (CMS ASC listing) still early in its adoption curve |
| Hugo / robotic surgery | **Unclear — the central open question, mirroring GEHC's AI-monetization gap** | Real, peer-reviewed clinical validation (BJU International publication) and a first US FDA clearance, but ZERO revenue disclosure found this run (facts.md O3). Cannot yet confirm this is an actual profit pool vs. a multi-year cash-burning bet against an entrenched, highly profitable incumbent (Intuitive Surgical) |
| Diabetes (pre-spinoff) | **Moderate, being deliberately exited** | +7.9% organic growth (healthy), but management's own stated rationale is a business-MODEL mismatch (B2C vs B2B), not a profit-pool-quality complaint — this is a strategic-focus decision, not evidence the segment itself is a bad business |
| China / VBP-exposed categories broadly | **Compressed, but smaller and (per unverified commentary) stabilizing** | ~7% of revenue (FY2024 basis); tariff exposure to Chinese imports specifically <1% — MDT's manufacturing-diversification choices appear to have pre-emptively reduced this specific risk vector more than some peers |

## Bottleneck Candidates (developed further in bottleneck_map.md)

1. **Physician training/preference lock-in** — cardiac ablation, DBS programming, and robotic surgery all require meaningful physician training investment on a specific platform; switching carries real friction (retraining time, comfort/outcomes risk during the learning curve). This is MDT's closest analog to GEHC's "installed-base service annuity" — a soft but genuine switching cost, not an absolute monopoly.
2. **Regulatory/clinical-evidence generation capability** — Hugo's FDA clearance required a dedicated 137-patient IDE trial (Expand URO) that took years to design, enroll, and complete; this is a genuine time-and-capital barrier against a hypothetical fast-follower (domestic Chinese robotic-surgery entrants included), though NOT a permanent one — competitors run their own trials on their own timelines.
3. **Reimbursement/coding pathway navigation** — the Jan 2026 CMS ASC listing for cardiac ablation (facts.md E49) is the kind of payer-policy work that took years of advocacy to achieve and directly unlocked a lower-cost care-setting expansion; this is a genuine, hard-to-replicate-quickly capability, though it benefits the whole PFA category (including BSX), not MDT exclusively.
4. **China market access / VBP tendering** — structurally similar to the GEHC exemplar's inverted bottleneck (the CHANNEL favoring domestic manufacture in government-linked tenders), but MDT's exposure is smaller (~7% vs GEHC's ~9.85%) and, per unverified CEO commentary, may be past its steepest pricing-pressure phase. This is the one bottleneck candidate where MDT's evidence is meaningfully THINNER than the GEHC exemplar — a priority gap, not a confirmed advantage or disadvantage.

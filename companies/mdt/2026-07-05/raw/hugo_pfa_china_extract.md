## Pulsed Field Ablation (PFA) — Cardiovascular Growth Engine

**Source**: news.medtronic.com (Affera/Sphere-9 FDA approval PR), FDA.gov device clearance page, Clinical Trials Arena (HRS 2026 coverage), Guidepoint qsight blog, Cardiovascular Business (CMS ASC rule)

- Oct 24, 2024: FDA approved Affera Mapping and Ablation System + Sphere-9 Catheter — Medtronic becomes first company with 2 FDA-approved PFA modalities (PulseSelect single-shot PVI + Affera/Sphere-9 point-by-point/flexible)
- Market share: Medtronic ~48% of PFA spend share (early 2026) vs Boston Scientific ~41% (declining) — Sphere-9 alone = ~46% of Medtronic's own PFA spend
- Volume: ~250,000 PFA catheter units in 2025, +20% YoY (total market)
- Reimbursement: CMS added cardiac catheter ablation to ASC-covered procedures list effective Jan 1, 2026 — expands access to lower-cost outpatient settings
- Clinical: breakthrough device designation granted for Sphere-9 in ventricular tachycardia (VT) indication (separate from AFib); recent trial showed 65.5% freedom from VT recurrence at 6 months
- Direct financial linkage: Cardiac Ablation Solutions revenue +78% global / +124% US in Q4 FY2026 — the explicit standout line item cited in the earnings release

## Diabetes Business Spinoff

**Source**: Yahoo News (spinoff announcement), MedTech Dive, MDDI Online (MiniMed IPO), Cardiovascular Business, Medical Design & Outsourcing, Star Tribune, FierceBiotech (JPM26 coverage)

- Medtronic to spin off Diabetes business within ~18 months of announcement — standalone publicly-traded company combining insulin pumps (MiniMed) + CGM
- Preferred path: IPO followed by full spin-off/split
- Rationale (CEO Martha, direct paraphrase from search-sourced quote): diabetes is "predominantly B2C" vs the rest of Medtronic being "predominantly B2B" — a genuine business-model mismatch rationale, not just portfolio pruning
- New entity to be led by Que Dallara (current Medtronic Diabetes president), HQ in California (Northridge)
- Framed alongside a pivot toward new growth platforms: pulsed field ablation, renal denervation, robotic surgery (Hugo) — i.e., the spinoff and the PFA/Hugo growth bets are part of ONE coherent capital-allocation narrative, not unrelated initiatives
- FY2025 Diabetes revenue (last full year before spinoff announcement impact): $2.76B (+10.7% reported/+11.5% organic); FY2026: $3.112B (+7.9% organic) — the segment continued to grow well even amid spinoff planning

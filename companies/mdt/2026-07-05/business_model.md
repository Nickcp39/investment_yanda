# MDT Business Model (M2 Theme / Mechanism)

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E9-E62

## What MDT Sells: Four Segments (as-filed FY2026 structure, one — Diabetes — announced for spinoff)

### 1. Cardiovascular (largest and fastest-growing, $13.976B FY2026, +9.3% organic, ~38.4% of revenue)
Cardiac rhythm management (pacemakers, implantable defibrillators), **cardiac ablation solutions** (the standout growth line — pulsed field ablation via PulseSelect + Affera/Sphere-9, +78% global / +124% US revenue growth in Q4 FY2026 alone [facts.md E28]), structural heart (transcatheter valves), coronary and peripheral vascular. This segment is currently doing for MDT roughly what the GEHC exemplar's Pharmaceutical Diagnostics did for GEHC — being the clear, quantified, fastest-growing profit engine — except MDT's growth engine is a genuine SHARE-GAIN story against a well-capitalized peer (Boston Scientific), not merely organic category growth.

**Mechanism**: Sell implantable devices (pacemakers/ICDs, ablation catheters, structural heart valves) with a mix of capital-equipment-adjacent (mapping/ablation consoles) and true per-procedure consumables (catheters) → procedure-volume-driven recurring revenue as the installed base of trained implanting physicians and mapping-system consoles grows → new-therapy-category expansion (PFA, renal denervation) creates fresh multi-year growth legs on top of the mature CRM base.

### 2. Neuroscience (~$10.287B FY2026, +3.1% organic, ~28.3% of revenue)
Spine implants/instrumentation, neuromodulation (deep brain stimulation for Parkinson's/essential tremor, spinal cord stimulation for chronic pain), specialty therapies. Steadier, lower-growth segment — the "mature core" of the portfolio, similar in character to GEHC's Imaging segment (large, established, single-digit organic growth).

### 3. Medical Surgical (~$8.815B FY2026, +2.9% organic, ~24.2% of revenue)
General surgery, ENT, respiratory/GI/renal — **and the Hugo™ soft-tissue robotic-assisted surgery (RAS) system** [facts.md E39]. This is the slowest-growing of the three continuing segments in aggregate, but contains the single highest optionality item in the entire portfolio for Nick's career thesis: Hugo just received its first US FDA clearance (December 2025, urology) after years of ex-US-only commercialization, and general surgery + gynecology indications are in the pipeline (Embrace Gynecology enrollment complete). Hugo's revenue contribution is NOT separately disclosed (facts.md O3) — it is currently a rounding error within a $8.8B segment, but is the segment's most consequential multi-year catalyst if the US ramp succeeds.

### 4. Diabetes (~$3.112B FY2026, +7.9% organic, ~8.6% of revenue) — **BEING SPUN OFF**
Insulin pumps (MiniMed) and continuous glucose monitors (CGM). CEO Geoff Martha announced plans to spin this off within ~18 months as a standalone public company (preferred structure: IPO then full split), explicitly because "diabetes is predominantly B2C, whereas [the rest of] Medtronic is predominantly B2B" [facts.md E51-E55]. This is a genuine business-model-fit rationale, not just portfolio pruning — Diabetes requires direct-to-consumer marketing, patient-facing app/software UX, and payer/pharmacy-benefit navigation that is structurally different from MDT's hospital-capital-equipment-and-physician-relationship-driven core.

## The Core Mechanism: Implant + Procedure-Volume + New-Therapy-Category Expansion

Unlike GEHC's three-layer "equipment + service + consumables" model, MDT's is better described as a **"mature base + new-category share-gain" model**:

1. **Mature, high-share implant categories** (CRM/pacemakers, spine, neuromodulation) — slow-growing (2-3% organic) but stable, high-switching-cost businesses (implanting physicians train on and prefer familiar systems; referral/reimbursement relationships are sticky)
2. **New-therapy-category expansion, where MDT is currently WINNING share** — pulsed field ablation is the clearest current example: MDT entered a Boston-Scientific-pioneered new procedure category and, within roughly a year of its second approved PFA modality, took outright share leadership (48% vs BSX 41%) [facts.md E47]. This is a genuinely rare pattern — a large, diversified incumbent out-executing a more PFA-focused peer in a fast-growing new category — and is the single most decision-relevant "is the moat working" data point in this dossier.
3. **Long-duration optionality bets** — Hugo (robotic surgery, competing directly with Intuitive Surgical's da Vinci near-monopoly) and renal denervation are multi-year bets where regulatory clearance has only just arrived (Hugo, Dec 2025) or is still maturing; neither is yet a proven P&L contributor.
4. **Portfolio simplification via spinoff** — removing the B2C Diabetes business to sharpen the remaining company's B2B focus, freeing management attention and (eventually) capital for the PFA/Hugo/renal-denervation growth bets [facts.md E55].

## FY2026 Was a Genuine Multi-Quarter Inflection, Not a One-Quarter Beat

Full-year organic growth of +5.8% (the best in 10 years, per management's own characterization) was NOT concentrated in one segment or one quarter — Cardiovascular (the largest segment) was ALSO the fastest-growing (+9.3% organic), and the growth accelerated further into Q4 specifically (+6.6% organic company-wide, +10.1% Cardiovascular) [facts.md E15-E16, E23-E24]. This is a materially different setup than the GEHC exemplar, where margin/growth were DECELERATING across three consecutive periods. FY2027 guidance (+6.75-7.25% organic) implies management expects the acceleration to continue, not merely hold — a real forward claim that will be tested at Q1 FY2027 (facts.md O9, not yet reported as of as_of).

## Margin Nuance: GAAP Up, Non-GAAP Down — A Flag, Not Yet a Verdict

FY2026 GAAP operating margin was flat (17.8%) and Q4 GAAP margin was UP sharply (+300bps to 19.1%), while non-GAAP operating margin COMPRESSED in both periods (FY26 down ~130bps to 24.4%; Q4 down ~230bps to 25.5%), with tariffs explicitly cited as a 50-80bps drag [facts.md E17-E18, E25-E26, E22, E29]. This divergence needs unpacking in financial_quality.md — it likely reflects one-off/adjustment items (possibly Diabetes-spinoff-preparation costs, restructuring, or amortization normalization) flattering the GAAP number while the non-GAAP (closer to run-rate) figure absorbs the real tariff cost — but the specific bridge was not found in sources reviewed this run (facts.md O4).

## Geographic Mix (China) — Directionally Opposite of the GEHC Exemplar, But Less Rigorously Confirmed

China is approximately **7% of total MDT revenue** on a FY2024 basis (facts.md E57) — smaller than GEHC's already-small China exposure (~9.85%, per the GEHC exemplar). Unlike GEHC (where China revenue is CONFIRMED shrinking −15% YoY), MDT's China narrative — per unquoted/undated CEO commentary — is that VBP pricing pressure is "largely behind us" and China sales are "growing at the corporate average" [facts.md E61-E62]. **This is a materially less rigorously-sourced claim than GEHC's hard geographic-segment figure** — treated as directional color, not a decisive fact, and flagged as the single highest-priority item for the next MDT refresh (facts.md O2).

## Why This Matters for the AI/China/Robotics Career Lens

MDT's Hugo is the most direct, named career-relevant asset in this dossier: a large incumbent's second-generation entrant into a market (soft-tissue robotic surgery) that Intuitive Surgical's da Vinci has dominated for two decades, now finally reaching the US market (its largest, highest-ASP addressable market) after building an ex-US commercial track record since ~2021. The China angle is two-layered: (1) Hugo itself will eventually compete in China against BOTH Intuitive's da Vinci AND domestic entrants like MicroPort MedBot (术锐/Toumai) — not examined in this run's sourcing (OPEN, flag for next refresh); (2) MDT's broader China VBP exposure, while smaller and (per unverified commentary) more stabilized than GEHC's, is still a real structural dynamic worth monitoring as MDT's other segments (Cardiovascular/PFA, Neuroscience) also face China tender pressure not separately quantified this run.

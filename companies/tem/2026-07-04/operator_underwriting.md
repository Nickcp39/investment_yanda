## Management Assessment: Grade 2/5

### Founder/CEO/Chairman: Eric Lefkofsky

**Track record — serial founder, 4 prior companies, mixed public-market outcomes**:

| Company | Founded/Co-founded | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| InnerWorkings | 2001 | IPO 2006; sold to private equity 2021 for "a fraction of its IPO market cap" [facts.md E5] — a clear value-destroyer over a 15-year hold |
| Echo Global Logistics | 2005 | IPO 2009; acquired 2021 at a premium — the one clear WIN in the track record |
| Groupon | 2008 (co-founded) | IPO 2011 at ~$13B valuation; stock subsequently fell >90% from IPO highs as the daily-deals business model collapsed — the most FAMOUS entry in his track record, and not a flattering one. This was a genuine business-model failure (daily deals proved non-durable), not fraud or malfeasance, but it is the highest-profile data point on Lefkofsky's judgment about durable business models and matters directly here because Tempus's bull case ALSO depends on a not-yet-fully-proven business model (data monetization) scaling durably. |
| Mediaocean | various | Private; no public-market read available |
| **Tempus AI** | 2015 | IPO June 2024; current subject of this dossier |

**Honest read**: This is a 1-clear-win-out-of-3-public-outcomes track record (Echo = win, InnerWorkings = loss, Groupon = high-profile loss), not the "serial-winner" narrative sometimes implied in profile pieces. Lefkofsky is unambiguously a talented company-BUILDER (he has founded/scaled 5 companies to meaningful size, including one, Tempus, that has grown revenue from ~$531.8M FY2023 to $1,271.8M FY2025 — genuine execution) but his history as a public-company STEWARD of shareholder capital through a full cycle is decidedly mixed. Groupon in particular is a cautionary precedent: a business that looked like it had a durable moat (network effects between merchants and consumers) at IPO and did not — precisely the kind of judgment test Tempus's "data moat" thesis is currently undergoing.

**Governance structure — a genuine red flag, not a neutral fact**:
- Class B common stock carries **30 votes per share** — high even among aggressive dual-class tech structures (10/share is typical, 20/share considered high) [facts.md E3]
- Combined voting power **~59.9%** as of the most recent disclosed date, on a MINORITY economic (share-count) stake [facts.md E6]
- This means Lefkofsky retains effectively unilateral control over board composition and major corporate actions regardless of Class A shareholder sentiment — the opposite of GEV's or MSFT's clean one-share-one-vote structure (see exemplar dossiers). If the data-monetization bet does not pay off as hoped, Class A shareholders have essentially NO structural mechanism to force a correction in strategy or force a sale process, unlike at MSFT (one-share-one-vote, correctable) or GEV (no founder control).
- The Pathos-related-party relationship (facts.md E43: Lefkofsky co-founded Pathos, which is now a fast-growing related-party revenue counterparty to Tempus, up to $21.8M/quarter from ~$0.6M a year earlier) sits directly at the intersection of this governance concentration — Lefkofsky sits on BOTH sides of a growing intercompany revenue relationship. Nothing in the primary record indicates impropriety, and related-party transactions of this kind are disclosed (not hidden), but the combination of (a) super-voting control + (b) a fast-growing related-party revenue stream he economically touches on both sides is a textbook governance-scrutiny item, not a footnote.

**Compensation/incentive alignment**: Not separately extracted in this run (OPEN — proxy statement not fetched); Lefkofsky's large personal equity stake (39.8M+ Class A shares) does align him with the Class A share price economically, which is a mitigant, though the super-voting structure means his DOWNSIDE governance accountability does not match his upside alignment.

## Governance

- Dual-class structure: Class A (1 vote) + Class B (**30 votes**, 100% held by Lefkofsky) [facts.md E3]
- Combined voting power: **~59.9%** as of most recent disclosed date, on a clear minority of total shares outstanding [facts.md E6]
- No poison-pill/sunset-clause detail extracted in this run (OPEN — would need proxy statement to confirm whether the super-voting structure has a time-based or ownership-based sunset)
- Related-party revenue relationships (SB Tempus VIE, Pathos Master Agreement) are disclosed per GAAP related-party rules but represent a genuine complexity/scrutiny surface given Lefkofsky's dual economic interest [facts.md E41-E43]

**Governance verdict**: NOT clean. This is a founder-controlled company with one of the more extreme super-voting structures in the current public-market dual-class universe, a related-party revenue stream that intersects the founder's other ventures, and a founder whose most famous prior public-company outcome (Groupon) was a severe value-destruction event. None of this means the Tempus AI thesis is wrong — but it means Class A shareholders are making a concentrated bet on ONE person's continued good judgment with essentially no structural recourse if that judgment proves as flawed here as it did at Groupon.

## Operator Grade: 2/5

**Rationale**: Undeniable execution ability (revenue has more than doubled in 2 years, multiple accretive-seeming acquisitions integrated, pharma deals signed) earns credit — this is NOT a "flee immediately" governance profile. But the combination of (1) a 30-votes/share super-voting structure with ~60% combined control on a minority economic stake, (2) a fast-growing related-party revenue stream the founder touches on both sides, (3) an aggressive, debt/equity-funded M&A cadence pre-profitability, and (4) a highly public prior track record that includes one severe value-destruction outcome (Groupon) at a company with a superficially similar "network effect" moat claim that did not hold up — together justify a BELOW-AVERAGE operator grade despite genuine respect for the company-building execution. This is meaningfully weaker governance than either exemplar (GEV: clean one-share-one-vote, 4/5 operator grade off pure execution merit; MSFT: one-share-one-vote, correctable governance, 5/5). Grade: **2/5** — capable builder, weak governance/accountability structure, unproven capital-allocation discipline at this stage of the company's life.

## Binding Constraint

**Business-quality and governance COMPLETENESS, not price.** Price offers a real, quantified margin of safety — the base case clears the 8% hurdle with a genuine cushion, structurally different from GEV (zero MOS at any scenario) and closer to MSFT's positive-MOS finding. But TEM's underlying moat is NOT yet established: the company's own 10-K admits a reimbursement disadvantage vs Foundation Medicine/Guardant on 75% of revenue, the AI-native Algorithms line is 1.6% of revenue and explicitly called "limited," and the operator grade is below average (2/5) given Lefkofsky's 30-votes/share super-voting control, his Groupon precedent (a business with a superficially similar network-effect claim that did not hold up), and a fast-growing related-party revenue stream he touches on both sides. This caps the size at a SMALL starter, not a CORE-track position.

- K-C: Share count growth >10%/yr for 2 years without adj EBITDA margin expansion → dilution outpacing value creation → trim/exit
- K-D: Any governance/related-party scrutiny event (SB Tempus/Pathos, Class B structure) → immediate re-underwrite, likely exit
- K-E: NRR falls meaningfully below 126% or pharma deal-signing stalls 2+ quarters → growth engine faltering → downgrade
- K-F: Price above ~$84 without leg confirmation → no-chase, MOS consumed

## Runner Dissent (abbreviated — full text in decision_card.json)

The mechanical read lands STARTER at a small 1.5% size, and this direction is earned (base case clears 8% hurdle with a real 28% cushion, not a razor-thin pass). But this is explicitly NOT a high-conviction call: size is calibrated down because (a) the core moat is unproven and partially admitted-weak by the company itself, (b) the bear case represents REAL capital loss (−5.7%), not just underperformance, unlike either exemplar dossier, and (c) the governance structure (30-votes/share, Groupon precedent, growing related-party revenue) is a genuine risk factor that should not be waved away by an exciting AI-healthcare narrative.

# TEM Moat Map (M3)

## Moat Summary: PARTIAL, TWO-SPEED MOAT — STRONG ON VOLUME/GROWTH, WEAK ON PRICE/REIMBURSEMENT

Business quality grade: **GOOD AND IMPROVING, NOT YET EXCEPTIONAL**. This is the central finding that should discipline the verdict: TEM has real, primary-sourced evidence of commercial traction (revenue growth, pharma deal-signing, NRR 126%) but the company's OWN 10-K admits the core Diagnostics moat (75% of revenue) is not yet price-competitive with direct comps, and the AI/data moat (the exciting 25%) is still small, lumpy, and partly related-party.

---

## Moat 1: Multimodal Clinical-Genomic Data Asset — REAL BUT UNPROVEN AT COMPETITIVE SCALE

**Evidence for**:
- Tempus explicitly integrates genomic + clinical outcomes + (increasingly) imaging/pathology data (Paige AI acquisition) — a broader data capture than pure-genomic competitors [facts.md E2, business_model.md]
- AstraZeneca + Pathos deal explicitly aims to build "the largest multimodal foundation model in oncology" [facts.md E35] — an external validation the data asset is differentiated enough for a major pharma to co-invest ~$200M
- Total Remaining Contract Value >$1.1B, Net Revenue Retention 126%, >70 data-agreement customers signed in 2025 [facts.md E15, E39] — real, primary-sourced commercial pull

**Evidence against / durability test**:
- Foundation Medicine (Roche-owned) and Guardant Health have BOTH been generating large-scale genomic testing data for longer than Tempus (Guardant IPO'd 2018, FMI acquired by Roche 2018) — Tempus's data-volume lead, if any, is not established in the primary record pulled.
- The company's own risk factors (facts.md E44) frame reimbursement catch-up as aspirational ("would expect... to be more in line with FMI and Guardant"), which implicitly concedes those competitors have a more mature commercial/reimbursement infrastructure — a proxy for a longer or better-monetized data operation, not a weaker one.
- Data-licensing "moat" claims (companies "building proprietary AI models directly on Tempus' platform" per trade press) are S1-tier (sentiment), not yet confirmed via SEC-primary contract-structure disclosure (OPEN item O1 in facts.md).

**Verdict**: This is a REAL and DIFFERENTIATED data-integration strategy (multimodal breadth is a genuine technical choice, not marketing), but "moat" implies durability and pricing power that is not yet demonstrated at scale — it is closer to a promising, well-funded R&D bet with early revenue proof points than an established moat like GEV's services-annuity or MSFT's switching-cost lock-in (see exemplar dossiers for contrast). Grade: MEDIUM, IMPROVING.

## Moat 2: CLIA Lab Scale / Regulatory Barrier — MODERATE, SHARED WITH COMPETITORS

**Evidence**:
- CLIA-certification, physician-relationship networks, and NGS-panel validation studies are genuine barriers to a brand-new entrant — but Foundation Medicine, Guardant Health, Natera, Exact Sciences, and Caris Life Sciences have ALL cleared this bar already. This is a barrier to NEW entrants, not a differentiator among current genomic-testing competitors.
- Ambry Genetics acquisition (Feb 2025) added an established, already-CLIA-certified Hereditary testing franchise rather than building one from scratch — a buy-vs-build decision that itself signals the organic barrier-to-entry in a new test category is real (Tempus chose to acquire rather than build).

**Verdict**: Real but NOT proprietary to Tempus — an industry-wide barrier that Tempus shares with 4-5 well-capitalized public/private competitors. Grade: LOW-MEDIUM as a source of Tempus-specific competitive advantage.

---

## Moat 3: Turnaround Time / Operational Execution — MODEST, CONTESTABLE

**Evidence**: Company states ~9-day (xT) / ~8-day (xF) average turnaround "meets or exceeds many of our competitors" [facts.md E45] — an operational claim, not a structural moat. Turnaround time is a capacity/process-investment variable any well-funded competitor can match or beat with capital investment; it is not protected by IP, regulation, or network effects.

**Verdict**: A competitive FEATURE, not a durable MOAT. Grade: LOW as a long-term differentiator.

## Moat Weaknesses (disclosed, not inferred)

1. **Reimbursement rate gap vs FMI/Guardant is company-admitted, not analyst speculation** (facts.md E44) — on 75% of revenue, TEM is currently a price-taker relative to more-established comps, actively working to close the gap via a new MAC (Palmetto) relationship and commercial payer negotiations. Until closed, unit economics on the core business are structurally worse than the two most direct public comps.
2. **Data-licensing "moat" concentration in a handful of large pharma relationships** (AstraZeneca, GSK, Merck, BMS, Gilead) — a small-N customer base is vulnerable to renewal risk, and a meaningful and FAST-GROWING slice of this segment's growth in the most recent quarter is RELATED-PARTY revenue (SB Tempus + Pathos, $21.8M of $87.0M Q1 2026 Data and Applications revenue = ~25%) — a related-party concentration that requires monitoring for arm's-length pricing, not yet a confirmed problem but a genuine caution flag (facts.md E43, O3).
3. **Applications/Algorithm layer — the most exciting piece of the AI story — is 1.6% of revenue and explicitly described by the company as "limited"** [facts.md E10]. The multiple the market may be paying for "Tempus as an AI company" is disproportionate to the AI-native revenue actually recognized today.

**Overall moat verdict**: TEM does NOT yet have an "exceptional" moat comparable to the GEV services-annuity or MSFT switching-cost exemplars. It has a genuinely differentiated DATA STRATEGY with real early commercial validation (pharma deals, NRR, TCV), riding on top of a Diagnostics lab business that is currently a reimbursement price-taker relative to its two most direct comps by the company's own admission. The investment case is a bet that the data/AI flywheel compounds fast enough to (a) close the reimbursement gap via volume/negotiating leverage and (b) grow the Data/Applications segment into the dominant, high-margin value-capture layer — before competitors (FMI/Roche, Guardant, or a hyperscaler health-data entrant) replicate the multimodal integration. This is a plausible but NOT YET PROVEN moat. See `bottleneck_map.md` for the network-effect stress test and `inversion_map.md` for the failure modes.

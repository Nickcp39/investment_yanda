# TEM Inversion Map / Kill Paths (M5)

Last updated: 2026-07-04 | as_of: 2026-07-04

*"Invert, always invert" — Munger. Start from: how does this business permanently impair capital?*

---

## Permanent-Impairment Test: Does TEM Pass?

**Baseline question**: Can the permanent value of this business fall below current price?

Unlike GEV or MSFT (both FCF-positive, net-cash, moat-established businesses where the question is purely "is the multiple too rich"), TEM is a genuine **pre-profitability, capital-structure-dependent** company where permanent impairment IS a live, non-trivial possibility — not merely a multiple-compression risk. At $60.27 (~$10.8B market cap), the market is pricing revenue growth (+25-36%) and a path to modest positive adj EBITDA (~$65M FY2026 guide, ~4% margin) continuing for years. If that path breaks — competitively, financially, or via a reimbursement/regulatory setback — the downside is structurally larger than in a cash-generative moat business, because there is no dividend, no buyback, and a capital structure reliant on continued market access.

---

## Kill Path F1: Reimbursement Gap Never Closes (or Widens) — HIGH PROBABILITY, MODERATE SEVERITY

**Mechanism**: The company's own 10-K admits current per-test reimbursement lags Foundation Medicine and Guardant Health (facts.md E44). If Tempus cannot get its new Raleigh, NC (Palmetto MAC) lab technically approved, or cannot win improved in-network commercial rates, Diagnostics gross margin (75% of revenue) stays structurally compressed vs the two most direct comps indefinitely — capping the FY2026 adj-EBITDA-positive trajectory from ever reaching a meaningfully profitable margin.

**Status**: ACTIVE, UNRESOLVED as of as_of. Company states this is an ongoing negotiation, not yet won.

**What has to be true for this to kill the thesis**: Reimbursement rate gap persists >3 years AND volume growth alone cannot offset the margin gap AND competitors (FMI/Guardant) use their reimbursement advantage to price-compete more aggressively for volume.

**Evidence against a full kill**: Volume growth is strong enough (Oncology +26-29%, Hereditary +23-29%) that revenue is compounding even at a margin disadvantage; MRD is a genuinely differentiated new product category (+500% volume YoY) less exposed to legacy reimbursement-code fights.

**Assessment**: Yellow-to-orange flag — the single most probable, most quantifiable risk in this dossier, directly admitted by the company, and NOT yet resolved.

## Kill Path F2: The "AI Data Moat" Proves Narrower or Slower Than Priced — MODERATE PROBABILITY, HIGH SEVERITY

**Mechanism**: If the market is valuing TEM at an AI/data-company multiple on the WHOLE business (not just the ~25% Data/Applications segment), and the bottleneck_map.md analysis is correct that the network-effect bottleneck is narrow (multimodal integration specifically) and unproven at scale vs FMI/Guardant/a hyperscaler entrant, then a de-rating from "AI company" multiple to "genomic-testing lab company" multiple (which would look more like Guardant Health's or Natera's multiple) could be severe and swift — a classic multiple-compression kill path, similar in character (though not magnitude) to GEV's core risk.

**Status**: Not yet triggered; this is a valuation-perception risk, not a fundamental one. Watch for: (a) a hyperscaler or large pharma announcing an internal/competing multimodal data initiative, (b) FMI or Guardant announcing a comparable imaging+clinical+genomic integration product, (c) deceleration in NRR (currently 126%) or new pharma-deal signings.

**Evidence against**: AstraZeneca/Pathos ~$200M co-investment and reported Merck/Gilead/GSK deals are real, if trade-press-sourced, signals that sophisticated buyers currently see Tempus's data asset as differentiated. This is not (yet) contradicted by any primary evidence gathered in this run.

**Assessment**: The single most important LONG-TERM watch item — see `bottleneck_map.md` for full analysis.

## Kill Path F3: Dilution Compounds Faster Than Fundamental Value Creation — MODERATE PROBABILITY, MODERATE SEVERITY

**Mechanism**: Continued ATM sales + M&A stock consideration + SBC-driven RSU vesting + TWO convertible-note tranches (strikes $69.26 and $84.19) could grow share count faster than the underlying business value compounds, eroding per-share value even if the consolidated business does well. This is the most MECHANICAL/financial-engineering kill path (as opposed to the competitive/regulatory ones above).

**Status**: Shares outstanding +11% in 5 quarters; a live, disclosed $300M ATM facility remains; the May 2026 refinancing added a CLOSER dilution overhang ($69.26 strike, only 15% above as_of_price).

**What has to be true for this to kill the thesis**: Share count growth (historically ~9-11%/year) persistently outpaces per-share intrinsic value growth, meaning the STOCK underperforms even if the BUSINESS succeeds — a scenario where "the company wins but the shareholder doesn't."

**Evidence against**: FY2026 guide to positive adj EBITDA (~$65M) is a real inflection that, if sustained and grown, should eventually reduce the NEED for dilutive financing — but this has not yet happened, and the company's acquisition appetite (4 deals in ~1 year) suggests continued capital deployment ambition that could keep the dilution engine running.

**Assessment**: Real and quantifiable — see `valuation.md`/`model/scenario_model.csv` for how share-count assumptions affect the IRR math directly.

## Kill Path F4: Related-Party Revenue Scrutiny / Governance Event — LOW-MODERATE PROBABILITY, HIGH SEVERITY IF TRIGGERED

**Mechanism**: Fast-growing related-party revenue (SB Tempus + Pathos, ~$21.8M in Q1 2026 vs ~$0.6M a year earlier) sits inside a company where the founder/CEO/Chairman holds 30-votes/share super-voting control (~60% combined voting power) and has an economic interest in at least one of the related-party counterparties (Pathos). If this relationship attracts regulatory, activist, or short-seller scrutiny (a governance-structure combination that has drawn scrutiny at OTHER founder-controlled companies historically), the resulting reputational/legal event — even if ultimately resolved favorably — could trigger a severe de-rating given TEM's already-thin profitability cushion and reliance on capital-markets access.

**Status**: Not triggered. No evidence of impropriety found in this run's primary-source review — this is a STRUCTURAL risk (the combination of super-voting control + related-party revenue growth), not an identified wrongdoing.

**Evidence against a kill**: Related-party transactions are GAAP-disclosed (not hidden); the dollar amounts, while fast-growing, remain a modest fraction of total revenue (~6% of Q1 2026 total revenue, ~25% of the Data/Applications segment specifically).

**Assessment**: A genuine, non-fabricated governance caution flag that should influence SIZE discipline (see `operator_underwriting.md`), even absent any current evidence of a triggering event.

## Kill Path F5: Capital-Markets Access Closes (Macro/Rate Risk) — LOW PROBABILITY (currently), HIGH SEVERITY IF TRIGGERED

**Mechanism**: TEM's financing model depends on continued receptive convertible-debt and equity markets (2 convertible-note offerings within ~12 months, active ATM usage). If risk appetite for unprofitable/thin-margin growth-tech-adjacent names deteriorates sharply (a repeat of 2022's growth-stock de-rating), TEM could face materially higher-cost financing or an inability to refinance on favorable terms as the 2030/2032 Notes approach maturity, forcing either a dilutive equity raise at a depressed price or a genuine liquidity constraint.

**Status**: Not triggered — the May 2026 refinancing (0.00% coupon, oversubscribed enough to exercise the full upsize option) suggests currently STRONG capital-markets access, not weak.

**Assessment**: A macro/tail risk, not a company-specific one; monitor general risk-appetite conditions for growth-stage healthcare-AI financing as a leading indicator.

## Survivability Test (Permanent Impairment Verdict)

| Scenario | FY2028E Revenue | FY2028E Adj EBITDA Margin | Would Capital Be Impaired? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reimbursement gap persists + dilution compounds (bear) | ~$2.0B | ~2-4% (barely profitable) | AT RISK — thin margin cushion, share count could grow 30%+ cumulative, per-share value stagnant or down |
| Base execution (gap partially closes, moat holds ~25% segment) | ~$2.6B | ~8-10% | LIKELY OK but requires real execution on BOTH segments |
| AI/data moat fully validated + reimbursement gap closes | ~$3.2B+ | ~15%+ | Strong — but requires the bottleneck_map.md bull case to resolve favorably, not yet proven |

**Verdict**: Unlike GEV/MSFT, permanent capital impairment IS a plausible (not merely theoretical) outcome for TEM shareholders in the bear case — not because the underlying healthcare-AI/precision-medicine trend is wrong, but because TEM specifically must simultaneously (a) close a company-admitted reimbursement gap, (b) prove the data/AI moat is real and durable rather than narrow/contestable, and (c) avoid dilution outpacing value creation — a THREE-PART compound bet, any one leg of which failing materially dents the case. This is a meaningfully different risk profile than the exemplar dossiers and should be reflected in SIZE discipline regardless of the price-level verdict.

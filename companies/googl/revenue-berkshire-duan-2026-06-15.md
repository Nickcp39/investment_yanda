## 2. Berkshire Hathaway: What Is Fact vs Inference

### Public filing facts

- Berkshire Q1 2026 13F reported Alphabet Class C shares: 3,585,215 shares, value $1.028B.
- Berkshire Q1 2026 13F reported Alphabet Class A shares across managers totaling 54,249,798 shares, value about $15.600B.
- Total Q1 2026 disclosed Alphabet value: about $16.63B as of 2026-03-31.
- Alphabet's June 2026 FWP disclosed a $10B private placement to Berkshire: $5B of Class A at $351.81/share and $5B of Class C at $348.20/share.
- The later 424B5 specifies 14,212,035 Class A shares and 14,359,656 Class C shares to an affiliate of Berkshire Hathaway, subject to closing conditions.

Important caveat:

- This is Berkshire Hathaway, not necessarily Warren Buffett personally. Buffett remained chairman, but Berkshire CEO succession matters. Public filings do not identify whether Buffett, Greg Abel, or another manager initiated the original 13F position.

### Why Berkshire may like Alphabet

These are inferences, not direct Berkshire explanations:

1. The old missed-opportunity thesis finally became investable.
   Buffett and Munger publicly regretted missing Google. Their own operating data through GEICO gave them early evidence that search ads had unusually strong economics: high customer value with almost no incremental cost at the ad-click level.

2. Search is still an extraordinary cash engine.
   Even after ChatGPT / AI search fears, Q1 2026 Search & other revenue grew 19.1%. This supports the idea that AI is, so far, increasing query depth and monetizable engagement rather than simply destroying the search business.

3. Google Cloud may have crossed the quality threshold.
   Cloud grew 63.4% in Q1 2026 and reported a 32.9% operating margin. If Cloud can sustain high margins while converting backlog into revenue, Alphabet becomes Search cash cow + Cloud compounder.

4. TPU / full-stack AI changes the capex story.
   Alphabet can present AI capex as a vertically integrated system: model, TPU, infra, cloud distribution, search distribution, consumer subscriptions. This is different from a company that only buys GPUs and hopes to resell capacity.

5. Berkshire got scale and terms.
   The private placement price appears below the market price cited in contemporary reporting, and the size is meaningful enough for Berkshire's cash pile. It is not a tiny tracking position anymore.

### The hard question for a Buffett lens

Alphabet used to look capital-light. The 2026 version is less capital-light:

- Q1 2026 PPE purchases: $35.674B.
- Q1 2026 buybacks: $0.
- 2026 capex guide cited in the June 2026 FWP: $180B-$190B, with 2027 expected to increase significantly.
- June 2026 equity raise: originally $80B expected aggregate, later described in SEC documents as up to $84.75B including public offerings, ATM program, and private placement.

So the key question is not "is Google a great business?" It is:

> Can Alphabet turn AI capex into durable incremental owner earnings before dilution and depreciation eat the old Search cash machine?

## 3. Duan Yongping / H&H: Did He Buy Google?

### Public 13F facts

H&H International Investment Q4 2025 13F:

- Alphabet Class C (GOOG): 1,855,400 shares.
- Reported value: $582.225M.

H&H International Investment Q1 2026 13F:

- Alphabet Class C (GOOG): 3,706,000 shares.
- Reported value: $1.063B.

Change:

- Shares increased by 1,850,600.
- Share count roughly doubled (+99.7%).
- The filing did not show Alphabet Class A (GOOGL), only Class C (GOOG).

Caveats:

- 13F is delayed and does not show cash, non-U.S. positions, all derivatives, or personal accounts outside the reporting manager.
- H&H International Investment is widely associated with Duan Yongping, but the filing itself is under the manager name, not a personal real-time trade log.

Answer:

> Based on public SEC 13F filings, yes: H&H held GOOG and increased the position materially in Q1 2026.

## 4. Working Thesis

Base case:

Alphabet is no longer just a search advertising company being disrupted by AI. It is a search cash machine trying to convert itself into a full-stack AI infrastructure and application platform. The strongest current evidence is not model demos; it is Q1 2026 revenue acceleration in Search, Cloud, and subscriptions at the same time.

Why Berkshire and Duan may both show up:

- For Berkshire: Search cash flow plus Cloud/AI optionality, with enough proof of earnings power to fit a business-owner lens.
- For Duan: Google resembles the kind of business he tends to understand when product, ecosystem, user habit, and long runway line up. But his public filing also shows he is treating AI leaders as a broader basket, not only Google.

Main risk:

The thesis can fail even if AI usage grows. If capex, depreciation, debt/equity issuance, and regulatory fines absorb the incremental revenue, the business may grow while owner earnings quality deteriorates.

## Search Unit Economics

Alphabet 10-Q defines paid clicks and cost-per-click as Search & other monetization metrics. It states that paid clicks represent user engagement on Google Search properties and other owned properties, and cost-per-click is click-driven revenue divided by paid clicks.

Q1 2026 10-Q MD&A says Google Search & other revenue growth was driven by:

- paid clicks growth of 13%;
- cost-per-click growth of 5%;
- user adoption of AI Overviews and AI Mode, plus advertiser strength.

Q1 2026 Exhibit 99.1 gives:

| Metric | Q1 2025 | Q1 2026 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Google Search & other revenue | 50,702 | 60,399 |
| YouTube ads revenue | 8,927 | 9,883 |
| Google Network revenue | 7,256 | 6,971 |
| Google advertising revenue | 66,885 | 77,253 |
| Google subscriptions, platforms, and devices | 10,379 | 12,384 |
| Google Services total revenue | 77,264 | 89,637 |
| Total TAC | 13,748 | 15,228 |

Derived:

- Search & other revenue growth: 19.13%.
- Google advertising revenue growth: 15.50%.
- TAC / Google advertising revenue: 20.55% in Q1 2025 versus 19.71% in Q1 2026.
- Paid clicks index 100 -> 113.
- CPC index 100 -> 105.
- Implied paid-click x CPC index: 118.65, roughly matching Search revenue growth.

## Financing / Forward Capex

The June 2026 FWP describes an expected $80B equity capital raise, including underwritten offerings, ATM program, and Berkshire private placement. It says proceeds are intended for general corporate purposes including AI infrastructure and global compute.

The 424B5 states:

- 2026 capex expected in the range of $180B to $190B.
- 2027 capex expected to significantly increase versus 2026.
- Berkshire private placement: 14,212,035 Class A shares at about $351.81 and 14,359,656 Class C shares at about $348.20, gross proceeds $10B.

## P0 Interpretation

The new evidence changes the center of the Google analysis:

1. Search still looks strong at the unit-economics level because paid clicks and CPC both grew.
2. TAC rate improved despite high AI investment, which supports the owned-surface strength thesis.
3. Cloud quality improved sharply, but needs multi-quarter validation.
4. The capex bridge is the central unresolved issue: capex and assets not yet in service are rising much faster than OCF.
5. The buyback pause and rising debt show the capital allocation regime has changed.

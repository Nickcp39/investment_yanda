## 0. One-Minute Business Underwriting

Alphabet is not merely a search-engine company. It is a commercial-intent and AI-infrastructure company.

Users reveal intent through Search, YouTube, Android, Chrome, Maps, Gmail, and now Gemini. Advertisers pay for measurable ROI against that intent. Enterprises pay for Google Cloud, Workspace, AI infrastructure, and AI applications. The company may be a quality compounder because the same system reinforces itself: distribution creates data, data improves products and ad relevance, ads and cloud generate cash, cash funds infrastructure and models, and infrastructure strengthens Search, YouTube, Cloud, and Gemini.

The bear case is not that Search revenue collapses tomorrow. The bear case is subtler: AI answers and agents change the interface, Search still grows for a while, but each dollar of revenue requires more compute and capex to defend the moat. In that world reported revenue grows while owner earnings are permanently diluted.

**Research candidate:** yes.

**Investment candidate today:** no. The business is strong enough to study, but the AI capex / owner earnings branch is not underwritten enough to buy.

## 1. Direction Selection - Why Study Google Now?

Google is a valid research target because it sits at the intersection of five live profit pools:

1. **Commercial intent:** Search and shopping-like queries remain one of the highest-value ad surfaces in the world.
2. **AI interface shift:** generative answers may either strengthen Google Search or disintermediate it.
3. **Cloud / AI compute:** Google Cloud is now large, profitable, and growing fast.
4. **Infrastructure bottleneck:** TPU, data centers, power, networking, and server supply may become the real constraint.
5. **Capital allocation controversy:** capex, debt issuance, equity financing, and buyback pause mean the market must re-underwrite owner earnings.

This is exactly the kind of company a buy-side research process should study: obvious quality, major technology transition, high institutional disagreement, and a price that may embed a strong assumption about the future.

## 2. Business Thesis / Quality Gate

### Pass As Research Candidate

The business can be explained in one minute:

> Alphabet aggregates user attention and commercial intent through dominant consumer surfaces, monetizes that intent through advertising, and increasingly sells the same infrastructure and AI stack to enterprises through Google Cloud.

### Why Customers Pay

| Customer | Pays For | Why They Pay | Durability |
|---|---|---|---|
| Advertisers | Search, YouTube, Network ads | Measurable high-intent customer acquisition | Strong on owned surfaces; weaker on Network |
| Enterprises | GCP, Workspace, AI infrastructure, Gemini Enterprise | Compute, data, AI models, productivity workflows | Strong if workload migration and RPO convert |
| Consumers | YouTube Premium, Google One, Gemini plans, devices | Convenience, storage, ad-free media, AI access | Medium; disclosure less granular |
| Developers | APIs, cloud tooling, platform access | Distribution and infrastructure | Medium to strong if Google AI stack stays competitive |

### Thesis Gate

Google passes the "good company candidate" gate, but not the "buyable investment" gate.

The unresolved question is whether AI changes the old search economics in a way Google can capture, or whether it forces Google into a lower-FCF, higher-capex version of the same business.

## 4. Accounting Trend Validation

The financial statements matter, but v2 treats them as trend validation, not as the starting thesis.

### What Looks Strong

- Q1 2026 revenue growth was strong across company level and core Search.
- Operating income grew faster than revenue.
- Search monetization still had both paid-click growth and CPC growth.
- Cloud is now a large and highly profitable segment in the latest quarter.
- TAC rate declined, helped by revenue mix shift.

### What Looks Dangerous

- FCF is under pressure despite strong revenue and operating income.
- Q1 2026 capex consumed most of operating cash flow.
- 2026 capex guide and 2027 expected increase imply the owner earnings problem is forward, not backward.
- Buybacks paused in Q1 2026, so SBC and dilution are more visible.
- Net income is not the clean read because Q1 2026 had a large non-operating equity securities gain.

### Key Read

Revenue and operating profit say the business is strong today. Free cash flow and capital allocation say the next decade depends on whether AI capex earns high incremental returns.

The right accounting question is:

> Does each new dollar of AI capex increase normalized owner earnings per share after maintenance capex, SBC, dilution, and depreciation?

Today, that is not yet proven.

## 5. Industry / Value Chain

The old internet profit pool was:

`user intent -> search results -> ad auction -> advertiser ROI -> Google profit`

The new AI profit pool may be:

`user task -> AI answer / agent -> transaction or decision -> compute cost -> model / infra provider -> ad / subscription / cloud monetization`

Google can win in this new chain because it owns several layers:

- user distribution: Search, Chrome, Android, YouTube, Gmail, Maps;
- data and intent: queries, location, video, app ecosystem;
- monetization: ad auction, subscriptions, enterprise cloud;
- infrastructure: data centers, TPU, network, Gemini models;
- enterprise channel: GCP, Workspace, Vertex / Gemini Enterprise.

But the value chain can also leak profits to:

- Nvidia / Broadcom / semiconductor suppliers;
- power and data center providers;
- cloud competitors;
- AI-native answer engines and agents;
- regulators forcing adtech or distribution changes.

The key industry question is not "AI good or bad." It is:

> In the AI value chain, which layer captures scarce profit: the user interface, the model, the compute, the chip, the enterprise workflow, or the ad transaction?

Google has claims on several layers, but capex shows those claims are becoming more capital intensive.

## 6. Moat / Bottleneck

### Existing Moats

1. **Search habit and default distribution:** still strong; Q1 Search & other growth argues against immediate collapse.
2. **Ad auction liquidity:** advertisers, queries, and conversion measurement reinforce each other.
3. **YouTube network effect:** creator supply, audience habit, recommendation data, ads and subscriptions.
4. **Cloud scale and switching costs:** enterprise workloads, backlog, data gravity, AI infrastructure.
5. **Android / Chrome / ecosystem distribution:** user access and defaults.

### Moat Migration

The relevant question is not whether Google has moats. It does.

The question is whether the moat is migrating from:

`Search box + blue links + ad auction`

to:

`commercial-intent AI interface + full-stack compute + data + agent workflow`

If Google captures this migration, AI capex becomes a new moat. If Google only funds the migration defensively, capex becomes a tax on the old moat.

### Bottleneck Test

Google needs to prove at least one of these bottlenecks:

- AI Search monetization is at least comparable to traditional Search.
- TPU / full-stack infrastructure produces structural cost advantage.
- Cloud AI workloads lock in enterprises at high margins.
- Gemini and Android / Chrome distribution create default AI-agent behavior.
- Scale lets Google serve AI answers cheaper than competitors can.

Today, the evidence is promising but incomplete.

## 7. Operator Underwriting

The operator question is:

> Should minority shareholders trust Page / Brin / Pichai / CFO to allocate hundreds of billions into AI infrastructure?

### Positive Evidence

- Founder control supports long-term investment.
- Cloud execution has improved materially.
- The company disclosed capex intensity and financing plans clearly.
- Google has balance sheet strength and operating cash flow.
- The business has survived multiple platform shifts.

### Negative Evidence

- Dual-class control limits outside correction.
- Q1 buybacks paused while capex and financing rise.
- The company has not yet provided enough public ROI discipline for the AI capex cycle.
- If capex is wrong, the same governance structure that enables long-termism makes it hard for shareholders to stop.

### Operator Verdict

Conditional trust.

The team is credible, but credibility alone does not underwrite $180-190B+ capex. The required evidence is a capital allocation framework: expected utilization, payback period, margin contribution, depreciation life, and incremental ROIC.

## 8. Inversion / Risk

### The Most Dangerous Failure Path

The worst path is not a sudden collapse in Search revenue.

The worst path is:

1. AI answer interfaces reduce the ad inventory and attribution value of traditional search.
2. Google must spend heavily to maintain relevance and AI response quality.
3. Cloud grows, but at lower incremental return than the market expects.
4. Depreciation, capex, SBC, debt, and dilution absorb the operating profit growth.
5. Revenue grows, but owner earnings per share stagnate.
6. The market eventually re-rates Google from quality compounder to capital-intensive utility-like platform.

### Kill Criteria

Any of the following would force a downgrade:

- Search & other growth falls below high single digits for two consecutive quarters without a macro-only explanation.
- AI Overviews / AI Mode monetization is materially below traditional Search.
- Cloud operating margin falls sharply while revenue still grows.
- Capex / OCF stays above roughly 70% without clear ROI evidence.
- FCF/share declines for multiple years.
- Share count turns upward because buybacks remain paused and equity issuance continues.
- DOJ / EU remedies become structural rather than financial.
- Management still does not explain the AI capex return framework after multiple quarters.

### Disconfirming Evidence

The bear case weakens if:

- Search AI monetization is equal to or better than traditional Search.
- Cloud margin stays above 30% through multiple quarters.
- Capex peaks and FCF/share begins growing again.
- Google shows that TPU / full-stack AI materially lowers unit compute cost.
- Management gives a credible capex ROI framework.
- Regulatory outcomes are fines / behavioral remedies rather than structural breakups.

## 9. Valuation / Margin Of Safety

Valuation comes last.

The old v1 model remains useful as a warning, not as the research center. It indicated that at roughly $370, the market was not offering a margin of safety under the local owner-earnings model. As of the latest finance check on 2026-06-16, GOOGL was around $372.63, with market cap around $4.51T.

The correct v2 valuation process is:

1. Start with normalized owner earnings, not reported net income.
2. Strip non-operating gains.
3. Treat SBC as a real cost.
4. Split maintenance capex from growth capex.
5. Build owner earnings branches:
   - defensive capex branch;
   - growth capex branch;
   - mixed / delay branch.
6. Ask what current price already assumes about:
   - Search growth;
   - AI Search RPM;
   - Cloud margin;
   - capex intensity;
   - terminal P/OE;
   - share count.
7. Only then calculate buy-below prices.

### Valuation Verdict

The current price does not rescue the uncertainty. It amplifies it.

At today-like prices, an investor is paying for a successful AI capex conversion without yet having enough evidence that the conversion is complete.

Therefore valuation caps the decision at `WATCH`.

## 10. Decision / Monitoring

### Decision

`WATCH / 0% position`

Google is a high-quality research candidate, but not yet a buy under this process.

The reason is not "financial statements are bad." The reason is that the financial statements show a transition: old cash-generative moats are funding a new capital-intensive AI infrastructure layer. The investment case depends on whether that new layer has high incremental ROIC.

### Upgrade Conditions

Move from `WATCH` to `STARTER` only if several conditions converge:

1. Search & other continues double-digit growth while AI interface penetration rises.
2. AI Search monetization evidence shows no material RPM dilution.
3. Cloud margin remains strong across multiple quarters.
4. Capex / OCF peaks and begins normalizing.
5. FCF/share turns up.
6. Share count does not materially rise.
7. Management provides credible AI capex ROI disclosure.
8. Price offers a real margin of safety under a conservative owner earnings branch.

### Downgrade Conditions

Move from `WATCH` to `REJECT / AVOID` if:

1. Search growth cracks while capex keeps rising.
2. Cloud margin proves temporary.
3. AI capex is repeatedly raised without return evidence.
4. Equity issuance / dilution becomes a recurring funding tool.
5. Regulatory remedies impair Search or adtech structure.
6. The market price still requires a bull branch to work.

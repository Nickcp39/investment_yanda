Status: candidate facts for v2 rerun. These facts are eligible for promotion after final audit reconciles them with the main `facts.md` and `claim_ledger.csv`.

## Layer 1 - Direction Selection

- Alphabet is worth studying now because the same company is exposed to Search advertising, YouTube, Cloud AI infrastructure, consumer AI products, and a very large AI capex cycle.
- Q1 2026 reported revenue was $109.896B, up 22% year over year.
- The investable controversy is not "is Google a good historical business?" but whether AI capex converts into owner earnings per share.

## Layer 2 - Business Thesis / Quality Gate

- Google Search & other revenue was $60.399B in Q1 2026, up 19% year over year.
- YouTube ads revenue was $9.883B in Q1 2026, up 11% year over year.
- Google Network revenue was $6.971B in Q1 2026 and declined year over year.
- Google Cloud revenue was $20.028B in Q1 2026, up 63% year over year.
- The company still passes the research-quality gate because core Search and Cloud were both growing strongly during the AI transition.

- The hardest remaining claims are explicitly open, not silently assumed: AI Search RPM, maintenance/growth capex split, Cloud backlog margin, and numeric AI capex hurdle rate.

## Layer 4 - Accounting Trend Validation

- Q1 2026 operating cash flow was $45.790B.
- Q1 2026 purchases of property and equipment were $35.674B.
- Q1 2026 free cash flow was $10.116B, materially below operating cash flow because capex consumed about 77.9% of OCF.
- Q1 2026 stock repurchases were zero, versus $15.068B in Q1 2025.
- Q1 2026 proceeds from debt issuance, net of costs, were $31.379B.
- 2026 capex guidance is $180B to $190B, and 2027 capex is expected to be significantly higher than 2026.
- These facts move the accounting question from reported EPS to normalized owner earnings after AI capex, depreciation, SBC, dilution, and financing.

## Layer 5 - Industry / Value Chain

- Management says compute capacity is constrained and Cloud revenue would have been higher if more demand could be served.
- Management says just over half of Cloud backlog should convert into revenue within 24 months.
- Cloud backlog margin by workload type is not disclosed, so backlog cannot be treated as fully underwritten profit.

## Layer 6 - Moat / Bottleneck

- Alphabet disclosed paid clicks growth of 13% and cost-per-click growth of 5% for Google Search & other in Q1 2026.
- The product of paid-click growth and CPC growth roughly explains Search revenue growth, supporting near-term ad auction health.
- TAC as a percentage of Google advertising revenue declined from about 20.55% in Q1 2025 to about 19.71% in Q1 2026.
- Management says more than 30% of Search spend uses AI-enabled campaigns, but it does not disclose AI Search RPM.

## Layer 7 - Operator Underwriting

- Management refers to ROIC frameworks and tangible demand signals for capex allocation.
- Management does not disclose a numeric AI capex hurdle rate, utilization target, or payback period.
- Alphabet's June 2026 financing plan included an expected $80B equity raise tied to AI infrastructure and global compute.
- Berkshire's $10B private placement is external validation of financing participation, not proof that the public market price offers margin of safety.
- H&H reported 3.706M Class C shares in Q1 2026 and approximately doubled the share count from Q4 2025, but 13F data is not personal real-time Duan Yongping trading data.

## Layer 8 - Inversion / Risk

- The main failure path is not immediate Search collapse. The more dangerous path is that Search keeps growing while AI capex, depreciation, dilution, and financing costs permanently reduce owner earnings per share.
- A second failure path is that AI-native interfaces capture user tasks before Google can monetize them at comparable RPM.
- A third failure path is that Cloud growth is real but low-margin or high-depreciation, causing revenue growth without equivalent owner earnings growth.

## Layer 9 - Valuation / MOS

- The current valuation layer must use owner earnings branches, not raw GAAP EPS alone.
- Current v2 owner earnings and implied expectations models are drafts until the valuation workstream finalizes financing dilution, capex branches, and current price.

## Layer 10 - Decision / Monitoring

- A buy decision remains blocked until the final monitor defines thresholds for Search growth, CPC, paid clicks, TAC, Cloud margin, capex/OCF, FCF/share, share count, and financing dilution.
- Current v2 position status remains WATCH / 0% unless the checker is upgraded after all workstreams are audited.

## Facts That Must Stay OPEN

- AI Overviews / AI Mode RPM versus traditional Search RPM.
- Maintenance versus growth capex split.
- Cloud backlog margin by workload type.
- Numeric capex hurdle rate or payback period.
- Full effect of ATM, private placement, and any preferred structure on share count and per-share value.

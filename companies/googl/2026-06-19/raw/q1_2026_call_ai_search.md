## Why This Source Matters

The SEC filings prove the numbers. The call transcript helps explain management's causal story:

- why Search still grew despite AI interface changes;
- whether management sees AI as usage expansion or monetization risk;
- how management frames capex allocation and ROIC;
- what Cloud backlog means and when it may convert.

---

## Search / AI Interface

Management said Search & other grew 19% and that AI Mode / AI Overviews were bringing users back to Search more often. The transcript states that people are using AI experiences and that queries reached an all-time high.

For monetization, Philipp Schindler framed Search as an expansionary moment rather than a cannibalization moment:

- AI Overviews and AI Mode are said to drive greater Search usage and growth in overall queries, including commercial queries.
- Management said AI improves intent understanding and can help monetize longer, more complex searches that were previously difficult.
- Ads in new AI experiences are still early. Management discussed Direct Offers in AI Mode and tests with retail partners.
- On Gemini app monetization, management said subscriptions and AI plans are current focus, and ads may come later if they are useful to users.

P0 implication:

Search is not showing revenue-level damage yet. Management's story is that AI expands query volume and commercial intent coverage. However, no disclosed metric directly proves AI Search RPM is equal to or better than traditional Search RPM. That remains the top open question.

---

## Advertiser Tools / Coverage

Management highlighted AI Max and Performance Max adoption:

- more than 30% of customers' Search spend uses AI-enabled campaigns;
- AI is being deployed across ads infrastructure;
- new tools are intended to improve matching, targeting, creative, and conversion performance.

P0 implication:

The Search growth branch has a plausible mechanism beyond generic traffic growth: better ad relevance and better monetization of complex queries. The missing piece is quantitative conversion / ROI proof by AI product cohort.

---

## Capex / Compute Allocation

Anat Ashkenazi said Q1 capex was mostly technical infrastructure for AI opportunities. She said approximately 60% of technical infrastructure investment was in servers and 40% in data centers and networking equipment.

Management also said:

- compute is constrained in the near term;
- Cloud revenue would have been higher if demand could be met;
- capacity allocation starts with frontier model R&D needs, then long-range plans across Search, YouTube, Cloud, and other core areas;
- management refers to ROIC frameworks and tangible demand signals, but the transcript does not give a numeric hurdle rate.

P0 implication:

The call improves the capex story: management is not presenting capex as blind spending; it ties capex to constrained demand and ROIC frameworks. But because no numeric ROIC threshold, utilization target, or payback period is disclosed, the capex bridge remains unfinished.

---

## Cloud / Backlog

Management said Cloud revenue grew 63%, backlog was over $460B, and enterprise AI solutions became a primary growth driver for Cloud.

For backlog conversion:

- a majority of the backlog is still GCP agreements;
- just over half of backlog is expected to convert into revenue in the next 24 months;
- TPU hardware sales are included in Cloud backlog;
- a small percent of TPU hardware sales is expected later in 2026, with most in 2027.

P0 implication:

Backlog quality is better than just a headline number because management gives rough conversion timing. But margin by backlog type remains open, especially for large AI infrastructure / TPU-related contracts.

---

## Cloud Margin

Anat Ashkenazi attributed Cloud margin expansion to strong top-line growth, efficiency, technical infrastructure productivity, internal AI usage, and real estate optimization. She also warned that depreciation from higher capex will be a headwind.

P0 implication:

Cloud margin improvement is plausible, not merely accounting noise, but the depreciation headwind means the current 32%+ margin cannot be extrapolated without multi-quarter validation.

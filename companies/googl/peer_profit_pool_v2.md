## 1. Direction Selection Verdict

Google is absolutely one of the companies worth studying, but it is not the cleanest single-name expression of every AI profit pool.

The reason to study Google is specific: it is the public company where the old internet profit pool and the new AI profit pool collide most visibly. Search ads still monetize commercial intent at enormous scale, while AI agents and answer engines threaten to change who owns the user task, who gets attribution, and how much compute is needed per monetized interaction. At the same time, Google is spending like an AI infrastructure company and trying to turn that capex into GCP, TPU, Gemini, Search, YouTube, and agentic commerce economics.

The buy-side framing should not be "AI winner or loser." It should be:

> Does Google remain the default owner of commercial intent as the interface shifts from search results to AI answers and agents, and can it earn attractive incremental returns on the AI infrastructure required to defend and extend that position?

Research selection:
- Study Google for the `commercial intent transition + AI capex conversion` problem.
- Prefer NVDA / AVGO / TSM if the desired expression is `current AI compute bottleneck monetization`.
- Prefer MSFT if the desired expression is `enterprise workflow + cloud + AI software distribution`.
- Prefer AMZN if the desired expression is `AWS compute + commerce ads + first-party retail data`.
- Prefer META if the desired expression is `AI-enhanced consumer ads with less search-query cannibalization`.
- Prefer ORCL only for a higher-risk `AI cloud backlog / financing / capacity buildout` expression.
- AI-native agents are the most relevant attack surface, but private-company economics are not public enough to underwrite from filings.

## 2. Where The Money Is

| Profit pool | Current winner(s) | Why money accrues there | Google position | Better pure-play expression? |
|---|---|---|---|---|

| Accelerators / networking | NVDA, AVGO | Scarce chips, networking, software ecosystem, custom ASICs | Google is buyer and partial substitute via TPU; still pays upstream AI tax | NVDA / AVGO |
| Advanced foundry / packaging | TSMC | Advanced nodes, CoWoS/advanced packaging, yield, customer trust | Google indirect customer through TPU/supplier chain | TSM |
| Data centers / power | Hyperscalers, utilities, power equipment, land/EPC suppliers | Scarce grid interconnection, land, power, cooling, transformers | Google owns scale but capex and energy are cost pressure | Utilities/power equipment may be cleaner, but not covered here |

Core conclusion: Google's current profit is still in ads, especially Search. The fastest-growing marginal dollars in the AI stack are increasingly captured upstream by semis and advanced manufacturing, and downstream by enterprise cloud commitments. Google is rare because it participates in both ends, but that also makes it harder to underwrite: the company is both a toll collector and a toll payer.

## 3. Peer Map

### Google / Alphabet

Primary source anchors:
- Alphabet Q1 2026 10-Q: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1652044/000165204426000048/goog-20260331.htm
- Alphabet Q1 2026 earnings release: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1652044/000165204426000043/googexhibit991q12026.htm
- Alphabet Q1 2026 call transcript: https://abc.xyz/investor/events/event-details/2026/2026-Q1-Earnings-Call-2026-nW8kCrBAKS/default.aspx
- Alphabet FY2025 10-K: https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1652044/000165204426000018/goog-20251231.htm

What Google owns:
- Search commercial intent.
- YouTube attention and creator ecosystem.
- Android / Chrome / Maps / Gmail / Workspace distribution.
- Google Ads auction and measurement infrastructure.
- GCP / Workspace enterprise channel.
- Gemini models, DeepMind R&D, TPUs, data centers, network.

What Google does not fully own:
- The highest-margin external accelerator pool, where NVDA has current pricing power.
- Foundry capacity, where TSMC remains the structural bottleneck.
- Power / grid interconnection / energy cost.
- The entire agent interface, because OpenAI, Microsoft, Anthropic, Perplexity, Apple, and vertical SaaS agents can capture task initiation.

Key current facts:
- Q1 2026 revenue $109.9B, +22%; operating income $39.7B, operating margin 36.1%.
- Q1 2026 Google Services revenue $89.6B; Services OI $40.6B; Services margin 45.3%.
- Q1 2026 Search & other revenue $60.4B, +19%; paid clicks +13%, CPC +5%.
- Q1 2026 YouTube ads revenue $9.9B, +11%.
- Q1 2026 Google Network revenue $7.0B, down 4%; Network impressions down 9%.
- Q1 2026 Google Cloud revenue $20.0B, +63%; Cloud OI $6.6B; Cloud margin 32.9%; backlog $462B, with just over 50% expected to convert within 24 months.
- Q1 2026 capex $35.7B; full-year 2026 capex guide $180-190B; 2027 capex expected to significantly increase.

Why study it:
- It is the best public test case for whether AI is accretive to the old ad moat or becomes a capex tax on it.
- If AI expands query volume, monetizable coverage, and GCP demand, Google owns multiple compounding levers.
- If AI agents disintermediate search and capex rises faster than owner earnings, Google can look optically strong while FCF/share disappoints.

Why not the cleanest AI expression:
- The most immediate AI dollar capture is upstream in chips and foundry.
- Google's capex creates a delayed-payback problem: revenue and backlog are visible now; maintenance/growth capex split and long-run ROIC are not.

### Microsoft

Primary source anchors:
- Microsoft FY2026 Q3 earnings release: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/earnings/fy-2026-q3/press-release-webcast
- Microsoft FY2026 Q3 call transcript: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/investor/events/fy-2026/earnings-fy-2026-q3

Current facts:
- Q3 FY2026 revenue $82.9B, +18%; operating income $38.4B, +20%.
- Microsoft Cloud revenue $54.5B, +29%; commercial RPO $627B, +99%.
- Azure and other cloud services revenue +40%.
- AI business annual revenue run-rate surpassed $37B, +123%.
- Q3 capex $31.9B; roughly two-thirds for short-lived assets, primarily GPUs and CPUs.
- Microsoft Cloud gross margin 66%, down year over year due to AI investments.

Position versus Google:
- Microsoft is stronger in enterprise workflow distribution: Windows, Office, Teams, GitHub, Dynamics, Azure, LinkedIn.
- It is less exposed to Search ad disintermediation, though Bing/Search advertising is a smaller growth vector.
- It depends more explicitly on OpenAI as strategic model/application partner, while Google owns more of its model stack internally.
- For an AI software-and-enterprise workflow thesis, MSFT is probably cleaner than Google.
- For a commercial intent / Search transition thesis, Google is the primary company to study.

Profit-pool read:
- MSFT captures money at the application/workflow layer and cloud layer.
- Its bottleneck is enterprise distribution, not consumer commercial intent.
- Its capex problem is similar to Google, but its monetization path runs through seats, usage, Azure consumption, and RPO rather than ad RPM.

Profit-pool read:
- AWS remains one of the strongest cloud profit pools.
- Amazon Ads is a major alternative to Google for purchase-intent dollars.
- Amazon's capex pressure is already visible in FCF, so the owner-earnings question is similar to Google.

Position versus Google:
- NVIDIA is the clearest current AI toll collector.

### AI-native agents and search alternatives

Primary source anchors:
- OpenAI ChatGPT agent product page: https://chatgpt.com/features/agent/
- OpenAI ChatGPT agent launch: https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-agent/
- OpenAI workspace agents launch: https://openai.com/index/introducing-workspace-agents-in-chatgpt/

Public economics:
- `OPEN`: OpenAI, Anthropic, Perplexity, and many agent-native companies are private or do not provide filing-level revenue, gross margin, capex, or churn disclosures.
- Any revenue/margin/share claims would require B/C sources and are intentionally not used here as underwriting numbers.

Why they matter anyway:
- Agents can own task initiation, browser navigation, research, commerce, forms, and workflow execution.
- If users ask an agent to complete the job rather than search the web, the traditional Search results page can lose impressions, clicks, attribution, and merchant relationship control.
- If the agent chooses merchants, cloud apps, or content sources, it may become the new demand allocator.

Google's answer:
- Put AI Overviews, AI Mode, Gemini, Lens, and agentic commerce back into Google-controlled surfaces.
- Move ad formats from links to answers/actions.
- Use Universal Commerce Protocol and merchant integrations to keep shopping and checkout inside Google-controlled journeys.
- Use Android/Chrome/Search/Gmail/Workspace distribution to make Gemini/AI Mode a default workflow layer.

Investment read:
- AI-native agents are the highest-concept threat to Google, but not currently the best public-company vehicle unless accessed through MSFT/OpenAI exposure, AMZN/Anthropic exposure, or Google itself.

## 5. Why Google Is Still Worth Research Time

Google deserves research time because it has five rare traits in one public company:

1. It owns the incumbent commercial-intent profit pool.
2. It owns massive consumer distribution where the AI interface can be deployed.
3. It has a credible model and AI infrastructure stack, including TPUs.
4. It has a fast-growing enterprise cloud business with visible backlog.
5. Its capex cycle is large enough to change owner earnings, making the stock genuinely debatable.

That combination creates a high-quality research setup: strong current business, credible technical assets, real transition risk, and unresolved economics.

The most important question is not whether Google can build AI products. It can. The question is whether the new AI interface preserves or improves the old Search economics after including compute, TAC, depreciation, energy, SBC, and dilution.

## 6. Why Other Companies May Be Better AI Vehicles

If the investor wants direct evidence of AI money being captured now:
- NVDA, AVGO, and TSM are better candidates. Their disclosures show immediate revenue growth and high margins at the bottleneck layers.

If the investor wants enterprise workflow lock-in:
- MSFT is probably better. Its distribution in Office/Teams/GitHub/Azure gives it a cleaner path from AI features to enterprise ARPU and consumption.

If the investor wants commerce-ad budgets:
- AMZN is better for retail-media / marketplace intent.

If the investor wants AI-enhanced consumer ads without Search-result-page risk:
- META is cleaner, although its capex intensity is also rising sharply.

If the investor wants high-beta contracted AI infrastructure:
- ORCL is more direct, but higher risk because the financing, customer concentration, and buildout assumptions are more fragile.

Google is best when the thesis is specifically:

> The market is mispricing whether the owner of Search commercial intent can migrate that intent into AI answers, agents, and cloud infrastructure without permanently diluting free cash flow.

## 7. Research Implications For The GOOGL Memo

What must be underwritten before Google can move from `WATCH` to investable:
- Search AI monetization: AI Overviews / AI Mode RPM vs traditional Search RPM.
- Query coverage: whether AI increases monetizable commercial queries or merely shifts them into more expensive answer formats.
- Agentic commerce: whether Google controls checkout/merchant attribution or agents route around Google.
- Cloud backlog quality: margin and conversion of the $462B backlog, especially TPU hardware and large AI infrastructure deals.
- TPU economics: actual cost/performance and mix of self-use vs external sales.
- Capex quality: maintenance vs growth split, utilization, depreciation life, energy cost, and incremental ROIC.
- Upstream leakage: how much AI value is captured by NVDA/AVGO/TSM/power suppliers before Google earns a dollar.

Working selection conclusion:
- `Research candidate: YES`.
- `Investment candidate from this module alone: NOT YET`.
- `Best relative thesis`: Google is a high-quality but capex-heavy call option on retaining commercial intent in the agentic AI era.
- `Cleaner AI bottleneck alternatives`: NVDA / AVGO / TSM.
- `Cleaner enterprise workflow alternative`: MSFT.
- `Cleaner retail-media alternative`: AMZN.
- `Cleaner AI-ad optimization alternative`: META.

## 9. Remaining Blockers

- No public disclosure of AI Overviews / AI Mode RPM relative to classic Search.
- No public disclosure of Search commercial-query share shift to AI Mode, Gemini app, or external agents.
- No quantified TPU cost/performance advantage or TPU vs GPU mix.
- No clean maintenance vs growth capex split for Alphabet's AI buildout.
- No disclosed margin mix for GCP backlog by core cloud, AI infrastructure, enterprise AI applications, and TPU hardware sales.
- AI-native agent companies are mostly private; public revenue/margin/capex economics remain unavailable without B/C sources.

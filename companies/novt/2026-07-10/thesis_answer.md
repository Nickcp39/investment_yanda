# NOVT — Batch Thesis Answer (ai_robotics_2026-07-10 §1) — as_of 2026-07-10

The batch question: *is there a robotics-chain leader with "demand exploded + bottleneck locked + already printing cash" (an NVDA-of-robotics), or is it option value that only pays off when humanoids scale → WATCH?* For NOVT, the honest answer is **the latter, with a twist**: the robotics-**components** business is real and profitable today, but it is a **modest, cyclical slice on a fully-priced quality compounder**, and the embodied-AI/humanoid piece is **prototype-stage option value (2027+)**. NOVT is **not** the robotics NVDA.

- Structured bracket:
  - **Industrial "Robotics & Automation" business** (ATI end-of-arm tooling + Celera/precision motion into industrial robots, cobots, warehouse automation) — a named business unit management gives bookings/revenue growth for. Sits inside AET. Estimated **~15–20% of total revenue** (~$150–200M). *Real, deployed, cash-generative.*
  - **Surgical-robotics components** (motion/photonics/light engines into robotic & minimally-invasive surgery) — inside Medical Solutions' "advanced surgery" (the +$33.7M FY2025 growth driver). A further slice, not separately sized.
  - **Humanoid / embodied-AI specifically**: **<1–2% of revenue, effectively pre-revenue** (Holoscan servo-drive selection is "prototype phase," meaningful 2027+).
- **Bottom line: ~15–25% of revenue is "robotics-linked" (mostly industrial automation + surgical components); embodied-AI/humanoid is a rounding error today.** The base is **medical 53% + advanced-industrial 47%**, most of it *not* robotics.

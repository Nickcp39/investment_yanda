# NOVT Bottleneck Map — as_of 2026-07-10

**Question: is Novanta a bottleneck the robotics industry must route through (NVDA-style), or a good component supplier?**

| Test | NVDA (reference) | NOVT |
|---|---|---|
| "Can't build the product without it"? | Yes (AI compute) | **No** — competitors exist for encoders, servo drives, lasers, end-of-arm tooling |
| Pricing power from scarcity | Extreme | **Moderate** (designed-in switching cost, not scarcity monopoly) |
| Demand visibly exploded into cash | Yes (DC +92% YoY) | **No** — robotics revenue +7%; organic +3% |
| Share of end-product value | Large | **Small** (component slice) |
| Supply barrier for a challenger | High (CUDA/full stack) | **Med-high in niche, low in the hyped humanoid land-grab** |

## Verdict
NOVT is a **conditional bottleneck within narrow niches** (a specific qualified precision part in a specific OEM platform is hard to swap mid-cycle) but **not an industry chokepoint**. The moat is switching-cost + know-how, which is real and durable for the installed base, but it does not confer NVDA-like "must route through us" economics. **On the batch's own test — "demand exploded + bottleneck locked + already cash" — NOVT fails the bottleneck leg and half-passes the cash leg (single-digit).** The humanoid/embodied-AI angle is a *future* potential bottleneck-adjacent position (safety-certified motion), not a present one.

# NOVT Working Memo v1 — as_of 2026-07-10

**Verdict: WATCH (new money 0%). Business good, price expensive, robotics mostly option.** status = DECISION_DRAFT (~60% complete).

## The one-paragraph case
Novanta is a high-quality precision-components compounder — lasers/beam-steering, precision motion (encoders, servo drives), robotic end-of-arm tooling, and medical devices/consumables — sold designed-in to medical (53%) and advanced-industrial (47%) OEMs at ~44% gross margins with a net-cash balance sheet. The batch asks whether it is an "NVDA-of-robotics" (demand exploded + bottleneck + already cash). It is **not**: the robotics business is real but ~15–25% of revenue (industrial-heavy, revenue only +7%), the humanoid/embodied-AI angle (NVIDIA Holoscan) is <1–2% and prototype-stage (management points to 2027), and Novanta is a good component moat rather than a chokepoint. Meanwhile the stock trades at ~47x adjusted EPS / ~23x forward EV/EBITDA with a sub-1% FCF yield, giving a base 5-year IRR of ~+1% versus an 8% hurdle. Good business, no margin of safety, robotics reason-to-own still anticipation → **WATCH**.

## Why not a starter on the robotics option?
Because you'd be paying a 47x multiple today for a <2% pre-revenue humanoid sliver plus a single-digit industrial-robotics business, with base-case returns below the hurdle even before the option. The option is real but cheap-to-wait-for: 2027 is management's own timeline, and the price gives no downside protection (bear ~−36% toward the 52-week low). Buy the business when the price pays you to wait (~$100–120) or when robotics converts bookings→sustained double-digit revenue.

## What would change my mind (→ STARTER)
1. De-rate into ~$100–120 (restores ≥8% base IRR), or
2. Robotics & Automation revenue accelerates to sustained double-digit + book-to-bill >1.1 for 2+ quarters (Q1's +37% bookings wasn't pull-forward) + FY26 OCF lands in the $145–185M guide (earnings quality confirms).

## Key risks
Multiple compression on a single-digit grower (base risk), bookings pull-forward reversing, humanoid option never converting, industrial cyclicality, M&A/roll-up missteps. No solvency risk (net cash).

# NOVT Business Model — as_of 2026-07-10

## One sentence
Novanta designs and sells **mission-critical precision sub-components** — photonics (lasers, beam steering), precision motion (encoders, servo drives, air-bearing spindles), robotic end-of-arm tooling, and medical devices (insufflators, light engines, OR technology, disposables) — that get **designed into** OEM capital equipment in medical and advanced-industrial markets.

## Revenue engines (FY2025, $980.6M)
| Segment | Rev | GM | Op profit | What it is | Robotics linkage |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| **Automation Enabling Technologies** | $500.8M (51%) | 47.8% | $114.5M | Lasers/beam steering (precision manufacturing), robotic & precision motion (Celera/Zettlex), **ATI end-of-arm tooling**, air-bearing spindles | **High** — the "Robotics & Automation" business + humanoid/Holoscan option live here |
| **Medical Solutions** | $479.8M (49%) | 41.6% | $51.2M | Insufflators/pumps + **disposables**, imaging/ID/RFID, advanced motion, light engines, integrated OR | **Partial** — supplies motion/photonics into robotic & minimally-invasive **surgery** |

- End markets: **medical ~53%, advanced industrial ~47%**.
- Recurring: medical **consumables/disposables now 15% of total revenue** — the highest-quality slice (razor-blade, sticky).
- Model = "arms dealer" of precision components: **designed-in, spec'd into multi-year OEM platforms, high switching cost, low customer concentration**, priced on performance not commodity cost.

## 10-year test (would I be happy owning it unlisted for 10y?)
- **For:** durable ~44% gross margins; designed-in switching cost; net-cash balance sheet; recurring consumables growing; multiple secular demand vectors (minimally-invasive surgery, industrial automation, and an optional humanoid tailwind); disciplined serial acquirer.
- **Against:** it is a **component supplier, not a system owner** — no chokepoint monopoly; organic growth is only **mid-single-digit** (FY2025 organic ~flat-to-low; Q1 2026 +3% organic); cyclically exposed to industrial capex; heavy reliance on M&A + amortization makes GAAP earnings thin (FY2025 FCF just $54M); "robotics" is not separately disclosed.
- Verdict: **good, ownable business — not exceptional/monopoly.** A quality specialty-components compounder, cyclical at the edges.

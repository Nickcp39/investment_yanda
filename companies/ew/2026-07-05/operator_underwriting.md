## CEO: Bernard J. Zovighian

**Tenure**: CEO since May 2023 (director since 2023); President immediately prior. ~10 years total at Edwards (joined January 2015).

**Career path AT Edwards (unusually deep internal operating history for a current-generation CEO)**:
- Jan 2015: VP & GM, Surgical Structural Heart business
- 2016-2018: Corporate VP, Surgical business (broader P&L responsibility)
- Jan 2018: Corporate VP, **newly-formed TMTT business** — built this from its founding
- 2023: President, then CEO

**Pre-Edwards**: ~20 years at Johnson & Johnson, including regional leadership outside the US and worldwide president of one J&J division. Dual master's degrees (science + business) from Universities of Marseille, France.

## Underwriting Grade: **4/5**

### Reasons for a strong (not perfect) grade:

**Positive signals**:
1. **Genuine hands-on franchise-building track record**: Zovighian personally ran BOTH of Edwards' two largest segments (Surgical, then TMTT from inception) before becoming CEO — this is a materially deeper operating pedigree than a CEO who rose through finance/corporate-development/general-management-only paths. He built TMTT, which is now the company's fastest-growing segment (+56.4% FY2025) and a direct product of the strategy he ran for 5 years before ascending.
2. **Financial execution since taking over has been strong and improving**: two consecutive quarterly guidance RAISES (Q4 2025 and Q1 2026 reports, both since Zovighian became CEO) and accelerating revenue growth (Q1 2026 +16.7% YoY vs FY2025's own +11.5%) — this is a genuinely positive, not just steady, trend under his tenure.
3. **Capital allocation discipline evident**: maintains a net-cash balance sheet (~$4.0B cash+investments vs ~$600M debt) while simultaneously funding a $500M ASR (Feb 2026), a bolt-on acquisition (Autus Valve Technologies, $128.9M), AND the (ultimately blocked) $945M JenaValve bid — shows willingness to deploy capital across multiple channels without over-levering.
4. **Handled the JenaValve setback without disrupting core-business momentum**: the FTC block and resulting $99.8M impairment occurred in the SAME reporting window Edwards raised full-year guidance (Q1 2026) — suggests the core TAVR/TMTT execution is not dependent on, or destabilized by, this one M&A misstep.

**Concerns / reasons this is not a 5/5**:
1. **JenaValve M&A judgment/antitrust-risk assessment is a real, recent miss**: pursuing an acquisition that the FTC successfully argued would create a duopoly-to-monopoly in a specific sub-indication (aortic regurgitation TAVR) suggests either (a) a genuine misjudgment of antitrust risk by Edwards' corporate development team, or (b) a calculated bet that the deal was worth pursuing even with meaningful blocking risk. Either way, it cost $99.8M in a written-off impairment and management time/legal cost through a six-day federal trial. This is Zovighian's first major public M&A setback as CEO and should be weighed, even though it did not derail the core business.
2. **Litigation expense spiked 8x (FY2024→FY2025, $40.4M→$325.4M) without a clearly disclosed, specific cause found this run**: whether this reflects (a) prudent, aggressive IP enforcement that will pay off, (b) unfavorable settlement of claims against Edwards, or (c) some mix, is not distinguishable from available sources (facts.md O2). A more transparent operator would typically have this more clearly explained in earnings-call commentary — worth checking the actual transcript on next refresh before fully absolving this as "normal industry litigiousness."
3. **Relatively short CEO tenure (since May 2023, ~3 years/2 full fiscal years of full public CEO track record)**: strong results so far, but the sample size for judging multi-cycle capital-allocation and crisis-management judgment (e.g., how he'd navigate a genuine down-cycle, not just a strong-growth period) is still limited.

## Comparison to GEHC (same batch, exemplar reference)

GEHC's CEO Arduini has a longer track record (since the 2023 spin-off, plus prior public-company CEO experience at Integra LifeSciences) but is currently navigating THREE consecutive quarters of margin compression and a guidance CUT. Zovighian, by contrast, has a shorter pure-CEO tenure but a materially stronger recent execution record (two consecutive guidance RAISES, accelerating growth) and a demonstrably deeper hands-on operating history within the specific franchises he now oversees (he built TMTT himself). On a pure "trust the current trajectory" basis, Zovighian's grade edges GEHC's Arduini higher this run — though Zovighian's shorter full-CEO track record and the unresolved JenaValve/litigation questions keep this at 4/5 rather than higher.

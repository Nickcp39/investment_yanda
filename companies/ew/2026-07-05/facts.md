This reads as a GENUINE momentum-driven high, not a data artifact — but it also means there is essentially **zero margin of safety at the current price** by definition (any high-hug situation is definitionally a "priced for continued perfection" setup), which is the central valuation tension for this dossier (see valuation.md).
- I2. **GAAP vs adjusted earnings gap is real and one-off-driven, not core-business-driven**: FY2025 GAAP EPS $1.81 vs adjusted $2.56 (a 41% gap) is driven almost entirely by the $325.4M litigation + $146.9M impairment charges (E16), both of which are genuinely non-recurring in NATURE (a specific lawsuit outcome, a specific blocked acquisition) even if litigation as a CATEGORY recurs most years in this industry (E41-E42 show a history of med-tech patent wars). Trailing P/E of 51.14x (using depressed GAAP EPS) vs forward P/E of 30.80x (using clean guided-EPS basis) is the same phenomenon showing up in the multiple — the forward multiple is the more decision-relevant one, and even 30.8x is not cheap for a med-tech, but it is far more reasonable than the headline trailing figure suggests.
- I3. **TMTT is the standout growth engine and CEO Zovighian personally built it**: TMTT grew +56.4% (FY2025) then accelerated further in guidance to 35-45% for FY2026 (E25) — this is Edwards' second-largest growth vector after TAVR's installed base, and notably, current CEO Zovighian ran TMTT from its founding (2018) through his promotion to President then CEO (E9). This is a genuinely unusual degree of hands-on franchise-building experience directly relevant to the company's #2 growth pillar — a positive operator signal (see operator_underwriting.md).
- I4. **JenaValve FTC block is a real strategic setback, but contained**: The blocked $945M JenaValve deal (E27-E31) cost Edwards a $99.8M impairment and removed a bolt-on aortic-regurgitation sub-indication asset from the roadmap. This is a genuine near-term negative (management's M&A judgment/antitrust-risk-assessment is now in question for that one deal), but it is NOT existential — Edwards continued to raise guidance in the SAME quarter the deal was abandoned (E26), suggesting the core TAVR/TMTT franchise is not dependent on this bolt-on. Flagged as a monitorable governance/execution data point, not a thesis-breaker.
- I5. **China is the single most important CAREER-RELEVANT finding and it is structurally unfavorable to Edwards today**: Edwards entered China LATE (2020) after Venus Medtech, JC Medical, and CardioFlow Medtech already had approvals (2017-2019) (E43), and the result is a striking role-reversal from Edwards' global position: Edwards is a ~60% global TAVR share leader, but Chinese domestic players hold **~80% of the China TAVR market** (E44-E45) — the inverse of GEHC's situation (where the incumbent GPS group still leads high-end share even as United Imaging gains fast). Venus Medtech's clinical data claims (E48) and ~40% price advantage look like durable, structural China-specific moats FOR the domestic players, not transient. However, the China TAVR market itself is still small in absolute revenue terms today (E49) — this is a "lost the early beachhead, but the war is still mostly ahead" situation given the 55.5% China TAVR market CAGR forecast (E49), not a "China revenue already collapsing" situation like GEHC's confirmed -15% YoY. The correct read for Nick's thesis: **watch whether Edwards' global R&D/imaging-integration/clinical-data advantages (68 years of valve engineering, PARTNER-trial-scale evidence base) can be deployed to defend against Venus Medtech/Peijia/JC Medical as China's TAVR market scales 55%/yr from a small base** — this is a live, unresolved competitive question, not a settled one either way.
- I6. **Litigation is elevated and this run could not identify the SPECIFIC Q4 2025 case(s) driving the $208.6M charge**: Unlike GEHC (which had a clean, discrete, one-off "resolved in-quarter" explanation for its PDx supplier issue), Edwards' litigation expense TRIPLED+ in a single year ($40.4M FY2024 → $325.4M FY2025) without this run identifying the specific new case(s) by name (only the older/historical Meril and Boston Scientific matters were confirmed, both NOT contemporaneous with the FY2025 charge). This is a material OPEN item — flagged prominently for the next refresh, since patent litigation risk quantum matters a great deal to a company whose core moat IS its patent-protected device IP.
- I7. **R&D intensity (18% of sales) + trial cadence (PARTNER 3/2 long-term data, SAPIEN M3 approval, CMS NCD reconsideration) show a genuinely evidence-driven moat**: This is the clearest "clinical-evidence moat" story in the S&P500-medical batch so far — Edwards' core competitive advantage is not manufacturing scale or price, it is a multi-decade accumulation of randomized-trial evidence (PARTNER program) that shapes clinical guidelines (ESC 2021 update referenced, E38) and payer coverage decisions (CMS NCD, E37) in Edwards' favor. This is a genuinely durable, hard-to-replicate moat distinct from GEHC's more commoditizable hardware-plus-service model — but it is also PRECISELY the kind of moat that a fast-follower with "good enough" 10-year clinical data (Venus Medtech's claim, E48) could erode over a decade-plus horizon, first in China, then potentially in other price-sensitive EM markets.

## Key Value-Chain Chokepoints

1. **Clinical trial evidence generation (upstream)**: This is Edwards' single biggest structural advantage — PARTNER 3 (7yr) and PARTNER 2 (10yr) data (E36) represent well over a decade of accumulated randomized evidence that a new entrant literally cannot compress in time, only in trial size/speed. This is the chokepoint that is HARDEST for China domestic players to replicate quickly, but Venus Medtech's own 10-year VenusA-Valve data claim (E48) shows they are attempting exactly this catch-up race.
2. **Regulatory approval timing (China-specific chokepoint, currently UNFAVORABLE to Edwards)**: Edwards entered China 2020, a full 1-3 years after domestic competitors (E43) — in a chokepoint-driven business, being late to a chokepoint (regulatory approval) has a durable, compounding negative effect on installed clinical relationships, physician training/preference, and case-volume-driven data collection, all of which favor whoever got there first.
3. **Guideline citation (mid-chain)**: The 2021 ESC guideline update referenced as "reshaping clinical discussions" (E38) shows how a SINGLE guideline revision event can be a discrete, quotable catalyst for demand expansion — this is a chokepoint Edwards actively manages via its trial-evidence pipeline, but it is also a chokepoint outside Edwards' direct control (guideline committees are independent).
4. **Payer coverage (CMS NCD reconsideration, E37)**: A pending 2026 catalyst — if CMS expands TAVR coverage criteria, this directly expands Edwards' (and Medtronic/Abbott's) addressable US patient population without any new product launch required. This is a binary, exogenous, high-leverage catalyst.
5. **Manufacturing/supply (least differentiated risk this run)**: No supply-chain or manufacturing-capacity constraint was identified in any source this run — R&D and clinical evidence, not manufacturing throughput, appear to be the binding constraints on growth (contrast with a capacity-constrained business).

## China-Specific Value Chain Divergence (career-relevant)

In China, the value chain sequence is materially different in ONE critical respect: **domestic competitors reached the regulatory-approval chokepoint 1-3 years before Edwards** (E43), meaning they built physician relationships, case-volume data, and guideline/reimbursement traction in China WHILE Edwards was still pre-approval. This is analogous to (but structurally distinct from) GEHC's China problem — GEHC's China erosion (per the exemplar dossier) is about an ALREADY-established incumbent losing share to a fast-following domestic entrant on price/localization; Edwards' China problem is closer to NEVER having established the incumbent position domestically in the first place, because it arrived late to the regulatory gate. This is a more structurally difficult position to reverse (you cannot "win back" share you never held), though the offsetting factor is that China's TAVR market is still small and growing 55%/yr (E49) — meaning the total pie is expanding fast enough that Edwards' ABSOLUTE China revenue could still grow meaningfully even while its RELATIVE share stays low, if it can convert its global evidence base into incremental share gains as the market matures and higher-end/imported-device segments potentially develop (unverified this run — flagged OPEN).

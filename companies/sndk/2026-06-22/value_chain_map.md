```
Equipment (ASML/AMAT/Lam/TEL)
        |

  (wafer fab; ~$150B cumulative capex over 25yr; extended to 2034)
        |

        |
End markets:  Datacenter/AI SSD  |  Client/Edge SSD  |  Consumer cards/drives
        |
Buyers: hyperscalers/AI builders (NBM customers) | OEM PC/mobile | retail/channel
```

## Where the economics sit
- **The scarce resource right now = NAND bits / fab capacity.** In a shortage, the fab + allocation hold the pricing power — and SanDisk shares that fab with Kioxia. So the profit pool is real but **co-owned**, and the same JV is now expanding capacity (BiCS10 2027, +40–66% capex), which is what eventually ends shortages.
- SanDisk's *own* value-add: controller/firmware IP, enterprise-SSD qualification, the SanDisk consumer brand, and customer relationships (now formalized via NBM). This is the part that could carry a durable premium; the raw-bit part is commodity.

## Who pays / unit economics
- Unit of value: a GB of qualified flash (priced far above cost at the peak).
- Unit of cost: a wafer from Flash Ventures (variable cost SanDisk is obligated to fund) + opex.
- Profit pool: ASP − wafer cost − opex. At the peak this spread is enormous (78% GM); historically it has gone negative (Q3 FY25).

## Bottleneck position (link to bottleneck_map.md)
SanDisk sits *at* the bottleneck today (scarce NAND), but does not *own* the bottleneck exclusively — it is one of ~5 NAND suppliers (Samsung, SK hynix/Solidigm, Micron, Kioxia/SanDisk) and shares its fab. Bottleneck rents are being competed for and will be competed away as capacity lands.

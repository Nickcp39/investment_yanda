## INTERPRETATION (reasoned, labeled)
- **This is a violent cyclical PEAK, not a new compounder plateau.** Gross margin went 22% → 78% in four quarters. That magnitude of margin expansion is the *signature* of a commodity-memory shortage at its apex, driven by ASP (price), not by a structural cost or moat advantage. The same franchise printed a −$13.33 GAAP loss exactly one year ago (Q3 FY25).
- **The NBM model genuinely raises durability — but only partially.** $42B of minimum commitments + >$11B guarantees + >1/3 of FY27 bits under contract is a real, unprecedented demand-visibility floor for a NAND maker, and the $511M contract-liability balance is hard evidence customers are pre-paying. It dampens the *trough* and de-risks FY27 volume. It does **not** convert SanDisk into a price-stable franchise: pricing is fixed+variable (so ASP still falls in a glut on the variable portion), and the contracts cover one-third, not all, of bits. NAND remains a commodity with a JV-shared cost base SanDisk does not fully control.
- **Balance sheet is now a fortress** (net cash $3.7B, zero debt, +$2.75B retained earnings, $3B/qtr OCF). The 2025 survival risk is gone. This *removes* the bankruptcy/dilution tail — a clear positive — and funds the $6B buyback.
- **Valuation is the binding problem for NEW money.** At ~$2,274 the stock trades ~76x trailing and ABOVE the average analyst 12-mo target (~$1,751–1,843) and above DCF fair-value estimates [S015/S020]. On *peak* annualized earnings (Q4 guide ~$30-33/qtr × 4 ≈ $120-132) the forward multiple is only ~17-19x — but that denominator is peak-cycle earnings power that has historically reverted. Paying a mid-teens multiple on peak NAND earnings is the classic cyclical valuation trap (low P/E at the top).
- **Supply is being added for 2027** (BiCS10 mass production 2027; Kioxia capex +40-66%; 3rd Kitakami fab). NAND's history is that shortages get solved by capacity and margins normalize hard. Whether the AI-datacenter SSD demand curve absorbs it before margins crack is the single open variable.

# SNDK Bottleneck Map — as_of 2026-06-22

## The 2026 bottleneck: NAND bit supply / fab capacity
- AI datacenters reportedly consume ~70% of memory supply in 2026; NAND contract prices +70–75% QoQ in Q2 2026 [S013]; suppliers deliberately not adding capacity fast enough → "allocation exhaustion."
- SanDisk is a **beneficiary sitting at the bottleneck**, capturing shortage rents (78% GM, Datacenter +645% YoY).

## But the bottleneck is temporary and being relieved
- Capacity IS being added for 2027: **BiCS10 mass production 2027** [S009], **Kioxia capex ¥470B/yr FY26-28 (+66%)**, Kioxia–SanDisk capex **+40% YoY** [S009/S010], 3rd Kitakami fab under consideration.
- Historically, every NAND shortage is solved by capacity and margins normalize hard. The open question is *timing*: do AI-storage demand and the NBM volume floor absorb the new bits before ASPs crack?

## One metric that forces a market update
- **NAND ASP trend / SanDisk gross margin trajectory.** The first quarter where GM stops rising and/or ASPs roll over is the inflection. The Q4 FY26 print (~late July/Aug 2026, after as_of) and FY27 prints are the tells.
- Secondary tell: NBM coverage and pricing terms holding as new capacity lands.

## Implication
The bottleneck supports the *current* earnings but is **self-correcting** — it is a cyclical peak signal, not a durable moat. New money entering at the bottleneck's apex (parabolic price) is buying the rent at its most expensive, just as the supply response is being funded.

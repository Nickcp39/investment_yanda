# SNDK Business Model — as_of 2026-06-22

## One-sentence business
SanDisk designs and sells NAND flash memory and storage products (datacenter/enterprise SSD, client SSD, consumer cards/drives) and manufactures the wafers through the **Flash Ventures** 50/50 joint venture with **Kioxia** — a pure-play, vertically-integrated NAND maker carved out of Western Digital in Feb 2025.

## Revenue engine (Q3 FY2026)
- **Datacenter $1,467M** (+645% YoY) — enterprise/AI SSD; the fastest-growing, highest-value leg and the strategic pivot.
- **Edge $3,663M** (+295% YoY) — client SSD + embedded/industrial.
- **Consumer $820M** (+44% YoY, −10% QoQ) — cards, USB, retail drives (the legacy SanDisk brand).
- Economics are **ASP × bits shipped**. The 2026 surge is overwhelmingly **price (ASP)** driven — the NAND shortage — layered on a mix shift toward datacenter.

## The "New Business Model" (NBM) overlay
Multi-year customer supply agreements with **firm financial commitments** (prepayments + third-party guarantees): 5 signed through Q4 FY26; secondary reports put it at **~$42B minimum revenue commitments / >$11B guarantees / >1/3 of FY27 bits** [S011]. Contract liabilities on the balance sheet rose to **$511M** [S002]. Intent: dampen NAND's boom-bust by locking volume and a pricing floor (fixed + variable).

## 10-year test (would I want to own the whole business for 10 years?)
- **Mostly NO at the franchise level, conditionally maybe at the price level.** NAND is a commodity with brutal historical cyclicality (this same entity lost −$13.33/sh GAAP one year ago). The cost base is **shared with Kioxia** via the JV — SanDisk does not fully control its own wafer cost or capacity decisions. The NBM model is a genuine attempt to raise the floor, but it does not make NAND a 5–10-year hold-through-anything compounder.
- A 10-year owner would have ridden a −$13 loss to +$23/qtr and must assume the reverse is possible. The business is **own-the-cycle, not own-forever.**

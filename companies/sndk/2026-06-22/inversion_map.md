# SNDK Inversion / Trap Test — as_of 2026-06-22

## Question: how does a buyer at ~$2,274.69 lose money permanently (or lose 50–90%)?

### Path 1 — Cyclical peak reverts (the BASE bear case, ~highest probability)
NAND is commodity-cyclical. GM went 22%→78% in 4 quarters; the symmetric risk is 78%→back down as (a) BiCS10 + Kioxia +40–66% capex land in 2027, (b) AI-storage demand proves partly restocking/allocation pull-forward, (c) ASPs roll over. Earnings collapse from ~$120/yr peak toward $10–35/yr normalized → at a cyclical 9–14x the stock is worth ~$108–450 → **−80% to −95%**. This is the textbook "bought a commodity at peak earnings on a low forward P/E" trap (the PFE/cyclical pattern).

### Path 2 — Capacity glut overshoots (deeper version of Path 1)
The entire NAND industry is funding capacity into the shortage (Samsung, SK hynix, Micron, Kioxia/SanDisk). Coordinated 2027–28 capacity additions → oversupply → margins below mid-cycle → losses again, as in 2018-19 and 2022-23. NBM floors only ~1/3 of bits.

### Path 3 — NBM disappoints
If the $42B/$11B NBM commitments prove softer than billed (variable-pricing dominates, customers renegotiate in a glut, guarantees are smaller than reported), the "durable earnings power" narrative breaks and the premium evaporates. Currently the precise NBM economics are secondary-sourced [S011] — an evidence gap.

### Path 4 — Capital misallocation at the peak
Executing the **$6B buyback near an all-time-high / cyclical peak** destroys value if the stock subsequently halves. Watch repurchase pace.

### Path 5 — Kioxia / Flash Ventures stress
$2,967M max loss exposure + the JV being the cost/capacity decision-maker. In a prolonged trough the JV obligations are a fixed claim; SanDisk cannot unilaterally cut capacity. (Lower probability given the fortress balance sheet — survival is not at risk, but it magnifies a downcycle.)

## What is NOT the risk (vs the 2025 thesis)
- **NOT bankruptcy/dilution** — zero debt, $3.7B net cash, $3B/qtr OCF. Survival risk is gone.
- **NOT a below-book asset story** — that edge vanished; this is now an expensive-multiple story.

## Kill criteria (feeds decision_card)
1. Gross margin rolls over (Q4 FY26 / FY27 prints below guide, or sequential GM decline) → cycle peaked → for any holder, trim hard.
2. NAND ASPs turn negative QoQ (industry data) → mechanism breaking.
3. NBM economics revealed materially weaker than the $42B/$11B/1-3-of-bits framing → durability premium unjustified.
4. Buyback executed aggressively into the peak then stock halves → capital-allocation red flag.
5. Capacity additions (BiCS10, 3rd Kitakami) accelerate beyond demand → glut forming.

## Trap classification
**Cyclical at/near peak with an inverted risk/reward for NEW money.** Not a structural-decline trap (the franchise is healthy) and not a false-cheap value trap in the accounting sense (earnings are real, not an illusion) — but it is a **"cheap-on-peak-earnings" cyclical trap**: the low forward multiple is a mirage because the E is unsustainable.

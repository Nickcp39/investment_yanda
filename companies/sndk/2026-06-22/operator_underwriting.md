## Founder & Core Team Life-Arc (condensed; full formation analysis in backtest prior S018)
- **Goeckeler:** long-arc systems/networking executive (Cisco → WDC CEO → SanDisk). Proven operator; the dark-arc is that he is the *architect* of this separation and has a sunk-cost incentive to defend "durable NAND earnings" — discount his durability narrative accordingly.
- **Visoso:** serial big-tech CFO (Amazon/Cisco/Ericsson); strong finance operator; dark-arc = a pattern of shorter tenures → key-man/continuity risk on a 2-person team.
- **Team-level:** now ~16 months together as a standalone — still a *young* team, but one that has now executed a hard deleveraging and a complex up-cycle + a novel NBM commercial model. Cohesion is better-evidenced than in 2025, but it has only been tested in a TAILWIND. The true test (holding discipline through the NAND bust) has not happened.

## Team dark-arc / fracture inversion
- Break risks: CFO departure (tenure pattern); CEO over-committing to the durability thesis and mis-allocating at the peak (buyback, capacity, M&A); board waving through value-destructive moves.

## Verdict → durability ceiling
- **Team life-arc grade: 3.5/5** (up from 3/5 in 2025 — execution proven, but only in an up-cycle, with governance concentration and a 2-person key-man profile intact).

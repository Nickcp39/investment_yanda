## The durability test
- **Profit pool is cyclical-commodity**, not a toll. The 78% gross margin is a *shortage rent*, not a moat rent — it appears and disappears with the NAND cycle. One year ago this franchise earned a 22.5% GM and a GAAP loss.
- **The NBM model is the only genuine moat-deepening development**: locking >1/3 of FY27 bits with prepaid, guaranteed multi-year commitments is real demand stickiness and would raise the *trough* margin. But (a) it covers a minority of bits, (b) pricing is fixed+variable so ASP still falls on the variable portion in a glut, and (c) it has never been tested through a downturn. It moves the moat from "none" to "narrow," not to "wide."

## Conclusion for the ceiling
A narrow, partly-shared, cyclical moat → **durability is MEDIUM-LOW**. Per METHODOLOGY this caps the position **below Core** (a NAND cyclical is not a 5–10-year hold-through-anything name), independent of how strong the current print is.

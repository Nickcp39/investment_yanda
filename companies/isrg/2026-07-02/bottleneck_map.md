# ISRG Bottleneck Map (M3)

As-of: 2026-07-02

## The bottleneck question: who holds the scarce resource?

In robotic surgery the scarcest resources are **(1) trained surgeons and their embedded OR workflow**, and
**(2) the validated, cleared, integrated system + proprietary consumable stack**. ISRG holds both at scale —
a ~11,400-system installed base with hundreds of thousands of trained surgeons and 25 years of clinical
evidence. This is the node where the economics concentrate: the per-procedure instrument annuity.

## Bottleneck stack

| Layer | Scarce? | Who holds it | ISRG position |
|---|---|---|---|
| Robotics / vision / control IP | Somewhat | ISRG + Medtronic + J&J + others | Leader; integrated & proprietary |
| **Cleared, validated integrated system** | **YES — regulatory + evidence gate** | ISRG (dominant); Hugo (urology, new); Ottava (pending) | **Dominant, but first real challengers arriving** |
| **Trained surgeons + OR workflow** | **YES — the deepest gate** | ISRG (hundreds of thousands trained) | **Overwhelming incumbency** |
| **Proprietary per-procedure instruments** | **YES — captive consumable** | ISRG (single/limited-use, non-substitutable) | **The profit node — ~76% recurring** |
| Service network | Moderate | ISRG | Broad global installed-base service |
| Hospital capital budget | Moderate (gates *placements*) | Hospitals | Where competition & cyclicality bite first |

## Why this matters for the thesis

- The bottleneck explains the **66% gross margin and the durability of the recurring I&A + service annuity**:
  economics sit at the *trained-surgeon + captive-consumable* node, not at system manufacturing or distribution.
- It explains why ISRG can **give back some instrument life (6->15 uses)** to defend share and still keep a
  premium annuity — the surgeon/workflow lock-in is stronger than the per-instrument price.
- It also localizes the competitive risk: rivals (Hugo/Ottava) first contest the **hospital-capital / new-placement**
  layer (the razor), which is the *least* profitable and most cyclical layer — not the trained-surgeon annuity.
  Displacing the annuity requires re-training surgeons and re-validating, which is slow.

## Bottleneck risk

- The bottleneck is **incumbency-cyclical at the placement layer, structurally sticky at the annuity layer.**
  A hospital-capex slowdown or a Hugo win reduces *new placements*; it does not quickly remove ISRG's installed
  base or its trained-surgeon lock-in. So the bottleneck protects the *recurring profit pool* even as competition
  and the instrument-life give-back pressure *growth rate* and *I&A-per-procedure*.

## One metric that forces a market update

**Worldwide procedure growth + I&A-per-procedure trend (and da Vinci system-placement growth).** If procedures
keep compounding ~14-15% and I&A-per-procedure holds despite the reuse-limit change, the annuity thesis is
validated and the de-rate was an overreaction. If procedures decelerate toward high-single-digits AND
I&A-per-procedure falls materially (instrument-life cannibalization) AND placements stall (Hugo share-take), the
bear case is materializing. Q2 2026 (2026-07-16) is the next read.

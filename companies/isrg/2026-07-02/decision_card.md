## The one-paragraph thesis

ISRG is a surgical-robotics **near-monopoly** on a textbook **razor-and-blade** model: it places da Vinci/Ion systems (431 da Vinci in Q1'26, 232 of them da Vinci 5) to grow a large, sticky installed base (**11,395 da Vinci + 1,041 Ion, +12% YoY**), which drives procedures (**+17% worldwide** in Q1'26) that consume proprietary per-procedure instruments — a **~76-86% recurring**, 66%-gross-margin annuity that is the bulk of profit and compounds with the base, not the system-sales cycle. Deep switching costs (surgeon training + hospital workflow + 25 years of clinical evidence), ~$8B net cash / zero debt, ~15% revenue CAGR, ~$2.8B FCF, and an **engineer-builder CEO (Dave Rosa, who built Ion + SP) with Guthart as Exec Chair**. It also genuinely **diversifies** the user's all-AI/liquidity book. The business says own it — and, for the first time in this HWM/GEV series, the price does not forbid entry.

## The open inflection (why not bigger)

Two genuine, novel risks the ~30% de-rate is pricing: (1) **instrument-life cannibalization** — ISRG voluntarily extended reuse limits on 5 of 6 force-feedback instruments (6→15 uses) to defend hospital cost-per-procedure; the market fears it guts I&A-per-procedure (magnitude OPEN; 2020 precedent suggests ~2% not the feared ~7%); (2) **Medtronic Hugo got US urology FDA clearance (2025-12-03)** — the first scaled US soft-tissue competitor in 20+ years (J&J Ottava filed De Novo 2026-01-07). Both attack new *placements* first, not the trained-surgeon annuity (2-5 yr to bite). **Q2 2026 (2026-07-16) is the read** — it will show procedure growth, I&A-per-procedure post-reuse-change, and placements.

## Balance sheet (fortress)

- **Cash + investments $7,979.2M** ($2,006.5M cash + $2,510.8M ST + $3,461.9M LT investments); **ZERO debt.**
- **Net cash ~$8.0B** = ~5.3% of the $150.9B market cap; EV ~$142.9B. Cannot go bankrupt.
- **Capital-allocation critique:** this ~$8B has sat largely idle for years — only modest buybacks, no dividend.
  It is a *drag* on per-share compounding (an operator weakness, not a solvency risk). Dry powder for a
  buyback-at-a-discount or M&A if management chooses to act.

## Owner-earnings sanity

- FY2025 FCF $2,491M / TTM $2,834M on ~$10.6B revenue → **~27% FCF/revenue** (excellent for a growth medtech).
- Owner earnings ≈ FCF (capex normalized; growth capex modest post-build). At $150.9B mcap, **P/TTM FCF ~53x** —
  rich but consistent with 15% growth + 66% gross margins + net cash + a recurring annuity.
- ROIC is very high (net-cash-heavy, asset-moderate, 30% operating margins) — a genuine compounder.

## Verdict

Financial reality **confirms the thesis (signal +2)**: durable ~66% gross margins, ~15% revenue CAGR, ~28-30%
net/operating margins, rising FCF (~$2.8B), zero debt, ~$8B net cash, and heavy R&D. The 2022-23 dip is fully
explained by a capex build now paying off. The only financial *critique* is capital-allocation passivity (idle
cash), not accounting quality. The financials are NOT the problem — the questions are the instrument-life I&A
mix (F1) and whether ~40x forward, post-de-rate, is now a fair price (M6).

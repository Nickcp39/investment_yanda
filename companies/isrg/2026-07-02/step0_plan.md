## Decision question

At ~$426 (after a ~28% slide from the Jan-2026 high of ~$593, and just above the 52-wk low), is ISRG
a new-money buy for a concentrated Duan-style book, and what should an existing holder do? Crucially:
ISRG diversifies a BTC/GOOGL/NBIS single-factor ("AI + liquidity") book — does the price now offer a
margin of safety on a genuinely diversifying, near-monopoly medtech compounder?

## One thesis gate

> ISRG is a da Vinci surgical-robotics near-monopoly on a razor-and-blade model — a large, growing
> installed base of systems pulling a high-margin, recurring stream of instruments/accessories +
> service that is the bulk of profit, protected by deep switching costs (surgeon training + hospital
> workflow + qualification). The questions that decide the call: (1) is the razor/blade moat still
> compounding (procedure growth, recurring-mix) and NOT structurally threatened by Medtronic Hugo /
> J&J Ottava?; (2) after the de-rate, does ~52x trailing / ~40x forward leave a positive multi-year
> IRR and a margin of safety, or is it still a premium-priced quality name with a negative base IRR
> like HWM/GEV?

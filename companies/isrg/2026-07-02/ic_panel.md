# ISRG IC Panel — Five Souls

As-of: 2026-07-02 | As-of price: $426.01

> No fabricated quotes. Each "voice" is a LENS — the documented investing principles of each investor applied
> to ISRG. Where a principle is paraphrased, it is labeled (lens); no primary quote is attributed.

## 1. Duan Yongping (段永平) — chief judge (lens)

- **本分 / circle of competence:** Is this a business I understand and would own for 10 years? *Yes — a
  razor-and-blade near-monopoly with a growing installed base, ~76-86% recurring revenue, 66% gross margins,
  ~$8B net cash and zero debt is exactly the kind of durable, comprehensible franchise 段 favors.*
- **管理层是否靠谱:** Rosa and Guthart are engineer-builders promoted from within (Rosa built Ion/SP); clean
  one-share-one-vote governance; honest instrument-life give-back to protect customers. **靠谱** — the one
  watch is the idle ~$8B cash (allocation passivity). This is the *opposite* of a courtier/finance operator.
- **价格 / "好生意 + 好价格":** After a ~30% de-rate, base 5y IRR is **positive (~+9%)** — 段 waits for the fat
  pitch, and this is *closer* to one than HWM. Not screamingly cheap at ~40x fwd, but a good business at a
  fair-to-slightly-rich price with a real inflection to watch. Verdict: **STARTER; 小仓试, 等 Q2 确认再加.**

## 2. Warren Buffett (lens)

- "Wonderful company at a fair price." ISRG is wonderful (moat, ROIC, recurring annuity, net cash). The price,
  post-de-rate, is *fair-ish* rather than cheap — but "**it's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair
  price than a fair company at a wonderful price**." Buffett prizes the durable moat + no debt. The idle cash
  would bother him (he'd want it deployed or returned). Verdict: **own a starter; the quality justifies it, the
  price no longer forbids it.**

## 3. Charlie Munger (lens)

- "A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price." ISRG is a great business,
  and unlike HWM at 57x, ~40x forward with a positive base IRR is defensible for this quality. Munger would flag
  the **competition inflection** (Hugo) and the **instrument-life give-back** as things to actually understand,
  not wave away — but would respect that switching costs make the annuity sticky. Verdict: **great business,
  now-fair price — a starter is rational; invert on the competition and size accordingly.**

## 4. Howard Marks (lens)

- **Where are we in the cycle / sentiment?** ISRG sentiment is *pessimistic* (down 30%, target cuts, Hugo fear,
  OpenAI-robotics scare) — the OPPOSITE of HWM's euphoria. "**The most dangerous thing is paying a price that
  assumes everything goes right**"; here the price assumes several things go *wrong*. Second-level thinking: the
  bad news is substantially in the price, and the base IRR is positive. Marks: **asymmetry has improved to
  roughly balanced-to-favorable → a measured starter, with room to add if fear overshoots.**

## 5. Seth Klarman (lens)

- **Margin of safety is the cardinal rule.** A *positive* base-case 5y IRR with a ~$8B net-cash floor and a
  bear case of only ~-3% is a real (if thin) margin of safety — unlike HWM's negative base. Klarman would still
  want more cushion given the F1/F2 business risks (instrument-life, Hugo), so he'd **start small and demand the
  CORE size only near ~$364** (conservative-exit MOS). Verdict: **STARTER now; add on price/Q2 confirmation.**

**Consensus: a measured STARTER at $426.01** — the first name in this series where the panel is constructive on
new money, because the ~30% de-rate created a positive base IRR on a genuine near-monopoly that also *diversifies*
the book. The discipline (all five agree): **start small, let Q2 (2026-07-16) confirm the instrument-life/annuity
question, and reserve larger sizing for lower prices (~$412 / ~$364).** This aligns with the mechanical M6 (+1)
and the runner's view.

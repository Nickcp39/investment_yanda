## Value-capture logic

- **Upstream (components/IP):** ISRG is vertically integrated on the crown-jewel IP — the robotics,
  wristed-instrument mechanisms (EndoWrist), 3D vision, and control software are proprietary and built in-house.
  It buys commodity inputs but owns the hard parts. Some tariff exposure (~100 bps to FY26 gross margin) and
  specialty-input cost, but the integrated position protects margin.
- **ISRG node (the choke point):** The *integrated system + proprietary consumables + surgeon training +
  clinical evidence + regulatory clearances* together form the choke point. No single piece is the moat; the
  **bundle** is. A hospital cannot buy da Vinci systems and run third-party instruments — the instruments are
  proprietary and single-/limited-use, which is exactly what makes I&A a ~76% recurring, per-procedure annuity
  at 66%+ gross margin.
- **Downstream (hospitals/surgeons):** The customer is the hospital (capital buyer) AND the surgeon (the true
  lock-in). A surgeon trained on da Vinci over hundreds of cases will not easily re-train on Hugo/Ottava; the
  hospital has sunk $1-2M+ per system and built OR workflow, scheduling, and credentialing around it. Switching
  is slow, costly, and clinically risky.
- **Aftermarket (the prize):** Every one of the ~11,400 da Vinci + ~1,040 Ion systems consumes instruments and
  a service contract for its ~10-15 year life. The installed base compounds the annuity; ISRG captures the
  recurring economics regardless of the year's system-sales cycle.

## Pricing power evidence

- Gross margin ~66% consolidated (67.8% non-GAAP), stable for a decade; I&A carries the profit.
- I&A revenue +23% in Q1'26 — scales with installed base × utilization, a recurring compounding stream.
- Per-procedure consumable pricing + cost-of-failure asymmetry (a surgical instrument failure is unacceptable)
  = structural pricing power, though the *instrument-life extension* (6->15 uses on force-feedback tools) is a
  self-inflicted, deliberate give-back to lower hospital cost-per-procedure and defend share (see below).

## Where the chain is weakest / under pressure

- **Instrument-life extension (the 2026 de-rate crux):** ISRG voluntarily raised reuse limits on 5 of 6
  force-feedback instruments (6->15 uses). This *reduces* I&A-per-procedure to keep hospitals' cost-per-case
  competitive vs cheaper rivals — a moat-defense move that the market fears cannibalizes the blade. Precedent
  (2020 Extended-Use Program) suggests a smaller-than-feared hit (~2% vs ~7% feared) that reaccelerated; but the
  magnitude this cycle is genuinely OPEN until Q2 (2026-07-16).
- **Systems (razor) is the cyclical/competitive edge:** system placements are hospital-capex-sensitive and are
  where Hugo/Ottava compete first; ISRG increasingly *leases* systems (usage-based) to defend placements, which
  shifts revenue into recurring but lowers upfront systems revenue.
- **China:** domestic robots (Edge Medical, MicroPort Toumai) won ~52% of Chinese public tenders in 2025 — the
  clearest structural crack, though China is only ~mid-single-digit % of revenue today.

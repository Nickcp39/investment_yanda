# ISRG Business Model (M2 / M3 input)

As-of: 2026-07-02 | Pipeline: lean-6module-v1.1

## One sentence

Intuitive Surgical sells (and increasingly leases) da Vinci and Ion robotic systems near cost to seed a
large, sticky installed base, then earns the bulk of its profit from a ~75-86% recurring, high-margin
stream of per-procedure instruments/accessories and multi-year service contracts — a razor-and-blade
near-monopoly protected by surgeon training, hospital workflow, and years of clinical/regulatory validation.

## The flywheel (mechanism)

1. **Place systems** (431 da Vinci in Q1'26, incl. 232 da Vinci 5) → **installed base grows** (11,395 da Vinci + 1,041 Ion at Q1'26, +12% YoY).
2. **Installed base drives procedures** (worldwide procedures +17% in Q1'26; da Vinci +16%, Ion +39%).
3. **Each procedure consumes instruments/accessories** → recurring I&A revenue (+23% in Q1'26), which scales with the *installed base × utilization*, not with new-system sales.
4. **Service contracts** layer a second recurring stream on the same base.
5. **R&D (~13% of revenue) funds the next platform** (da Vinci 5, Ion, SP, digital/AI) → deepens the moat and re-seeds the flywheel.

The critical feature: **once a hospital owns da Vinci and its surgeons are trained, the recurring I&A + service
stream is highly annuity-like** and grows for the ~10-15 year life of the system regardless of that year's
system-sales cycle. This is why the business compounds ~15% with 66% gross margins and ~$2.8B FCF.

## Demand drivers

1. **Procedure growth** — the master variable. Guided **13.5-15.5% (FY26, ~14.5% mid)**; historically 15-20%. Driven by expanding indications (general surgery, bariatric, thoracic, gyn, urology) and rising minimally-invasive-surgery penetration globally.
2. **Installed-base growth** (+12%/yr) — compounds the I&A annuity base.
3. **da Vinci 5 upgrade cycle** — 870 placed in FY2025 (vs 362 in 2024), 232 in Q1'26; drives system revenue + higher-value instruments.
4. **Ion (lung biopsy)** — installed base 1,041, procedures +39-51%; the fastest-growing platform, built by the current CEO.
5. **Ex-US penetration** (32% of revenue) — long runway, but China is a growing competitive/reimbursement risk.

## 10-year test (would you own it for a decade?)

- **For the business: yes, high-conviction.** A near-monopoly razor-and-blade with a growing installed base,
  ~75-86% recurring revenue, 66% gross margins, ~$8B net cash, no debt, heavy R&D, engineer-builder CEO, and
  a secular MIS-penetration tailwind. This is a genuine quality_compounder AND a real diversifier from the
  user's AI/liquidity book.
- **At this price: closer than HWM.** After a ~30% de-rate to ~40x forward / ~52x trailing, the base-case 5y
  IRR is **positive (~+9%/yr)** and roughly clears an 8% hurdle — a thin but real margin of safety (unlike
  HWM/GEV, whose base IRR was negative). The open risks are the instrument-life recurring-revenue question
  and newly-cleared competition (Hugo). See valuation.md / inversion_map.md.

# ISRG Moat Map (M3)

As-of: 2026-07-02

## Moat sources (ranked)

| Source | Strength | Evidence | Durability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Surgeon switching cost / training lock-in** | Very strong | A surgeon proficient on da Vinci over hundreds of cases won't re-train on a rival mid-career; credentialing + muscle memory | Structural (career-length) |
| **Installed-base + proprietary consumables lock-in** | Very strong | ~11,395 da Vinci + 1,041 Ion systems; instruments are proprietary, single/limited-use → captive I&A annuity | 10-15 yr per system |
| **Clinical evidence + regulatory clearances** | Very strong | 25+ yrs, millions of procedures, thousands of studies, broad FDA indications across specialties | Structural; compounds |
| **Integrated system + ecosystem (razor+blade+SW+service)** | Strong | The bundle, not any one piece; digital/AI, training, service network | Structural |
| **Scale / R&D reinvestment** | Strong | ~$1.3B/yr R&D funds da Vinci 5, Ion, SP, digital; rivals are years behind on the ecosystem | Compounding |
| **Brand / KOL network** | Strong | "da Vinci" is the category; teaching hospitals train residents on it → generational lock-in | Durable |

## Moat test by platform

- **da Vinci multiport (core) — DEEP & DURABLE.** The switching-cost + installed-base + evidence + ecosystem
  quadruple-lock is one of the strongest in medtech. ~76-86% recurring revenue at 66% GM confirms it. In mature
  categories (urology) it is entrenched, though this is exactly where Hugo now competes.
- **da Vinci 5 — DEEP (upgrade moat).** Force feedback + compute + new instruments; 870 placed FY2025, 232 Q1'26.
  Deepens the lock-in and refreshes the base — but the instrument-life give-back is the tension.
- **Ion (endoluminal / lung biopsy) — DEEP & FAST-GROWING.** 1,041 installed, procedures +39-51%; a differentiated
  robotic bronchoscopy franchise with few credible competitors (J&J Monarch, ex-Auris/Moll, is the main one).
- **da Vinci SP (single-port) — GOOD, narrower.** Differentiated for specific approaches; smaller base.

## What could erode it (and how fast)

- **Newly-cleared competition (the real 2026 change):** **Medtronic Hugo got US urology FDA clearance
  2025-12-03** — the first scaled soft-tissue robotics competitor in the US in 20+ years. **J&J Ottava** submitted
  De Novo (2026-01-07). These attack the *razor* (system placements) and the *newest* accounts first; they do
  NOT quickly dislodge trained surgeons or the installed base. Realistic timeline to dent ISRG's share: 2-5 years,
  and initially at the margin (new placements, price). Watch urology share and system-placement growth.
- **Instrument-life extension cannibalizing the blade:** self-inflicted margin-of-the-annuity risk; magnitude OPEN.
- **China domestic robots:** structural share loss ex-US (won ~52% of 2025 Chinese tenders); small $ today.
- **Reimbursement:** China DRG overhaul (unresolved to 2027); US reimbursement broadly stable but a watch item.
- **Long-dated tech disruption:** AI/autonomy or a new surgical modality — long-dated, and ISRG is the most likely
  leader of that transition (heavy R&D), not the victim; the OpenAI-robotics scare is speculative sentiment.

## Verdict

Moat is **deep and durable for the profit-dominant recurring core**, anchored by surgeon switching costs and a
compounding installed base — a genuine near-monopoly. **BUT it is inflecting for the first time in two decades:**
Hugo's US clearance and the self-inflicted instrument-life give-back are real, novel pressures. This still
justifies a quality_compounder label and a premium multiple — the question (M5/M6) is whether ~40x forward, after
the de-rate, now prices those pressures *sensibly* (closer to yes than HWM's 57x did).

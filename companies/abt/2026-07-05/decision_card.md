## Career Note: FreeStyle Libre CGM + Structural Heart + China Diagnostics (AI/imaging + China-medtech lens)

Abbott's **FreeStyle Libre** franchise is, in this run's assessment, the single deepest consumables-annuity moat identified across the entire S&P 500 medical batch reviewed to date: ~8 million users across 60+ countries, ~52.83% global CGM revenue share, with a sensor-replacement cadence (~every 14 days) that generates genuine, prescription/insurance-reauthorization-locked recurring revenue [facts.md E50-E51]. This is structurally comparable to — and arguably deeper than — GEHC's OEM-agnostic Pharmaceutical Diagnostics consumables model, because Libre's moat operates within Abbott's OWN branded installed base (real switching friction) rather than monetizing any vendor's equipment. However, this is NOT an uncontested moat: the US CGM market is a near-even duopoly split (Abbott 48.5% vs Dexcom 44.7%) [E51], and one independent technical metric shows Dexcom's G7 with a measurable accuracy edge over Libre 3 (MARD 8.2% vs 9.2%) [E53] — a useful, concrete reminder that even Abbott's strongest moat asset faces a genuine, well-capitalized, technically-competitive rival, not a token one. On the diagnostics/China side, Abbott's China revenue fell ~-9.75% YoY in FY2025 (from $2.11B to $1.91B), explicitly attributed by the company to COVID-testing runoff AND China's volume-based-procurement (VBP) programs [E61-E62] — the same structural mechanism identified in the GEHC dossier, but here appearing smaller in magnitude and, notably, already showing a partial positive inflection in Q1 2026 (Diagnostics comparable growth turned positive, +1.8%) [I2], a more favorable early signal than GEHC had at an equivalent point in its own evidence timeline. Separately, the newly-acquired Exact Sciences platform (Cologuard, Cancerguard multi-cancer early detection) represents a genuinely different AI/genomics-adjacent growth vector — molecular/liquid-biopsy cancer screening — where China's own domestic diagnostics companies (BGI, Burning Rock, and other liquid-biopsy players) are separately investing heavily; this is a distinct but parallel front to the imaging-AI United Imaging comparison developed in the GEHC dossier, and worth tracking as this batch's China-medtech-AI thesis develops across more names.

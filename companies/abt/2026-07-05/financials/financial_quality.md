## Balance Sheet / Leverage Trend — THE DOMINANT FINANCIAL-QUALITY STORY THIS RUN

| Item | FY2024 | FY2025 | Q1 2026 (post-Exact-Sciences) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total debt | $14,125M | $12,929M | **$34,047M** |
| Cash & equivalents | $7,616M | $8,522M | $6,803M |
| Net debt (derived) | ~$6,509M | ~$4,407M | **~$27,244M** |
| Total equity | $47,901M | $52,771M | $52,701M |
| Total assets | $81,414M | $86,713M | $110,429M |
| Goodwill + intangibles | $29,755M | $29,561M | $53,096M |
| D/E (as separately disclosed) | 0.26 | 0.19 | not directly disclosed; ~0.65x derived (34,047/52,701) — OPEN, facts.md O3 |

**Read**: This is the single most important financial-quality fact in this dossier. Abbott's leverage roughly **6x'd in one quarter** (net debt ~$4.4B -> ~$27.2B), entirely attributable to the Exact Sciences acquisition, funded almost entirely with NEW debt (not equity — total equity is essentially flat FY2025 -> Q1 2026, $52.77B -> $52.70B) [facts.md E41, E43]. This is a MUCH larger relative leverage event than GEHC's Intelerad-driven Q1 2026 net-debt increase (GEHC's net debt rose ~40% in a quarter to fund a $2.3B deal; Abbott's rose ~6x to fund a ~$20.6-21B deal). Abbott entered this deal from a position of LOWER starting leverage than GEHC (D/E 0.19 vs GEHC's 0.91 pre-deal), so even post-deal, Abbott's leverage is likely still moderate in absolute terms (~0.65x estimated D/E) — but the DIRECTION and MAGNITUDE of the single-quarter change is the largest, most consequential capital-structure event across this entire S&P 500 medical batch reviewed so far.

**Mitigant**: Management has stated intent to pay down ~$3B of debt in 2026 [facts.md E46] — a credible, quantified, near-term deleveraging signal. Whether this actually executes on schedule is a key monitoring item.

## Financial Quality Verdict

**GOOD, with a genuinely different risk profile than the GEHC exemplar.** Revenue growth is healthy and broad-based (not masked by a single overwhelming headwind), adjusted EPS continued growing through the period reviewed, and the Diagnostics segment shows a real, already-partially-realized positive inflection (organic growth improving quarter-over-quarter) rather than an unresolved "is this transient" open question. However, the Exact Sciences acquisition has introduced a MUCH larger, more consequential leverage event (net debt ~6x'd in one quarter) than anything seen in the GEHC exemplar, and this deal is too recent (closed 2026-03-23) to have any demonstrated integration success evidence. This financial profile supports a "healthy core business, successfully executing a large and financially consequential — but not yet proven — strategic bet" read, which is a genuinely different risk shape than GEHC's "steady business absorbing real but bounded cost headwinds" profile: Abbott's downside case is more concentrated in ONE large, binary-ish M&A execution risk, while GEHC's downside case was more diffusely spread across multiple smaller, ongoing operating headwinds (China, PCS, tariffs).

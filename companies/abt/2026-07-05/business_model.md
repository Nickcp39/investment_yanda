# ABT Business Model (M2 Theme / Mechanism)

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E10-E37, E50-E60

## What Abbott Sells: Four Segments (as-filed FY2025 structure, plus new Cancer Diagnostics sub-business)

### 1. Medical Devices (largest segment, 48% of FY2025 revenue at $21,387M, +11.9% organic)
Diabetes Care (FreeStyle Libre CGM), Structural Heart (TriClip, Amplatzer Amulet, MitraClip/TriClip G5), Electrophysiology, Rhythm Management, Heart Failure, Neuromodulation. This is Abbott's growth engine — consistently double-digit organic growth across FY2025 and Q1 2026, driven by CGM volume growth and cardiovascular device share gains. Q1 2026: $5,539M, +8.5% comparable — the standout segment.

**Mechanism**: FreeStyle Libre operates a genuine razor/razorblade model — sensor hardware (replaced every 14 days) generates ongoing recurring revenue from an installed base of ~8M users. Structural heart/electrophysiology devices are procedure-tied capital + disposable sales into a growing, aging-population-driven cardiovascular procedure volume base.

### 2. Diagnostic Products (20% of FY2025 revenue at $8,937M, −4.5% organic — the declining segment, now being reshaped by M&A)
Core Laboratory (Alinity platform — clinical chemistry, immunoassay, molecular), Rapid/Point-of-Care diagnostics (i-STAT), and — as of 2026-03-23 — the newly acquired **Cancer Diagnostics** business (Cologuard, Oncotype DX, Oncodetect, Cancerguard) via the Exact Sciences acquisition. Legacy Diagnostics has been shrinking on COVID-19 rapid-test revenue runoff and China VBP pricing pressure, even as Core Lab itself grew organically (+3.6% Q4 2025). The Exact Sciences deal is explicitly the strategic answer to this segment's growth problem.

### 3. Nutritional Products (19% of FY2025 revenue at $8,451M, +1.2% organic — the weakest segment)
Pediatric nutrition (Similac, PediaSure) and adult/specialty nutrition (Ensure, Glucerna). Q4 2025 organic growth was sharply negative (−9.1%), attributed to volume declines, a specific cited contract loss, and new strategic pricing actions intended to rebuild volume. This segment also carries the NEC infant-formula litigation overhang (see inversion_map.md), which is specific to the preterm-infant formula sub-line within Nutrition.

### 4. Established Pharmaceutical Products / EPD (13% of FY2025 revenue at $5,536M, +7.4% organic)
Branded generic pharmaceuticals sold primarily outside the US, concentrated in emerging markets (Key Emerging Markets: India, Latin America, Middle East cited as particular growth drivers). A steady, growing, geographically-diversified franchise with less direct competitive overlap against the China med-device/AI theme than Medical Devices or Diagnostics.

## The Core Mechanism: Diversified Portfolio Insulated Against Single-Segment Cyclicality, Anchored by a Genuine CGM Consumables Annuity

Abbott's model is fundamentally different from a pure med-tech-hardware play (GEHC) or a pure diagnostics play — it is a four-pillar diversified healthcare conglomerate where:

1. **Medical Devices (CGM + structural heart)** provides the highest-growth, highest-quality recurring revenue — FreeStyle Libre in particular is a genuine consumable-sensor annuity business (sensors replaced every ~14 days, ~8M-user installed base) that is structurally similar to GEHC's Pharmaceutical Diagnostics segment in its recurring-revenue character, but arguably stronger because it is closer to a subscription-like cadence tied directly to a chronic condition (diabetes) rather than episodic imaging volume.
2. **Diagnostics (Core Lab)** provides an installed-base instrument + reagent annuity (Alinity platform) — classic razor/razorblade — but total segment growth has been masked by COVID-test runoff and China VBP pressure; the Exact Sciences acquisition is a large, decisive bet to re-accelerate this segment via a completely different (molecular/liquid-biopsy cancer screening) sub-vertical.
3. **Nutrition** is the weakest, most commoditized, and currently most legally-exposed (NEC litigation) segment — the closest analog to GEHC's underperforming Patient Care Solutions, in that it lacks a clearly articulated near-term turnaround story in available sources.
4. **Established Pharma** is a steady, lower-profile, EM-exposed cash generator that diversifies overall company risk away from a single-country or single-modality concentration.

## The Exact Sciences Acquisition: A Decisive, Large-Scale Bet on Cancer Diagnostics (2026-03-23 close)

At ~$20.6-21B (roughly 12-13% of Abbott's own market cap), this is Abbott's largest acquisition in years and is entirely debt-financed. Strategic logic: Abbott is buying the market-leading noninvasive colorectal cancer screening franchise (Cologuard) plus a genuine multi-cancer early detection (MCED) platform (Cancerguard) and applying its 160-country global distribution network to a business that previously had limited ex-US commercial reach. This is the mirror image of GEHC's Intelerad logic (buying software/AI capability to bolt onto an existing hardware installed base) — here, Abbott is buying a completed molecular-diagnostics product platform to bolt onto its existing lab/diagnostics distribution and sales infrastructure, at a much larger scale relative to the acquirer's own size than the GEHC-Intelerad comp.

**Immediate financial consequence**: net debt roughly 6x'd in a single quarter (~$4.4B FY2025 -> ~$27.2B Q1 2026 estimate), and FY2026 guidance already embeds $0.20 of EPS dilution from financing costs. This is the dominant near-term financial-quality question for this dossier (see financials/financial_quality.md).

## Geographic Mix and China (parallels the GEHC exemplar's China VBP finding)

China revenue: FY2025 ~$1.91B, down ~−9.75% YoY from FY2024's ~$2.11B, explicitly attributed by the company to COVID-19 test runoff plus VBP program impact — concentrated in the Diagnostics segment specifically (facts.md E61-E65). The precise % of total ABT revenue that China represents was not cleanly reconciled this run (a discrepancy between two cited figures, ~4.35% and ~4.44%, appeared in the same secondary source — facts.md O4) — but directionally, ABT's China exposure appears smaller as a % of total revenue than GEHC's (~9.85%), while the VBP mechanism and negative direction are the same pattern.

## Why This Matters for the AI/China Career Lens

Abbott's most career-relevant asset is **FreeStyle Libre** (Medical Devices/Diabetes Care): a genuine, large (~8M users, 60+ countries), growing consumables-annuity business with ~48-53% global CGM revenue share, competing directly against Dexcom (a real, not token, competitor — US market share is a near-even 48.5% vs 44.7%, and Dexcom's G7 has a measurable technical accuracy edge on one independent metric). This is directly relevant to Nick's imaging/DL background in the sense that CGM sensor data + AI-driven glucose-prediction/insulin-dosing algorithms are an active area of medtech AI development, though no source found this run quantifies any AI-specific revenue or differentiation for Abbott's CGM software layer specifically. On the diagnostics/AI side, Abbott's Alinity platform references "AI-enabled diagnostics" in its own marketing, but — exactly as with GEHC's imaging AI narrative — this remains financially unquantified. The Exact Sciences acquisition (molecular diagnostics / liquid biopsy) is the more immediately relevant China-and-AI-adjacent story: Abbott is betting on genomics-based cancer screening as a growth vector, a domain where China's own diagnostics companies (e.g., BGI, Burning Rock, domestic liquid-biopsy players) are also investing heavily — a dynamic worth tracking in parallel to the imaging-AI United Imaging comparison developed in the GEHC dossier.

# ABT Operator Underwriting — CEO Robert B. Ford

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E13-E15, E30, E68

## Background

Robert B. Ford joined Abbott in **1996** in the Diabetes Care division — meaning his career at the company spans essentially the entire modern history of Abbott's diabetes/CGM franchise. He rose through leadership roles: SVP Diabetes Care (during which he oversaw the **original FreeStyle Libre launch** — a directly relevant, hands-on product background for the company's single largest current growth driver), EVP Medical Devices (led the **St. Jude Medical integration**, Abbott's prior large structural-heart/electrophysiology acquisition, completed 2017 — directly relevant experience for evaluating his Exact Sciences integration execution today), President & COO (2018-2020). He became **CEO in October 2018** (~7.6 years tenure as of this dossier) and **Chairman of the Board in December 2021** [E13].

**This is a genuinely differentiated background versus a typical "outside hire" CEO**: Ford has direct, hands-on operating experience with BOTH of Abbott's two largest recent capital-allocation bets — the original Libre launch (which he helped build) and the St. Jude Medical integration (a comparable-in-kind, if smaller-in-scale, acquisition-integration precedent for the current Exact Sciences deal.

## Track Record at Abbott (as CEO, 2018-present)

**What has gone right**:
- FreeStyle Libre has grown from a promising new product (at the time Ford was SVP Diabetes Care) into an ~$5-8B+ annualized, ~8M-user, global-share-leading franchise under his tenure — a genuine, multi-year value-creation track record in the company's most important current asset [E50-E51]
- Consistent multi-year revenue growth: FY2023 $40.1B -> FY2024 $42.0B -> FY2025 $44.3B [E21]
- 54 consecutive years of dividend increases maintained/extended through his tenure — Dividend King discipline preserved [E15]
- Decisive, large-scale capital allocation: the Exact Sciences acquisition is a genuinely bold strategic bet to reposition Diagnostics away from a COVID-testing-runoff/China-VBP-pressured legacy business toward a structurally higher-growth cancer-diagnostics vertical — directionally similar in spirit to (though larger in relative scale than) GEHC CEO Arduini's Intelerad bet

**What has gone wrong / under pressure**:
- Q1 2026 GAAP net income fell 18.7% YoY and GAAP diluted EPS fell to $0.61 from $0.76, driven substantially by financing costs from the Exact Sciences deal [E29-E30] — a real, quantified near-term earnings hit from the acquisition, consistent with what was disclosed/guided (not a surprise miss), but still a genuine cost of the decision
- Nutrition segment has shown a clear, multi-quarter negative trend (Q4 2025 −9.1% organic) with a cited contract loss and no clearly articulated turnaround plan found in sources reviewed this run [E24]
- NEC infant-formula litigation: Ford's public posture (stated preference to potentially **pull the product from the market entirely** rather than settle, framing courtroom outcomes as improperly overriding physician/regulator judgment) [E68] is a genuinely combative stance that could be read either as principled conviction in the science, OR as a management team underestimating/mishandling a serious, escalating legal-tail-risk (a $70M jury verdict already realized, active Congressional lobbying underway) [E66-E69]. This is a meaningfully different posture than a typical "settle and move on" crisis-management playbook, and its ultimate financial/reputational consequence is genuinely unresolved.
- Diagnostics segment (ex-Exact-Sciences) has now shown negative organic growth for multiple consecutive periods (FY2025 −4.5%, though Q1 2026 legacy Diagnostics returned to +1.8% comparable growth) — the China VBP and COVID-runoff headwinds took multiple years to fully play out before the Exact Sciences answer arrived.

## Capital Allocation

- **M&A**: Exact Sciences ($20.6-21B, ~12-13% of Abbott's own market cap) is a genuinely large, decisive, all-debt-financed bet [E32-E33] — a much larger relative commitment than GEHC's Intelerad (~7.7% of GEHC's market cap). This is Ford's largest single capital-allocation decision as CEO, and it came with immediate, disclosed EPS dilution ($0.20 in FY2026 guidance) [E36] rather than being papered over.
- **Debt management**: Company has stated intent to pay down **~$3B of debt in 2026** [E46, C73] — a credible, quantified near-term deleveraging signal following the large debt issuance, suggesting management is not simply "loading up" without a repayment plan.
- **Dividend**: Raised quarterly dividend to $0.63 in January 2026 (+6.8%), maintaining the 54-year Dividend King streak even in the same year as the large debt-funded acquisition [E15] — indicates capital-allocation discipline was maintained across BOTH growth-investment and shareholder-return priorities simultaneously, a genuinely positive signal (contrast with a company that might cut/pause dividend growth to fund a large deal).
- **Capex**: stated disciplined pattern of ~$2.2B/year [E48, C72] — not aggressive, consistent with a mature, diversified capital allocator rather than an empire-building pattern.

## Grade: 4/5

**Rationale**: Ford has a genuinely differentiated, multi-decade, hands-on operating background directly relevant to Abbott's two largest capital-allocation bets (he personally helped build FreeStyle Libre and led the comparable St. Jude Medical integration precedent). Under his tenure as CEO, Abbott has delivered consistent multi-year revenue growth, preserved its 54-year Dividend King streak even while funding a large acquisition, and made a genuinely bold (not timid) strategic move to address Diagnostics' structural growth problem via Exact Sciences — with a disclosed, not hidden, near-term EPS cost and a credible stated deleveraging plan. This is a HIGHER grade than the GEHC exemplar's CEO Arduini (3/5), reflecting: (a) longer, more directly-relevant tenure and hands-on product/integration experience specific to Abbott's largest bets, (b) capital allocation that visibly balanced growth investment (Exact Sciences) against shareholder returns (dividend increase) in the same period rather than sacrificing one for the other, and (c) no guidance cut analogous to GEHC's post-Q1-2026 reduction — Abbott's FY2026 guidance shift was a pre-disclosed, deal-related dilution figure, not a surprise operational miss.

**Docked from 5/5 because**: (a) the NEC litigation public posture is a genuine, unresolved reputational/financial risk where the optimal management response is not obviously clear-cut, (b) Nutrition's multi-quarter weakness lacks a clearly articulated turnaround plan in sources reviewed, (c) the Exact Sciences deal is too recent (closed 2026-03-23, ~3.5 months before this as_of date) to have any demonstrated integration success evidence yet — the "distribution leverage" thesis is entirely unproven as of this dossier, and (d) full compensation/proxy detail and insider share-ownership-versus-total-outstanding-equity alignment were not independently verified this run.

**What would move this to 5/5**: A subsequent quarter/year showing (a) demonstrated ex-US Cologuard/Cancerguard revenue acceleration attributable to Abbott's distribution network (validating the core M&A thesis), (b) Nutrition stabilization or an explained/credible turnaround plan, (c) the stated $3B 2026 debt paydown actually executed, and (d) a resolution (settlement, legislative shield, or a durable court-of-appeals win) to the NEC litigation that removes ongoing tail-risk uncertainty.

## Upstream -> Abbott -> Downstream

**Upstream inputs**:
- Semiconductor/electronics components (CGM sensor electronics, cardiac device electronics)
- Specialty biosensor materials (glucose-oxidase enzyme membranes for CGM sensors)
- Reagent/assay chemistry inputs (Core Lab consumables)
- Molecular diagnostics inputs — sequencing reagents, stool-collection kits (Cologuard, post-Exact-Sciences)
- Medical-grade polymers/metals (structural heart devices — nitinol, biocompatible materials)
- Dairy/nutritional raw materials (infant formula, nutritional shakes)
- Active pharmaceutical ingredients (Established Pharma branded generics)
- Tariff-exposed global manufacturing network: ~90 production sites worldwide (per general search finding — company cites this network plus ~$500M in new Illinois/Texas investment as tariff mitigation)

**Abbott internal value-add**:
- R&D / device engineering (CGM sensor miniaturization, structural heart device design, electrophysiology mapping/ablation systems)
- Manufacturing (device assembly, reagent/assay production, nutrition product manufacturing, formulation)
- Regulatory affairs (FDA/CE-mark/NMPA clearances across all four segments — a genuine, recurring capability)
- Molecular/genomic diagnostics platform (Cologuard/Oncotype DX/Cancerguard, newly internalized via Exact Sciences)
- Direct + distributor sales force across 160 countries — the single most-cited strategic asset in the Exact Sciences deal rationale
- Branded consumer marketing (CGM direct-to-consumer, Nutrition consumer brands)

**Downstream customers**:
- People with diabetes (CGM — direct-to-consumer + healthcare-system-reimbursed)
- Hospitals / cardiac catheterization labs (structural heart, electrophysiology capital equipment + disposables)
- Clinical/reference laboratories (Core Lab instrument + reagent annuity)
- Primary care physicians / gastroenterologists (Cologuard ordering channel)
- Retail / e-commerce (Nutrition consumer products)
- Government/public health systems and pharmacies (Established Pharma branded generics, emerging markets)
- China VBP tender bodies (Diagnostics — a distinct, price-sensitive procurement channel, same dynamic as the GEHC exemplar)

## Where the Profit Pools Sit

| Value chain link | Profit pool depth | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FreeStyle Libre CGM (Medical Devices) | **Deepest** | Genuine consumable-sensor annuity (~14-day replacement cycle), ~8M-user installed base, ~48-53% global share — the closest thing Abbott has to a GEV-style structural annuity |
| Structural heart / electrophysiology (Medical Devices) | Deep | Procedure-tied device + disposable sales into a growing cardiovascular procedure base; genuine technology/regulatory moat (TriClip, Amplatzer) but more competitive than CGM (Edwards, Medtronic, Boston Scientific all compete directly — see the broader S&P medical batch) |
| Core Laboratory (Diagnostics) | Deep but currently under pressure | Classic instrument + reagent razor/blade (Alinity), but total segment growth masked by COVID-runoff + China VBP |
| Cancer Diagnostics (Exact Sciences, new) | **Unproven at Abbott scale, but Cologuard itself has a genuine standalone moat** | Cologuard is the market leader in noninvasive colorectal screening with real switching/guideline-inclusion barriers; whether Abbott's global distribution actually accelerates ex-US growth is the key open question |
| Established Pharma (branded generics) | Medium | Steady, EM-diversified, but generic-drug economics are inherently more commoditized/price-competitive than device or CGM franchises |
| Nutrition | Weak currently | −9.1% organic Q4 2025; distinct competitive set (Nestle/Reckitt in infant formula); carries NEC litigation legal-tail-risk specific to the preterm-formula sub-line |

## Bottleneck Candidates (developed further in bottleneck_map.md)

1. **FreeStyle Libre installed base + reimbursement/prescription relationships** — switching an established CGM user to a competitor requires a new prescription, insurance reauthorization, and behavior change; real but not absolute friction (Dexcom actively competes for and wins share, per facts.md E51).
2. **Cologuard guideline inclusion / physician ordering habit** (post-Exact-Sciences) — colorectal cancer screening guideline inclusion and established physician-ordering workflows are a genuine, slow-to-replicate barrier for new entrants, though this is Exact Sciences' pre-existing moat, not one Abbott built.
3. **China VBP tendering** — the inverted bottleneck (channel structurally tilted against Abbott/foreign device+diagnostics makers in government-linked procurement), concentrated in the Diagnostics segment specifically, same mechanism as identified in the GEHC exemplar.
4. **Regulatory approval cycles (FDA/CE-mark/NMPA)** — a genuine, recurring, time-based moat across CGM, structural heart, and diagnostics products, but not a permanent barrier (competitors clear their own products on their own timelines).

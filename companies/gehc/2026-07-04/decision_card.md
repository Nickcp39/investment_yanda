## Career Note: GEHC vs United Imaging / Chinese OEMs (AI + Imaging Lens)

GEHC's China revenue fell **−15% YoY in FY2025** (from 10.85% to 9.85% of total revenue) [facts.md E44b] — the clearest, most decision-relevant data point in this dossier for the China med-device/AI competitive thesis. Over the same window, United Imaging (联影医疗, independent industry data, not GEHC-filed) grew revenue at a **31% CAGR (2018-2024)** and reached **~18.2% share** in hospitals level-2-and-above by August 2025 — #3 overall, #1 among domestic high-end brands [facts.md E43-E44]. GPS (GE+Philips+Siemens combined) still holds high-end share leadership (68%/62%/92% in MR/CT/DSA as of 2024), so this is NOT (yet) a story of United Imaging overtaking the global incumbents at the top end — but the trend line, and GEHC's own revenue print, both point the same direction. Meanwhile, GEHC's own AI/imaging strategy (Photonova Spectra2 photon-counting CT, MIM Software oncology AI, Intelerad enterprise imaging, the new "Advanced Imaging Solutions" segment) is real, well-articulated, and — as of this dossier — **completely unproven financially**: no source reviewed quantifies AI-attributable revenue or ASP premium anywhere in GEHC's business. For Nick's 出海/AI-competition research, this is the key open question to track: does GEHC's AI-software layer let it defend premium pricing against cheaper, fast-improving domestic hardware, or is AI becoming table-stakes that both incumbents and challengers now offer — in which case the real battleground reverts to price/scale, which favors United Imaging's trajectory.

## Upstream → GEHC → Downstream

**Upstream inputs**:
- Semiconductor components (memory chips — explicitly named as a Q1 2026 cost-pressure driver [facts.md E35]), superconducting magnet materials (MRI), specialty detector materials (CT — GEHC's proprietary "Deep Silicon" photon-counting detector [facts.md E45]), radioisotope/precursor supply (PDx — subject to the "discrete supplier disruption" that hit Q1 2026 margins [facts.md E35]).
- Contract manufacturing / component suppliers subject to tariff exposure (freight, oil, memory chips cited as FY2026 cost drivers).

**GEHC internal value-add**:
- R&D / device engineering (proprietary detector tech, AI software stack — MIM oncology AI, Clarify DL reconstruction, Photonova Spectra2 PCCT [facts.md E45-E49])
- Manufacturing (Imaging capital equipment — long lead-time, high-precision)
- Radiopharmaceutical production + cold-chain logistics (PDx — short-half-life isotopes require regional manufacturing, a genuine geographic-scale barrier)
- Software/AI layer (increasingly the differentiation layer per management's stated strategy — E50)
- Direct + channel sales force, service/field-engineer network (the installed-base servicing annuity)

**Downstream customers**:
- Hospitals / health systems (capital equipment buyers — long replacement cycles, capex-budget-cyclical)
- Imaging centers / outpatient diagnostic chains
- Government/public health systems (esp. China VBP tenders — a distinct, price-sensitive procurement channel)
- Radiology/nuclear medicine departments (PDx consumable buyers — recurring, per-scan)

## Where the Profit Pools Sit

| Value chain link | Profit pool depth | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Capital equipment sale (Imaging/AVS) | Medium | Lumpy, capex-cycle-dependent, increasingly commoditized at the low/mid end (where United Imaging competes hardest) |
| Service/maintenance (installed base annuity) | **Deep** | Classic med-tech annuity; margin-accretive; the "why replace your MRI service contract" switching cost is real but not quantified this run (OPEN) |
| Consumables (PDx contrast/radiopharma) | **Deepest** | Recurring, high-margin, OEM-agnostic (monetizes ALL imaging volume, not just GEHC's installed base), requires cold-chain/regional-manufacturing scale that is hard to replicate — the least substitutable link in the chain |
| AI/software layer | **Unclear — the central open question** | Strategically positioned as the future differentiation layer (E50) but zero revenue/attach-rate quantification found this run (O7) — cannot yet confirm this is an actual profit pool vs. a bundled feature used to defend hardware ASPs |
| PCS (monitoring/life support) | Weak currently | −8.1% organic Q1 2026; distinct competitive set (Philips/Masimo/Medtronic/Mindray); root cause of weakness unconfirmed (O4) |

## Bottleneck Candidates (developed further in bottleneck_map.md)

1. **Regulatory approval (FDA/NMPA/CE-mark)** — every new AI-enabled imaging product must clear regulatory review (e.g., "View" viewer 510(k) clearance in Q1 2026, Clarify DL still pending [E48-E49]). This is a genuine time-based moat against fast-followers, but China's NMPA approval pathway for domestic players may be faster/more favorable for United Imaging in-market.
2. **Radiopharmaceutical cold-chain/regional manufacturing** — PDx requires production facilities within short transport radius of hospitals (isotope half-lives measured in hours). This is a genuine scale/infrastructure bottleneck not easily replicated by a hardware-only competitor.
3. **Installed base + service contracts** — switching an MRI/CT service contract has real friction (parts, trained field engineers, downtime risk) but is NOT impregnable — third-party service organizations (ISOs) exist in this market (not GEHC-specific, general medtech industry knowledge, not verified this run).
4. **China market access** — VBP tendering explicitly favors domestic-manufacture criteria in many tenders (general policy knowledge; GEHC-specific tender-loss-rate not quantified this run) — this is the CHANNEL bottleneck working AGAINST GEHC in China specifically, the inverse of a moat.

## Background

Peter J. Arduini has been President & CEO of GE HealthCare since its January 2023 spin-off from General Electric [E5, E9]. Prior to GEHC, he was CEO of Integra LifeSciences (2012-2022) — a mid-cap medical device company (neurosurgery, tissue reconstruction) — giving him direct med-tech CEO experience (not a first-time public-company CEO) before taking on GEHC. [E9, LINKEDIN-ARDUINI]

## Track Record at GEHC (2023-present)

**What has gone right**:
- Successfully executed the spin-off and established GEHC as a standalone reporting entity with 3 consecutive years of revenue growth (FY23 $19.55B → FY24 $19.67B → FY25 $20.63B) [E10, E15, E16]
- Adjusted EPS grew every year: $3.03 (FY23, note: this is GAAP diluted, adj EPS not separately confirmed for FY23) → $4.34 (FY24 GAAP) → $4.55/$4.59 GAAP/adj (FY25) [E14-E16]
- Record backlog and book-to-bill consistently >1.0x through FY2025 [E19]
- Proactive AI/software bolt-on M&A: MIM Software, Intelerad ($2.3B) — building the AI/software layer the company is betting its narrative on [E46-E47]
- Organizational simplification: launched the "Advanced Imaging Solutions" combined segment in 2026, installing a new dedicated segment CEO (Phil Rackliffe) — a decisive structural move rather than incremental tinkering [E7]

**What has gone wrong / under pressure**:
- FY2026 guidance CUT after just one quarter (Q1 2026): adj EBIT margin, adj EPS, and FCF guidance all reduced [E36] — this is a credibility mark against management, though partially attributable to external tariff shocks
- Patient Care Solutions segment is deteriorating sharply (−8.1% organic Q1 2026) with no clear public turnaround narrative identified in sources reviewed this run [E27]
- China revenue declining −15% YoY [E44b] — no explicit, quantified China-specific turnaround plan found in available extracts (only general "improving win rates" commentary, E42)
- Margin compression across 3 consecutive periods (16.3% → 15.3% → 13.5% adj EBIT) [E13, E29] raises the question of whether tariff/cost absorption is being managed effectively or whether this reflects a genuine structural mix shift (more PDx/lower-margin-per-unit vs Imaging, competitive pricing pressure in China, etc. — not disambiguated this run)

## Capital Allocation

- M&A: Intelerad ($2.3B cash) funded largely from cash reserves — cash fell from $4.52B to $2.29B in Q1 2026 primarily due to this deal [E34, E47]. This is a genuine, sizeable bet (2.3B against a ~$29.8B market cap, i.e., ~7.7% of market cap) on the AI/cloud-imaging-software thesis — consistent with stated strategy, not scattershot
- Debt: total debt has risen from $8.95B (FY24) to $10.0B (FY25) to $10.17B (Q1'26) [E21, E23, E33] while equity is $10.39B — debt/equity 0.91, moderate but rising, and new credit facilities (364-day revolver + $650M Delayed Draw Term Loan) suggest liquidity management is an active, not passive, concern right now [E38, E33]
- Dividend: company pays a dividend but the exact per-share/yield figure found this run ($0.14 / 0.21%) reads as internally suspect (flagged as data-quality OPEN item O6, not treated as reliable) — cannot confidently assess capital-return discipline this run

## Grade: 3/5

**Rationale**: Arduini is a credible, experienced med-tech operator (prior public-company CEO track record) who has made decisive strategic moves (segment reorg, AI/software M&A) consistent with a coherent stated strategy. This is NOT a founder-visionary or a proven multi-cycle capital allocator at the level of a Strazik (GEV, 4/5 in the exemplar) — the track record at GEHC specifically is only ~3.5 years old, includes a guidance cut within that window, and the two most concerning operating trends (PCS deterioration, China decline) do not yet have a clearly articulated, evidenced fix from management in the sources reviewed. The AI/software bolt-on M&A strategy is directionally sound but unproven (no revenue attribution yet). Docked from a higher grade specifically because: (a) guidance credibility took a hit after only one quarter of FY2026, (b) the two weakest parts of the business (PCS, China) lack a visible turnaround narrative in available sources, and (c) full compensation/proxy detail was not verified this run, preventing a complete incentive-alignment check (unlike the GEV/MSFT exemplars, which explicitly verified CEO comp structure).

**What would move this to 4/5**: A subsequent quarter showing (a) PCS stabilization or an explained/credible turnaround plan, (b) China revenue stabilizing (even a deceleration in the decline rate), (c) confirmed CEO comp structure showing majority equity-linked incentive alignment, and (d) FY2026 guidance held or reaffirmed rather than cut again.

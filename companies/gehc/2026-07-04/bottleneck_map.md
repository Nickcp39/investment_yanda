## Binding Bottleneck Candidate #1: Installed-Base Service Annuity (GLOBAL, OUTSIDE CHINA)

### The Structure
GEHC has decades of cumulative Imaging/AVS equipment installed globally (exact fleet size not disclosed in sources reviewed this run — OPEN). Hospitals that own a GEHC MRI/CT typically renew multi-year service contracts because: (a) OEM parts/diagnostics are often required or strongly preferred for warranty/liability reasons, (b) trained field engineers for GEHC-specific equipment are a genuine scarce resource, (c) downtime risk on critical-care imaging equipment makes hospitals risk-averse about switching servicers.

**Durability**: Real but NOT absolute — independent service organizations (ISOs) are an established channel in medtech generally (industry-general knowledge, not GEHC-quantified this run). Unlike GEV's gas-turbine LTSA (a near-legal monopoly on OEM parts for a 20-year contracted asset), GEHC's service moat is a **weaker, softer form of lock-in**: real switching friction, not a structural monopoly.

**Evidence gap**: No source this run disclosed GEHC's service-attach rate, service-revenue-as-%-of-total, or service-contract renewal rate. This is the single largest quantification gap versus the GEV/MSFT exemplar standard and should be top priority for next refresh (facts.md O1/O2).

## Binding Bottleneck Candidate #2: PDx Consumables — Cold-Chain / Regional Manufacturing Scale

### The Structure
Radiopharmaceutical/contrast agent production for PET/SPECT/CT requires regional manufacturing (isotopes with hour-scale half-lives cannot be shipped globally from a single plant) plus cold-chain logistics. This is a genuine infrastructure bottleneck: a new entrant (domestic or international) cannot simply out-engineer this — they need to build/license regional production capacity.

**Why this matters for the China thesis**: This segment is the LEAST exposed to United Imaging-style hardware substitution, because it monetizes imaging VOLUME (any scanner, any OEM) rather than GEHC EQUIPMENT specifically. PDx is +9.7% to +22.3% organic growth in the periods reviewed [facts.md E17, E27] — the strongest segment in the portfolio. If Nick's career thesis is "where does GEHC's moat survive Chinese OEM hardware competition," PDx is the most likely candidate for a durable answer — BUT: China-specific PDx revenue/growth was NOT separately disclosed this run (only total-company PDx growth), so this cannot be fully confirmed as a China-specific bottleneck advantage. Flagged as a priority verification item for next refresh.

## Binding Bottleneck Candidate #3 (INVERTED — this is a bottleneck AGAINST GEHC): China VBP Tendering + Local-for-Local Procurement Preference

### The Structure
China's volume-based procurement (VBP) tendering system pools hospital purchasing into centralized tenders, and — per general policy knowledge (not GEHC-quantified this specific mechanism this run) — increasingly favors domestic-manufacture criteria in government-linked/subsidized procurement. This is the MIRROR IMAGE of a bottleneck: instead of GEHC controlling a scarce resource, the CHANNEL itself (government tender criteria) is structurally tilted toward domestic OEMs (United Imaging, Mindray, Neusoft) [facts.md E43-E44, industry source].

### The Evidence (now quantified, not just qualitative)
- GEHC China revenue: **$2.14B (FY24, 10.85% of total) → $2.03B (FY25, 9.85% of total) = −15% YoY decline** [E44b] — this is DIRECT financial evidence the local-for-local dynamic is bindingly negative for GEHC today, not a hypothetical future risk.
- United Imaging (independent, non-GEHC source): reached ~18.2% share in hospitals level-2+ by August 2025 (#3 overall, #1 domestic high-end brand), with a 31% revenue CAGR 2018-2024 [E43-E44]. Meanwhile GPS (GE+Philips+Siemens combined) still holds 68%/62%/92% share in high-end MR/CT/DSA as of 2024 [E43] — meaning **GPS collectively still leads in absolute high-end share, but the trend line and GEHC's own revenue print both point the same direction: share is moving from GPS to domestic OEMs, and GEHC specifically is shrinking in-market.**
- Management's own qualitative commentary is cautiously constructive ("improving win rates, more robust imaging funnel, some tender wins" [E42]) but this has NOT yet shown up in the actual revenue line (−15% YoY is the FY2025 full-year print, i.e., AFTER whatever improvement management is describing had time to occur).

### Why This is THE Career-Relevant Finding
This is the most direct, decision-useful data point in the entire dossier for Nick's professional context (China med-device + AI 出海, GEHC as the global benchmark vs United Imaging). It says: **in the one market where a true apples-to-apples GEHC-vs-domestic-OEM comparison is possible, GEHC is currently losing, not winning, and the AI-differentiation strategy management is betting on has not yet been shown (in any source reviewed this run) to be reversing that specific trend.** China is still <10% of total revenue, so this is not yet an existential threat to GEHC as a whole — but it is the clearest available leading indicator of whether GEHC's AI/imaging moat can hold against a well-capitalized, fast-improving domestic competitor once that competitor has scale, and right now the answer trends toward "not fully."

## Bottleneck Durability vs Trend — The Core Tension

**The bull case**: China is <10% of revenue and shrinking, but the other 90%+ (US +6.4%, Other Countries steady) is intact and growing; PDx (the least China/hardware-exposed segment) is the fastest-growing line; GEHC retains high-end technology leadership (photon-counting CT, proprietary detector IP) that United Imaging has not yet matched at the top end per the share data reviewed (United Imaging leads in LOWER-tier CT rows, not the highest-end tier). The AI/software bundling strategy (Intelerad, MIM, segment reorg) is a genuine attempt to move the competitive battle from "spec/price" (where domestic OEMs compete hardest) to "integrated workflow/outcomes" (where GEHC's installed base + software stack + regulatory track record should be harder to replicate quickly).

**The bear case / kill path**: If United Imaging (or Mindray) continues compounding share in China AND begins meaningfully exporting outside China (their stated ambition per general industry knowledge — not verified as an imminent threat this run), the China playbook (VBP + local-for-local tender preference + fast domestic AI-in-imaging catch-up) could become a template repeated in other price-sensitive emerging markets, compressing GEHC's total addressable growth outside its current core (US/EMEA/developed-Other). This is a multi-year, not immediate, risk — but it is the single most important thing to monitor for the career thesis.

**The key open question**: Does GEHC's AI/software layer actually command an ASP premium or attach-rate advantage TODAY, anywhere (China or otherwise)? Zero sources reviewed this run answer this. Until it is answered, "AI is our moat" remains a management narrative, not a demonstrated fact — this should be the explicit, named research priority for the next dossier refresh.

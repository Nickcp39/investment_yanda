| Total equity | $8,464M | $10,390M | not separately confirmed this run |
| Debt/Equity | — | 0.91 | — |

**Read**: Debt rose steadily across all three periods shown. Cash SPIKED in FY2025 (likely debt-funded build-up or timing) then COLLAPSED in Q1 2026 — the Q1 decline is attributed to the Intelerad acquisition ($2.3B cash deal) [E34, E47]. Net debt, after falling FY24→FY25, appears to have JUMPED roughly 40% in a single quarter (Q1 2026) on a derived basis. This is a real, if M&A-explained, leverage increase, and it is happening at the same time adj EBIT margin is compressing — i.e., the numerator (EBITDA) is shrinking while the denominator (net debt) is growing, a combination that mechanically raises leverage multiples faster than either factor alone would suggest. **New credit facilities (364-day revolver, $650M Delayed Draw Term Loan) established in the same period** [E38] suggest active liquidity management, which is prudent but also confirms the balance sheet is not simply "resting" on excess cash — it required incremental facilities.

**Verdict**: Leverage is NOT alarming in absolute terms (D/E 0.91, investment-grade-appropriate profile for a med-tech spin-off with $10B+ of debt inherited/assumed at separation) but the DIRECTION (rising debt, falling cash, compressing margin, M&A-funded from the balance sheet) is a genuine, monitorable yellow flag, not a green one. This is materially different from GEV's "net cash fortress" exemplar profile — GEHC carries real leverage and is currently adding to it during a margin-compression period.

This means, if anything, current reported figures may UNDERSTATE normalized earnings power once tariff impact fades per guidance — a modestly constructive read, though not yet confirmed by an actual guidance beat.

## Financial Quality Verdict

**GOOD but NOT clean/exceptional.** Revenue growth is steady and orders/backlog remain healthy in absolute terms (record backlog, book-to-bill >1x through FY2025), but: (1) margin has compressed 3 consecutive periods, (2) FCF conversion (FCF/NI ~0.70x) is mediocre, not excellent, (3) leverage is rising at the same time margins are falling — a combination that, if it continues for 2+ more quarters without stabilization, would be a genuine credit/quality concern rather than a transient one. This financial profile does NOT support an "exceptional business" verdict on its own — it supports a "solid, moderately-levered med-tech incumbent currently absorbing real cost/competitive headwinds" read, consistent with the moat_map.md conclusion.

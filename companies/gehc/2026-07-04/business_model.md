## What GEHC Sells: Four Segments (as-filed FY2025 structure)

### 1. Imaging (largest segment, ~45% of Q4 2025 revenue at $2.55B)
MRI, CT, Molecular Imaging (PET/SPECT), X-ray, Women's Health (mammography). This is the historical core of "GE Medical Systems" — heavy capital equipment sold to hospitals/imaging centers, increasingly bundled with AI-enabled software (workflow, reconstruction, triage). Q1 2026: $2,299M, +3.8% organic — steady, not spectacular.

**Mechanism**: Sell large capital equipment (6-7 figure ASPs) → multi-year service/maintenance contract attach → periodic software/AI upgrade sales → equipment replacement cycle (7-10 years) drives recurring capex-cycle revenue.

### 2. Advanced Visualization Solutions / AVS (~$1.52B Q4 2025, +5.9%)
Ultrasound (point-of-care + cart-based), procedural guidance, cardiovascular interventional solutions, surgical innovations (mobile C-arms). Lower ASP, higher unit volume than Imaging; faster replacement cycle; competes more on price/portability than Imaging.

### 3. Patient Care Solutions / PCS (~$704M Q1 2026, **−8.1% organic — the weak segment**)
Patient monitoring, diagnostic cardiology (ECG/Holter), maternal-infant care (incubators/fetal monitors), anesthesia delivery, consumables, digital/connected-care solutions. This is a different competitive set (Philips, Masimo, Medtronic, Mindray) than Imaging, and is showing the sharpest deterioration in the portfolio — root cause not yet confirmed (facts.md O4).

### 4. Pharmaceutical Diagnostics / PDx (fastest-growing, +22.3% Q4 2025 / +9.7% Q1 2026)
Contrast media (for CT/MRI) and radiopharmaceutical imaging agents (PET tracers) for radiology/nuclear medicine. This is a **consumable, recurring-revenue** business — every contrast-enhanced scan on ANY vendor's equipment (not just GEHC's) consumes a dose. Structurally the highest-quality revenue stream in the portfolio: high margin, recurring, and non-cyclical relative to capital equipment.

## The Core Mechanism: Installed Base + Consumables + Service Annuity

GEHC's business model mirrors the classic "razor/razorblade" med-tech pattern, but with THREE monetization layers rather than one:

1. **Capital equipment sale** (Imaging, AVS) — the "razor," lumpy, capex-cycle-dependent, increasingly AI-differentiated
2. **Service/maintenance contracts** — the classic annuity; multi-year, high-margin, tied to the installed base of scanners already in the field
3. **Consumables (PDx contrast agents)** — the true recurring "razorblade," monetized per-scan regardless of which OEM's scanner performed the scan

This third layer is a genuine structural advantage: GEHC's Pharmaceutical Diagnostics business earns from imaging volume on ANY hospital's equipment (Siemens, Philips, Canon, United Imaging scanners all use contrast agents), not just GEHC-installed scanners. It is the least import-substitutable of the four segments because it requires radiopharmaceutical manufacturing/distribution infrastructure (short-half-life isotopes require regional production and rapid logistics), not just device engineering.

## FY2025 Segment Reorganization (forward-looking structural change)

Effective for FY2026 reporting, Imaging + AVS combine into "**Advanced Imaging Solutions**" (AIS), a ~$14.6B-revenue segment under new President/CEO Phil Rackliffe [facts.md E7]. Management's stated rationale: simplify operating model, enable "growth and margin acceleration in 2026 and beyond," and better integrate the AI-enabled portfolio across MR/CT/Molecular Imaging/Women's Health/X-ray/Ultrasound/procedural guidance/enterprise imaging under one P&L. This is directionally consistent with CEO Arduini's stated ambition to be measured "less as an imaging hardware vendor" and more as an integrated "healthcare solutions provider" [facts.md E50] — i.e., bundling hardware + AI software + service into a differentiated (harder-to-commoditize) offering, which is the textbook defensive playbook against hardware-only competitors like United Imaging on pure price/spec.

## Why This Matters for the AI/China Career Lens

GEHC's model is the "premium global incumbent" playbook: high-end hardware + deep installed base + increasingly AI-software-differentiated + consumables annuity. United Imaging's model (per independent industry data, not GEHC-filed) is "fast-follower domestic scale + government-procurement alignment + rapid share gain in China specifically." The two are NOT yet head-to-head globally — GEHC's China revenue is shrinking but is only ~10% of the total book; United Imaging is not (yet) a credible global (ex-China) competitor at meaningful scale in the sources reviewed this run. The moat_map.md and bottleneck_map.md develop this tension in depth.

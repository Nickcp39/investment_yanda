## 1. Five-Soul Initial Verdicts

### 1.1 Warren Buffett — Can I hold this 10 years? Do I understand the owner earnings? Is it cheap enough?

- **Verdict: `WATCH`, leaning STARTER-curious**
- **One-line rationale**: I understand BSX's business model easily — sell procedural devices into a growing pool of cardiac/vascular patients, train the physicians, build the evidence base, repeat. - **Application to BSX**: The parallel to American Express is genuinely apt here — a real, if embarrassing, competitive/disclosure stumble (EP share loss + guidance credibility) has hit a fundamentally strong, high-return business, and the price has moved much more than the actual earning power has. ### 1.2 Charlie Munger — Where does this most likely fail? Are incentives poisoned? Is there avoidable stupidity risk?

The avoidable-stupidity risk I'd flag: committing to a $14.5B acquisition 3 weeks before your own stock was about to reveal significant bad news is not itself stupid (the timing was almost certainly not foreseeable), but it DOES mean less balance-sheet flexibility exists right now than would be ideal given the operating turbulence — a self-inflicted (if unlucky) constraint.

## 3. Chair Synthesis (段永平 as lead, consistent with exemplar convention)

### 3.1 Don't-Do List Applied
1. **Don't invest in what I don't understand**: The business model (procedural devices into growing cardiac/vascular patient pools) is clear; the ONE thing I do NOT fully understand is whether Watchman's standalone deceleration is transient or structural — acknowledge this gap explicitly rather than paper over it (this is why the position is a STARTER, not a CORE, allocation).
2. **Don't predict short-term market moves**: Not predicting BSX's next-quarter price; judging business + price today, with Q2 2026 earnings (2026-07-29) as an explicit, near-term confirmation checkpoint, not a precondition for entry.
3. **Don't buy because "everyone else is buying"**: Sell-side "Buy" consensus (30 analysts, $75 target) is noted but explicitly NOT the reason for the verdict — the reason is the independently-derived 35x→16x multiple math plus the confirmed-still-growing absolute EP revenue.
4. **Don't pay up without a margin of safety**: The margin of safety here is REAL but PARTIAL — a genuine, large multiple correction has occurred (unlike GEHC, where no multiple discount existed at all vs peers), but three genuinely unresolved risks (EP trajectory, Watchman cause, litigation) mean the margin of safety is not yet a FULL one. **This is precisely why the verdict is STARTER (small), not CORE or a larger initial size.**
5. **(Added for this case) Don't conflate "management made a forecasting error" with "management committed fraud"**: The securities litigation allegations are unresolved and unproven — treat the litigation as a genuine, trackable tail risk (sized into the position via the SMALL initial allocation), not as a presumption of guilt that would otherwise force an AVOID.

### 3.2 100% Privatization Test (Chair's Own Application)
Buying all of BSX for $67.3B, unable to sell for 10 years: the overall cardiovascular + MedSurg franchise (Endoscopy, Neuromodulation, concomitant Watchman, and EP's still-growing ABSOLUTE revenue even at reduced relative share) would very likely still be larger and more profitable in 10 years (high confidence, backed by a genuinely strong 5-year revenue/margin trend). Whether BSX specifically stabilizes as a durable #2 in EP (vs continuing to lose ground to Medtronic), whether Watchman standalone recovers, and how the Penumbra integration and securities litigation resolve are all genuinely uncertain (medium confidence) — but these are TRACKABLE, resolvable-within-1-2-years uncertainties, not permanent black boxes. **Verdict: yes, with appropriately sized conviction reflecting the real (not manufactured) uncertainty — a small starter position that can be added to as the EP-share and Watchman-standalone questions resolve, is the correct expression of this confidence level.**

### 3.3 Final Verdict

# **STARTER — small initial position (new money) / HOLD-and-monitor (existing positions) — ~2% initial size**

**One-line summary**: Boston Scientific is a genuinely strong, high-growth medtech franchise (revenue nearly doubled 2021-2025, margin +800bps) whose flagship EP/Farapulse business just underwent a real, fast, and unusually well-documented moat stress-test (US PFA share falling from ~100% to ~41% since 2023, with Medtronic now leading at ~48%), compounded by Watchman standalone deceleration, Urology stagnation, an active securities fraud class action, and a large pending debt-funded acquisition (Penumbra, $14.5B). The stock has ALREADY absorbed a massive, well-evidenced multiple correction (~35x→~16x EV/EBITDA) — meaning much, though perhaps not quite all, of the bad news is priced in. This is neither GEV's "exceptional business, wrong price" nor MSFT's "great business, good price, just finish the research" nor GEHC's "ambiguous business quality, fair-not-cheap price" — it is a FOURTH pattern: **"strong, high-growth business, real and partially-realized competitive stress test, price has already given back most of its growth premium, several trackable (not permanent) uncertainties remain unresolved."** The panel split 3-2 toward STARTER after critique, reflecting a genuinely closer call than either exemplar, appropriately expressed via a SMALL initial position size rather than a confident CORE allocation.

- **Panel acceptance boundary**: 3/5 STARTER (small) post-critique, 2/5 WATCH (both explicitly "softened," not firm) — a real, non-unanimous split honestly disclosed rather than forced to false consensus.
- **What would move this to CORE / larger size**: (a) Q2 2026 earnings (2026-07-29) confirming NO fourth guidance cut, (b) OPAL HDx penetration data showing continued scaling correlated with EP share stabilization, (c) Watchman standalone root cause clarified as transient (not structural), (d) securities litigation resolving without a material adverse finding, (e) Penumbra integration proceeding on-plan post-close.
- **What would move this to AVOID**: A fourth guidance cut, EP share continuing to erode past ~35% with no stabilization signal, an adverse litigation discovery revealing genuine concealment, or Penumbra integration problems compounding materially with the existing operating pressures.

### 3.4 Kill Criteria (observable thresholds)

1. **K-A [EP share continues eroding]**: US PFA share falls below ~35% with no stabilization signal by Q4 2026 → re-underwrite the EP franchise's long-term competitive position downward, consider trimming.
2. **K-B [Fourth guidance cut]**: Any further FY2026 (or initial FY2027) guidance reduction citing the same EP/Watchman/Urology factors → the "three cuts and done" thesis is falsified, treat management's forward guidance with much heavier discounting, move toward AVOID.
3. **K-C [Watchman standalone structural]**: Standalone Watchman procedures remain negative for 2+ more consecutive quarters without a credible, specific (not generic) management explanation → treat as a second structural franchise problem, not a one-off.
4. **K-D [Litigation adverse outcome]**: Any court ruling, settlement admission, or discovery revelation indicating genuine (not just alleged) concealment → immediate downgrade to AVOID regardless of price, given the governance implications extend beyond this single issue.
5. **K-E [Penumbra integration/leverage deterioration]**: Post-close (H2 2026) leverage ratio rising meaningfully past ~3.0x without a clear deleveraging path, or integration costs materially exceeding the guided -$0.06 to -$0.08 EPS dilution → credit-quality and capital-allocation concern, reassess sizing.
6. **K-F [Valuation discipline]**: Price rises above ~$55-58 without a corresponding fundamentals confirmation (pure multiple re-expansion before the open questions resolve) → no-chase, do not add at a re-inflated multiple until the underlying questions are actually answered.

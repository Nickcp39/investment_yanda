## Career Note: BSX's Farapulse/EP Story — A Clean Case Study in First-Mover Moat Erosion (AI/China Lens)

The single most decision-useful fact in this dossier for Nick's career context (medical imaging + deep learning + China med-device/出海): Boston Scientific's Farapulse pulsed-field-ablation (PFA) franchise went from a **100% US market-share monopoly (2023)** — a genuine first-mover position built on real technological differentiation (non-thermal ablation, ~20 assigned patents, the largest cumulative clinical evidence base at 500,000+ patients treated) — to **trailing Medtronic (41% vs 48% share) within about 2.5 years**, as Medtronic (Jan 2024), J&J/Biosense Webster (Dec 2024), and now Abbott (Jan 2026) all entered with their own independently-developed PFA technology. This happened DESPITE BSX's genuine technical and evidence-base advantages, demonstrating with unusually clean data that **first-mover advantage + real technological merit is NOT sufficient to sustain durable category leadership absent a structural (not merely timing-based) barrier** — the patents protected BSX's SPECIFIC implementation, not the PFA modality itself, so well-capitalized competitors simply built their own. BSX's best current answer to this problem — the OPAL HDx mapping system + FARAVIEW software, an exclusive hardware+software ecosystem bundle — is real and growing (now used by ~1-in-3 Farapulse accounts) but not yet proven to defend share at scale. This is DIRECTLY transferable to evaluating any China-vs-West AI-imaging or AI-diagnostics first-mover thesis (e.g., United Imaging vs. incumbents, or any AI-native medtech startup's competitive position): the critical underwriting question is never "who got there first" but **"what SPECIFICALLY prevents a well-resourced fast-follower from replicating this within 2-3 years"** — patents on one implementation, without a broader structural lock-in (true switching costs, network effects, or an ecosystem that competitors cannot quickly replicate), erodes fast once serious, well-funded competition arrives. Separately, unlike the GEHC exemplar (where China revenue is cleanly disclosed and directly comparable to United Imaging), BSX does NOT separately disclose China revenue — it is bundled into a broader "Emerging Markets" category — meaning this dossier's China-specific findings are much thinner, and GEHC remains the more directly relevant precedent for a clean China-vs-domestic-OEM comparison within this research batch.

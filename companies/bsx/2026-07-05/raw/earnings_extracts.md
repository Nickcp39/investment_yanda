## Bernstein 42nd Annual Strategic Decisions Conference (2026-05-27) — SECOND guidance cut

Not a standalone SEC 8-K (verbal/presentation-based guidance revision, common for conference remarks). CEO Mahoney cited THREE unanticipated factors (same three areas as April, but WORSE): (1) slower-than-expected WATCHMAN FLX standalone adoption — standalone procedures declining since February 2026 for the first time, concomitant procedures still strong; (2) competitive encroachment in US electrophysiology (share erosion continuing/worse than April framing); (3) macro pressure on hospital capital spending affecting Urology and broader capital-equipment sales.

**Revised guidance**: FY2026 organic growth 6.5-8.0% (narrowed/lowered from the April 7.0-8.5% reported / 6.5-8.0% organic); Q2 2026 organic growth guided at just 5-7%.

**Stock reaction**: -10.3% to -12.5% intraday, new 52-week low $50.75 that day. Analyst reaction: Daiwa downgraded to Neutral (target $83→$60); Bank of America cut target $105→$68 (maintained Buy).

## Penumbra Acquisition (announced 2026-01-15)

Definitive cash-and-stock agreement: Penumbra valued at $374/share, ~$14.5B enterprise value. Consideration: ~73% cash / ~27% stock (Penumbra holders can elect $374 cash or 3.8721 BSX shares, subject to proration). Cash portion (~$11B) to be funded via cash on hand + new debt issuance (explains the $3B/$2B/$6B new credit facilities seen in the Q1 2026 balance sheet, raw/earnings_extracts.md above). Expected close: H2 2026, subject to Penumbra shareholder vote (held 2026-05-06) and regulatory clearance. Expected EPS impact: -$0.06 to -$0.08 dilutive to adjusted EPS in year 1 post-close, neutral-to-slightly-accretive year 2, accretive thereafter. Strategic rationale: re-entry into neurovascular market (Penumbra is a neurovascular/thrombectomy device maker).

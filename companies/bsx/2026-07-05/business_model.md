## What BSX Sells: Two Reportable Segments (as-filed, post Q4-2025 reorg)

### 1. Cardiovascular (66% of FY2025 revenue, $13.25B, +20.8% organic — the growth engine)

Formed in Q4 2025 by combining the legacy Cardiology and Peripheral Interventions businesses. Comprises four product/business lines:

- **Electrophysiology (Farapulse PFA + OPAL HDx mapping)**: BSX's fastest-growing, highest-profile, and most contested franchise. Q1 2026 organic growth +22% globally (+18% US, +30% international) — still growing FAST in absolute terms, but LOSING RELATIVE SHARE (US PFA share fell from ~100% in 2023 to ~41% by early 2026 as Medtronic (48%), J&J (11%), and now Abbott (1%, just launched) all entered). This is the single most consequential business-quality question in this dossier.
- **WATCHMAN (LAAC — Left Atrial Appendage Closure)**: Q1 2026 sales $506M (+19.2% YoY), following +29% growth in both Q4 2025 and FY2025. Management flagged a split emerging since February 2026: STANDALONE Watchman procedures declining for the first time, while CONCOMITANT procedures (implanted alongside another cardiac procedure) remain strong — one of the three cited drivers of the FY2026 guidance cuts.
- **Cardiac Rhythm Management (CRM)**: pacemakers/ICDs — a mature, lower-growth product line (Medtronic-dominated category); not separately broken out in disclosures reviewed this run.
- **Interventional Cardiology / Peripheral Interventions**: coronary stents, structural heart (BSX withdrew from US TAVR in 2020, reportedly re-entering with Acurate Neo2), peripheral vascular devices (incl. Silk Road Medical carotid/TCAR technology, acquired 2024).

### 2. MedSurg (34% of FY2025 revenue, $6.824B, +6.7% organic — the stable, lower-growth base)

Three business units, Q1 2026 figures:
- **Endoscopy** ($736M, +6.8% organic) — GI devices, largest MedSurg unit
- **Urology** ($646M, +0.5% organic — the third named guidance-cut driver; growth stalled to near-zero)
- **Neuromodulation** ($318M, +15.4% organic — fastest-growing MedSurg unit; includes the 2026 Nalu Medical acquisition, peripheral nerve stimulation for chronic pain)

## The Core Mechanism: Device-Sale + Procedural-Franchise Model

Unlike a pure consumables/annuity model (e.g., GEHC's contrast-agent PDx business), BSX's model is closer to a **procedural-franchise razor/razorblade** pattern specific to interventional medicine:

1. **Capital equipment / mapping systems** (OPAL HDx) — sold or placed with hospitals, creates the platform
2. **Disposable procedural devices** (Farapulse catheters, Watchman implants, stents) — the true recurring revenue, consumed PER-PROCEDURE, tied to procedure VOLUME growth (an aging population + rising AFib/structural-heart-disease incidence is a genuine multi-decade tailwind) rather than a fixed installed base
3. **Physician training/certification** — a genuine, if unquantified, switching-cost moat: physicians trained and credentialed on BSX's Watchman/Farapulse workflow are less likely to switch to a competing device mid-career without a strong reason, though this moat is clearly NOT strong enough to have prevented the PFA share collapse (see moat_map.md)
4. **Software/ecosystem lock-in (emerging)**: FARAVIEW software, exclusive to OPAL HDx + FARAWAVE NAV catheter, is adopted by "1 in 3" Farapulse accounts — a genuine, growing (not yet majority) hardware+software bundle that is harder for competitors to replicate than the catheter alone

## The Three-Event Guidance-Cut Narrative (facts.md E7) — Why This Matters for the Business Model

Boston Scientific's FY2026 guidance was cut from an initial 10.5-11.5% reported growth (Feb 4, 2026) to 7.0-8.5% (Apr 22, 2026) to 6.5-8.0% organic (May 27, 2026 Bernstein conference) — three sequential downward revisions in under 4 months, driven by the SAME three factors each time (EP competitive share loss, Watchman standalone deceleration, Urology softness), each time WORSE than management's own prior framing suggested. This pattern — repeatedly under-forecasting the SPEED of competitive/demand deterioration in its own highest-profile franchise — is a legitimate operator-credibility concern (see operator_underwriting.md) as much as a business-quality concern.

## Capital Allocation: The Penumbra Acquisition ($14.5B EV, announced 2026-01-15)

BSX agreed to acquire Penumbra (neurovascular/thrombectomy devices) for ~$14.5B enterprise value ($374/share, ~73% cash/~27% stock), re-entering the neurovascular market. The ~$11B cash portion is being funded via cash-on-hand + new debt (BSX added $3B revolver + $2B 364-day revolver + $6B delayed-draw term loan in Q1 2026, clearly pre-positioned for this). Expected to be modestly EPS-dilutive (-$0.06 to -$0.08) in year 1, accretive thereafter. This is BSX's largest-ever acquisition, announced right as the EP-driven stock decline was beginning (the merger agreement predates the Feb 4 gap-down by 3 weeks) — meaning BSX committed to a large, debt-funded, dilutive deal just before its own stock (which could have been a cheaper acquisition currency) began a ~58% decline. This is a genuine capital-allocation-timing risk worth monitoring (see financials/financial_quality.md and inversion_map.md).

BSX's broader M&A cadence has been active: Silk Road Medical ($1.18B, Sept 2024), Axonics ($3.7B, Nov 2024), Bolt Medical (completed Mar 2025), SoniVie (May 2025), Elutia bioenvelope ($88M, Oct 2025), Nalu Medical ($533M), and now Penumbra ($14.5B, pending). Goodwill + intangibles were ~59% of total assets as of the most recent balance-sheet data found (Q3 2025 basis, $18.2B goodwill + $7.2B intangibles / $42.7B total assets) — consistent with an M&A-driven growth model, not a purely organic one.

## Why This Matters for the AI/China Career Lens

BSX's model is fundamentally different from GEHC's installed-base-imaging model: it is a **high-growth procedural-device franchise** business, most exposed to (a) fast-moving MEDTECH COMPETITIVE dynamics within the US (the PFA share story is a US-centric competitive battle among Medtronic/J&J/Abbott/BSX, not primarily a China-vs-West story), and (b) a genuinely under-quantified China/Emerging-Markets exposure (BSX does not break out China separately — it is bundled into "Emerging Markets," +15.4% reported/+13.0% operational FY2025 per search-derived data, O-item pending primary confirmation). Unlike GEHC (where China domestic-OEM substitution is a clean, directly observable, decisive data point), BSX's most decisive competitive story this run is a DOMESTIC US battle between global med-tech giants (Medtronic overtaking BSX in PFA share) — a different, but equally career-relevant, lesson: even a category-creating first-mover (BSX invented/pioneered clinical PFA adoption via the Farapulse acquisition) can lose share to fast-following, well-capitalized incumbents within ~2 years absent a structural (not just first-mover-timing) moat. This is directly relevant to how Nick should think about AI/imaging competitive dynamics generally — first-mover advantage alone, without a structural lock-in (patents that block the CATEGORY not just one implementation, true switching costs, or network effects), erodes fast once well-resourced competitors decide to compete seriously.

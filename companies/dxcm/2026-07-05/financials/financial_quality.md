**The one specific margin-quality caveat**: FY2025's FULL-YEAR gross margin actually DECLINED 40bp (60.5% -> 60.1%), with the company's own 10-K attributing this to "inefficiencies associated with ensuring supply availability, build configurations that lowered production yield, and total replacement costs" [facts.md E25] — meaning the Q1 2026 610bp YoY gross-margin expansion is recovering FROM a self-acknowledged FY2025 manufacturing stumble, not simply extending an uninterrupted improvement trend. The Q1 2026 print is genuinely strong, but it is one quarter of recovery data following one full year of acknowledged manufacturing inefficiency — worth continued monitoring, not yet a fully "proven" multi-year trend.

**Read**: This is the single most distinctive financial-quality fact in this dossier relative to its batch peers. While ABT levered UP ~6x in one quarter to fund Exact Sciences, and GEHC levered up to fund Intelerad, **DexCom's total debt actually DECLINED** year-over-year (from $2,441.4M to $1,240.9M) as the 2025 Notes matured and were repaid in cash — DexCom chose to retire debt with cash on hand rather than refinance or lever up further. The company now carries only ONE debt instrument: $1.25B of 0.375%-coupon convertible notes due May 2028 — an extremely cheap, long-dated, non-amortizing liability. Combined with $2.0B of cash + short-term securities, DexCom sits in a genuine net-cash position (~$760M), a materially more conservative balance sheet than either ABT or GEHC in this same batch. This gives DexCom real optionality (hence the new $1B buyback) without needing to delever first — though it also means DexCom has, to date, chosen NOT to pursue the kind of large, TAM-expanding acquisition ABT made (Exact Sciences) or GEHC made (Intelerad), instead betting on organic R&D (G8) and internal product expansion (Stelo) to grow the addressable market. Whether this conservative posture is optimal (lower risk) or suboptimal (under-leveraging a clearly successful franchise) is a genuine, unresolved capital-allocation question — see operator_underwriting.md.

## Summary Financial-Quality Verdict

DexCom's FY2025-into-Q1-2026 financial trajectory is the most unambiguously POSITIVE financial-quality pattern identified in this S&P 500 medical batch to date: organic revenue growth accelerating (not decelerating), gross and operating margins expanding sharply (not compressing), a genuine net-cash balance sheet (not net-debt), no material one-off earnings distortions requiring a "normalized" recalculation, and credible, recently-raised forward guidance. The counterweights are: (a) this recovery follows a severe, real 2024 crisis whose root cause (distribution-channel management) has arguably recurred in a different form in 2026 (see bottleneck_map.md), so the "quality" of the improvement should be read as genuine-but-recent, not yet multi-year-proven; and (b) the FY2025 gross-margin softness (self-acknowledged manufacturing inefficiency) means the Q1 2026 margin expansion is partly a recovery from an own-goal, not purely structural improvement — a nuance worth carrying into the valuation discussion rather than extrapolating Q1 2026's rate of margin expansion indefinitely.

# DXCM Business Model (M2 Theme / Mechanism)

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E19-E37, E42-E49

---

## What DexCom Sells: A Single-Category, Two-Tier CGM Portfolio

Unlike ABT (four diversified segments) or GEHC (imaging + services), DexCom is a **single-category pure-play**: continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) systems. All revenue derives from one core mechanism sold across two tiers:

### Prescription CGM (the core franchise)
- **Dexcom G7 / G7 15 Day**: flagship integrated CGM system. G7 launched 2023; G7 15 Day (extended wear to 15.5 days) launched late 2025, FDA-authorized April 2025. This is the "performance-first" premium tier — smaller sensor, shorter warm-up, direct smartphone integration.
- **Dexcom G6**: prior-generation system, still sold into markets/payer contracts not yet transitioned to G7.
- **Dexcom ONE+**: value-tier CGM explicitly positioned to displace fingerstick blood-glucose testing — a lower-cost/broader-access SKU.

### OTC / Non-Prescription Biosensor (the new growth vector)
- **Stelo**: launched August 2024 as the first FDA-cleared **over-the-counter** glucose biosensor in the US, targeted at adults with prediabetes and non-insulin-using Type 2 diabetes. Pediatric clearance added June 2026 (E69) — the first OTC CGM cleared for children. This is DexCom's direct answer to the "125 million Americans with prediabetes/T2D" addressable population that prescription CGM structurally cannot reach (no diagnosis/prescription required).

## The Core Mechanism: A Genuine Consumable-Sensor Razor/Blade, With a Newly-Added OTC Funnel

DexCom's model is the purest version of the CGM consumables annuity in this entire S&P 500 medical batch: **100% of revenue is CGM-derived** (vs. ABT's ~48% Medical Devices / broader diversified base). Sensors are disposable and replaced on a fixed cadence (10 days for G6, 10 days for G7, 15.5 days for G7 15 Day) — genuine recurring revenue tied to a chronic condition (diabetes) that does not depend on capital-equipment replacement cycles, closely paralleling the ABT/FreeStyle-Libre mechanism described in the ABT exemplar, but WITHOUT the diversification cushion ABT has from its other three segments. This concentration cuts both ways: DexCom's fortunes rise and fall entirely with CGM volume/pricing/competitive dynamics — there is no Established-Pharma-style ballast segment to absorb a bad quarter.

**Revenue channel structure** (FY2025): Distributor channel (DME/pharmacy) = 85% of revenue ($3,959.0M); Direct channel = 15% ($703.0M), disproportionately International-weighted. The Distributor channel is also where the 2024 crisis concentrated (DME share loss, facts.md E52) and where the May 2026 stolen-sensor incident originated (unauthorized distributor Pharmsource, facts.md E57) — i.e., DexCom's Achilles' heel operationally is channel-partner control, not its own direct commercial organization.

## Geographic Mix: US-Concentrated, Internationally Expanding, China Undisclosed

FY2025: US 71.5% of revenue ($3,334.9M) / International 28.5% ($1,327.1M). Q1 2026: US 69.8% / International 30.2% — International mix is expanding modestly. Unlike ABT and GEHC in this batch, **DexCom's own SEC filings do not break out China (or any single country) separately** — the 10-Q explicitly states no individual country outside the US exceeded 10% of revenue (facts.md E23). This means DexCom's China exposure, while flagged as a competitive-risk item in its own 10-K ("emerging competitors primarily located in China," E61), is structurally un-quantifiable from company disclosure alone — a genuine evidence gap rather than a confirmed "small exposure" finding.

## FY2026 Strategic Pivot: From "CGM for Diagnosed Diabetics" to "Biosensing for Everyone" (May 2026 Investor Day)

The May 2026 Investor Day reframed DexCom's addressable market explicitly beyond insulin-using diabetics: 2030 targets include >10% organic revenue growth, gross margin expansion to 67-69% (from 60.1% FY2025), covered lives growing from ~23M to ~52M, and a next-generation **G8** platform (smaller, cheaper to manufacture, "measures signals beyond glucose") targeted for ~18-month-out launch. Combined with Stelo's OTC/pediatric expansion and the CONNECT trial's non-insulin-T2D clinical validation (facts.md E68), the strategic narrative is a shift from a diabetes-management device company toward a broader "metabolic health / biosensing" platform — explicitly invoking hospital, pregnancy, and general-wellness use cases in the 10-Q's own "New Opportunities" language (facts.md E44 area). This is DexCom's version of a TAM-expansion bet, structurally similar in ambition to ABT's Exact-Sciences-driven TAM expansion (into cancer diagnostics) but pursued organically/internally (G8, Stelo) rather than via large acquisition — a meaningfully lower-capital-risk approach given DexCom's net-cash balance sheet (facts.md E43), though also slower and less immediately provable.

## Why This Matters for the AI/China Career Lens

DexCom's single-category concentration makes it the cleanest CGM-duopoly comparison point to ABT/FreeStyle-Libre in this entire batch. Both companies explicitly, directly name each other as primary competitors in their own SEC filings (facts.md E60, cross-referenced against ABT's own risk factors) — this is a genuine head-to-head rivalry, not an incidental overlap. The Stelo OTC + Signos AI-partnership angle (facts.md E70) is the most direct AI-adjacent thread: DexCom's sensor data is being used as the substrate for third-party AI-powered glucose-and-weight-management products, which is conceptually adjacent to Nick's imaging/DL background (raw biosensor signal -> ML-driven interpretation/prediction layer) even though DexCom itself does not yet monetize this AI layer directly (no source found this run quantifies AI/software-attributable revenue — see facts.md I10/O7). On China specifically: DexCom's own 10-K flags "emerging competitors primarily located in China" as a named risk, but (unlike ABT/GEHC) provides zero disclosed China revenue or market-share data — meaning any comparison to Chinese domestic CGM entrants (e.g., Sinocare, Micro Tech Medical) would need to be built entirely from third-party/industry-research sources, not DexCom's own filings. This is a genuine, career-relevant monitoring gap: if China's domestic CGM makers are indeed "emerging" as DexCom itself states, DexCom currently has the least visibility into (and the least disclosed exposure to) that specific competitive threat of any name reviewed in this batch so far.

## Upstream -> DexCom -> Downstream

**Upstream inputs**:
- Glucose-oxidase enzyme membrane / biosensor chemistry (the core sensing element of every CGM sensor)
- Semiconductor/electronics components (transmitter chips, Bluetooth radios, custom silicon — the "new silicon chip design" explicitly cited for the future G8 platform, facts.md E46)
- Medical-grade adhesives and biocompatible polymers (sensor housing, wearable adhesive patch)
- Contract manufacturing / assembly capacity (DexCom's own FY2025 10-K flags "inefficiencies associated with ensuring supply availability, build configurations that lowered production yield" as the explicit driver of FY2025's gross-margin decline, facts.md E25 — i.e., DexCom's own upstream manufacturing execution is a directly disclosed, recent point of weakness)
- Mobile OS platform dependency (iOS/Android compatibility required for G7/G7 15 Day's smartphone-integrated display model, facts.md E19 area)

**DexCom internal value-add**:
- R&D / sensor engineering (miniaturization, wear-time extension 10-day -> 15.5-day -> future G8, accuracy/MARD improvement — the company's stated core competitive differentiator, facts.md E65)
- Regulatory affairs (FDA 510(k) clearances — G7 15 Day April 2025, Stelo pediatric June 2026 — a genuine, recurring, demonstrated capability)
- Clinical trials infrastructure (CONNECT RCT, facts.md E68 — used both for label expansion and competitive/marketing differentiation)
- Manufacturing (in-house + contract, currently the site of the FY2025 margin-drag issue, and the site of the May 2026 scrap-destruction-process failure that enabled the stolen-sensor incident, facts.md E55)
- Direct sales force (US + select international) + distributor network (DME/pharmacy) — the site of BOTH the 2024 crisis (sales-force reorg disruption) and the 2026 quality incident (unauthorized distributor Pharmsource), i.e., channel/distribution execution is DexCom's most recurring operational vulnerability, appearing in two separate multi-year incidents
- Software/app layer (Dexcom Clarity, Follow, Share) — data visualization and caregiver-sharing tools bundled with hardware, not separately monetized (facts.md I10/O7)

**Downstream customers**:
- People with Type 1 and insulin-using Type 2 diabetes (prescription G6/G7/G7 15 Day — reimbursed via insurance/DME benefit)
- Non-insulin Type 2 diabetics and prediabetics (Stelo — OTC, cash-pay, no prescription required; now including pediatric)
- Endocrinologists, physicians, diabetes educators (the prescribing/influencing channel DexCom's direct sales force targets, facts.md E22)
- Insulin pump / automated-insulin-delivery manufacturers (integration partners — DexCom's 10-K itself flags Abbott's parallel AID integrations as a competitive dynamic, facts.md E60 area)
- Third-party digital-health platforms (Signos — AI-powered weight-loss/GLP-1-adjacent glucose tracking built on DexCom sensor data, facts.md E70) — DexCom's clearest current AI-ecosystem touchpoint, monetized by the partner, not DexCom directly
- Unauthorized/gray-market distributors (Pharmsource — the unintended downstream node that enabled the May 2026 stolen-sensor incident)

## Where the Profit Pools Sit

| Value chain link | Profit pool depth | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Prescription CGM (G6/G7/G7 15 Day) | **Deep, but 100% of the company** | Genuine consumable-sensor annuity (10-15.5 day replacement cycle); ~33.9% global / ~44.7% US CGM revenue share (facts.md E64) — no diversification cushion if this franchise stalls, unlike ABT's four-segment structure |
| Stelo (OTC biosensor) | **Real optionality, financially unquantified** | Explicitly excluded from DexCom's own "net new customers" headline metric (facts.md E30) — the company itself has not yet folded it into primary growth disclosure, suggesting immateriality-to-date rather than a disclosure oversight |
| Manufacturing / supply chain | **Currently a demonstrated point of weakness, not strength** | FY2025 margin decline (E25) + May 2026 stolen-sensor incident (E55-E59) both trace to manufacturing/scrap-process execution — two distinct, recent, primary-source-confirmed operational failures in the same value-chain link |
| Distribution/channel (DME, pharmacy, unauthorized resellers) | **Recurring vulnerability** | Both the 2024 crisis (sales-force/DME disruption) and the 2026 quality incident (unauthorized distributor) originate here — a genuine pattern across two multi-year, unrelated incidents, worth flagging as a structural (not one-off) risk area |
| Software/data layer (Clarity, Signos partnership) | Shallow/unmonetized directly | Real strategic potential (AI-driven glucose prediction, third-party ecosystem), zero quantified DexCom-direct revenue this run |

## Comparison to ABT/FreeStyle Libre (same value chain, different company)

DexCom and Abbott's CGM businesses run through structurally identical value chains (enzyme-sensor chemistry -> wearable hardware -> prescription-or-OTC channel -> chronic-disease consumer), competing directly at every downstream node. The key structural difference: Abbott's CGM sits inside a four-segment company that can absorb a CGM-specific stumble; DexCom's entire enterprise value rides on this one value chain succeeding, which is both the source of its focus/execution advantage (better MARD accuracy, faster feature iteration) and its concentration risk (the 2024 crisis had nowhere to hide within a broader portfolio, unlike an equivalent stumble would for Abbott).

## Inversion Question: How Does This Investment Permanently Impair Capital?

Working backward from failure — what would have to be true for a DexCom investment at $71.25 to be a genuine, permanent loss (not just a volatile drawdown, which this stock has already demonstrated it is highly prone to — beta 1.45, a 40% single-day crash in 2024)?

---

## Kill Path 1: Abbott's Libre Franchise Wins the Global CGM Duopoly Decisively, Squeezing DexCom's Growth and Pricing Power

**Mechanism**: Abbott already leads globally (52.83% vs DexCom's 33.89% revenue share, facts.md E64) via superior international distribution reach (160 countries per the ABT dossier). If Abbott's larger global scale lets it out-invest DexCom in emerging-market expansion, out-negotiate on payer/rebate terms, or simply out-execute on new-market entry faster than DexCom can follow, DexCom's growth could structurally decelerate toward Abbott's more "accessibility/affordability"-priced tier, compressing DexCom's premium ASP and gross margin over a multi-year horizon — even without DexCom losing its US technical-accuracy edge.

**Evidence for**: Abbott's global share lead is real and already-realized, not speculative. DexCom's own 10-K names Abbott as its primary CGM competitor for BOTH its prescription line AND Stelo specifically (facts.md E60) — i.e., Abbott competes with DexCom at every tier, not just the premium segment. DexCom's International revenue mix (28.5% FY2025) is smaller than Abbott's non-US-driven global lead would suggest DexCom has fully captured — DexCom is structurally more US-concentrated than Abbott.

**Evidence against / mitigant**: The US market — the single largest, highest-ASP CGM market — is genuinely near-even (44.7% DexCom vs 48.5% Abbott, facts.md E64), and DexCom holds a measurable, independently-verified accuracy edge (MARD 8.2% vs 9.2%, facts.md E65) that gives it a real basis to keep winning new-patient starts on clinical merit, not just installed-base inertia. DexCom's Q1 2026 results (accelerating organic growth, expanding margins) show no current evidence of Abbott-driven pricing/share pressure materializing — if anything, the opposite (DexCom accelerating faster than the CGM market's ~16.6% CAGR, facts.md E67).

**Verdict**: The single most important, largest-magnitude, multi-year (not 1-2 quarter) structural risk in this dossier — directly the mirror image of Kill Path 4 in the ABT dossier (Dexcom eroding Abbott's leadership). As of this as_of date, the EVIDENCE currently favors DexCom (accelerating growth, margin expansion, accuracy edge, near-even US share), but the STRUCTURAL risk (Abbott's larger global scale/distribution advantage) remains real and unresolved over a 3-5 year horizon.

## Kill Path 2: GLP-1 Adoption Substitutes For, Rather Than Complements, CGM Usage (COMPANY-ACKNOWLEDGED RISK, NOT JUST BEAR-CASE SPECULATION)

**Mechanism**: DexCom's OWN 10-K explicitly states: "we are also aware of the increasing use of GLP-1 products for the treatment of obesity and Type 2 diabetes. While we believe that GLP-1s are a companion product... these treatments could potentially compete with our CGM systems and reduce sales of our products" [facts.md E62]. The mechanism: if GLP-1 drugs (Ozempic/Wegovy/Zepbound-class) become so effective at normalizing blood glucose and driving weight loss that patients' physicians/payers no longer see CGM monitoring as clinically necessary — particularly for the large non-insulin Type 2 and prediabetic population Stelo specifically targets — CGM could be structurally displaced rather than complemented, cutting into DexCom's TAM-expansion thesis at its core.

**Evidence for**: This is a DIRECT company admission in a primary regulatory filing, not analyst speculation — a genuinely unusual level of self-disclosed candor on a structural risk. The population most exposed to this substitution risk (non-insulin T2D/prediabetic, GLP-1-eligible) is EXACTLY the population Stelo targets — meaning DexCom's own TAM-expansion growth vector is the same population most at risk of GLP-1 substitution, not a diversified hedge against it.

**Evidence against / mitigant**: The CONNECT trial (facts.md E68) provides genuine clinical evidence supporting the complementary thesis — CGM use produced statistically significant A1C improvement in non-insulin T2D patients, a population that would plausibly ALSO be on GLP-1s. The Signos partnership (facts.md E70) is a live, real-world commercial proof point of CGM being bundled WITH GLP-1-adjacent weight-management programs, not displaced by them, in the market today. If GLP-1 users still benefit from real-time behavioral feedback (seeing how diet/exercise affects glucose) even while on medication, CGM's proposition to this population may survive independent of insulin dependency.

**Verdict**: A REAL, company-acknowledged, structurally important risk — genuinely more concerning than a typical bear-case argument BECAUSE DexCom itself concedes the substitution risk in its own filing. Not yet showing up in the numbers (Q1 2026 growth is accelerating, not decelerating), but this is exactly the kind of risk that could take multiple years to either resolve favorably (CGM proves complementary and durably necessary) or unfavorably (GLP-1 penetration eventually erodes the addressable non-insulin population DexCom is counting on for future growth).

## Kill Path 3: Distribution/Channel Control Fails a Third Time, Confirming a Structural Management Weakness

**Mechanism**: Two distinct, primary-source-confirmed incidents (2024 sales-force/DME-channel crisis; 2026 stolen-sensor/unauthorized-distributor incident) both trace to the same value-chain link: distribution and channel control (bottleneck_map.md Candidate #2). If a third incident occurs — a further sales-force disruption, another quality/supply-chain breach reaching patients via an uncontrolled channel, or an FDA quality-systems action arising from the May 2026 incident — this would confirm a genuine, structural management-competence gap in exactly the part of the business DexCom does not fully control end-to-end, rather than two unrelated, coincidental events.

**Evidence for**: The pattern is real and already twice-realized within roughly 24 months. New CEO Jake Leach was COO during the 2024 crisis (operator_underwriting.md) — meaning the leadership team most responsible for preventing a recurrence is largely the SAME team that oversaw the first failure, not a clean-slate replacement.

**Evidence against / mitigant**: The May 2026 incident, while genuinely concerning, appears (per available disclosure) more narrowly scoped than the 2024 crisis — two specific lot numbers, no severe adverse events reported, and prompt public disclosure via FDA safety communication and company press release (facts.md E55-E58) rather than a delayed or minimized response. This suggests DIFFERENT root causes (2024 = commercial/sales-force strategy error; 2026 = physical/manufacturing security control gap) rather than a single, recurring specific failure mode — genuinely two different kinds of distribution-adjacent problems, which is a somewhat less alarming pattern than if the exact same failure repeated.

**Verdict**: MODERATE, testable within 1-2 quarters (Q2 2026 earnings, 2026-07-29, is the near-term tell for whether the stolen-sensor incident has any measurable prescriber-confidence or DME-relationship impact) — genuinely worth monitoring closely given the historical pattern, but not yet confirmed as a structural (vs. coincidental) weakness.

## Kill Path 4: China's Domestic CGM Entrants Emerge Faster Than DexCom's Own Risk Disclosure Implies, and DexCom Has No Visibility to Respond

**Mechanism**: DexCom's 10-K names "emerging competitors primarily located in China" (facts.md E61) but discloses zero China-specific revenue, market share, or competitive-intensity data (facts.md E23). If domestic Chinese CGM manufacturers achieve regulatory approval, cost-competitive manufacturing, and domestic-market-favoring procurement dynamics (the same VBP-style mechanism identified in the ABT/GEHC exemplars for other device categories) faster than DexCom's own limited International expansion can establish a defensible position, DexCom could find itself structurally excluded from what may become one of the world's largest diabetes-population markets — without DexCom's own disclosure ever having flagged the magnitude of this risk clearly enough for investors to have priced it.

**Evidence for**: The risk-factor language itself is a genuine company acknowledgment, not analyst speculation. China's diabetes population is enormous (China has one of the world's largest diabetic populations per general public-health data, not independently re-verified this run) and its healthcare-procurement system has already demonstrated VBP-style domestic-preference dynamics in other device categories (ABT/GEHC exemplars in this same batch).

**Evidence against / mitigant**: DexCom's International revenue mix is growing (28.5% FY2025 -> 30.2% Q1 2026), suggesting the company IS expanding internationally, even if China-specific detail is undisclosed. No evidence found this run of an ACTUAL competitive loss or share erosion in China specifically — this is a genuinely unresolved unknown, not a confirmed negative finding.

**Verdict**: The LEAST resolvable risk in this dossier given the total absence of company disclosure — genuinely a "known unknown," and the single most important research gap to close at the next refresh (see bottleneck_map.md Candidate #3). Given Nick's China med-device career lens, this deserves dedicated, targeted research (specific Chinese CGM competitor names, their regulatory/technical maturity) independent of DexCom's own filings, which by definition cannot resolve this gap further.

## Kill Path 5: Stelo (OTC) Cannibalizes Prescription CGM Revenue Rather Than Expanding the Total Addressable Market

**Mechanism**: If Stelo's lower price point and no-prescription-required convenience causes existing or would-be prescription-CGM patients (particularly non-insulin T2D patients who might otherwise have been prescribed G7/ONE+) to switch to or start with the cheaper OTC tier instead, DexCom could see average selling price (ASP) compression and channel-mix shift toward a lower-margin product, without genuinely expanding the total patient base as the Investor Day narrative implies.

**Evidence for**: DexCom's OWN FY2025 10-K explicitly excludes Stelo customers from its headline "net new customers" metric (facts.md E30) — this could be read as evidence the company is deliberately avoiding conflating Stelo growth with core-franchise growth, precisely BECAUSE some portion of Stelo adoption may not represent fully incremental TAM capture.

**Evidence against / mitigant**: Stelo targets a population (non-insulin T2D, prediabetic, no formal diagnosis in some cases) that is largely NOT currently prescribed CGM at all under current standard-of-care/reimbursement norms — the addressable population genuinely is different in kind, not just price, from the prescription-CGM population. The CONNECT trial and pediatric clearance (facts.md E68-E69) both point toward genuine population expansion (new clinical indications) rather than pure price-tier migration within an existing population.

**Verdict**: LOW-MODERATE risk, genuinely unresolved due to a complete lack of Stelo-specific unit economics or cannibalization-rate disclosure (facts.md O5) — this is a real, plausible risk that cannot currently be confirmed or ruled out with available evidence, and should be a specific question to probe at the next refresh (ideally via the Q1/Q2 2026 earnings call transcript, which was not read this run under the DECISION_DRAFT time constraint).

## Summary: Which Kill Path Is Most Likely to Actually Materialize?

Ranked by (a) evidence strength for the risk actually being live today and (b) potential magnitude if it does: **Kill Path 2 (GLP-1 substitution)** is the most structurally important because DexCom itself concedes it and it directly intersects with the company's stated growth strategy (Stelo/non-insulin expansion); **Kill Path 4 (China visibility gap)** is the least resolvable with current evidence and the most career-relevant to flag for follow-up; **Kill Path 3 (channel-control recurrence)** is the most immediately testable (next 1-2 quarters); **Kill Path 1 (Abbott global-scale squeeze)** is real but currently contradicted by DexCom's own accelerating recent results; **Kill Path 5 (Stelo cannibalization)** is the most speculative/least evidenced of the five.

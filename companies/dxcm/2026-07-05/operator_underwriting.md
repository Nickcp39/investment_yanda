## IMPORTANT CORRECTION: DexCom's CEO is Jacob S. Leach, NOT Kevin Sayer

This dossier's original brief assumed the incumbent CEO was Kevin Sayer. **This is stale as of the as_of date.** Verified directly from the FY2025 10-K's own signature block (filed 2026-02-12): **Jacob S. Leach signs as "President, Chief Executive Officer, and Director (Principal Executive Officer)"**; **Kevin R. Sayer signs separately as "Executive Chairman of the Board of Directors."** This is corroborated by independent press coverage (MedTech Dive, DrugDeliveryBusiness, FierceBiotech, company IR) [facts.md E14-E15]. The transition timeline: Leach assumed interim principal-executive-officer duties in **September 2025** during Sayer's medical leave; Sayer's resignation as CEO became effective **2026-01-01**; the board expected Sayer to resume an active (Executive) chairman role around March 2026. As of this as_of date (2026-07-05), Leach has been PERMANENT CEO for roughly six months — a genuinely new, still-being-proven tenure, not an established multi-year track record.

## Background — Jake Leach

Leach is a **21-year DexCom employee** (joined near the company's earliest commercialization era) — not an outside hire. Career path: Chief Technology Officer (2018-2022) -> Chief Operating Officer (late 2022) -> President (May 2025) -> interim/then permanent CEO (Sep 2025 / Jan 2026 formal). This is a genuinely deep, technical, product-side background: as CTO, Leach would have been directly involved in the engineering decisions behind the G6-to-G7 product transition and the underlying sensor technology that produces DexCom's measurable MARD accuracy advantage over Abbott's Libre (facts.md E65) — a strong, directly-relevant technical pedigree for a company whose core moat asset IS its sensor technology.

**What this background does NOT yet include**: no evidence found this run of prior public-company CEO experience elsewhere, and — critically — his *interim* CEO period (Sep 2025-Jan 2026) and the first ~2 quarters of his *permanent* tenure occurred during a period when the company was simultaneously (a) recovering from the 2024 crisis's aftermath, (b) navigating a fresh activist-investor relationship (Elliott Management, facts.md E17), and (c) preparing a major strategic reset (the May 2026 Investor Day). This is, in effect, a "trial by fire" start — Leach has not yet had a quiet, normal-conditions quarter to establish an independent baseline track record.

## Track Record So Far (interim + first ~2 quarters as permanent CEO)

**What has gone right, on his watch**:
- Q1 2026 results (the first full quarter reported under his permanent CEO title) showed genuinely strong operating repair: gross margin +610bp YoY, operating margin +850bp YoY, net income +89% YoY, revenue +15% reported/+12% organic [facts.md E32-E35] — this is a real, primary-source-verified acceleration, not just easier comps (FY2025 full year, largely under Sayer/Leach-interim, had already shown operating margin improvement too, facts.md E27)
- Successfully delivered the May 2026 Investor Day — a credible, detailed 2030 long-range plan (>10% organic growth, 67-69% gross margin target, G8 roadmap) that the market responded to constructively (sell-side price-target hikes clustered in the following weeks, facts.md E8) [facts.md E45-E49]
- Raised FY2026 guidance (operating margin 23-23.5% from 22-23%, EBITDA margin 31-31.5% from 30-31%) at the Q1 2026 report under his direct management [facts.md E45]
- Navigated the May 2026 stolen-sensor incident with what appears (per FDA/press disclosure) to be prompt, transparent public communication — no evidence found this run of a delayed or minimized disclosure [facts.md E55-E58]

**What is still unresolved / too early to grade**:
- No evidence yet of how Leach would independently handle a genuine crisis-of-his-own-making (the 2024 crisis predates his CEO tenure; the May 2026 stolen-sensor incident, while disclosed under his watch, originated in a manufacturing/scrap process, not a strategic decision he made)
- The Elliott Management activist relationship's full terms and DexCom management's negotiating posture are not independently confirmed this run (facts.md O4) — unclear whether Leach is operating with genuine board support or under close activist scrutiny/pressure
- Capital allocation under his direct leadership is limited to one data point so far: the $1B buyback announced at the May 2026 Investor Day [facts.md E48] — too early to assess capital-discipline pattern (M&A appetite, dividend policy, buyback pacing) over a full cycle

## Kevin Sayer's Legacy (relevant because he remains Executive Chairman, actively engaged)

Under Sayer's ~10-year CEO tenure, DexCom grew from ~$250M to >$4.6B in annual revenue [facts.md E15] — a genuinely exceptional multi-year value-creation record, encompassing the entire G4-through-G7 product-generation arc and the original Stelo OTC launch (August 2024, i.e., DURING the same year as the 2024 crisis — a reminder that even DexCom's best-performing era included a severe execution stumble). Sayer's continued presence as Executive Chairman (not a full departure) provides institutional-knowledge continuity through the leadership transition — a meaningfully lower-risk succession pattern than an abrupt, clean-break CEO change would represent, though it also means the prior leadership's blind spots (the 2024 sales-force reorg misstep happened on Sayer's watch, facts.md E50-E54) have not been fully "cleared out" by new leadership — Leach was COO during that exact crisis, meaning he shares some direct operational accountability for the 2024 failure, not distance from it.

## Capital Allocation

- **Balance sheet posture**: net cash (~$760M net of the only outstanding debt, $1.25B 0.375% converts due 2028) [facts.md E43] — genuinely conservative, undeployed-toward-M&A capital structure, in sharp contrast to ABT's debt-funded Exact Sciences bet in this same batch. This could reflect either capital discipline OR a lack of clear inorganic-growth conviction (DexCom has made no comparable large acquisition in recent years per sources reviewed this run) — ambiguous which interpretation is correct.
- **New $1B buyback** (May 2026) is the first major capital-return signal under Leach's tenure [facts.md E48] — a shareholder-friendly move, though it also means DexCom has chosen buybacks over either a first-ever dividend or a large strategic acquisition, keeping optionality narrow (single-category focus) rather than diversifying.
- **No dividend, ever** [facts.md E6] — consistent long-standing policy, not new under Leach, and the credit agreement historically restricts it; unclear if the new Investor Day economics (67-69% long-range gross margin target) change management's thinking on this over time.

## Grade: 3/5

**Rationale**: Jake Leach has a genuinely strong, directly-relevant technical background (21-year DexCom veteran, former CTO — meaning he personally has domain expertise in the exact sensor technology that produces DexCom's measurable accuracy edge over Abbott) and his first two quarters as permanent CEO have shown real, primary-source-verified operating improvement (margin expansion, raised guidance, a credible 2030 long-range plan). This is a meaningfully positive start. However, the grade is capped at 3/5 (below both the GEHC exemplar's Arduini at 3/5-equivalent-context and well below ABT's Ford at 4/5) because: (a) six months of permanent-CEO tenure is genuinely too short a track record to grade with confidence — this is a "promising start," not a "demonstrated" record; (b) Leach was COO during the 2024 crisis, meaning he carries some direct operational accountability for that failure rather than being a clean outside-hire distancing the company from it; (c) the fresh activist-investor relationship (Elliott) and the somewhat unclear dual-chairman governance structure introduce a live, unresolved governance-fluidity dimension not present in the ABT/GEHC comparables at their respective as_of dates; and (d) capital-allocation judgment (the aspect most CEOs are ultimately graded on) has essentially one data point so far (the buyback) — genuinely insufficient to assess a pattern.

**What would move this to 4/5**: A full year (2-3 more quarters) of sustained margin/growth execution consistent with Q1 2026's trajectory, without a repeat of either a distribution-channel failure (echoing 2024/2026's recurring weak point) or a walked-back guidance figure; clearer public resolution of the Foletta/Sayer dual-chairman governance structure; and either a demonstrated, disciplined capital-allocation pattern (buyback pacing, or a credible/sized M&A move) or an explicit, well-reasoned rationale for continuing to hold the net-cash position rather than deploying it.

**What would move this to 2/5 or lower**: Any indication that the May 2026 stolen-sensor incident escalates (reported adverse events, an FDA quality-systems action) suggesting the manufacturing/scrap-process failure reflects a broader quality-culture problem rather than an isolated incident; a third distribution/channel-control failure within 18-24 months (which would confirm this as a structural management weakness rather than two unrelated incidents); or evidence the Elliott relationship reflects genuine boardroom conflict rather than constructive activist collaboration.

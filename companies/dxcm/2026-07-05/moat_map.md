# DXCM Moat Map (M3)

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E19-E71

---

## Moat Summary: A SINGLE, GENUINE, TECHNICALLY-DIFFERENTIATED CGM CONSUMABLES ANNUITY — DEEP BUT NARROW, AND CONTESTED BY A REAL DUOPOLY RIVAL

Business quality grade: **GOOD, concentrated, not diversified** — DexCom's one moat asset (prescription CGM consumables) is genuinely strong on a technical/accuracy dimension and is showing real signs of operational repair after a severe 2024 stumble, but the company has no segment diversification to fall back on if this single franchise falters, and it faces a durable, well-resourced, larger-by-global-share rival (Abbott) in a near-even US market split. This caps DexCom's moat profile at "good, narrow, and genuinely contested" rather than "exceptional" — a meaningfully different risk shape than a diversified peer like ABT, even though DexCom's core technology may be the best in class on a pure accuracy basis.

---

## Moat 1: Prescription CGM Consumables Annuity (G6/G7/G7 15 Day) — DEEP, TECHNICALLY DIFFERENTIATED, BUT IS THE ENTIRE COMPANY

**Evidence**:
- ~33.89% global CGM revenue share (2025) vs Abbott's ~52.83%, Medtronic's ~10.10% [facts.md E64]
- US-specific 2025 CGM share: DexCom ~44.7% vs Abbott ~48.5% — a genuinely close, near-even domestic race [facts.md E64]
- Sensor replacement cadence (10 days G6/G7, 15.5 days G7 15 Day) creates the same genuine recurring-revenue mechanism as Abbott's Libre — tied to a chronic condition, independent of capital-equipment cycles
- Added ~600,000-700,000 net customers worldwide in FY2025 (ex-Stelo) [facts.md E30]; Q1 2026 revenue growth accelerated to +15% reported/+12% organic [facts.md E32]

**The technical edge (the crux of the DXCM-vs-ABT comparison)**: Independent MARD (Mean Absolute Relative Difference, lower = more accurate) comparison shows **DexCom G7 at 8.2% vs Abbott's Libre 3 at 9.2%** [facts.md E65, cross-referenced from ABT dossier E53] — a real, measurable accuracy advantage on this specific metric. Industry commentary frames this as DexCom pursuing a "performance-first" strategy vs. Abbott's "accessibility/affordability" positioning [facts.md E66] — both are legitimate, coexisting competitive strategies, not one side "winning" outright.

**Durability**: Strong on the technical dimension, genuinely contested on market share. Unlike Abbott, whose CGM franchise sits alongside three other segments (Diagnostics, Nutrition, Established Pharma), **100% of DexCom's revenue depends on this single moat holding** — there is no other business line to cushion a CGM-specific setback. The 2024 crisis (facts.md E50-E54) demonstrated exactly this: a purely execution-level failure (sales-force reorganization) in ONE part of the value chain (distribution) produced a 40% single-day stock decline, because there was no other segment to reassure the market that the core franchise itself was intact.

**Comparison to ABT's Libre moat (direct, both-sided verification)**: Abbott's Libre moat is described in the ABT exemplar as "narrower (only Abbott's own installed base) but arguably deeper (real brand/prescription lock-in) within that narrower base" relative to GEHC's OEM-agnostic model — the equivalent framing for DexCom is that its moat is **narrower still in a different sense**: DexCom has no adjacent, non-CGM installed base to leverage or fall back on at all (Abbott's Diagnostics/Nutrition/Pharma businesses do not compete with DexCom, but they do provide Abbott diversification that DexCom structurally cannot replicate without acquiring outside its category — which, notably, DexCom has NOT done; its net-cash balance sheet, facts.md E43, sits entirely undeployed toward diversification, unlike ABT's Exact Sciences move).

## Moat 2: Stelo (OTC Biosensor) — GENUINE STRATEGIC OPTIONALITY, ZERO QUANTIFIED MOAT YET

**Evidence**:
- First FDA-cleared OTC glucose biosensor in the US (launched August 2024); pediatric clearance added June 2026 [facts.md E20, E69]
- Targets a structurally different, much larger population (125M Americans with prediabetes/T2D per one analyst estimate) that prescription CGM cannot reach by definition (no prescription required)
- CONNECT trial (facts.md E68) provides genuine clinical validation for the adjacent non-insulin-T2D population
- Third-party AI ecosystem forming around it: Signos (GLP-1/weight-management AI app) built on DexCom sensor data [facts.md E70]

**Durability**: Genuinely unproven as a moat. DexCom's OWN FY2025 10-K explicitly excludes Stelo from its headline "net new customers" metric (facts.md E30) — a real tell that the company itself does not yet consider Stelo material enough to fold into primary growth disclosure. This is NOT evidence of failure, but it IS evidence that this is a call option, not a demonstrated financial moat, consistent with the AI/new-product-monetization-gap pattern seen across this entire batch (GEHC O7, ABT O7).

## Moat 3: Manufacturing / Technical R&D Capability (G8 roadmap) — REAL BUT RECENTLY DEMONSTRATED AS FRAGILE

**Evidence**:
- Consistent product-cycle cadence: G6 -> G7 (2023) -> G7 15 Day (late 2025) -> G8 (targeted ~18 months from May 2026, i.e. roughly late 2027/early 2028) [facts.md E20, E46]
- G8 described as 50% smaller, more accurate, lower manufacturing cost, new silicon chip design measuring "signals beyond glucose" [facts.md E46]
- 2030 gross-margin target of 67-69% (vs. 60.1% FY2025) implies management's own confidence that manufacturing efficiency is a genuine, addressable lever, not a permanent cost structure [facts.md E46]

**The counter-evidence (a genuine, recent crack in this moat, not resolved)**: DexCom's OWN 10-K attributes the FY2025 full-year gross-margin decline (60.5% -> 60.1%) explicitly to "inefficiencies associated with ensuring supply availability, build configurations that lowered production yield, and total replacement costs" [facts.md E25] — company-acknowledged manufacturing execution weakness in the same year it is claiming a long-range path to margin expansion. Compounding this, the **May 2026 stolen-sensor incident** (facts.md E55-E59) originated in DexCom's own scrap-destruction process — sensors meant for destruction were stolen and resold before reaching that process's completion. Two distinct, recent, primary-source-confirmed manufacturing/quality events in roughly a 12-month window is a genuine pattern worth weighting, not a coincidence to dismiss.

## Moat Weaknesses / Where DexCom is Genuinely Vulnerable

1. **Zero segment diversification**: unlike every other name reviewed in this S&P 500 medical batch so far (ABT's four segments, GEHC's imaging+services), DexCom is a single-category pure-play. This concentrates both the upside (100% leverage to a franchise DexCom is winning on accuracy) and the downside (no cushion if CGM-specific competitive or execution risk materializes) to a degree not seen elsewhere in the batch.
2. **The 2024 crisis was a genuine, severe, execution-level failure, and its root cause (sales-force/distribution-channel management) recurred in a different form in 2026** (the Pharmsource stolen-sensor incident also traces to channel/distribution control, not core R&D) — this is a real, repeated operational-competence question, not a one-off.
3. **GLP-1 is a company-acknowledged double-edged risk, stated in DexCom's OWN risk factors, not just bear-case speculation** (facts.md E62) — this is a more direct, explicit self-disclosed risk than typically seen in med-tech 10-Ks, and should be weighted as genuine management concern, not merely analyst caution.
4. **China competitive exposure is the LEAST quantifiable in this entire batch**: DexCom's 10-K names "emerging competitors primarily located in China" as a risk (facts.md E61) but discloses zero China-specific revenue or market data (facts.md E23) — worse visibility than either ABT or GEHC had into their own China dynamics.
5. **Recent CEO transition + fresh activist-investor relationship (Elliott Management)** adds a governance-fluidity dimension not present in the ABT/GEHC exemplars at their as_of dates (facts.md E14-E17) — not necessarily negative (activist involvement often precedes operational discipline improvements, consistent with the Q1 2026 margin acceleration), but genuinely unresolved/unproven as of this as_of date.

**Overall moat verdict**: DexCom has a genuinely deep, technically-real moat asset in prescription CGM (the best independent accuracy metric found in this comparison, MARD 8.2% vs Abbott's 9.2%), but it is a single-category company with no diversification cushion, a recent history of severe execution failures concentrated specifically in the distribution/channel layer (2024 crisis, 2026 stolen-sensor incident), and the least-quantified China exposure of any name in this batch. This is a narrower but arguably more technically-differentiated moat than ABT's Libre franchise — the CGM duopoly is genuinely a duopoly, not a one-sided Abbott-dominant market, and DexCom's own primary-source risk disclosures (GLP-1 substitution risk, China competitor emergence) are unusually direct and self-aware relative to typical med-tech 10-K boilerplate. This justifies a DECISION_DRAFT (not CLEAN) audit ceiling, consistent with the batch's ~65% target — the moat is real but the concentration risk and recent operational-incident pattern are equally real and unresolved.

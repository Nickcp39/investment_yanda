Product line: **Dexcom G7 and G7 15 Day** (integrated CGM), **Dexcom G6** (prior-gen CGM), **Dexcom ONE+** (value-tier CGM replacing fingerstick testing), **Stelo** (OTC biosensor for adults with prediabetes/non-insulin T2D) [SEC-10K-FY25 / DXCM-SA-STATS]

## INTERPRETATION (derived/judgment)

- I1. **This is a genuine, credible operational-turnaround story, not a speculative one**: the 2024 crisis (E50-E54: -40% single-day crash, sales-force reorg damage, guidance cut) was real and severe, but the subsequent data is not merely a stock-price recovery narrative — Q1 2026's operating metrics (gross margin +610bp YoY, operating margin +850bp YoY, net income +89% YoY) show the underlying operating engine actually repairing itself, not just easier comps. This is a materially different, more favorable pattern than either the GEHC or ABT exemplars in this batch, which show ongoing (not resolved) margin/growth headwinds.
- I2. **The CEO transition is real, disclosed, and appears orderly, but is recent enough to be a live monitoring item**: Jake Leach becoming permanent CEO (effective 2026-01-01, formalized ~Feb 2026) followed Kevin Sayer's medical leave (from Sep 2025) and eventual resignation from the CEO role — this is NOT a forced-out-for-cause departure narrative, and Sayer remains engaged as Executive Chairman, suggesting continuity of institutional knowledge. However, Leach has been permanent CEO for only ~5 months as of this as_of date, and the company is simultaneously navigating a fresh activist-investor relationship (Elliott Management, new directors, new Board committee) — a genuinely fluid governance picture that should be monitored, not assumed stable.
- I3. **Balance sheet is a clear, structural positive versus this batch's larger-cap peers**: DexCom carries only $1.25B of long-dated (2028), low-coupon (0.375%) convertible debt against ~$2.0B of cash+short-term securities — a net cash position. This is the opposite balance-sheet posture from ABT (net debt ~6x'd via Exact Sciences) or GEHC (debt-funded Intelerad deal) in this same batch. DexCom has genuine capital-allocation optionality (hence the new $1B buyback) without needing to delever first.
- I4. **The CGM "duopoly" with Abbott is real, roughly balanced in the US, and Abbott-favored globally** — this directly parallels and completes the ABT exemplar's own framing (I6 there): Abbott leads on global share (driven by non-US markets) and price/accessibility positioning, while DexCom holds a near-even US share split (44.7% vs Abbott's 48.5%) and a measurable technical accuracy edge (MARD 8.2% vs 9.2%). Neither company has an uncontested position; this is genuinely a duopoly with differentiated competitive strategies (DexCom = performance-first/premium, Abbott = accessibility/affordability + broader global distribution), not a one-sided moat.
- I5. **GLP-1 is explicitly a double-edged sword in DexCom's OWN risk disclosure, not just an external analyst debate**: E62's direct 10-K language ("could potentially compete with our CGM systems and reduce sales") is a genuine, company-acknowledged structural risk, not merely bear-case speculation — this is a materially more direct/explicit company admission than typically seen in med-tech risk factors, and should be weighted accordingly. The CONNECT trial (E68) and Signos partnership (E70) are the company's counter-narrative (CGM as a *companion* to GLP-1/weight-management, not a substitute), but the risk-factor language itself concedes the substitution risk is real, not hypothetical.
- I6. **China is a smaller, less-quantifiable risk for DexCom than for ABT/GEHC in this batch, but for a different reason: DexCom simply does not disclose China exposure at all.** The 10-K explicitly flags "emerging competitors primarily located in China" (E61) as a competitive risk, but with International revenue only 28.5% of FY2025 total (E31) and no single country over 10% of revenue (E23), DexCom's own disclosure suggests China is not currently a large direct revenue dependency — but this also means the risk is genuinely unquantified (an evidence gap, not a confirmed "small exposure" finding) and the domestic Chinese CGM competitive landscape (referenced in ABT/GEHC-adjacent industry commentary) was not independently verified this run for DexCom specifically.
- I7. **Stelo (OTC) is a genuine strategic call option, not yet a quantified financial driver**: pediatric clearance (E69), the underlying August 2024 launch, and the explicit BTIG-style framing of a 125M-person prediabetes/T2D TAM are all real strategic developments, but no source found this run discloses Stelo-specific revenue, unit volumes, or attach-rate figures — FY2025's "~600,000-700,000 net customers, EXCLUDING Stelo" (E30) explicitly separates Stelo out, suggesting the company itself does not yet consider Stelo customer growth as decision-relevant enough to fold into its primary growth metric. This is the DexCom-specific parallel to the GEHC/ABT AI-monetization gap identified elsewhere in this batch: a real, plausible strategic vector, not yet a demonstrated financial one.
- I8. **The stolen-sensor incident (E55-E59) is a genuine, quantifiable-severity-so-far-low quality/supply-chain event, disclosed transparently and recently (very close to but just after typical LIVE-freshness windows)**: no severe adverse events reported, narrow lot-specific scope, and traced to an unauthorized third-party distributor (not a DexCom manufacturing-floor failure reaching legitimate channels) — this reads as a genuine but so-far-contained event, not a Boeing-style systemic quality collapse. However, it is recent (8-K filed 2026-05-28, ~5 weeks before as_of) and warrants explicit monitoring at the next refresh for: (a) any escalation in reported adverse events, (b) whether it triggers a broader FDA quality-systems inquiry, (c) reputational/prescriber-confidence impact in Q2 2026 results (2026-07-29).

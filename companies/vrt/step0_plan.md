## Thesis hypothesis (analyst prior — knowledge to 2026-01, MUST verify in Block 1)

- Critical digital infrastructure: **power** (UPS, busbar, switchgear) + **thermal**
  (air + **liquid cooling**) for datacenters. Leader alongside Schneider Electric / ABB.
- Bull case = AI rack power density (GB200-class) forces **liquid cooling** adoption →
  Vertiv's $-content per rack steps up; book-to-bill / backlog reportedly strong;
  raised medium-term (2029) targets at 2024 investor event.
- Bear case = it is the **most capex-sensitive, highest-beta** link in this batch —
  pure downstream of hyperscaler spend, no PTC floor, no multi-year contracted PPA.
  Cooling could commoditize; direct-to-chip / in-house designs (Supermicro, ODMs)
  and Schneider compete. A toll booth only as long as orders compound.

## Why now

- The "配套高增" downstream node — completes the batch's toll-booth↔cycle spectrum:
  CEG (contracted cash flow) → GEV (equipment oligopoly) → VRT (highest beta).
- Best single read on whether the capex wave is still accelerating (orders/book-to-bill
  is a near-real-time demand gauge).

## Non-Goals

- Not a momentum/earnings-beat trade.
- Do not extrapolate one strong orders quarter into a permanent moat.
- No memo before evidence layer is built.

## Kill criteria (pre-registered)

- Book-to-bill / organic orders roll below 1.0 for >1 quarter.
- Liquid-cooling commoditizes / share shifts to ODMs / direct-to-chip bypasses VRT.
- Margin-expansion targets missed → it was a low-quality cyclical, not a toll booth.
- **Shared switch:** hyperscaler capex guidance slope turns down — VRT is the most

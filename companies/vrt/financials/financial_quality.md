# Financial Quality — VRT

Last updated: 2026-06-19

Source base: VRT Q1 2026 release/8-K (S1), Q1'26 call (S2), Q4/FY2025 release (S3), 2026 Investor Conference (S4). Facts: `../facts.md` / `../claim_ledger.csv`.

## One-line read

**Top-tier and accelerating.** Revenue +26% organic FY2025 with a ~20% adjusted operating margin and ~$1.9B adjusted FCF; Q1'26 *beat its own margin guide by ~180bps*; FY26 guide raised; 2030 ceiling raised. The accounting is clean and the economics are genuinely high-quality — **the single blemish is not in the numbers, it is the disclosure: the headline quarterly orders / book-to-bill / backlog series was withdrawn in Q1'26 after a +252% Q4** (the binding gap, C012/C027).

## Quality Questions

- **Is revenue recurring?** **Partly.** Services (lifecycle, monitoring) is the higher-margin recurring layer; power + thermal hardware is order-driven (backlog-converted), not annuity. **No PTC-style floor, no multi-year take-or-pay** — backlog converts ~12–18 months and is cancellable. (C016, C023, C024)
- **Does growth require rising capital intensity?** No — this is the *opposite* of NBIS. VRT is FCF-positive and margins are *expanding* as it grows. Growth is order-driven, not capex-treadmill.
- **Are margins expanding from scale, accounting, pricing, or utilization?** A genuine mix of **operating leverage + favorable mix toward integrated systems + pricing** — Q1'26 beat its *own* guide by ~180bps (C009/C032). Not an accounting tailwind. The margin path is *ahead* of the 2024 investor-day schedule.
- **Is SBC / dilution a problem?** Not flagged as material in the pack; capital allocation is net-cash with modest buybacks (share count not a headwind).
- **Buybacks / dividend?** **Modest buybacks; negligible dividend (~0.07%)** — capital reinvested into growth + bolt-on thermal M&A (Thermal Key, dry coolers). (C028)
- **Is reported FCF overstating owner earnings?** No evidence of that — FCF conversion is honest and improving ($1.89B FY25 → $2.2B FY26E mid; Q1'26 +147%).

## Red / Yellow Flags

1. **🚩 (BINDING GAP) Headline quarterly orders / book-to-bill / backlog withdrawn in Q1'26** after a blow-out Q4 (organic orders +252%, book-to-bill ~2.9x). Management gave words ("orders up YoY," backlog "elongated") but no numbers. This is a **disclosure-quality** flag, not an accounting one — but it removes the cleanest demand gauge on the highest-beta, no-floor name in the basket. Surfaced consistently in `../inversion_map.md`, `../monitor.md`, `../research_status.md`. (C012, C027)
2. **🟡 EMEA organic −29% in Q1'26** — demand is *not* uniformly immune; strength is concentrated in the Americas (+44%). A regional air-pocket exists even mid-super-cycle. (C008)
3. **🟡 No contracted floor** — backlog ($15.0B) is cancellable and converts in ~12–18 months; in a capex-slope downturn there is no take-or-pay annuity to catch the fall. (C024)

## So what

VRT is close to a Terry-Smith "clean compounder" on the numbers — high growth, expanding margins, strong cash conversion, net cash, no accounting gimmicks. **The reason it is not a clean CORE buy is not financial quality; it is (i) the price (~48x forward — see `../valuation.md`) and (ii) the one disclosure blemish (withheld orders/B2B) that degrades the signal needed to underwrite the downside on a no-floor, highest-beta name.** The economics are high-quality; the *transparency* degraded exactly where it matters most.

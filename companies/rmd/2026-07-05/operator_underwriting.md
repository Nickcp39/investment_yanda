## Background

Michael J. "Mick" Farrell has been Chairman and CEO of ResMed since **March 2013** — over 13 years of tenure as CEO, making him a genuinely long-tenured operator (not a recent appointee, unlike GEHC's Arduini at ~3.5 years) [E8]. He became Chairman of the Board in January 2023 (in addition to CEO). In December 2025, he was named **Chair of AdvaMed** (the U.S. medical device industry's principal trade association) for a two-year term starting January 2026 — an industry-recognition signal of standing among med-tech peers, not merely a self-reported credential [E8, ADVAMED-ANNOUNCEMENT].

## Track Record at RMD (2013-present, focus on evidence gathered this run: FY2023-Q3FY2026)

**What has gone right**:
- Consistent, accelerating revenue growth: FY2023 $4,223M → FY2024 $4,685M → FY2025 $5,146M (E12, E15, E17) — every year growing, no down-year in the window evidenced
- Margin EXPANSION every single period evidenced: gross margin 55.78% (FY23) → 56.67% (FY24) → 59.36% (FY25) → 62.2% (Q3 FY26) [E13] — this is the opposite pattern from GEHC's 3-period compression, and a genuinely strong execution signal (successfully navigating the post-Philips-recall competitive landscape while ALSO expanding margin, not just capturing share at the expense of profitability)
- Net income growth outpacing revenue growth: FY2023 $897.56M → FY2025 $1,400.72M (+56% over 2 years) vs revenue +22% over the same period [E15, E17] — genuine operating leverage
- Active, disciplined capital allocation: deleveraged the balance sheet (debt $1,580M FY23 → $851.81M FY25) WHILE building cash ($227.89M → $1,209M) AND returning capital via buybacks ($500M in 9M FY26) and dividends, AND still funding a bolt-on acquisition (Noctrix, $340M) — a genuinely well-sequenced, multi-lever capital allocation record, not a single-lever story [E18-E21, E32, E34]
- Proactive AI investment with regulatory follow-through: Smart Comfort achieved actual FDA clearance (Dec 2025), not merely an announced initiative [E35] — a meaningfully more "delivered" outcome than GEHC's mix of cleared/pending AI products
- Publicly, directly addressed the GLP-1 bear case with company-generated real-world data (1.7M and 2.1M patient cohort studies) rather than avoiding the topic — a transparency signal, whether or not the data fully settles the debate [E40-E43]

**What has gone wrong / under pressure**:
- Q3 FY2026 revenue MISSED consensus by ~1.4% [E25] — a real, if modest, miss, and one of the contributing factors (alongside CFO transition and Noctrix M&A announcement) cited for the stock's continued slide to a 52-week-low trough in late May 2026 [O8, QUIVERQUANT-52WKLOW]
- CFO transition (Sandercock retiring after 26 years, Bloomer from Exact Sciences stepping in, effective May 2026) [E9, E39] — while framed as normal succession, it removes a long-tenured finance leader at the same time other changes (Noctrix M&A) were being absorbed by the market; no evidence this run suggests the transition itself was forced or troubled, but it is a genuine leadership-continuity item to monitor
- The GLP-1 studies presented (E40-E43) answer a narrower question (does GLP-1 hurt RETENTION of existing combo-therapy patients) than the market's primary fear (does GLP-1 shrink the NEW-patient diagnosis funnel) — whether this reflects a deliberate narrow framing or simply the data that happens to be available is not verifiable this run, but it is worth noting management chose to lead with the more favorable-framing dataset

## Capital Allocation

- **M&A discipline**: Noctrix Health ($340M, ~$24M revenue run-rate, RLS adjacency) is a genuine bolt-on — small relative to RMD's ~$30B market cap (~1.1%), in an adjacent-but-distinct clinical area (restless leg syndrome) with stated higher growth/margin than the core business [E34]. This is a much smaller, lower-risk-profile acquisition than GEHC's Intelerad ($2.3B, ~7.7% of GEHC's market cap) — appropriately scaled bolt-on M&A rather than a bet-the-company move.
- **Debt**: actively reduced from $1,580M (FY23) to $851.81M (FY25) — a genuine deleveraging track record, not just a stable-debt story [E18].
- **Shareholder returns**: $500M in buybacks over 9M FY2026 plus a raised quarterly dividend ($0.60/share, ~1.14% yield) — returning meaningful capital while simultaneously building cash and funding M&A, evidence of a management team not capital-constrained or forced to choose between competing uses of cash [E32, E51].
- **Dividend discipline**: unlike GEHC (where the dividend figure found was internally inconsistent/suspect), RMD's dividend figure is internally consistent and cross-checked (quarterly $0.60 × 4 = $2.40 annualized = 1.14% yield at $209.63, matching both the capital-allocation disclosure and the screener yield figure) [E32, E49, E51].

## Grade: 4/5

**Rationale**: Farrell has a genuinely long (13-year), evidenced track record at RMD specifically — not just a credible prior-CEO résumé applied to a new company (unlike GEHC's Arduini, whose GEHC-specific tenure is only ~3.5 years). The evidence gathered this run shows CONSISTENT execution across growth, margin expansion, deleveraging, and shareholder returns SIMULTANEOUSLY — a harder combination to achieve than any single lever alone, and one GEHC's Arduini has not (yet, per evidence reviewed) matched at GEHC. The AI/digital-health strategy has delivered an actual FDA clearance, not just an announced roadmap. The GLP-1 response (transparent, data-driven, if narrowly scoped) is a reasonable, evidence-based management posture rather than denial or silence.

Docked from 5/5 because: (a) the Q3 FY2026 revenue miss + simultaneous CFO transition + M&A announcement in a single earnings release created real, not-yet-fully-resolved market uncertainty that management's communication did not fully preempt (the stock continued falling for ~a month afterward to a fresh 52-week low), (b) full compensation/proxy-incentive-alignment detail was not independently verified this run, and (c) the central strategic risk (GLP-1 funnel impact) genuinely cannot be graded as "handled" yet — it is being actively managed and transparently discussed, but the multi-year outcome is not yet knowable, and a 5/5 grade would imply a level of proven crisis-navigation this specific risk has not yet required.

**What would move this to 5/5**: A subsequent 1-2 quarters showing (a) revenue reacceleration back above consensus, (b) a smooth CFO transition with no disruption to reporting quality/guidance credibility, (c) confirmed comp-structure alignment (majority equity-linked), and (d) either resolution or continued absence of any GLP-1-driven new-patient-funnel deterioration in the device-revenue growth rate specifically.

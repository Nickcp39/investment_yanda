# RMD Business Model (M2 — Theme / Mechanism Thesis)

Last updated: 2026-07-05 | as_of: 2026-07-05 | sources: facts.md E5-E38

---

## One-Line Description

ResMed sells connected CPAP/respiratory devices and consumable masks/accessories for obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) and related respiratory conditions, monetized through a hardware-sale-plus-consumables-resupply-annuity model, layered with a cloud digital-health platform (AirView) and AI-enabled software (Smart Comfort) that improves patient adherence — plus a smaller, distinct B2B SaaS segment (Brightree/MatrixCare) selling practice-management software to home-health/hospice/DME agencies.

---

## Segment 1: Sleep and Breathing Health (~88% of FY2025 revenue mix by combined device+mask %)

**What it is**: APAP/CPAP/BiLevel devices (flagship: AirSense 11 platform) plus masks, diagnostic products (including the NightOwl home sleep apnea test, launched U.S.-wide April 2025), and accessories/consumables [E6, E7].

**Revenue mechanics**:
- **Devices** (~52% of FY2025 net revenue, E7): one-time-ish capital-equipment-style sale (though replacement cycle exists — devices typically replaced every ~5 years as batteries/motors wear and technology improves), sold to DME (durable medical equipment) providers, hospitals, and increasingly direct-to-consumer/telehealth channels.
- **Masks, diagnostic products, and accessories** (~36% of FY2025 net revenue, E7): TRUE recurring consumables — masks need replacement every ~3-6 months per typical insurance/resupply cycles, cushions/tubing/filters even more frequently. This is the structural "razor-blade" annuity of the business, and it is the FASTEST-GROWING line within Sleep and Breathing Health in the most recent quarter (Q3 FY26 masks/accessories +15% vs devices +9%, E26) — a genuinely positive mix signal (higher-margin consumables outgrowing hardware).

**Growth drivers evidenced this run**:
1. Category growth — the global OSA-diagnosed population is expanding (partly via easier at-home diagnostics like NightOwl, and per the GLP-1 evidence below, partly via new diagnosis pathways opened by weight-loss-drug patients engaging with the healthcare system).
2. International strength — Europe/Asia/Other Markets grew +16% reported / +7% constant currency in Q3 FY26 (E27), outpacing the +9% domestic US/Canada/LatAm print — suggests continued international penetration, not just a mature-market replacement cycle.
3. Historical Philips-recall share gain (see moat_map.md) — now a maturing, largely-normalized-into-the-base tailwind rather than a fresh driver (facts.md I6).

## Segment 2: Residential Care Software (~3-4% of Q3 FY26 revenue, E25)

**What it is**: Brightree and MatrixCare — B2B SaaS platforms for home-health, hospice, home-medical-equipment (HME), and long-term/post-acute-care agencies to manage billing, scheduling, clinical documentation, and compliance [E6].

**Strategic role**: This is a genuinely distinct business (software subscription revenue, not device/consumables) that diversifies RMD's revenue mix and gives it a foothold in the broader home-care software ecosystem — adjacent to, but not dependent on, the core CPAP hardware cycle. Growing steadily (+6% YoY, E26) but slower than the core Sleep and Breathing Health segment — a stable, lower-growth software annuity rather than a high-growth story in its own right this run.

## The AI / Digital-Health Layer (career-relevant, cuts across both segments)

This is NOT a separate reporting segment but is the most strategically important cross-cutting asset for Nick's career-relevant thesis:

1. **AirView** — cloud-based remote patient monitoring platform. Scale: **34M+ patients**, **31.5M+ 100%-cloud-connectable devices**, **24B+ cumulative nights of respiratory data** [E36]. Generative AI added in 2025 for large-scale pattern analysis / at-risk-patient flagging [E37].
2. **Smart Comfort** — FDA-cleared (2025-12-08) as the first FDA-cleared AI-enabled medical device that personalizes CPAP comfort settings, trained on 100M+ nights of de-identified sleep data [E35]. This directly targets the #1 driver of CPAP non-adherence (discomfort in the first weeks of therapy) with a genuine ML model, not just a rules-based algorithm.
3. **myAir** — patient-facing app (consumer companion to AirSense 11 devices) that delivers Smart Comfort recommendations and engagement/coaching content.

**Mechanism**: More connected devices → more data → better ML models (Smart Comfort, AirView analytics) → higher patient adherence (>87% for connected-device patients per E38) → higher resupply/consumables revenue per patient AND better clinical outcomes data to support payer/provider relationships → reinforces the device/mask installed-base moat. This is a genuine data-network-effect flywheel, though (as with GEHC's AI narrative) NOT YET quantified in dollar/ASP terms this run (O5).

## The GLP-1 Question (central career-relevant debate)

**Bear mechanism** (market's fear, not yet resolved by company data): GLP-1 weight-loss drugs (Ozempic/Wegovy/Zepbound-class) could (a) reduce obesity-driven OSA severity enough that some patients no longer need CPAP, (b) let patients discontinue therapy early after weight loss, or (c) capture patients into a drug-only treatment pathway that never initiates CPAP diagnosis/therapy in the first place — each of which would shrink RMD's addressable funnel.

**Bull mechanism** (company's counter-thesis, WITH real evidence this run): GLP-1 drugs are bringing a large new population of patients into contact with the healthcare system (weight-loss-drug visits), and OSA screening/diagnosis is increasingly bundled into that engagement — expanding the diagnosed population rather than shrinking it. Company data: patients on BOTH GLP-1 and PAP show HIGHER resupply/adherence rates than PAP-only patients (E40, E41), and 2.1M+ patients are already on both therapies concurrently (E42) — CEO Farrell frames this explicitly as "a once-in-a-generation demand gen opportunity" [E39].

**What the evidence actually shows vs. does not show** (see facts.md I3 for full reasoning): the company's studies measure RETENTION/ADHERENCE of patients already on combination therapy — they show GLP-1 does not cause existing combo-patients to drop off, and may even help engagement. They do NOT directly measure or refute whether the total NEW-PATIENT diagnosis/initiation funnel is being reduced by patients who lose enough weight on GLP-1 alone to avoid an OSA diagnosis altogether, or whether average therapy DURATION per patient is shortening as more patients achieve meaningful weight loss. This is the single most important open empirical question for the thesis and is not resolved by data available this run.

## Business Model Verdict (M2 signal)

**Coherent, evidenced, genuinely differentiated mechanism.** Unlike GEHC (real product launches, zero AI monetization evidence, weakest segment unexplained), RMD shows: (a) margin EXPANSION not compression across every period evidenced, (b) a consumables mix shift toward the HIGHER-margin, higher-growth mask/accessories line, (c) a genuinely regulatory-cleared (not just launched) AI product with a defensible data-scale moat, and (d) real (if narrowly-scoped) company data addressing the single biggest bear-case risk. The mechanism is sound and evidenced; the central unresolved question (does GLP-1 shrink the NEW-patient funnel) is a genuine, unresolved risk rather than a fully answered one — appropriately flagged, not papered over.

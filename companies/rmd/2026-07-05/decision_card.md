## Career Note: Digital-Health AI + GLP-1 (Sleep/Respiratory Lens)

RMD's AI/digital-health platform is a genuinely stronger, more concrete moat claim than the GEHC comparison point: **Smart Comfort achieved actual FDA clearance on 2025-12-08** (the first FDA-cleared AI-enabled medical device of its kind), built on a training set of **100+ million nights** of de-identified sleep data — a data asset that took RMD's multi-decade connected-device deployment to accumulate and that a new entrant (Philips rebuilding trust post-recall, or a China-based fast-follower like BMC Medical/Jiangsu Yuyue) cannot quickly replicate. AirView, the underlying cloud platform, now spans **34M+ patients, 31.5M+ connected devices, and 24B+ cumulative nights of data** [facts.md E35-E36]. This is a materially more evidenced, regulatory-validated AI moat than GEHC's mix of cleared/pending imaging AI products.

The **GLP-1 debate** is the central career-relevant tension for this name and genuinely differs in kind from GEHC's China-competition thesis: it is a demand-side, clinical-mechanism question rather than a supply-side, competitive-substitution question. ResMed's own real-world data (1.7M and 2.1M de-identified patient cohorts, presented on the Q3 FY2026 earnings call) shows patients on BOTH GLP-1 and PAP therapy have HIGHER resupply/adherence rates (+5.1% at 2yr, +6.2% at 3yr) and are MORE likely (not less) to initiate PAP therapy than PAP-only patients — directly supporting CEO Farrell's framing of GLP-1 as "a once-in-a-generation demand gen opportunity" [facts.md E39-E43]. However, this evidence addresses RETENTION of patients already engaged with both therapies — it does NOT directly measure whether the total NEW-patient diagnosis funnel is being narrowed by patients who achieve enough weight loss on GLP-1 alone to avoid an OSA diagnosis altogether. This is the single most important open empirical question for the thesis, unresolved by any evidence found this run, and it deserves its own dedicated tracking framework distinct from the China-competition lens that dominates the parallel GEHC/med-device-imaging thesis — it is a novel risk category specific to chronic-disease-management device businesses intersecting with a rapidly-adopted pharmaceutical class, worth monitoring closely as a template for how AI-enabled device companies respond to drug-class disruption more broadly.

## LEAD answer — durable toll booth, or cyclical surge?

**GEV genuinely owns a binding electrification bottleneck and is monetizing it like an owner — but "durable rent" is proven only for the services annuity; the equipment pricing premium is a cyclical peak the market is capitalizing as permanent.**

FOR: 3-player heavy-duty gas-turbine oligopoly (~two-thirds share), slots **sold out through 2029**, **+10–20% pricing** on 2026 orders, customers **pre-paying deposits** ($5.6B contract-liability inflow) to hold slots — textbook bottleneck-owner behavior. The **$87B services RPO** (54% of backlog) is the sticky, annuity-like rent on ~7,000 installed turbines. AGAINST: the order spike is demand-pull from one vertical; a 2-yr+ lead time means today's bookings depend on demand staying hot through 2028–30; **Wind losses are widening** (−$382M Q1 vs −$146M), best case "small profit on a shrinking business."

## Valuation (⚠️ screener multiples distorted by one-offs)

Headline trailing P/E 32 / EV-EBITDA 85x are inflated by the $2.9B tax release + $4.3B Prolec/Proficy gains. **Clean lens on 2026 guide** (adj EBITDA ~$5.7–6.4B): **EV/fwd-EBITDA ~46–51x**; EV/FCF ~39–45x (FCF flattered by advance payments). **Premium to Siemens Energy ~39x.** Priced for the toll-booth being permanent AND 2028 22%/22% margins landing.

| Scenario | Path | Value |
|---|---|---|
| Bear | 2027 capex digests, GT premium fades, Wind drags, ~25x normalized | ~$600–700 (−40%) |
| Base | Targets ~met (2028 rev $52B, ~20% EBITDA), ~30x | ~flat over 2–3 yr |

At −6% off ATH the asymmetry is unattractive: thin margin of safety, full downside if capex digests.

## IC Panel (5 souls)

- **Buffett — business worth owning?** Yes — oligopoly + services annuity + demonstrated pricing power is among the best moats in industrials. But "wonderful business at a fair price" — the *price* fails here.
- **Greenblatt / Graham — price reasonable?** No. ~46–51x clean fwd-EBITDA, premium to Siemens, no margin of safety. A great company is not a great investment at any price.
- **Marks — what's priced, paid for risk?** Market believes the toll-booth is permanent and 2028 targets hit. That's fully in the price; you are *not* paid for the cyclical/Wind/2027-digestion risk. Classic "everyone knows it" → priced.
- **Munger — fooling ourselves?** That the *equipment* pricing peak is permanent rent (it isn't — it's a cyclical high). Wind is a real, widening drag. Capacity expansion ($10B capex) could compress ROIC. Don't confuse the quality of the business with the wisdom of the entry.
- **Lynch / Fisher — growth evidence?** Strong and tangible (sold-out slots, +10–20% pricing, prepaid deposits). The growth is real — but already paid for.

**Panel read:** unanimous that the *business* is excellent and the *price* is not. Converges **WATCH**, buy on a pullback.

## Decision & sizing

**WATCH.** No position at $1,110. Start a STARTER only **sub-~$950**, or on confirmation that 2029–30 slots fully convert reservations → firm orders (proving durability, not just a booking peak). The services annuity is the part worth paying up for; don't pay a permanent-rent multiple for a cyclical equipment peak.

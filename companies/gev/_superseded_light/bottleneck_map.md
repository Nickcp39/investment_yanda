# Bottleneck Map — GEV

Last updated: 2026-06-19

Purpose: identify where scarcity creates pricing power, strategic control, or vulnerability — and answer the LEAD question: **does GEV own a DURABLE toll booth on electrification, or ride a CYCLICAL order surge? Does it set the price, or just ride demand?**

---

## TL;DR — the LEAD question, answered

**GEV owns a real, currently-binding physical bottleneck and IS setting price like an owner — but the rent is partly cyclical. Durability rests on the services annuity + oligopoly discipline, NOT on today's sold-out equipment slots.**

1. **It owns the bottleneck (it sets the price).** Heavy-duty gas turbines are a **3-maker oligopoly** (GEV + Siemens Energy + Mitsubishi ≈ two-thirds), entry effectively impossible (metallurgy/IP, multi-year qualification). Slots **sold out through 2029** (100 GW under contract: 44 GW firm + 56 GW reservations); GEV is raising 2026 equipment pricing **+10–20%** and **rationing** capacity (20→24 GW/yr). Customers **pre-pay deposits** (~$5.6B contract-liability inflow) to hold slots. That is owner behavior, not price-taking. (C014–C018)
2. **The durable rent is the services annuity, not the equipment slots.** **$87.4B (54%) of the $163.3B RPO is services** — a multi-decade, OEM-locked aftermarket on ~7,000 installed turbines (~25%+ world fleet). This is the part that survives the cycle. (C011)
3. **The equipment pricing premium is CYCLICAL.** It is demand-pull from one vertical — ~20% of the 100 GW is data-center-tied, but **DC capex is what moved pricing**. A 2-yr+ lead time means today's bookings depend on demand staying hot through 2028–30. The +10–20% premium is a *peak being capitalized as permanent.* (kill d)
4. **Solving the bottleneck costs capital.** $10B 2025–28 capex/R&D; as 24 GW/yr capacity lands 2027–28, watch whether it compresses ROIC (kill c). The measured pace argues *against* this today, but the risk is back-end-loaded.

> So: is it a durable toll booth or a cyclical surge? **Both, in different layers.** The *structure* (oligopoly) and the *services annuity* are a durable toll booth. The *equipment-pricing premium* is a cyclical surge. The mistake is paying a permanent-toll-booth multiple for the whole — including the cyclical surge.

## The "durable vs cyclical" question, dissected

| If "the toll booth" means… | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| The **oligopoly structure** (3 makers, no entry) | **DURABLE** | metallurgy/IP/qualification barriers persist across cycles (C019) |
| The **services annuity** ($87B RPO, ~7,000-turbine aftermarket) | **DURABLE** | contractual, sticky, OEM-locked, low-cyclicality (C011) |
| Today's **equipment pricing premium** (+10–20%, sold-out slots) | **CYCLICAL** | demand-pull from hyperscaler capex; a peak, not a permanent level (C016, C030) |

**Does GEV own the bottleneck or merely depend on it?** It **owns** the turbine-capacity and services bottlenecks (it sets price, rations capacity, collects deposits) — not a dependence. The thing it *depends on* is the **hyperscaler capex cycle** for the *equipment* premium's persistence.

**The durability test (the cleanest upgrade signal):** **2029–30 slots converting reservations → firm orders.** 56 GW of the 100 GW is *reservations* (softer than the 44 GW firm). If the soft book hardens at the premium price, the rent is proven durable; if reservations cancel / pricing rolls over, the equipment premium was a booking peak. **Reservation-fee/cancellation terms are undisclosed — the key open gap.**

---

## Risk — the bottleneck is not a permanent moat

1. **It eases for everyone eventually.** The scarcity that lets GEV charge +10–20% also fades when the capex cycle normalizes — so the equipment premium is mean-reverting, even though the oligopoly and services annuity are not.
2. **It's a cost sink at the margin.** "Solving" it = $10B 2025–28 capex; watch ROIC as 24 GW/yr lands 2027–28 (kill c).
3. **The premium is hostage to one upstream variable.** The hyperscaler capex slope (kill d / shared switch). A **2027 capex-digestion pause is the primary thesis-killer** for the equipment premium (services largely insulated).
4. **Wind is a live, widening drag** inside the otherwise high-quality structure (kill b — mildly triggered, C028).

**Bottom line:** GEV is a *real* toll booth — but the toll has two parts: a **durable rent** (oligopoly + services annuity) and a **cyclical surcharge** (the equipment-pricing premium). At $1,109 the market is paying for both as if both were permanent. The surcharge isn't.

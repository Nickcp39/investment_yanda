## Kill Criteria (→ feed `monitor.md`)

We should downgrade / stay-out (or, if held, trim) if:

- [ ] **(A) GT slot pricing rolls over OR 2029–30 reservations fail to convert to firm orders** — the equipment premium was a booking peak, not durable rent.
- [ ] **(B) Wind FY loss > ~$400M guide / continues widening** — escalate from mild to material.
- [ ] **(C) Segment ROIC compresses** as the 24 GW/yr capacity lands 2027–28 — capex sink confirmed.
- [ ] **(D) Hyperscaler 2027 capex guidance decelerates** — the primary thesis-killer for the equipment premium (shared switch turns red).

## Disconfirming Evidence (what makes the bear case wrong)

- [ ] **2029–30 slots convert reservations → firm orders** at the premium price (durable rent proven, not just a booking peak) — the clean UPGRADE signal.
- [ ] **Wind reaches break-even** ahead of the 6%/2028 path (drag removed).
- [ ] **Capacity lands 2027–28 without ROIC compression** (measured-adds discipline validated).
- [ ] **Hyperscaler 2027 capex guides flat-to-up** (shared switch stays green).

## The 3 that matter most (ranked)

1. **D — hyperscaler 2027 capex digestion** — kills the *equipment* pricing premium; the premium is a cyclical peak hostage to this slope. (The services annuity survives.)
2. **B — Wind losses widen** — already mildly triggered; the company-specific drag inside the quality story.
3. **C — capex compresses ROIC** — back-end-loaded as 24 GW/yr capacity lands 2027–28.

> Note the asymmetry: the kills threaten the **cyclical equipment layer and Wind**, not the **durable oligopoly + services annuity**. That is exactly why the verdict is **WATCH (buy on a pullback)** rather than avoid — the durable core is worth owning at the right price; the current price overpays for the cyclical layer.

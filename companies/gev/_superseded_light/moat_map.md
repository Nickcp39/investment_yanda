## ROIC–WACC Lens

- **Current ROIC:** improving but understated by one-offs (operating income $1.39B FY2025 on a large asset base post-spin). The *trajectory* (Power/Electrification 16%→22% EBITDA by 2028) implies a widening, positive spread — **if** the cyclical equipment premium holds and Wind stops bleeding.
- **Estimated WACC:** mid-single-digit-to-low-teens (IG balance sheet, <1x leverage, but cyclical-industrial beta).
- **Spread:** positive and widening on the base case — but part of the spread is *cyclical* equipment pricing, not purely structural.
- **Reinvestment runway:** large (electrification + AI load + grid replacement), but capacity adds are *measured* (rationing) — so reinvestment is disciplined, not unlimited.
- **Competitive advantage period:** long for the **services annuity + oligopoly structure** (decades); **shorter/cyclical** for the equipment-pricing premium (one capex cycle).

## Moat verdict

**Among the best moats in industrials — but split into a durable core and a cyclical surcharge.** The **durable** moat = (1) the 3-maker oligopoly with effectively impossible entry, and (2) the $87B services annuity (OEM-locked switching costs on ~25%+ of the world fleet). The **cyclical** layer = today's equipment-pricing premium (+10–20%), which is demand-pull from the hyperscaler capex slope and will fade when the cycle normalizes. **The market is pricing the cyclical surcharge as if it were part of the durable core** — that is the central valuation error (see `valuation.md`).

## Moat Falsification — the moat is weaker (or more cyclical) than we think if:

- [ ] **2029–30 slot reservations fail to convert to firm orders** → today's 56 GW reservation book was a booking peak, not durable demand (the key durability test).
- [ ] **Equipment pricing rolls over** as hyperscaler capex digests (2027) → the +10–20% premium was cyclical, not structural.
- [ ] **Services attach/margin erodes** (customers self-service, third-party parts, life-extension stalls) → the annuity is less sticky than assumed.
- [ ] **Capacity adds compress ROIC** as 24 GW/yr lands 2027–28 → "solving the bottleneck destroys returns."

## Notes

Guardrail: don't call something a moat unless it causes *persistent* excess returns. GEV's **services annuity + oligopoly structure clear that bar**; the **equipment-pricing premium does not** (it's a cyclical high). The discipline is to value the durable part richly and the cyclical part modestly — not to pay a permanent-rent multiple for the whole. See `bottleneck_map.md` (bottleneck ≠ permanent moat) and `valuation.md`.

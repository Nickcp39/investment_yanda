# GEV Moat Map (M3)

Last updated: 2026-06-21 | as_of: 2026-06-19 | sources: facts.md E37-E44, GEM-GT-MARKETSHARE, GEV-8K-1Q26-TRANSCRIPT

## Moat Summary: OLIGOPOLY + INSTALLED-BASE ANNUITY

Business quality grade: **EXCEPTIONAL** for Power/Services core; **UNCERTAIN** for Wind; **GOOD and improving** for Electrification

---

## Moat 1: Scale + Technology Barrier (Gas Turbines) — DEEPEST

**Evidence**:
- Three global heavyduty GT OEMs (GEV, Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi) supply ~two-thirds of all plants under construction globally [GEM-GT-MARKETSHARE]
- GEV has ~38% of Asia's gas turbine market under construction [GEM-GT-MARKETSHARE]
- Total manufacturing run-rate targeting 20 GW/yr by Q3 2026 [GEV-DEC25-INVESTOR-UPDATE]
- Competing at this scale requires: decades of metallurgy IP (single-crystal blades), combustion simulation capability, global service network, and billions in installed-base relationships

**Durability**: A new entrant would need 15-20 years and $10B+ capex to replicate GEV's turbine production capability. No credible new entrant exists.

**Current strength**: STRONGEST EVER — slots sold out through 2029, pricing +10-20% YoY, 100 GW backlog [E37, E38, E25]

---

## Moat 2: Installed-Base Lock-In (Services Annuity) — VERY DEEP

**Evidence**:
- Services RPO: ~**$87.4B** services backlog (contracted multi-year maintenance agreements) [E18, confirmed Q1 2026 ~$87B per transcript]
- Gas Power services run-rate: **$12B/yr current** → **$22B/yr by 2035** target [E40]
- LTSAs are 12-20 year contracts covering major inspections, parts, upgrades
- Only GEV can supply genuine OEM parts, diagnostics, and performance upgrades for GEV turbines
- ~100 GW of contracted backlog will all require LTSAs → services backlog grows as equipment backlog is delivered

**Durability**: Near-permanent. A utility that installs a GEV HA turbine is a GEV services customer for 20+ years. The installed base of ~7,000 gas turbines globally represents an irreplaceable annuity.

**Why this matters for valuation**: The services RPO is not at risk from 2027 hyperscaler capex slowdown. Even if new orders plateau, the services stream continues and grows as the new 2024-2027 equipment backlog converts.

---

## Moat 3: Electrification Data-Center Pull — GROWING

**Evidence**:
- Electrification equipment backlog: **$38.6B** (+75% YoY, +$16.6B) [E25]
- Data center orders: **$2.4B in Q1 2026 alone** (exceeded all of FY2025) [E39]
- Prolec GE acquisition secures transformer manufacturing for the Americas market
- 2028 target: $60B Electrification backlog (doubling from $30B) [E30]

**Durability**: Medium-deep. HVDC/transformers have switching costs (proprietary installation, standards) but less monopolistic than gas turbine services. Competitors: ABB, Siemens Energy, Hitachi.

---

## Moat 4: BWRX-300 SMR First-Mover — OPTIONALITY

**Evidence**:
- First commercial SMR in Western world under construction (Darlington, May 2025) [E42]
- UK Step 2 GDA passed (December 2025) [E43]
- US NRC license expected 2026; DOE $400M grant to TVA [E44]
- Government support: up to $40B for US + Japan projects

**Durability**: Very deep IF execution succeeds. SMR is 20-year optionality. If BWRX-300 delivers on schedule and budget, GEV is the global SMR platform of choice. Risk: nuclear construction overruns (historically common).

---

## Moat Weaknesses

1. **Wind has NO moat**: Onshore wind turbines are largely commoditized; Vestas and Siemens Gamesa are equally capable. GEV's offshore wind (Haliade-X) has scale but not a durable pricing moat. Wind is a drag on moat quality.
2. **Equipment pricing is cyclical**: The +10-20% pricing is a function of supply scarcity (slots sold out). In 2028-2030, as capacity expands to 20 GW/yr and new orders normalize, equipment pricing WILL moderate. This is the primary valuation risk.
3. **Services pricing has limits**: CPI-escalated but customers will push back at renewal if gas plants face political/regulatory headwinds.

## Moat Scorecard

| Moat Source | Depth | Durability | Trend |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gas turbine oligopoly + technology | VERY DEEP | 15+ years | Strengthening |
| Installed-base services annuity | DEEP | 20+ years | Growing |
| Electrification / data center | MEDIUM | 5-10 years | Growing fast |
| BWRX-300 nuclear optionality | DEEP (if executes) | 30+ years | Early construction |
| Wind turbine equipment | NONE | N/A | Deteriorating |

**Overall moat verdict**: GEV has a genuine, multi-decade moat in its services annuity and gas turbine oligopoly position. The business earns its "exceptional" quality tag from the services layer alone, which would be a top-decile asset in any portfolio. The question is exclusively about PRICE — does the market already fully price this moat?

# GEV Inversion Map / Kill Paths (M5)

Last updated: 2026-06-21 | as_of: 2026-06-19

*"Invert, always invert" — Munger. Start from: how does this business permanently impair capital?*

---

## Permanent-Impairment Test: Does GEV Pass?

**Baseline question**: Can the permanent value of this business fall below current price?

At $298B market cap on ~$3.2B normalized adj EBITDA (8.4% margin), the market is paying ~93x trailing clean EBITDA. On the 2026 guide of ~$5.9B, it's ~49x. On the 2028 targets of ~$10.4B, it's ~28x.

**For permanent impairment at current price**: You need the EBITDA trajectory to reverse — i.e., the path from $3.2B → $5.9B → $10.4B to stall or reverse.

---

## Kill Path F1: Hyperscaler Capex Digestion (2027) — MOST DANGEROUS

**Mechanism**: AWS, Azure, Google, Meta collectively committed ~$725B capex in 2026. In 2027, if demand growth slows and they enter "digestion mode" (absorb existing capacity before adding more), new gas turbine order flow and new Electrification orders stall.

**Status**: ACCELERATING per Q1 2026 data. But it is forward-looking — Q1 2026 Electrification data center orders $2.4B in one quarter; this cannot be extrapolated to perpetuity.

**What has to be true for this to kill the thesis**:
1. Hyperscaler capex decelerates >30% in 2027 (from ~$725B to <$500B)
2. Gas turbine reservation-to-firm conversion rate falls significantly
3. New orders below replacement of the 83-100 GW backlog delivery rate

**Evidence against**: ~$87B services backlog is independent of new order flow. Even at zero new orders, services continue. The terminal case is not catastrophic — it's a plateau.

**Assessment**: Yellow flag. Not a 2025-2026 risk; a 2027 monitoring item. Does not kill the ANNUITY thesis.

---

## Kill Path F2: Wind Losses Widen / Become Permanent — NEAR-TERM DRAG

**Mechanism**: Tariffs on imported wind components (Biden → Trump continuation), ongoing Offshore Wind contract losses (fixed-price legacy), onshore volume decline → Wind EBITDA worsens from −$400M guide to −$700M+. Becomes a persistent drag that absorbs $2-3B per year of corporate capital.

**Status**: Wind Q1 2026 loss −$382M vs full-year guide ~$400M → back-half improvement assumed. If back half doesn't improve, full year could be −$800M to −$1B.

**Evidence**: Offshore Wind has a finite stock of loss-making legacy contracts — these do roll off. Onshore Wind depends on tariff environment (OPEN — political).

**Kill-status**: TRIGGERED (mild) but bounded. Even if Wind loses $600M/yr permanently, Power + Electrification generating $6.5B+ adj EBITDA by 2026 absorbs it. Wind is a drag but not a killer at the consolidated level.

---

## Kill Path F3: Gas Turbine Capacity Expansion Erodes Pricing — MEDIUM-TERM RISK

**Mechanism**: GEV scales to 20 GW/yr; Siemens Energy and Mitsubishi do the same; combined supply 50-60 GW/yr by 2029 vs demand that may have peaked — pricing premium erodes from +15% to flat or negative.

**Evidence against**: Demand pipeline is enormous (100+ GW/yr globally to 2030+ per IEA net-zero scenarios). The constraint is not just GEV supply — it is the entire global manufacturing ecosystem for large gas turbines.

**Timing**: This is a 2028-2030 risk, not present. The current 100 GW backlog locks pricing through approximately 2028.

**Kill-status**: NOT triggered. Will become monitoring item in 2027 when order rate vs delivery rate converges.

---

## Kill Path F4: BWRX-300 Construction Failure — TAIL RISK

**Mechanism**: OPG Darlington construction encounters severe delays or cost overruns (history of nuclear projects), credibility of the BWRX-300 program is damaged, order pipeline dries up.

**Evidence**: May 2025 start is recent. Construction of nuclear plants have a long history of overruns (Vogtle, Hinkley Point C). Even a small reactor is not immune.

**Kill-status**: Tail risk; SMR is NOT in the base case valuation. Failure would eliminate optionality but not impair the core thesis.

---

## Kill Path F5: ROIC Compression from Prolec + Services Capex

**Mechanism**: Prolec acquisition ($5.3B) requires returns; capex/R&D of ~$10B over 2025-2028 must generate ROIC > WACC (~8-9%). If returns disappoint, the $22B FCF target is wrong.

**Evidence**: Prolec transformer margin is reportedly similar to or above GEV average; acquisition multiple was reasonable (power infrastructure scarcity). Early evidence positive ($2.4B Q1 data center Electrification orders include Prolec capacity).

**Kill-status**: Not triggered. Yellow on monitoring.

## Survivability Test (Permanent Impairment Verdict)

| Scenario | EBITDA in 3 yrs | Implied Multiple | Would Capital Be Impaired? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hyperscalers decel + wind worsens | ~$5B | ~58x EV/EBITDA | AT RISK — thin margin, no IRR cushion |
| Services annuity + Electrification sustain | ~$8B | ~36x | UNLIKELY — high but not crazy for monopoly services |
| 2028 targets achieved | ~$10.4B | ~28x | IRRELEVANT — base case already priced in |

**Verdict**: Capital is NOT at immediate permanent-impairment risk from the business fundamentals. The $87B services annuity alone is worth substantial value at any reasonable discount rate. **The risk is not impairment — it is the opportunity cost of paying 49x 2026 EBITDA when the multiple needs to compress to 28x in 2 years just to break even.** The bear case is not "lose everything" — it is "earn 0-3% IRR for 3-5 years while the market de-rates the multiple."

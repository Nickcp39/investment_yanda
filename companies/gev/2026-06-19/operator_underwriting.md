## Management Assessment: Grade 4/5

### CEO: Scott Strazik

**Track record**:
- Led GE Vernova business since **November 2021** (pre-spin); navigated the Wind segment crisis, Offshore Wind contract losses, and the organizational complexity of a carve-out from GE
- Appointed CEO at spin (April 2024)
- **Completed the impossible**: Took a division with −$2.9B operating income in 2022 to $1.4B in 2025 while maintaining customer relationships and executing the largest industrial spin-off in recent memory
- **Capital allocation track record at spin**: Immediately raised and then doubled dividend, raised buyback $6B→$10B (Dec 2025) — returning $3.6B to shareholders in year 1 as a public company
- **Guidance credibility**: Dec 2025 targets raised significantly above original spin-off targets; Q1 2026 guidance beat and raise — consistent under-promise / over-deliver pattern so far (2 data points only)

**Background**: 20+ years at GE; Cornell B.S. Industrial Labor Relations, Columbia SIPA M.S. (Economics & Public Policy). Deeply operational, not a financial engineer.

**Compensation structure**: 2024 LTI $9.5M (was $6.0M in 2023, calibrated for public CEO); predominantly equity-linked. Aligned.

**Evidence of quality**:
- CEO quote (Q1 2026 call): *"there is no company better positioned to serve and transform the global electricity system than GE Vernova Inc."* — confidence rooted in 100 GW backlog and data center pull. Not empty boosterism given the evidence.
- Sold-out supply position achieved ahead of plan; managed the Prolec GE acquisition through Feb 2026 close efficiently

**Limitations**:
- 2 years as public company CEO is a short track record for a Grade 5
- Wind segment losses persist ($382M Q1 alone) — either he hasn't solved it or tariffs are beyond management control
- "Up to $40B government support" for SMR includes committed and potential — some optimism in framing

### CFO: Kenneth Parks

- Joined as CFO at spin; previously CFO at Arconic and other industrials
- Executed the spin financial architecture cleanly
- Conservative guidance style has been rewarded (actuals beating guidance)

### Management Grade: **4/5**

**Rationale**: Strazik is demonstrably a capable operator who has executed a complex transformation. The capital return actions (doubling dividend, raising buyback) at a time when Wind is still losing money show financial discipline — not milking the peak. The consistent guidance raise pattern (Dec 2025 → April 2026) suggests either genuine visibility into backlog or competent conservatism. Wind losses are the only mark against an otherwise strong operating record. Deduct 1 point for: (a) short public company track record, (b) Wind execution remaining unresolved, (c) some CFO opacity on advance-payment normalization.

## Governance

- GEV shares: **one-class, standard NYSE listing** (no dual-class, no founder control)
- Board: 9 directors; GE spinout corporate governance standard (independent board)
- No founder / controlling shareholder — institutional ownership 78.6%
- This is a positive vs many industrial companies where legacy family or founder structures limit accountability

**Governance verdict**: Clean. One-share-one-vote. If Strazik or capital allocation goes wrong, the board has the ability to correct. No entrenched power structure.

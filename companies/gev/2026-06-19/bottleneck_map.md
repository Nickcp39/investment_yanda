## Binding Bottleneck: GAS TURBINE MANUFACTURING SLOTS

### The Structure

GEV has **~100 GW** under contract (firm + slot reservations) against current manufacturing capacity of roughly **10-12 GW/yr** scaling to **20 GW/yr** by Q3 2026 [E25, GEV-DEC25-INVESTOR-UPDATE]. This creates a multi-year production bottleneck:

| Year | Capacity (GW/yr) | Slots Consumed | Remaining |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2025 | ~10 GW | 83 GW total by YE25 | - |
| 2026 | ~12-14 GW | Growing to 100 GW | 10 GW slots remain in '29-'30 |
| 2027 | ~16-18 GW | Sold out 2025-2029 | Selling 2030 |
| 2028+ | ~20 GW | - | Targeting sold out 2030 by YE26 |

**Implication**: Customers who want a gas turbine in the next 5 years **must** go through GEV (or one of its two oligopoly peers). This is a STRUCTURAL bottleneck created by:
1. ~15-year manufacturing ramp lag (turbine facilities are not built quickly)
2. Oligopoly concentration (only 3 credible global suppliers of heavy-duty gas turbines)
3. Unprecedented demand surge (AI data centers + grid decarbonization + industrial electrification)

### Why This Bottleneck is Durable (Not Temporary)

- **Supply side**: GEV is expanding manufacturing at Greenville SC and Belfort France. Even at full ramp to 20 GW/yr by 2028, demand continues to exceed supply.
- **Demand side**: Hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, Google) committed to data center buildout through at least 2028. Utilities facing grid reliability mandates. Developing markets (Middle East, Asia) building new generation capacity.
- **Competitor capacity**: Siemens Energy and Mitsubishi equally constrained. Combined capacity of all three OEMs unlikely to exceed 40-50 GW/yr by 2028 vs demand pipeline of 100+ GW/yr globally.

### The Reservation Deposit Signal

Q1 2026 FCF of $4.8B included **~$5.6B of contract liability inflows** — customers paying deposits to reserve turbine slots [E24]. Customers do not pre-pay years in advance unless they are convinced they need the asset AND the supplier is the bottleneck. This is unambiguous demand confirmation.

## Bottleneck Durability vs Cyclicality — The Core Tension

**The bull case**: Gas turbine slot scarcity is structural and will persist through at least 2028-2030. Pricing power of +10-20%/yr is therefore durable, not a one-cycle peak.

**The bear case / kill path**: If hyperscaler capex decelerates materially in 2027 (capex digestion hypothesis), new gas turbine orders could slow, reservations could be cancelled or not converted to firm orders, and the pricing premium could erode faster than expected. The 56 GW of slot reservations (vs 44 GW firm) is the vulnerability.

**The key question**: What % of slot reservations have non-refundable deposits or meaningful cancellation penalties? This is OPEN (O3). The Q1 deposit inflow suggests financial commitment, but cancellation terms are undisclosed.

## Services Bottleneck: Installed Base Lock-In

**The annuity bottleneck**: GEV is the ONLY supplier of OEM parts, major inspections, and software upgrades for its own installed base of ~7,000 gas turbines. No independent servicer can fully substitute.

This is NOT cyclical — it is a structural monopoly on each turbine's maintenance. Even if no new turbines were built after 2026, the services stream would grow for a decade as the 100 GW pipeline enters operation and requires its first major inspection cycles.

**Threat**: Third-party parts suppliers (aftermarket) do exist and are growing. GEV must maintain technology leadership (upgrades, efficiency gains) to prevent commoditization of services.

---

## Electrification Bottleneck

**Transformer shortage**: The US and European transformer market is in multi-year backlog (delivery times stretched to 2-3 years). Prolec GE acquisition provides GEV with manufacturing capacity in a tight market, acting as a bottleneck participant for data center and grid customers.

**Grid upgrade mandate**: US FERC order and EU grid directives require massive spending on transmission upgrades over 2025-2035. GEV Electrification is a primary beneficiary.

---

## Wind: No Bottleneck

Wind turbine manufacturing is not a bottleneck. Capacity exists globally. The constraint is demand — and demand has been suppressed by tariffs and offshore project cancellations. GEV has no pricing power in Wind. This is a pure volume/cost business with sub-standard returns until tariff environment improves and offshore contracts reset to inflation-adjusted pricing.

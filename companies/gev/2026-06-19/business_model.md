## What GE Vernova Does

GE Vernova is the world's largest installed-base owner of gas turbine technology and a dominant grid electrification supplier. It was carved out of GE in April 2024 as a pure-play energy infrastructure company. Three segments address different parts of the electricity value chain:

### Segment 1: Power (~52% of FY2025 revenue, ~$19.8B)
Revenue split: **equipment** (~50%) + **services** (~50%)

- **Gas Power**: design, build, and service heavy-duty gas turbines (HA-class, 7HA/9HA) and aeroderivatives. Primary profit engine. Customers: utilities, IPPs, sovereign energy companies globally.
- **Nuclear Power (BWRX-300 SMR)**: GE Vernova Hitachi (GEH) joint venture building the BWRX-300 small modular reactor. Construction started May 2025 at OPG Darlington (Canada). US (TVA Clinch River), UK, Japan in pipeline. Long-cycle optionality.
- **Steam/Hydro**: heritage steam turbines + hydropower; mature maintenance services.

Key commercial dynamics:
- Gas turbines sold as equipment (~$50M+/unit for HA-class) plus a **long-term service agreement (LTSA)** lasting 12–20 years, covering major inspections, parts, and upgrades.
- Services revenue (~$12B/yr) is highly contractual, margin-rich (~25–30% EBITDA), and sticky. Services RPO is the annuity core.
- Pricing: **+10–20% on $/kW vs Q4 2025** for new 2026 orders (E37); slots sold out through 2029 (E38).

### Segment 2: Wind (~24% of FY2025 revenue, ~$9.1B)
- **Onshore Wind**: turbines (2–5+ MW class) for North America and international markets. Struggling with tariff headwinds; Q1 2026 EBITDA loss −$382M.
- **Offshore Wind**: Haliade-X and Haliade-150 turbines for major offshore projects. Elevated losses from legacy contract losses (fixed-price contracts signed before inflation spike).
- 2026 guide: ~$400M EBITDA loss; path to 6% EBITDA margin by 2028 requires tariff relief + lower offshore losses.

### Segment 3: Electrification (~24% of FY2025 revenue, ~$9.2B, growing fast)
- **Grid Solutions**: high-voltage direct current (HVDC), gas-insulated switchgear, substations
- **Power Conversion**: marine/industrial electrical systems
- **Prolec GE**: transformer manufacturing (50% acquired full control Feb 2026 for $5.275B)
- **Grid Software**: SCADA, energy management systems (EMS), Proficy (divested Q1 2026)
- Backlog: Electrification equipment backlog **$38.6B** at Q1 2026 (+75% YoY) — driven by data centers and grid modernization
- 2026 guide: $14.0–14.5B revenue, 18–20% EBITDA margin; 2028 target 22% EBITDA margin, $20B revenue

## Revenue Composition

| Revenue Stream | ~% Total | Durability | Pricing Power |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gas turbine equipment | ~20% | Cyclical (slot-locked for 3-5 yrs) | HIGH — oligopoly +10-20%/yr pricing |
| Gas/steam/nuclear SERVICES | ~25% | HIGH — LTSA contracts 12-20 yrs | MEDIUM-HIGH — CPI-linked escalators |
| Electrification equipment | ~20% | High-medium — book-to-bill 2.5x | MEDIUM — competitive; data center pull |
| Wind equipment | ~20% | Cyclical — under tariff pressure | LOW — commoditizing onshore |
| Wind/electrification services | ~10% | Medium — shorter contracts | MEDIUM |
| Other | ~5% | Various | Various |

**The critical insight**: Revenue durability is bimodal — the services layer (~$87B+ RPO, contractual, 12-20 yr horizons) is among the most durable cash flows in industrials; the equipment layer (especially gas turbines at cycle peak pricing) is cyclical and price-sensitive.

## Value-Creation Levers

1. **Gas turbine pricing expansion**: Oligopoly + sold-out slots → pricing power that is structural, not cyclical
2. **Services RPO monetization**: $87B+ services backlog converts to high-margin revenue over 10+ years
3. **Electrification backlog realization**: $38.6B and growing driven by data centers + grid upgrade
4. **Wind turnaround**: Path from −$382M Q1 loss to +6% EBITDA margin by 2028 (biggest uncertainty)
5. **BWRX-300 optionality**: First-mover in commercial SMR; optionality not yet in base case

```
[Fuel/Energy Source] → [GENERATION] → [TRANSMISSION] → [DISTRIBUTION] → [End User]

                        GEV Power         GEV Electrification
```

GEV operates in generation AND transmission, capturing value at two distinct points in the chain. This is strategically superior to peers who are in only one layer.

**GEV's value chain position**:
- DOES NOT own fuel, transmission infrastructure, or customer operations
- OWNS: the highest-IP component of the system (turbine core, blade metallurgy, combustion control), AND the most recurring revenue part (aftermarket services/parts)
- The LTSA creates a 12-20 year monopoly on servicing the installed base — GEV is the only OEM that can source genuine OEM parts for its own turbines

## Electrification Value Chain

```
Power Plant → HV Substation → HVDC Backbone → MV Distribution → Transformer → Customer
                    ↑               ↑                                  ↑
              GEV Grid Sol.    GEV HVDC                          GEV Prolec GE
              (switchgear,    (converters,                       (transformers)
               protection)    cables)
```

**Data center angle**: Hyperscalers need: (1) gas-fired backup/baseload generation (GEV Power), AND (2) transformers + switchgear to deliver power to their campus (GEV Electrification). GEV captures BOTH links for data center customers.

## Supply Chain Dependencies (Bottleneck Map preview)

- **Forgings and castings**: Large turbine discs/casings require specialized forge shops — long lead times (18-24 months); GEV has established supplier relationships
- **Advanced coatings/alloys**: single-crystal blade technology is GEV IP; coatings suppliers are specialized
- **Power transformers (Prolec)**: Steel laminations, copper windings — supply has been tight 2023-2026; Prolec acquisition secures some of this
- **Construction labor**: BWRX-300 needs nuclear-certified construction workers — scarce globally
- **EPC capacity**: Project execution for gas plants depends on EPC availability; not GEV's bottleneck but a customer's bottleneck that can delay deliveries

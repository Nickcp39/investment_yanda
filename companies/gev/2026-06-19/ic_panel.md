## The Five-Soul Framework Applied to GEV at $1,109.73

---

### Soul 1: 段永平 (Duan Yongping) — Chair Vote

**Framework principles**: Business durability > price; "stop loss" never — only add to great businesses; buy companies so good you could hold forever; focus on business model correctness; OK to pay up for exceptional quality but requires certainty of durability.

**GEV analysis through this lens**:
- Business model correctness: **STRONG** — GEV has a genuine "toll bridge" on global electricity infrastructure. Gas turbine services annuity is among the most durable cash flows in industrials.
- Durability confidence: **HIGH for Power core; LOW for Wind; MEDIUM for Electrification**
- Price: **CONCERNING** — paying 49x forward adj EBITDA means you are betting on BOTH (a) the business durability AND (b) no multiple compression in the next 3 years. DYP would want certainty on both.
- His typical stance: Pay quality premiums, but not for cyclical pricing peaks disguised as permanent rent.

**Vote**: **WATCH** — Business passes the quality screen; price does not pass the "comfortable holding forever from this entry" screen. Would become **STARTER sub-$950**.

---

### Soul 2: Warren Buffett — Anchor Vote

**Framework principles**: Buy great businesses at fair prices; moat matters most; seek durable competitive advantage; pricing power is the single best indicator of moat; management must be honest and capable.

**GEV analysis**:
- Moat check: **PASSES** — oligopoly gas turbine + services annuity + growing Electrification. Buffett would love the LTSA structure (recurring, high-margin, hard to cancel).
- Pricing power check: **PASSES** — +10-20% pricing in 2026 vs 2025 with customers PREPAYING for future slots. This is extraordinary pricing power evidence.
- Management check: **PASSES** — Strazik's turnaround from −$2.9B operating income (2022) to $1.4B (2025); doubled dividend, raised buyback.
- Price check: **FAILS** — Buffett rarely buys at 49x forward EBITDA. His entry in most industrial positions has been at 12-20x EBITDA. He famously avoided dot-com. The 49x is a price of perfection, not a bargain.
- Quote (paraphrase, documented framework): *"It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price"* — GEV is a wonderful company, but this is not a fair price.

**Vote**: **WATCH** — Would wait for at least $850-950 entry before STARTER.

### Soul 3: Charlie Munger — Analytical Vote

**Framework principles**: Invert; avoid folly more than seek brilliance; worldly wisdom; mental models; understand where you're in the cycle; "all I want to know is where I'm going to die, so I'll never go there."

**GEV inversion**:
- What kills this investment? Paying 49x for a business that executes perfectly but the market re-rates to 28x as multiple compression from "peak capex cycle scarcity" → return = 0% in 3 years.
- What would I need to believe for this to be right? (a) 2028 targets are certain, AND (b) 28-32x is a reasonable 2028 multiple for a business then generating $10.4B EBITDA. Munger would note (b) requires the market to continue paying a massive premium to the sector median.
- Folly check: "Am I confusing a great business with a great investment?" — YES, that confusion is the trap here.
- Cycle check: Gas turbine equipment pricing is a CYCLE PEAK (slots sold out, customers prepaying, pricing +20%/yr). Great businesses at cycle peaks have historically delivered poor forward returns.

**Vote**: **WATCH** — "The price you pay determines your rate of return. I don't care how good the business is." Would monitor for a significant pullback.

### Soul 4: Howard Marks — Risk/Cycle Vote

**Framework principles**: Know where you are in the cycle; understand what the crowd believes; second-level thinking; risk is not volatility, it is permanent loss; the most dangerous words are "this time it's different."

**GEV cycle positioning**:
- Where is the crowd? Consensus "Buy" (38 analysts), $1,212 price target, stock near ATH. This is maximum bullish consensus.
- What does the crowd believe? That the gas turbine scarcity is permanent, that hyperscaler capex will continue at $725B+/yr indefinitely, and that GEV's current slot prices represent the new normal.
- Second-level thinking: Even if the crowd is right about the BUSINESS, the price already reflects that view. Second-level question: "What happens to the multiple when the capacity expansion of 2027-2028 meets a 2027 hyperscaler digestion?"
- Permanent loss risk: LOW (annuity business provides floor). But OPPORTUNITY COST risk: HIGH — paying ATH prices for a cycle-peak-priced industrial is the classic Marks "risk without reward."

**Vote**: **WATCH / AVOID NEW ENTRY** — Excellent business, wrong point in the multiple cycle. "When everyone is bullish, that's when you need to be most careful about entry price."

### Soul 5: Seth Klarman — Deep Value Vote

**Framework principles**: Margin of safety above all; avoid overpaying; catalyst or discount required; process > outcome; short-term pain OK for long-term gain; price equals 50 cents on the dollar or wait.

**GEV margin of safety**:
- At $1,109.73, we calculated even the bull-case 3-year IRR is only +7.2% — BELOW the 8% hurdle.
- Klarman requires a material margin of safety: typically 30-50% discount to intrinsic value.
- Base case intrinsic value (3-year horizon): ~$974/share → current price is 14% ABOVE intrinsic value.
- For 50-cent-on-the-dollar: entry ~$487, which represents deep bear scenario.
- For a reasonable 20-25% MOS: entry ~$730-780.

**Specific concern**: The $87B services annuity is real and valuable. But even if we capitalize it at 15x = $1.3T... no, that's absurd. The $87B is a revenue backlog, not a P&L item. At $12B current run rate → 15x = $180B. Add Electrification at $14.5B revenue × 15x = $218B. Power Equipment residual ~$60B. Total EV ~$458B... which would imply the stock is actually cheap. **But this fails the earnings quality test**: Power EBITDA of $2.9B in 2025 growing to $22B EBITDA in... that requires $100B+ of revenue at 22%, which is a 2028 aspiration, not fact.

**Vote**: **WATCH / AVOID** — No margin of safety at current price. First STARTER at $750-800; meaningful position at $600-650.

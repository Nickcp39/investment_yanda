## Earnings Quality Assessment

**Red flags**: NONE at the accounting level. Advance payments are disclosed and real (customer commitments). One-offs are clearly identified.

**Genuine risks to future earnings quality**:
1. If Wind losses persist beyond $400M guide → aggregate EBITDA disappointment
2. If gas turbine slot reservations cancel → revenue recognition timing shifts + potential writedowns
3. If advance-payment inflows normalize (i.e., pipeline fills up), FCF will be lower than guided as contract liabilities are worked down

**Overall financial quality**: GOOD-to-HIGH for the Power/Electrification segments. Wind is structurally loss-making and represents ~24% of revenue drag. Consolidated adj EBITDA margin of 8.4% in 2025 understates the Power core (14.7% margin, ~52% of revenue) because Wind drags the average negative.

**If Wind were isolated and valued separately** at $0 (zero value), Power + Electrification combined would trade at a lower multiple on cleaner margins — arguably a more rational base for valuation.

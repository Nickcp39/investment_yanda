## 1. THE MODEL IN ONE LINE

GEV is **equipment-led but services-monetized**: it sells heavy-duty gas turbines (and grid/wind equipment) at near-cost-to-low-margin to *install the base*, then locks in **multi-decade, high-margin parts/service/upgrade contracts** on that base. The razor is the turbine; the blades are the 20-year aftermarket. Electrification (grid + large transformers via Prolec) is a *second engine* — short-cycle, capacity-constrained, structurally higher-margin and rising.

## 2. THE SERVICES ANNUITY (the durable rent)

[PRIMARY] S2 — the single most important business-model fact:
- **Total RPO/backlog $163.3B** (31-Mar-2026), split:
  - **Equipment $75.9B (46%)**
  - **Services $87.4B (54%)** ← the annuity
- The $87.4B services book is the **aftermarket on ~7,000 installed gas turbines** (~25%+ of the world fleet). Once a turbine is in the ground, the owner buys GEV parts/service/upgrades for ~20+ years — high switching cost (OEM-qualified parts, performance/warranty tie-in), high margin, low cyclicality.

> **Why this matters for the thesis:** the *durable* rent is the services annuity, not today's equipment-pricing premium. Services revenue is locked, recurring, and largely independent of the hyperscaler capex cycle. The equipment book ($75.9B) is where the *cyclical* +10–20% pricing premium sits — real today, but a peak being capitalized as permanent (see L5-6 raw).

## 4. REVENUE ENGINE

| Stream | Pricing unit | Volume driver | Price driver | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Gas equipment (Power)** | $/kW per turbine | slot availability (sold out thru 2029) | **+10–20% 2026 vs Q4'25**; path to ~$600/kW | cyclical — premium fades if DC capex digests |
| **Power services (annuity)** | $/unit aftermarket | installed base (~7,000) | sticky, contractual | low — most durable line |
| **Electrification** | $/unit grid gear / transformer | grid build-out, transformer shortage | rising (capacity-constrained) | eases if shortage resolves |
| **Wind** | $/turbine + service | renewables demand (declining) | weak; tariff headwind | structural drag; loss-making |

## 5. SEGMENT MIX & MARGIN ARCHITECTURE

- **Power + Electrification carry the company.** Q1'26 EBITDA margins 16.3% / 17.8%, guided to 22% / 22% by 2028.
- **Wind is dilutive** — Q1'26 −$382M, FY2026 ~−$400M EBIT, only 6% by 2028 on *declining* revenue.
- **Electrification second engine:** backlog $42B (from $9B YE2022), book-to-bill ~2.5x — the fastest-growing, capacity-constrained leg, now fully owned after the Prolec buy-in.

## 6. TEN-YEAR EXISTENCE TEST

- **Problem in 10 years?** Yes — firm/dispatchable power and grid capacity are structurally short (electrification + AI load growth + grid age). Someone must build and service heavy-duty turbines and transformers.
- **Why GEV remains well-placed?** 3-maker oligopoly with effectively impossible entry (metallurgy/IP, multi-year qualification); ~25%+ of the installed turbine fleet → a services annuity that compounds with every unit sold; full ownership of Prolec in transformers.
- **What changes the thesis?** A multi-year *demand-pull reversal* (hyperscaler capex digestion) that normalizes equipment pricing; a faster-than-expected shift to alternatives (SMR timing, storage+renewables displacing gas peakers); Wind staying a cash drain.

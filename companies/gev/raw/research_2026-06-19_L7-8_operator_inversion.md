## PART B — INVERSION / KILL CRITERIA

How does the bull thesis die? Four pre-registered kills (from `step0_plan.md`), scored against current evidence:

### Kill (a) — GT slot pricing rolls over / cancellations appear
- **Status: NOT triggered — opposite.** Pricing **+10–20%** on 2026 orders; customers **pre-paying** ~$5.6B in deposits; slots sold out through 2029. [PRIMARY] S3
- **Watch:** reservation → firm-order conversion for 2029–30 slots. The 56 GW of reservations is softer than the 44 GW firm; a wave of cancellations or a pricing rollover would flip this kill. **Reservation-fee/cancellation terms undisclosed.**

### Kill (b) — Wind losses widen → **TRIGGERED (mild)**
- **Status: TRIGGERED (mild).** Q1'26 Wind EBITDA **−$382M vs −$146M** Q1'25 — the loss *widened*. [PRIMARY] S3
- Drivers: ~$250–350M/yr tariff headwind; weak onshore/offshore demand. FY2026 guide ~$400M EBIT loss; 2028 target only **6% margin on declining revenue**.
- **Escalation rule:** if FY Wind loss **> ~$400M** guide / keeps widening → escalate from mild to material.
- **Read:** Wind's *best* case is "small profit on a shrinking business." It is a real, current drag inside an otherwise high-quality company.

### Kill (c) — Capacity capex compresses ROIC
- **Status: not yet visible.** $10B 2025–28 capex/R&D. **Watch as the 24 GW/yr capacity lands 2027–28** — the classic "solving the bottleneck destroys returns via capex" risk. [INFERRED] S4
- Today the measured pace + pricing power argue *against* this; the risk is back-end-loaded.

### Kill (d) — SHARED SWITCH: hyperscaler capex slope turns down → **PRIMARY THESIS-KILLER**
- **Status: STILL ACCELERATING.** 2026 hyperscaler capex ≈ **$725B, +77%**: AMZN $200B, GOOGL $175–185B, MSFT $190B, META $115–135B. [SECONDARY] S9
- This is the single upstream variable common to GEV / CEG / VRT. ~20% of the 100 GW is DC-tied, but **DC capex is what moved equipment pricing** — so a **2027 capex-digestion pause is the primary thesis-killer** for the *equipment premium* (the services annuity is largely insulated).

### The 3 that matter most (ranked)
1. **(d) Hyperscaler 2027 capex digestion** — kills the equipment pricing premium; the premium is a cyclical peak hostage to this slope.
2. **(b) Wind losses widen** — already mildly triggered; the drag inside the quality story.
3. **(c) Capex compresses ROIC** — back-end-loaded as 24 GW/yr lands 2027–28.

### Disconfirming evidence (what makes the bear case wrong)
- 2029–30 slots **convert reservations → firm orders** at the premium price (durable rent proven).
- Wind reaches break-even ahead of the 6%/2028 path (drag removed).
- Capacity lands 2027–28 **without** ROIC compression (measured-adds discipline validated).
- Hyperscaler 2027 capex guides **flat-to-up** (shared switch stays green).

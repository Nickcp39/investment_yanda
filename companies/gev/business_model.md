# Business Model — GEV

Last updated: 2026-06-19

Source base: L4 business-model raw, S2 (RPO split), S3 (segment/model). Facts: `facts.md` / `claim_ledger.csv`.

## One-Sentence Business

GE Vernova sells heavy-duty gas turbines (and grid/wind equipment) at near-cost-to-low-margin to *install the base*, then earns the real money on a **multi-decade, high-margin parts/service/upgrade annuity** on ~7,000 installed turbines (~25%+ of the world fleet) — with **Electrification** (grid + large transformers via the now-fully-owned Prolec) as a fast-growing, structurally higher-margin second engine, and **Wind** as a loss-making drag being managed down.

## Revenue Engine

| Revenue stream | Pricing unit | Volume driver | Price driver | Main risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Gas equipment (Power)** | $/kW per turbine | slot availability (sold out thru 2029) | **+10–20% 2026 vs Q4'25**; path to ~$600/kW | cyclical — premium fades if DC capex digests |
| **Power services (annuity)** | $/unit aftermarket | installed base (~7,000) | sticky, contractual, high-margin | low — most durable line |
| **Electrification** | $/unit grid gear / transformer | grid build-out, transformer shortage | rising (capacity-constrained) | eases if shortage resolves |
| **Wind** | $/turbine + service | renewables demand (declining) | weak; tariff headwind | structural drag; loss-making |

**Backlog architecture (the key fact):** of **$163.3B total RPO**, **services are $87.4B (54%)** vs equipment $75.9B (46%) (C011, C012). The **durable rent is the services annuity**; the **cyclical premium sits in the equipment book.** Holding both halves of that statement is the whole thesis.

## Ten-Year Existence Test

- **Problem in 10 years?** Yes — firm/dispatchable power and grid capacity are structurally short (electrification + AI load + aging grid). Someone must build and service heavy-duty turbines and transformers.
- **Why GEV remains best-placed?** 3-maker oligopoly with effectively impossible entry (metallurgy/IP, multi-year qualification); ~25%+ of the installed fleet → a services annuity that compounds with every unit sold; full ownership of Prolec in transformers. (C013, C019, C011)
- **What changes the thesis?** A multi-year demand-pull reversal (hyperscaler capex digestion) normalizing equipment pricing; faster shift to alternatives (SMR timing, storage+renewables displacing gas peakers); Wind staying a cash drain. (see `inversion_map.md`)

**Net:** a **high-quality industrial** — oligopoly + sticky services annuity + demonstrated pricing power is among the best moats in the sector. The two caveats: (1) the *equipment* leg is cyclical, not structural; (2) Wind is a real, widening drag. Quality is not the question — **price** is (see `valuation.md`).

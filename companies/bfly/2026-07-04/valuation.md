## 2. Five-Year IRR, Three Scenarios (EV/Revenue exit method, see CSV)

Method: anchor FY2026E revenue at the guidance midpoint ($119M), compound a 5-year revenue growth rate to a FY2031E exit revenue figure, apply an exit EV/Revenue multiple to get an exit Enterprise Value, and solve for the 5-year IRR versus the current EV ($1.87B). This is the standard framework for a high-growth, pre-profitability company where no earnings-based multiple exists yet -- exactly analogous to how growth-stage software/platform companies are valued before an EBITDA or FCF base is established.

| Scenario | 5y revenue growth | Exit EV/Rev multiple | Exit revenue | Exit EV | **5y IRR** | Multiple needed for 8% hurdle at this growth |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Bear** | 8%/yr | 2.0x | $174.9M | $349.7M | **-29.5%** | 16.89x |
| **Base** | 16%/yr | 3.5x | $249.9M | $874.8M | **-15.3%** | 11.81x |
| **Bull** | 25%/yr | 5.0x | $363.2M | $1,815.8M | **-2.0%** | 8.13x |
| *(sensitivity)* Aggressive bull | 30%/yr | 7.5x | $441.8M | $3,313.8M | **+10.5%** | 6.68x |

**Reading the grid**: the bear case is a straightforward permanent-impairment scenario (growth stalls, multiple compresses to a level appropriate for a struggling small-cap medtech). The base case assumes growth *below* the company's own FY26 guide (16%/yr vs. guided ~22% midpoint, reflecting the historical deceleration pattern 24.5% -> 19.0% and the fact that FY26 guidance itself has not yet been proven over a full year) and a still-generous 3.5x exit multiple -- **and even this base case is solidly negative (-15.3%)**. The bull case requires growth *above* guide (25%/yr, meaning several of the "30-40 more [Embedded partners] in discussion" [E73] convert to signed revenue) **and** a full platform-company exit multiple of 5.0x -- which is itself roughly where BFLY trades on a FY26E-guide basis *today* (15.7x on EV/Rev-guide vs. our normalized starting point) scaled down for a 5-year-forward view. **Even this bull case falls short of the 8% hurdle, landing at -2.0%.** Only the non-base "aggressive bull" sensitivity row -- stacking above-bull growth (30%/yr) *and* further multiple expansion (7.5x, i.e. the market must decide BFLY deserves an even richer multiple in 2031 than it has today, not merely hold today's multiple) -- clears the hurdle, at +10.5%.

> **This is the central quantitative finding of this dossier**: a rich entry multiple (19.2x trailing / 15.7x forward) means ordinary bull-case assumptions are not sufficient to clear an 8% hurdle. Only a scenario that combines above-guide growth *and* multiple re-rating (not one or the other) works. This is a fundamentally different risk/reward shape from the MSFT/GOOGL comparables in this pipeline, where a *de-rated* mega-cap only needed growth to continue (not the multiple to expand further) to clear its hurdle.

## 4. Margin-of-Safety Judgment

- **No margin of safety exists at $7.68 under base-case assumptions.** The base case implies a fair price near $2.28 -- the current price is more than 3x that level.
- **Even the bull case (5.0x exit, above-guide growth) does not clear an 8% hurdle** from the current entry price -- it takes an aggressive, non-base combination of above-bull growth *and* further multiple expansion to get there.
- **Downside protection is thin but not zero**: no debt, $137.95M cash, >5-year runway at guided burn rates [I3], and a real (if early-stage and narrowing per moat_map.md) technology franchise mean the bear case is a value-destroying de-rating, not an existential/bankruptcy risk over the next several years. This distinguishes BFLY from a true zero-margin-of-safety trap (e.g., a company facing imminent dilution or covenant breach) -- but it does **not** create a positive margin of safety at this price.
- **Contrast with the pipeline's MSFT case** (same lean-6module framework): MSFT's base 8%-hurdle fair value ($436) sat *above* its de-rated price ($379.40), a genuine positive margin of safety earned by a market panic sell-off. **BFLY is the mirror opposite**: its price has been bid *up* on a momentum/re-discovery event, and even after a partial giveback, sits well *above* any base-case fair value. The honest read is: rich multiple, real but narrow/early-stage moat (moat_map.md grade 2.5-3/5), company still guiding to a cash loss through FY2026 -- **the setup does not compensate for the risk at this price.**

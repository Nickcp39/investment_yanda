## Top Kill Criteria

- [ ] **K-1 (Cash/burn discipline breaks)**: FY2026 actual Adjusted EBITDA loss exceeds the guided $21-25M range [E34] by a material margin for 2 consecutive quarters AND cash burn accelerates such that the >5yr runway estimate [I3] compresses below ~2 years without a credible offsetting explanation -> re-underwrite the entire investment case; this was previously a cash-burning story that only recently became a "the burn is shrinking and the runway is long" story, and that improvement is the load-bearing recent-quarters evidence.
- [ ] **K-2 (Gross margin reversal)**: Adjusted gross margin falls back below ~60% (from the current 68.9% Q1'26 trajectory [E26]) for 2 consecutive quarters without a one-time explanation -> the "genuine operating leverage" thesis [I1] is disproven, and the semiconductor-cost-advantage narrative loses its best piece of supporting evidence.
- [ ] **K-3 (Butterfly Embedded fails to convert)**: Zero net-new signed Embedded partners (currently 9, [E54]) over the next 2-3 quarters, or the Midjourney relationship is terminated/materially renegotiated downward -> the platform-optionality re-rating driver [I6] that took the stock from ~$5.71 to $9.69 [S3] is invalidated, removing the primary bull catalyst independent of the core POCUS business.
- [ ] **K-4 (Governance escalation)**: Rothberg's 10b5-1 plan [E43] is followed by a SIGNIFICANTLY larger follow-on sale program, Class B->A conversion activity, or any move that suggests declining founder confidence -> re-underwrite the founder-alignment assumption in operator_underwriting.md.
- [ ] **K-5 (Litigation materializes)**: The Rose securities class action [E44, O7] or the Sezonov-related indemnification exposure [E46] results in a settlement/judgment materially exceeding the current small accrual ($3.0M + $3.3M, partially offset by a $6.0M insurance recovery [E18, E30]) -> reassess balance-sheet adequacy and management credibility on loss-contingency estimation.
- [ ] **K-6 (Competitive share loss)**: Independent evidence emerges of Butterfly losing head-to-head hospital-system deals to GE Vscan Air, Philips Lumify, Clarius, or Exo Imaging on a recurring basis -> the moat_map.md "shallow-medium, narrowing" grade should be lowered further and the durability of even the STARTER-tier verdict re-examined.
- [ ] **K-7 (Valuation air-pocket)**: Price reverts materially below the ~$7.06 average sell-side target [E7] or back toward the pre-rally $5.71 level [S3] without a change in fundamentals -> this would actually be a signal to revisit for a BETTER entry, not a kill criterion for the business itself, but it invalidates the current as_of price as a fair reference point for sizing decisions made today.

## Verdict Impact

- **Does any failure path already have strong evidence?** Partially. The valuation-momentum risk (last row) has ALREADY partially materialized (the -20.7% pullback from the 52wk high within 3 trading days [E3] is the market itself starting to invert this exact concern). The governance-concentration risk is not hypothetical -- it is a standing structural fact today [E9-E10], not a forecast. The competitive-catch-up risk has a real, concrete data point (Clarius profitability-first, [E67]) but has not yet shown up as Butterfly LOSING share to any specific named competitor.
- **Does current valuation compensate for these paths?** Uncertain-to-marginal. At $7.68 / ~$2.01B market cap [E5], the stock trades ABOVE the average sell-side price target (~$7.06) [E7] and near the top of its 52-week range despite the underlying business still guiding to continued losses through FY2026 [E33-34] and carrying every risk enumerated above. The recent price action (a +56% single-day pop on 7-month-old news, S3) looks more like a re-rating driven by story/momentum than by a fresh, quantified improvement in the failure-path evidence. See valuation.md for the explicit IRR math.
- **Verdict ceiling after inversion**: Given (a) a real but shallow/narrowing moat (moat_map.md), (b) a 3/5 operator grade with a genuine governance-concentration flag (operator_underwriting.md), (c) a valuation that has already run ahead of average sell-side targets and just gave back 20%+ of a spike gain, and (d) a still-cash-burning (albeit improving) financial profile through at least FY2026 -- the inversion analysis caps this at **WATCH-to-small-STARTER at most**, not a CORE or high-conviction position, regardless of how compelling the underlying imaging-AI technology story is on its own terms. The technology and recent execution trend are genuinely good; the price, governance structure, and moat depth are not yet good enough to underwrite a large position.

## Final Verdict Summary

**WATCH · no new-money position recommended at $7.68 · a genuinely improving small business at a price that already assumes more good news than a generous bull case can support.**

- BFLY has real technology (Ultrasound-on-Chip, fabless via TSMC) and genuinely improving financials: FY2025 revenue $97.61M (+19.0%), adjusted gross margin 59.6% -> 68.9% over 5 quarters (no adjustment needed by Q1 2026), first-ever positive operating-cash-flow quarter (Q4 2025, +$6.3M), narrowing net-loss margin every year. Butterfly Embedded (Midjourney: $15M upfront + $10M/yr x 5yr + up to $9M milestones, reconciling exactly to the company's own "$74M" headline) is a real, contractually-verified second value-capture mechanism, not vaporware.
- BUT at $7.68 (19.2x FY2025 actual revenue / 15.7x FY2026E guidance-midpoint revenue), the stock has been bid UP (not de-rated) on a June 2026 rediscovery of the 7-month-old Midjourney 8-K, spiking from $5.71 to an intraday $9.69 before partially retracing to $7.68. Base-case 5y IRR (EV/Revenue exit method) is **-15.3%**; even the bull case (5.0x exit multiple) is **-2.0%** -- both below an 8% hurdle. Only a stacked "aggressive bull" (30%/yr growth AND multiple expansion to 7.5x) clears it, at +10.5%.
- **Mirror-opposite comparison to this pipeline's MSFT case (same framework, same batch period)**: MSFT reached STARTER because a fear-driven capex panic created a genuine positive margin of safety (base fair value $436 > price $379.40). BFLY sits at the opposite pole -- a real, improving business that the market has already re-rated UP, leaving no price-side margin of safety even after a partial giveback.
- **The binding constraint is compound, not singular**: (1) no valuation margin of safety; (2) an unresolved moat-durability question (3-5yr technical lead, fabless/non-exclusive TSMC dependency, a smaller private rival -- Clarius -- already reached profitability first); (3) a governance-concentration risk (Rothberg ~71% voting power, no sunset until 2028-02-12, a disclosed same-founder litigation pattern at Quantum-Si).

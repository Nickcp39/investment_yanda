### Competitive Positioning (company's own framing, per 10-K)
- E62. Company states its core technology differentiator is semiconductor ("Ultrasound-on-Chip") vs. incumbent lead-based piezoelectric-crystal probes used by "the top five manufacturers of legacy cart-based incumbent ultrasound devices" (unnamed in the 10-K, but understood industry-wide to include GE Healthcare, Philips, Siemens Healthineers, Canon Medical, Samsung/Mindray-class players) [BFLY-10K-FY2025]
- E63. Company claims RoHS (EU lead-restriction directive) compliance advantage since its chip-based probes don't rely on the lead-piezoelectric exemption that competitor handhelds use; BFLY formally petitioned the European Commission (Oct 2024) to revoke that RoHS exemption for handheld transducers industry-wide [BFLY-10K-FY2025]
- E64. Traditional cart-based ultrasound: $30,000-$120,000+ per device; incumbent POCUS/handheld devices (company's characterization, unnamed competitors): ~$10,000 average (3-7k/probe, often needing 2-3 probes for full-body coverage + up to ~$2,000 software) vs. Butterfly's single-probe $2,700-$3,900 all-in [BFLY-10K-FY2025]

- I2. **The FY2025 headline net loss overstates the true cash deterioration**: GAAP net loss -$77.1M vs. actual net cash used in operations of only -$12.7M (ex the $81M capital raise). The gap is almost entirely non-cash (SBC $23.4M, inventory/vendor-advance write-downs $17.6M -- one-time) plus working-capital timing (deferred revenue +$12.8M is a cash-positive item). **Ex-offering cash burn for FY2025 was ~$19.3M for the full year** -- roughly 4 months of Q1'26-run-rate cash usage restated to an annual basis, and it is shrinking further into 2026 (Q1'26 alone used $13.9M op cash, but that included annual bonus payout, a seasonally heavy quarter).

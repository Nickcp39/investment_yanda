# ASML IC Panel — Five Souls

As-of: 2026-07-02 | As-of price: $1769.32

> No fabricated quotes. Each "voice" is a LENS — the documented investing principles of each investor applied
> to ASML. Where a principle is paraphrased, it is labeled (lens); no primary quote is attributed.

## 1. Duan Yongping (段永平) — chief judge (lens)

- **本分 / circle of competence:** Is this a business I understand and would own for 10 years? *Yes — a literal monopoly on the deepest chokepoint in the chip stack, with a service annuity and net cash, is comprehensible and durable. Arguably the highest-quality business I've looked at.*
- **管理层是否靠谱:** Fouquet is a physicist who personally industrialized EUV — a hardship-formed technical builder, exactly the operator I trust; clean one-share-one-vote governance. 非常靠谱. (Only watch: buyback at ~62x, dropped bookings disclosure.)
- **价格 / "好生意 + 好价格":** This is where it fails, harder than HWM. 段's discipline is to *wait for the fat pitch* and to remember that even a wonderful business at a terrible price is a bad investment. ~62x trailing on **peak-cycle** earnings, with a **negative base-case 5y IRR even on ASML's own 2030 targets** — 这是看得懂的好生意,但贵得离谱. Verdict: **WATCH; 等回调到 ~$960 或更低 (AI-capex scare / China shock).**
- **不要把鸡蛋放一个篮子的隐藏版:** it also doubles my AI-capex bet (BTC/GOOGL/NBIS already ride it). Even at a fair price I'd size it *aware* it is not a diversifier.

## 2. Warren Buffett (lens)

- "Wonderful company at a fair price." ASML is *wonderful* — a monopoly with pricing power Buffett would admire (he loves a toll bridge). But "**price is what you pay, value is what you get**," and at ~62x on cyclical peak earnings the margin of safety is absent. Buffett also historically avoids deep-tech he can't forecast 10 years out; the monopoly he'd get, the *demand cyclicality and China policy* he'd flag. Verdict: **admire, wait.** No new money here.

## 3. Charlie Munger (lens)

- "A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price." ASML is a *great* business — but **"no matter how wonderful, it's not worth an infinite price."** ~62x / ~48x EV/peak-FCF borrows years of return. Munger would also invert: the surest way to lose is to overpay for even the best business at a cycle peak. Verdict: **great business, price too rich — invert and wait.**

## 4. Howard Marks (lens)

- **Where are we in the cycle?** AI-capex is at/near a euphoric peak; ASML's orders (record Q4'25 bookings) and multiple both sit at the optimistic end. "**The most dangerous thing is paying a price that assumes everything goes right.**" ASML's base case requires the full EUR44-60B 2030 model AND a permanent premium multiple. Marks: second-level thinking says the good news — the AI monopoly story — is fully in the price. Verdict: **WATCH; the risk is to the downside of both the multiple and the cycle.**

## 5. Seth Klarman (lens)

- **Margin of safety is the cardinal rule.** A negative base-case 5y IRR — computed on the company's *own* optimistic targets — is the opposite of margin of safety. Klarman would not own it here regardless of monopoly quality, and would specifically distrust valuing a **cyclical** off peak earnings. Verdict: **REJECT new money until price < ~$960 (ideally ~$821);** quality does not substitute for a margin of safety.

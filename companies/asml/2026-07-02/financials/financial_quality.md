- Revenue **+12%/yr CAGR** (2021-25); gross margin structurally **~50-53%** (world-class for capital equipment), trending toward a 56-60% 2030 target.
- **Cyclicality is visible in the cash flows, not the margins:** FCF fell from EUR8.2B (2022) to **EUR4.4B (2023, the down-cycle)** then rebounded to EUR12.3B (2025). This is the tell that ASML is a *deep cyclical wearing a compounder's margin profile* — the reason to value off NORMALIZED earnings, not the peak.
- Capex is light (~EUR1.6B on EUR32.7B, ~5% of sales); the heavy spend is R&D (~EUR4B/yr) — an offensive moat investment, not maintenance.
- Share count falling on the EUR12B buyback — EPS growth is not dilution-flattered.

## Balance sheet

- **Net cash** (cash comfortably exceeds debt; a fortress). No solvency risk under any plausible cycle. EUR12B buyback + EUR7.50 dividend are funded from FCF, not leverage. EV ~$676B ≈ market cap less net cash.

## Owner-earnings sanity

- FY2025 FCF **EUR12.25B ≈ $14.0B**; owner earnings ≈ FCF (capex is light, growth spend is R&D expensed above the line). At ~$676B EV, **EV/FY2025 FCF ≈ 48x** — the quality is real but capitalized at a peak-cycle multiple on peak-cycle cash.
- On NORMALIZED FCF (blend the 2023 trough with 2025 peak -> call it ~EUR8-9B mid-cycle ≈ $9-10B), EV/normalized FCF is **~68-75x** — the honest through-cycle multiple, and the crux of the M6 verdict.

## Verdict

Financial reality **confirms an exceptional, high-margin, cash-generative monopoly (business signal +2)** — but it is a **deep cyclical**, and the cash flows prove it (the 2023 trough). The financials are NOT the problem. The problem is that the price capitalizes *peak-cycle* earnings/FCF at ~62x / ~48x EV/FCF, and even on ASML's own optimistic 2030 model the forward return is poor (M6). Signal on financial quality: **+2 for quality, but read alongside the cyclicality flag that caps the size ceiling.**

# ASML Moat Map (M3)

As-of: 2026-07-02

## Moat sources (ranked)

| Source | Strength | Evidence | Durability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **EUV monopoly (technology + IP)** | **Extreme (100%)** | Only firm on earth that can build an EUV scanner; decades of R&D + Cymer light source + Zeiss optics integration | Structural, 10-20+ yr — no credible challenger |
| **Supplier-ecosystem lock (Zeiss/Cymer/Trumpf)** | Very strong | Owns Cymer; deep Zeiss high-NA optics partnership/stake; a rival would have to rebuild the whole web | Structural |
| **Installed-base service annuity** | Strong | ~28% of sales; every shipped tool feeds decades of service/upgrades | Multi-decade |
| **Customer switching cost / cost-of-failure** | Very strong | Re-tooling a fab around a different litho platform is effectively impossible; there is no alternative for EUV | Structural |
| **R&D scale** | Very strong | ~EUR4B/yr R&D; High-NA is a decade-long, multi-EUR-billion program peers cannot match | Structural |
| **Scale / cost** | Strong | Volume + learning-curve on a global service/parts footprint | Structural |

## Moat test by layer

- **EUV — DEEPEST MOAT IN THE STACK.** This is stronger than HWM's airfoil moat: HWM has a few peers + captive OEM capacity; **ASML has literally zero EUV competitors.** The barrier is a fusion of physics, 30+ years of R&D, the Zeiss/Cymer ecosystem, and customer qualification. High-NA (0.55 NA) extends it another generation.
- **DUV — MODERATE.** Immersion DUV is dominant but not a monopoly (Nikon/Canon exist); this is the layer exposed to China export policy.
- **Service — DEEP & DURABLE.** Annuity on the installed base; grows with shipments; far less cyclical than tool sales.

## What could erode it (honest bear on the moat)

- **China builds an indigenous EUV.** SMEE (China's litho champion) remains many years behind; the CEO frames the gap at "10-15 years." Real long-tail risk, not near-term — but the single most important thing to monitor for the *moat*, not just the *multiple*.
- **A demand-side, not supply-side, break:** the moat protects ASML's *position*, but not its *volume* — a semi-capex down-cycle (or an AI-capex digestion) cuts revenue hard even with the monopoly intact (this is the cyclicality risk, M5).
- **High-NA adoption stalls / TSMC's cost objection spreads.** TSMC declined High-NA for 1.4nm on ~$400M/tool cost; if High-NA economics don't work for the biggest customer, the 2030 EUR44-60B model's upper half is at risk (a growth/mix risk, not a monopoly-break).
- **Export controls escalate to EUV** (they have not, and ASML never shipped EUV to China) or a punitive DUV ban removes ~20% of revenue (M5).
- Additive/EUV-alternative patterning (nanoimprint, directed self-assembly) — long-dated, not a near-term threat to EUV leadership.

## Verdict

Moat is **the deepest and most durable in the entire semiconductor stack** — a genuine 100% monopoly on EUV, extended by High-NA and a growing service annuity, on a fortress balance sheet. This is a textbook "toll on every advanced chip." It justifies a premium multiple and a high durability ceiling for the BUSINESS. The constraint is NOT the moat — it is (a) the cyclicality of the *volume* the moat protects and (b) the price paid for it (M5/M6).

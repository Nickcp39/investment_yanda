# ASML Business Model (M2 / M3 input)

As-of: 2026-07-02 | Pipeline: lean-6module-v1.1

## One sentence

ASML is the sole company on earth that can build EUV lithography machines — the tools without which TSMC, Samsung and Intel cannot pattern a leading-edge chip — so it collects a monopoly toll on every advanced logic and DRAM wafer, plus a growing high-margin service annuity on a ~$400M installed base of machines that run for decades.

## Revenue engines (Q1 2026)

| Engine | Q1 rev | What it is | Why it earns |
|---|---:|---|---|
| **EUV systems** | EUR4.1B | Extreme-ultraviolet lithography (0.33 NA + High-NA 0.55 NA) — the crown jewel | **Global monopoly**; the only way to print <7nm features; ASP EUR150-350M+/tool |
| **DUV + other systems** | EUR2.1B | Deep-ultraviolet immersion/dry + metrology — mature-node & multi-patterning | Duopoly-ish (Nikon/Canon weak in immersion); the China-exposed layer |
| **Installed Base Management (service)** | EUR2.5B | Field service, spare parts, upgrades on the installed base | **Recurring annuity**; ~28% of sales, sticky, high-margin; compounds with every tool shipped |

Q1 2026 total: **EUR8.8B**, 53.0% gross margin, 31.4% net margin, EUR2.8B net income.

## Demand drivers

1. **AI leading-edge capex** — hyperscaler + foundry buildout for GPUs/TPUs/accelerators pulls TSMC N2/A16 + Samsung/Intel leading-edge, which is EUV-intensive. This is the primary structural tailwind. Korea was #1 at 45% of Q1 system sales (memory for AI).
2. **DRAM/HBM scaling** — AI memory (HBM) drives advanced-DRAM nodes onto EUV single-patterning; a double-digit EUV CAGR node per Investor Day 2024.
3. **High-NA EUV** — the next moat extension (EUR44-60B 2030 model leans on it); Intel first (EXE:5200B, Dec 2025), Samsung/SK hynix 2027-28; TSMC deferring on cost.
4. **Installed-base service** — every machine shipped adds decades of recurring service/upgrade revenue.
5. **(Fading) China DUV** — was 33% of 2025 sales; guided to ~20% in 2026, Q1'26 at 19% — a structural DECLINE under export controls, not a growth driver (see M5).

## 10-year test (would you own it for a decade?)

- **For the business: emphatically yes.** A literal monopoly on the deepest chokepoint in the most important supply chain of the era, ~50-53% gross margins, a growing service annuity, a fortress net-cash balance sheet, an EUR12B buyback, and a technologist CEO. This is arguably a higher-quality franchise than HWM.
- **At this price: the question is open.** ~62x trailing / ~44-47x forward earnings, +159% off the 52wk low, prices in years of the AI-capex up-cycle AND a permanent monopoly premium. For a deeply cyclical capital-equipment business, the honest lens is NORMALIZED through-cycle earnings — on which the multiple is even steeper (see valuation.md).

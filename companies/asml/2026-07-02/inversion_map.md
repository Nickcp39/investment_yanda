# ASML Inversion / Trap Test (M5)

As-of: 2026-07-02 | As-of price: $1769.32

> Invert: how does an owner LOSE money buying ASML here? Separate permanent business impairment
> (would trigger a veto) from valuation/cycle/policy risk (sizing/timing, not veto).

## Veto check (hard veto = thesis durability break OR balance sheet cannot survive)

- Durability: EUV is a 100% monopoly; no credible competitor; High-NA extends it; service annuity grows -> **moat intact. No veto.**
- Balance sheet: **net cash**, EUR12B buyback + dividend funded from FCF, no solvency risk in any cycle -> **survives. No veto.**
- => **Hard veto does NOT fire.** This is not a structural-decline or balance-sheet trap. The traps are (1) valuation, (2) cyclicality, (3) China/policy, (4) factor concentration.

## The actual traps (sizing & timing; signal -1)

## Why F1 (+ F2/F3) dominate

At **~62x trailing and ~48x EV/peak-FCF (~68-75x on NORMALIZED FCF)**, most of the expected return depends on the multiple staying elevated AND the AI-capex cycle staying at/above peak. **Even on ASML's own mid-Investor-Day 2030 model (EUR50B rev, 30% net margin) and a still-generous 28x exit, the 5-year IRR is negative (~-5.7%/yr).** The monopoly protects ASML's *position and margin* through a cycle, but it does not protect the *revenue level* (F2) or the *multiple* (F1) — and policy can subtract ~20% of revenue independent of the monopoly (F3). This is the HWM/GEV pattern, more extreme: an exceptional business where the price has borrowed years of future return.

## China / export-control status (LIVE — the flagged tail)

- **June 2026 (freshest):** US alleged a possible unauthorized EUV transfer to China; **ASML formally denies** (all 314 EUV machines accounted for). US cited EUV component/transport-equipment shipments -> **BIS enforcement risk on components** (the Applied Materials Feb 2026 settlement was for components/units, not full systems — so a fine/enforcement action is a real, if bounded, risk).
- **April 2026:** **MATCH Act** would ban ALL DUV to China (~20% of 2026 rev) and force NL alignment in 150 days.
- Net: China already fell 33% -> 19% (Q1'26) with margin *held* (AI EUV replaced low-margin DUV), so the *earnings* hit is smaller than the *revenue* optics. But the tail is live and evolving — the top kill-criterion to monitor.

## Quantified downside (5y, from valuation.md / scenario_model.csv)

- Bear (-14.8%/yr): AI-capex digestion + de-rate to 24x.
- Base (-5.7%/yr): mid Investor-Day model but de-rate to 28x — **still negative.**
- Bull (+2.9%/yr): top Investor-Day model + 32x kept — still below an 8% hurdle.
- Asymmetry at $1769.32 is unfavorable: capped upside vs material downside.

## What would prove the thesis WRONG (flip from WATCH)

- **Bullish flip (-> STARTER):** price falls toward **~$960 (8% IRR) / ~$821 (CORE)** on an AI-capex scare or a China shock, while the monopoly + AI EUV demand hold -> margin of safety appears.
- **Bearish confirmation (-> AVOID/moat re-underwrite):** a genuine China indigenous-EUV breakthrough (F6), or High-NA broadly rejected on cost (F4), or an AI-capex winter that proves the 2025/26 orders were a bubble.

## Verdict

No permanent-impairment veto; **signal -1 on M5** (valuation + cyclicality + live China/policy tail, but no balance-sheet/moat break). The business is a keeper — arguably the single best chokepoint in tech. The price, the cycle timing, the China tail, AND the fact that it doubles this book's AI factor are the problems.

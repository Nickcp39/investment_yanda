## What ALGN Sells: Two Segments

### 1. Clear Aligner (~80% of revenue — $3.2B FY2025 / $856M Q1 2026, +7.4% YoY)
Invisalign comprehensive treatment packages (adults/teens), Invisalign First (ages 6-10, mixed dentition), Vivera retainers. This is the core consumer-facing product: a physical, single-patient-custom-manufactured set of clear plastic aligner trays produced per an approved ClinCheck digital treatment plan. Billed per CASE (a full course of treatment), not per unit — Q1 2026 case volume 685.7K (+6.7% YoY) [facts.md E32, E36].

**Mechanism**: Orthodontist/GP dentist scans patient (via iTero or, per the EU antitrust allegation, historically constrained to ONLY iTero) → digital scan uploaded to Align → ClinCheck software generates a 3D treatment simulation → doctor approves/edits plan → Align manufactures a sequential series of custom aligner trays → doctor dispenses to patient over the treatment course (months) → doctor bills the patient/insurer; Align bills the doctor per case.

### 2. Systems and Services (~20% of revenue — $789.6M FY2025 / $184.1M Q1 2026, +0.9% YoY — SLOWING)
**iTero intraoral scanners** (hardware: the imaging capture device) + **exocad CAD/CAM software** (the design/planning software layer, applicable beyond just Invisalign — also used for crowns, implants, other restorative dentistry) + related services/training. This is the "picks and shovels" / infrastructure layer that captures the 3D oral scan data feeding INTO the Clear Aligner segment, but which Align also monetizes standalone (a general dentist can buy an iTero scanner + exocad software for restorative/implant work with zero Invisalign attach).

**Mechanism**: Sell scanner hardware (one-time or subscription-like recurring-upgrade capital good) → scanner becomes the doctor's default oral-imaging workflow tool → doctor's dental practice standardizes on iTero+exocad for BOTH restorative dentistry AND ortho referrals → creates a structural on-ramp/stickiness into the Invisalign ecosystem specifically because the scan data format and clinical workflow habit are already established.

## The Core Mechanism: Scanner-as-Gatekeeper to a Consumable-Case Annuity

Unlike GEHC's three-layer (equipment / service / consumables) model, ALGN runs a **two-layer, vertically-integrated digital-workflow model**:

1. **Imaging capture layer (iTero hardware + exocad software)** — the "razor" in a modified sense: not the primary profit driver by revenue, but the DATA-CAPTURE GATEWAY that determines which aligner-case orders flow to Align vs a competitor
2. **Custom-manufactured consumable case (Clear Aligner)** — the true recurring, per-patient, per-treatment revenue driver; each case is a bespoke manufacturing job (thermoformed plastic trays sequenced per the ClinCheck plan), high-margin (70.8% gross margin Q1 2026 [facts.md E29]) but NOT infinitely recurring per patient (a single patient typically completes treatment once, unlike GEHC's PDx contrast-agent-per-scan model which recurs indefinitely per imaging volume)

**The critical structural question (and the direct subject of the June 2026 EU antitrust investigation)**: does owning the scanner meaningfully gate which aligner-maker gets the case? Align's historical practice — per the EU Commission's stated theory of harm — was to refuse ClinCheck submission from THIRD-PARTY scanners (e.g., 3Shape, Straumann/other rivals), effectively requiring an iTero purchase as a precondition for offering Invisalign to patients [facts.md E58]. If true and if the mechanism is real, this converts the smaller, slower-growing Systems and Services segment (only +0.9% YoY, facts.md E28, E70) into a strategically load-bearing MOAT COMPONENT for the much larger Clear Aligner segment, even though it is NOT the primary revenue driver on its own P&L line. This is exactly the imaging/AI-adjacent mechanism most relevant to Nick's career lens — an intraoral 3D scanner (a genuine imaging device, not just a soft "digital" feature) functioning as a competitive gate.

## AI Layer: Where the "Digital Dentistry" Narrative Is Concretely Real (not just marketing)

Three specific, named AI/software product facts, all confirmed via company IR sources or industry trade coverage of the 2026 AAO conference [facts.md E64-E68]:

1. **ClinCheck Live Plan** — automates generation of an initial doctor-ready Invisalign treatment plan in ~15 minutes (down from a multi-day cycle); began global rollout Q1 2026. This is a genuine treatment-planning AI deployment with a disclosed before/after cycle-time claim (days → minutes), not a vague "AI-powered" marketing label.
2. **exocad DentalCAD 3.3 "Chemnitz"** — includes AI-enabled multi-unit design capability in the CAD/CAM software layer (applies to implants/restorative work, not solely aligners).
3. **iTero Lumina** — newest scanner generation with "Multi-Direct Capture" and a larger field of view — a genuine imaging-hardware capture-quality improvement, the direct analog to a next-gen medical imaging sensor upgrade.

**Career-relevant framing**: this is a real, three-layer digital-dentistry AI stack — capture hardware (iTero) → design software (exocad) → treatment-planning automation (ClinCheck) — that is structurally analogous to a medical-imaging pipeline (scanner → PACS/reconstruction software → AI-assisted diagnosis/planning). Unlike GEHC's AI layer (real product breadth, but ZERO monetization evidence found in that dossier), ALGN's Systems and Services segment DOES have a disclosed revenue line ($789.6M FY2025) — but that segment's growth (+0.9% YoY Q1 2026) is decelerating sharply relative to the Clear Aligner segment (+7.4% YoY), which is the opposite of what a strengthening AI/software moat thesis would predict. This tension is the single most important unresolved question in this dossier — see bottleneck_map.md.

## Why This Matters for the AI/China Career Lens

ALGN's model is architecturally different from GEHC's and, in some ways, a cleaner test case for Nick's specific thesis: it is a **single-category digital-imaging-to-consumable pipeline** (oral 3D scan → AI treatment plan → custom-manufactured product), not a multi-modality hardware conglomerate. Angelalign's model (per available evidence) is "domestic-scale, aggressive-pricing, CAD/CAM-based aligner manufacturer" — directionally the same "fast-follower domestic competitor compressing global-incumbent margins" pattern seen with United Imaging vs GEHC in this same batch, but here the competitive response has escalated to **simultaneous patent litigation in three jurisdictions** (US/EU/China, same day, Aug 2025) rather than GEHC's more passive "monitor the trend" posture. This is a materially more aggressive, more legally-contested competitive dynamic than the GEHC/United-Imaging case, and the EU antitrust investigation (which is fundamentally about whether Align's scanner-ecosystem control is an illegal moat-defense mechanism) adds a genuinely novel regulatory dimension not present in the GEHC dossier at all. See inversion_map.md and bottleneck_map.md for the detailed risk development.

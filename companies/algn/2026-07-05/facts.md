- I1. **Price has recovered sharply and is now in the upper-third of its 52-week range** (−11% off high, +48-51% off low), a striking contrast to the 2025 restructuring-era collapse (E46, stock fell ~34-36% around July/Aug 2025, likely near/at the 52wk low of ~$122-125). - I2. **The business has genuinely re-accelerated post-restructuring**: Q1 2026 revenue +6.2% YoY (E26) is faster than the full-year FY2025 rate of +0.9% (E15) and faster than Q4 2025's +5.3% YoY (E21) — a real sequential acceleration, not just an easy comp (FY2025 was itself weak). Combined with gross margin improving to 70.8% (E29, up from Q4's 65.3% GAAP — though Q4 included one-time restructuring drag not necessarily present in Q1) and a reaffirmed (not raised) FY2026 guide, the fundamental trend supports SOME of the price recovery, but the magnitude of the specific 07-01/07-02 spike (E2) is not obviously explained by any single fact found this run.
- I3. **Segment divergence is the moat-relevant tell**: Clear Aligner growing +7.4% YoY (Q1 2026) vs Systems and Services growing only +0.9% YoY (E27, E28) is a meaningful divergence. If the iTero/exocad/ClinCheck-AI stack is genuinely the moat-reinforcing mechanism (career thesis), one would expect Systems and Services to show attach-rate-driven growth roughly in line with or ahead of Clear Aligner — instead it is lagging materially. This could reflect (a) scanner unit sales cyclicality/replacement-cycle timing, (b) increasing scanner durability reducing repeat-purchase frequency, (c) genuine competitive pressure on the scanner side specifically (from iTero rivals like Straumann/3Shape, whose "refused scan submission" grievance is literally the subject of the EU probe), or (d) simply that Systems and Services is a smaller, lower-growth-by-design annuity layer. Not disambiguated this run — flagged for bottleneck_map.
- I4. **The EU antitrust investigation (E57-E63) is the single most career-relevant new fact in this dossier**: it is a regulator's OWN theory of harm that explicitly validates the mechanism Nick's career lens is designed to probe — that iTero scanner-ecosystem control is a deliberate, load-bearing moat-defense tool, not just an ancillary product line. This cuts both ways for investment purposes: (a) if the allegation is TRUE, it confirms the moat is real and strategically defended (bullish for moat durability, though carries legal/reputational/finable risk), (b) if regulators force Align to open iTero to third-party scan submission (interoperability remedy, common in EU tying cases), this would directly erode the scanner-to-aligner lock-in mechanism and could accelerate share loss to cheaper competitors (including Angelalign, which likely benefits from any forced interoperability). This is a genuine two-sided risk/moat-confirmation event, not simply a negative.
- I5. **Angelalign litigation (E50-E56) is a three-jurisdiction, same-day (Aug 15, 2025) legal campaign** — an unusually coordinated and aggressive posture that itself is evidence of how seriously Align's own legal/strategy function weighs the Angelalign threat. Litigation outcomes (injunctions, damages, patent validity rulings) in any of the three jurisdictions could be genuinely thesis-moving, especially the China Zhengzhou court case (import/domestic-market implications) and the EU Unified Patent Court case (interacts with the separate antitrust probe in a potentially awkward way — Align is simultaneously the antitrust DEFENDANT in Brussels and the patent-infringement PLAINTIFF against a China-based rival in two other venues). No verdicts/rulings from any of the three jurisdictions were found in sources reviewed this run — material OPEN item, see O-series.
- I6. **Balance sheet is a genuine strength, not a caveat**: net cash position (E33: $1.059B cash, zero credit-facility borrowings) is a meaningfully different starting point than GEHC's leveraged position in the prior dossier in this batch. This gives Align real flexibility to absorb litigation costs, continue buybacks (E35: $800M still authorized), and weather the EU investigation without near-term solvency/credit concerns, regardless of how the antitrust matter resolves.

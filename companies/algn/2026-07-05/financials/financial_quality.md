Unlike GEHC's still-unresolved "is the tariff/margin shock transient or structural" open question (only one quarter of post-shock data at GEHC's as_of), ALGN's dossier has TWO consecutive quarters of post-restructuring data (Q4 2025, Q1 2026) both confirming the recovery is real:

- Stated FY2025 target: GAAP operating margin ~13.0-14.0%, non-GAAP ~22.5%+ — both achieved per the full-year figures available [facts.md E19-E20, E45]
- FY2026 guidance: a further ~400bps GAAP operating margin improvement to "slightly below 18.0%," non-GAAP to ~23.7% [facts.md E42] — Q1 2026's 13.6% GAAP operating margin is running roughly in line with (not yet ahead of) a linear path toward that full-year guide, which is a normal seasonal pattern (Q1 is typically ALGN's seasonally softer quarter based on general med-device/consumer-elective seasonality, not independently confirmed via a multi-year quarterly cadence this run)

**Verdict**: This is a genuinely stronger financial-quality signal than GEHC's equivalent situation in this batch — a CONFIRMED (not merely guided) recovery with 2 quarters of supporting data, versus GEHC's single-quarter, not-yet-validated "transient tariff" claim.

| Item | Period | Impact | Recurring? |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2025 restructuring charges | Mostly Q3 2025 | $150-170M (~$40M cash) | **NO** — one-time, already substantially recognized; FY2026 guidance reflects the POST-restructuring cost base |
| Q1 2026 legal settlement expense | Q1 2026 | $30.6M, embedded in the gross-margin line | **UNCLEAR** — described as a "legal settlement," which by nature is typically one-time, but given the VOLUME of concurrent litigation (Straumann, Angelalign x3, EU antitrust), it is plausible (not confirmed) that further legal-expense drag could recur in coming quarters depending on how the pending matters resolve. | EU antitrust investigation | Opened 2026-06-30, ongoing | No P&L impact confirmed yet (investigation just opened) | Future legal-defense costs + potential fine (unquantified, statutory EU ceiling up to 10% of global revenue, NOT a probability-weighted estimate) are a genuine forward contingent liability, not yet reflected in any reported period this run |

**Read**: Unlike GEHC (cost-side one-offs depressing earnings, arguably making current earnings a conservative baseline) or a GEV-style pattern (one-off GAINS inflating earnings), ALGN's one-off registry is MIXED: the restructuring charges are a clearly-completed, one-time cost already absorbed; the $30.6M legal settlement is ambiguous (could recur given the litigation volume); and the EU antitrust matter is a genuine, unquantified FORWARD contingent risk not yet in any reported number. This means current Q1 2026 reported earnings likely UNDERSTATE the clean run-rate slightly (given the one-time legal settlement drag) but the forward risk registry (potential EU fine, ongoing legal costs, potential adverse patent rulings) is NOT yet reflected anywhere in the financials — a genuine asymmetry that valuation.md and the scenario model must account for explicitly rather than simply extrapolating Q1 2026 margins forward.

## Financial Quality Verdict

**GOOD, and in several respects (revenue re-acceleration, margin recovery being multi-quarter-CONFIRMED rather than single-quarter-hoped-for, net cash balance sheet, OCF exceeding net income) BETTER than GEHC's financial-quality profile in the same batch.** The core financial engine (Clear Aligner segment, ~80% of revenue) is genuinely re-accelerating post-restructuring with real, multi-quarter confirming evidence, not just management's forward guidance. The primary financial-quality CAVEAT is not embedded in the historical numbers themselves but in the FORWARD legal/regulatory contingent-risk registry (EU antitrust, Straumann/Angelalign litigation outcomes) — none of which have hit the P&L yet, and all of which are binary, unresolved, and could move materially in either direction. This supports treating ALGN's CURRENT financial quality as genuinely strong, while treating its FORWARD financial-quality trajectory as having real, quantifiable-in-neither-direction-yet legal/regulatory tail risk — a distinct risk profile from GEHC's (where the risk is a slow-moving, already-partially-quantified competitive/margin trend, not a binary legal outcome).

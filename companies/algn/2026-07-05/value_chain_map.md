## Upstream → ALGN → Downstream

**Upstream inputs**:
- Specialty thermoplastic polymer materials (multilayer aligner material — the subject of Align's own patent-infringement claims against both Straumann/ClearCorrect and Angelalign [facts.md E47, E51]), meaning the MATERIAL SCIENCE itself (not just software/design) is a claimed differentiator.
- Precision manufacturing / 3D printing capacity for the sequential aligner-tray production process (each case requires a unique multi-stage tray set per patient).
- Scanner hardware components (imaging sensors, optics) for iTero manufacturing.
- Software engineering talent (ClinCheck AI, exocad CAD/CAM) — an IP/talent input rather than a physical commodity input, structurally different from GEHC's semiconductor/detector-material upstream exposure.

**ALGN internal value-add**:
- R&D / materials science (aligner polymer formulations, "power ridge"/"bite ramp" mechanical features — both explicitly named in the Angelalign patent suits [facts.md E51], confirming these are viewed internally as genuinely defensible IP, not incidental design choices)
- Digital-imaging + AI software stack: iTero capture hardware → exocad CAD/CAM design → ClinCheck AI treatment-planning automation [facts.md E64-E68]
- Custom manufacturing at scale (mass-customization: every case is patient-unique, produced via automated/semi-automated production lines)
- Direct sales + clinical training organization (doctor acquisition/onboarding — "GP engagement" is an explicitly stated 2026 strategic priority [facts.md E68])
- Legal/IP enforcement function (an unusually active internal capability given the simultaneous 3-jurisdiction Angelalign litigation plus the separate Straumann/ClearCorrect suit plus the ITC complaint — this is real organizational investment in defending the moat, not passive IP ownership)

**Downstream customers**:
- Orthodontists (specialist doctors — the traditional, highest-value channel)
- General dentists / GPs (an EXPANSION channel explicitly targeted for growth — "GP engagement" priority [facts.md E68] — lower average case complexity but larger addressable doctor population)
- Patients (end consumer, but ALGN's direct commercial relationship is with the DOCTOR, who bills the patient — a B2B2C model, distinct from GEHC's more purely B2B hospital-capex model)
- Restorative/implant dentistry practices (exocad + iTero standalone buyers with no Invisalign attach — a separate, smaller addressable market for the Systems and Services segment)

## Where the Profit Pools Sit

| Value chain link | Profit pool depth | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Clear Aligner case manufacturing | **Deep** | ~80% of revenue, 70.8% gross margin (Q1 2026); re-accelerating (+7.4% YoY) post-restructuring; the primary profit engine |
| iTero scanner hardware | Medium, but STRATEGIC beyond its own P&L | Smaller, slower-growing (+0.9% YoY) on a standalone basis, but the subject of the EU antitrust probe precisely because it may function as a gate to the much larger Clear Aligner pool — the profit pool here may be better measured as "Clear Aligner cases UNLOCKED" rather than scanner hardware margin itself |
| exocad CAD/CAM software | Unclear, bundled | Part of the same Systems and Services line; genuine standalone addressable market (restorative/implant dentistry) not broken out separately this run |
| ClinCheck AI treatment-planning | **Not separately monetized (bundled)** | ClinCheck Live Plan is a workflow/cycle-time improvement (days→minutes) bundled into the existing case fee, not a separately priced AI product — similar to GEHC's finding that AI features currently defend/enable existing revenue rather than commanding an explicit price premium |
| Legal/IP enforcement (patents) | N/A — cost center, not profit pool | But genuinely load-bearing for protecting the other pools; the multi-jurisdiction Angelalign campaign + Straumann suit + ITC complaint represent real, ongoing legal spend defending the aligner-material and scanner-workflow IP |

## Bottleneck Candidates (developed further in bottleneck_map.md)

1. **Scanner-ecosystem interoperability rules** — the single most important, most LIVE bottleneck in this dossier. Whether Align can continue restricting ClinCheck submission to iTero-originated scans (the exact subject of the June 2026 EU investigation) directly determines whether the scanner functions as a genuine competitive gate or merely as a convenience feature. A forced-interoperability remedy (common outcome in EU tying cases) would be a structural moat-erosion event, not just a fine.
2. **Aligner material/mechanical-feature patents** ("power ridge," "bite ramp," multilayer materials) — genuinely litigated, multi-jurisdiction IP, but the FOUNDATIONAL Invisalign patents already expired in 2017 [facts.md E13], meaning the CURRENT patent estate (bite ramp, power ridge, multilayer materials, UI/treatment-planning patents) is a second-generation IP layer, not the original moat. Its enforceability against Angelalign/Straumann is actively being tested in court right now (facts.md O3, O4) — outcome unknown.
3. **Doctor workflow lock-in / switching cost** — once an orthodontic practice standardizes staff training, case-management software, and patient-communication tools around Invisalign/iTero/exocad, switching to a competing system carries real (though not quantified this run) retraining and workflow-disruption friction — analogous to GEHC's installed-base service-contract stickiness, but at the PRACTICE level rather than the hospital-capex level.
4. **China market access / regulatory + distribution** — Angelalign's China home-market advantage (distribution network, regulatory familiarity, price positioning) is the inverse bottleneck working AGAINST Align in China specifically — directly analogous to United Imaging's China advantage over GEHC, though ALGN's specific China revenue exposure is NOT quantified this run (facts.md O6), a genuine gap versus the GEHC dossier's clean geographic breakout.

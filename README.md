# Financial Analysis Lab

This is the research workspace for financial analysis and investment thinking.
It is meant to be the "brain" layer: thesis development, source collection,
company and sector notes, macro views, valuation work, dashboards, and decision
logs.

## Scope

Initial coverage can include:

- U.S. equities
- BTC and crypto market structure
- AI infrastructure and software companies
- Macro conditions that affect risk assets
- Personal portfolio research and decision records

## Output Formats

The repo is organized for several kinds of work:

- Research memos and market maps
- Company and sector templates
- Macro notes
- Valuation tables and dashboards
- Data collection scripts
- Source policies and citation records

## Boundary

This repo is analysis-first. It does not place trades, send orders, or manage
live execution. If an execution layer is built later, it should live separately
and treat this repo as research input.

## Repository Map

- `theses/` - market maps, investment theses, and decision memos
- `companies/` - company-specific research notes
- `sectors/` - industry and sector research notes
- `macro/` - macroeconomic and market regime notes
- `data/` - documented data inputs and derived datasets
- `notebooks/` - exploratory analysis notebooks
- `scripts/` - repeatable data or analysis scripts
- `sources/` - source quality rules, citation policy, and raw/source manifests
- `frameworks/` - reusable investor frameworks, checklists, and mental models

## Working Loop

1. Start with a question.
2. Gather primary and high-quality secondary sources.
3. Separate facts, estimates, and opinions.
4. Write the thesis with explicit assumptions.
5. Track what would change your mind.
6. Revisit the memo when new data arrives.

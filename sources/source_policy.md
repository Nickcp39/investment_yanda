# Source Policy

## Source Hierarchy

1. Primary sources: company filings, earnings transcripts, investor
   presentations, official statistics, central bank releases, exchange data, and
   regulatory documents.
2. High-quality secondary sources: reputable financial media, expert interviews,
   industry reports, and data vendors with clear methodology.
3. Market commentary: useful for sentiment and variant perception, but not a
   substitute for primary evidence.
4. Social media: useful only as a lead or sentiment signal unless backed by
   verifiable data.

## Citation Format

For every important claim, capture:

- Source name
- Publication or retrieval date
- Link or local file path
- Claim supported
- Notes on reliability or limitations

## Time-Sensitive Claims

For prices, market caps, rates, yields, rankings, management roles, laws, and
recent news, include the exact date. Re-check before using the claim in a fresh
memo.

## Evidence Discipline

- Separate reported facts from estimates.
- Label assumptions clearly.
- Prefer ranges over false precision.
- Write down contrary evidence.
- Keep a list of facts that would invalidate the thesis.
